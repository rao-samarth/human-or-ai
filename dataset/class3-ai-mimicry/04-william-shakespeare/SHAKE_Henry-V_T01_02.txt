He paced the small chamber, the moonlight casting his long shadow upon the floorboards as if it were a second, silent self. "Wherefore dost thou not seek thy rest, husband?" she asked from the warmth of the bed, her voice a soft reproach. He halted, his back to her, and gazed through the leaded glass at the sleeping village below. "Rest?" he uttered, the word a hollow sound. "How can peace find harbour in this breast, when every choice I make doth steer the fortunes of all those who slumber? I must decide if the widow's last coins shall be taken for the levy, or if the town's defense shall stand the weaker for it. They see a magistrate, a man of sober judgement; they do not see the thousand warring doubts that make my mind a battlefield, where every victory is but a new and deeper sorrow for the fallen."
