O, what a strange and cruel alchemy doth this long road work upon a faithful bond, to turn its gold to leaden silence! For since his eyes did first fall upon that lady in the tavern three nights hence, my sworn companion hath become a stranger to me, though we share this very dust and air. He prattles on of her bright wit and gentle form, a litany of praise I know too well, for 'tis the self-same prayer my own heart hath long kept secret. Each word of his honest adoration is a fresh wound to my soul, and I must feign a brother's joy whilst envy, that green-eyed fiend, doth gnaw upon my spirit. I look upon his face, once a comfort and a mirror to my own, and see naught but the architect of my coming sorrow.
