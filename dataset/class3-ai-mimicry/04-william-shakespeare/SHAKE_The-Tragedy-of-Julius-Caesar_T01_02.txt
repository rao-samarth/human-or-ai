Alas, what wretched crossroads is this, where my sworn duty and my soul's own virtue must take such different paths? My lord, to whom I pledged my very life upon a hallowed blade, now bids me ride forth and be the instrument of such a wicked enterprise. He calls it service; my own heart cries out that it is naught but blackest sin. To disobey is to be branded traitor, a faithless dog unworthy of his keep, and every man of worth shall shun my countenance.
