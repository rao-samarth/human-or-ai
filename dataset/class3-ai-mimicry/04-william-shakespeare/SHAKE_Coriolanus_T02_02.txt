The air in the great workshop, erewhile so thick with muttered discontent and the sour scent of resentment, was on a sudden cleared by the master's coming. He, who was ever a figure of distant command, did this day descend from his high office, not with ledgers and rebukes, but with a countenance of altered mien. He spoke to us then, not as a lord to his indentured servants, but as a pilot to his mariners who face the same sea, promising that the ship’s fortunes should henceforth be all men’s fortunes. A murmur arose amongst the benches, a sound not of the old and bitter sort, but one of cautious wonder, as if a spring had broken forth from barren ground. In that moment, the tools in our hands felt less like the chains of our station and more the very keys to a common prosperity, and the great chasm 'twixt the counting-house and the dusty floor did seem, at long last, a bridgeable divide.
