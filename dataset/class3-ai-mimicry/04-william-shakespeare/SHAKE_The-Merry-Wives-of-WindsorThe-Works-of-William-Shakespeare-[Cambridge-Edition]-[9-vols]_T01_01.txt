Master Borlace did present his ledger with a flourish, as if it were a conquered banner, and did proclaim its faultless sums with a voice that shook the very dust from the rafters. He, and he alone, had balanced the accounts in but half a day, a feat beyond the reach of lesser minds. When Mistress Annelise, whose quiet quill had scratched in the corner all the while, did venture forth a gentle query on a single column's logic, he laughed with scorn. "Peace, woman," he boomed, "thy mind is for the hearth, not for the heavy work of figures." Yet she stood her ground, her gaze as sharp and steady as a winter star. "Then pray, good sir," she replied, her voice a sudden steel that cut through his conceit, "explain how this one shipment, by your own magnificent hand, hath paid its due both on this page and on the next, thus creating profit from a phantom coin and making our rich venture seem a beggar's loss." A sudden hush descended, and in that dreadful quiet, his grand edifice of pride crumbled to but worthless sand.
