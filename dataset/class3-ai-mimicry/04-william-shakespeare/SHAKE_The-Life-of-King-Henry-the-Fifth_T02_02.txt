To our general, whose valiant charge hath brought us such splendid triumph!" cried the young duke, raising his goblet high amidst the revelry. The old soldier, his face a map of ancient scars, leaned closer, his voice a low rumble beneath the music. "You speak of triumph, my lord, a fine word for so fine a hall. But tell me, what triumph is there in the sound a boy makes, crying for his mother as the life quits his body in some forgotten, muddy ditch? The songs are of gleaming banners and the clash of noble steel, yet they make no mention of the ague, the rot, and the endless, weeping rain. They praise the crown of victory, but forget it is paid for with a thousand crowns of shattered bone and a river of common tears. Prithee, drink your wine, young sir, and let the poets keep their pretty lies; for the truth of it hath a most bitter taste.
