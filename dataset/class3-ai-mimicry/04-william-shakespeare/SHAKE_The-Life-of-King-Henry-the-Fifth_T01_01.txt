He sat alone, the midnight oil his sole companion, and stared upon the ledgers which spake not of coin but of souls. By day, he was a mountain, a fixed star to which all lesser men did turn their hopeful gaze, hearing his pronouncements as if from an oracle. But what knew they of the quaking heart within that stony breast, or how each stroke of his quill might seal the fortune or the want of a hundred families? In their presence he must ever play the part, feigning a surety his own spirit sorely lacked, and the clamour of their ready praise oft seemed a hollow echo against the silent court of his own conscience.
