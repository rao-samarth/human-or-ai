Cease this gilded prattle!" I cried, as the carriage jolted o'er the rutted lane. "You speak of duty and of noble cause, yet your every word is but a painted arrow, fletched with pretty lies to find its mark within a trusting heart. Dost thou take me for some simple fool, to be swayed by such a sweet and hollow music? You frame your reasons in a cunning guise, shaping them to seem as righteousness itself, whilst all the while they serve your own dark end. Nay, my lord; your tongue, a weaver's shuttle, flies so quick it makes a handsome cloth of foulest thread, a pleasing sound of most unholy thoughts. Save your breath, for I am not so easily unspooled, nor is my soul a bauble to be won by clever sounds and promises that break upon the ear.
