Hark, for what cause did we suffer our own fellowship to wither on the vine? Our fields grew choked with weeds of spite, and the ale in our cups turned sour with suspicion, not for want of good barley, but from the bitter gall of our own hearts. A foul wind, methinks, did blow through these quiet lanes, setting neighbour against neighbour, 'til the simple greeting of the morn was a thing of peril. Yet even the longest night must yield to dawn. We have stood together in this humble clearing, not as accusers, but as men who have seen the abyss and recoiled from its edge. Henceforth, our compact is this: to mend the fence broken by a careless word, to share the loaf though the harvest be lean, and to see in every face not the shadow of some past grievance, but the promise of a shared tomorrow. For we have learned, at grievous cost, that a house divided by its own hand provides but a cold and ruinous shelter.
