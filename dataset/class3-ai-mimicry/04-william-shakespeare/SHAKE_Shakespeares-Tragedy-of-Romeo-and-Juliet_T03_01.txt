A single sneer, a ghost of a smile from his rival across the chamber, was all the spark the dry tinder of his temper did require. In that hot instant, reason fled his heart, and he, a man possessed, lunged forth with a roar that tore the evening’s harmony to shreds. The great table, laden with the feast’s abundance, heaved upwards and crashed upon the stones, its bounty scattered like the spoils of some rude war. Wine bled into the rushes, and silver platters rang a dreadful clamour. Then came a hush, a sudden, fearful stillness, wherein he saw his folly mirrored in a hundred wide-eyed stares and, most terribly, in the cold and rising fury of his Prince. The guards’ hands were on his shoulders ere he could draw another breath, his honour, his station, and his liberty all forfeit for a moment’s worthless pride.
