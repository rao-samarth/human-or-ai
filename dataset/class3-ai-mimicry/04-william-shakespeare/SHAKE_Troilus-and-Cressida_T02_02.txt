Oh, uncle, prithee do not speak of ledgers and of lands! My Lord Lysander’s heart is my true dowry, and his whispered word my only coin.” Hark, the little songbird chirps a summer tune, her uncle muttered, not unkindly, yet with a weight that chilled the air betwixt them. “A star you deem your love, fixed in the firmament; yet even stars must yield to the grey dawn of duty. When winter’s frost doth bite, and the larders grow thin, will sonnets warm the hearth, or pretty vows fill a hungry belly? Nay, child, that heart you so prize is yet beholden to your father’s fortunes, and that whispered word shall soon be lost amidst the clamour of tenants and the vexations of a barren field. Love, such as yours, is but a gilded cage, my dear niece, whose bars are forged of a baser metal than you yet perceive.
