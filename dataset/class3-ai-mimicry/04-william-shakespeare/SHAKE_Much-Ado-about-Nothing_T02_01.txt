Must we proceed with such a foul disguise, my lord?” the younger man whispered, his face pale in the lone candlelight. “To praise him to his teeth, to bow and fawn when all my soul cries out for his swift ruin; it doth make a villain of me.” The elder statesman turned, his weary eyes holding no comfort. “And what art thou, a simpleton? Wouldst thou throw plain truth against a fortress wall and hope it crumbles? Nay, a lie well-told is but a sharper blade than any honest steel when wielded for a righteous cause. We speak with sweetened words to mask the bitter draught we brew for him; we fashion this false amity to be the very ground from which his downfall springs. Let not thy squeamish heart betray our purpose, for in this wretched court, a virtuous tongue is but a quick path to a silent grave. Clothe thyself in falsehood; ‘tis the only armour that shall keep our noble end alive.
