The midnight oil did burn but low, casting long and troubled shadows upon the chamber walls, where paced a man whose new-won honours brought him little ease. For his desire, a most insatiate and fev’rish thing, did ever crave for more, whispering of thrones beyond his rightful reach. It was a hunger that fed upon his very soul, making a banquet of his peace and turning goodly sleep to a most frightful enemy. He had built his house upon the bones of better men and thought the walls most strong; yet the very mortar, mix’d with perfidy and treason, did promise naught but ruin. What, after all, is such a climb but a fool’s ascent to a high and lonely precipice, from which the only path remaining leads downward into that dark abyss he so cunningly dug for others?
