In the great square, where commerce and clamour make their daily war, a silence fell, for the magistrate's decree was to be read aloud. A thousand eyes, hungry for a villain's fall, fixed upon the wretched soul brought forth in chains; he, a simple carter accused of some grand theft, wore not the mien of a cunning rogue, but the bewildered look of a beast caught in a snare it cannot comprehend. The law, that great and heavy book, had been shut upon his fate, and the crowd did roar its satisfaction. Yet who amongst that baying throng, or on that gilded bench, could truly know the heart's own secret? They cheered the sentence, for it gave them comfort and a sense of order restored.
