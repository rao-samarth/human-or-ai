He watches from the market cross, where humanity's great tide doth ebb and flow, and marks how all is but a fleeting show. That proud merchant, whose voice rings like a coin new-struck, shall soon be naught but hollow bone and silent dust. The mason's finest work, this very stone whereon he sits, already yields unto the patient siege of rain and wind; its sharp-cut glories growen soft with age. Where now are the kings who raised these walls, or the legions that once trod these lanes with brazen pomp? Their names are whispers, their monuments a feast for creeping moss and the slow, grinding tooth of years. Yea, even this clamorous, breathing city, this boast of mortal craft, must in its turn fall quiet, and surrender all its prideful towers unto that final, conquering stillness which consumes all things ere long.
