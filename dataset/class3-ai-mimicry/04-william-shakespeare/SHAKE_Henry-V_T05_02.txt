Observe you that smiling gentleman, whose tongue doth paint such pretty pictures in the air?” the elder lord murmured, his gaze fixed across the crowded hall. His younger companion followed his eye to where a favoured courtier was bent low in speech with a wealthy widow, her face, once a mask of sober grief, now alight with some newfound hope. “With what fine artifice he doth construct his speech; each word a polished stone set just so, building a bridge from his ambition to her coffers. He speaks not of himself, nay, but of her lonely state, her forgotten virtues, her need for a firm hand to guide her fortune—a fortune that shall, by happy chance, become his own. Mark me, the lady thinks these notions are her own heart’s fresh discovery, when they are but seeds he planted in her ear a moment since, which now do bloom at his command.
