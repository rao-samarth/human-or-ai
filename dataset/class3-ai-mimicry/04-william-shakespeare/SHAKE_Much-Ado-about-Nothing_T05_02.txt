Must I then play this part until the dawn, and fashion my poor countenance into a mask of sweet content? My father’s grim command doth echo still within these walls, that I must entertain this suitor’s empty praise and smile upon the bargain of my future state. Forsooth, every whispered word in the great hall feels a judgment, and every feigned courtesy a link in the chain that binds me to a fate not of my own design. They speak of honour, of alliance, of a name secured; yet in this silent chamber, I see naught but a gilded cage prepared for me, its door held open by a dozen watchful eyes. My heart, it seems, is but a trifle in this grand exchange, a foolish, beating thing to be subdued and silenced, lest its truth bring ruin upon us all. What choice hath a daughter but to bend her will to the world's harsh decree?
