Hark, what venom springs from a soul consumed by envious spite! The young clerk, whose nimble wit and honest toil had won the master's eye, stood praised before all in the bustling counting-house. But his elder, a man of leaden spirit and a sour heart, could not suffer such swift advancement. With a cunning hand and a purpose as black as ink, he did contrive a flaw within the great ledger, a false accounting that pointed its foul finger at the innocent. The master, a man of swift judgment and no patience for deceit, spied the blot at once. He called forth the youth, and with a voice like thunder, did denounce him as a thief before the assembled company! No trial was held, no defense was heard; the boy was cast into the street, his reputation undone in a moment, his future a barren waste. The elder villain watched from the shadows, his triumph a cold and bitter draught that would, in time, surely poison him as well.
