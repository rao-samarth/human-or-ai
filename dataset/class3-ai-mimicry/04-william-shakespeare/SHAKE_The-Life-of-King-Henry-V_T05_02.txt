The Lord Protector, whose pronouncements did shake the very timbers of the statehouse, crept with a great and studied stealth toward the larder, his noble stomach rumbling for a slice of yesterday's pie. Forsooth, he who commanded legions and made foreign kings to tremble had become a common thief in his own dominion, a shadow seeking cheese. But mark what mischief fortune plays upon the great! His royal toe, unarmoured by its daytime boot, did find in the gloom a low and villainous stool, placed there by some witless maid. Forth from his throat came not a roar to rival thunder, but a piteous, unmanly squeal, like a piglet trapped beneath a gate. He hopped then, a frantic stork in his nightshirt, clutching the offended digit and whispering such base curses against the carpenter's art as would make a hangman blush. In that moment, the fearsome ruler was but a man, undone by oak, his vast authority shrinking to the small, throbbing world of his own pain.
