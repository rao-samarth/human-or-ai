I have harboured within my breast a most insatiate fever, a desperate hunger for a station so high it rendered me blind to the goodly earth whereon we all stood. Each honour I pursued, each rung I gained, was but another bond of kinship that I didst spurn and sunder underfoot. And for what prize? Behold the barren banquet before you, the silent halls where laughter hath died; this hollowed-out inheritance is the sole kingdom I have won.
