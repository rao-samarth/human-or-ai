Pray, attend me now and see what petty arts may topple greater men. Yon merchant, who but yestermorn did walk as if he owned the very cobblestones beneath his feet, now shrinks into the shadows, a pariah scorned by all. And for what great crime, you ask? For what treasonous act is he so undone? I tell you, 'twas for nothing more substantial than a breath of air I fashioned into a rumour; a cunning phrase let slip in a courtier's ear, which, like a swift and noiseless plague, did spread from tongue to tongue until his good name was but a rotten husk. They say the sword is mightiest, but a well-aimed word can slay a reputation whilst the body yet draws breath. He deems it fortune's cruel turn, the poor fool, but 'twas my invention, my mere whisper, that hath unmade him utterly.
