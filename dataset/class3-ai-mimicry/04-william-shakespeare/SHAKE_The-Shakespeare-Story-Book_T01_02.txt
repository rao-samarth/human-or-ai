My friend, you burn with such a fire as might consume a forest, or might warm a hearth for all the winter long. This thirst for place, this craving to be first, is a most potent wine that mads the brain and makes a rival of a brother. But tame this spirit; turn its mighty force from scaling brittle walls of rank and envy. Let it instead perfect your daily task, make your accounts the truest, your letters the most fair. In this, you shall not climb above your peers, but build amongst them such a firm foundation of respect that you are lifted by their common will, a nobler rise by far.
