Hark, what a mighty war is waged within this chamber, not with sword and shield, but with the gossamer threads of what-if and perchance; a battle so fierce it renders the limbs as still as a churchyard stone. A most noble conflict, forsooth. Here sits a man, a general of his own undoing, who musters a thousand fears why the ink should not touch the page, each one a more potent soldier than the last, until the simple act of writing his name becomes a siege too perilous to undertake. He weighs each word upon the scales of dire consequence until it is heavier than lead, and in this grand deliberation, the candle burns low, the night grows old, and the deed itself—a paltry thing, a mere scratch of a quill—is smothered in the cradle of its own conception. Oh, what a hero of the mind he is, this conqueror of naught but his own resolve
