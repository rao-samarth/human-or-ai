Mark how the music plays and the revellers dance, each one a monarch in his own conceit, and then proclaim this place a house of liberty. Yet look you closer, and see the unseen chains they wear, though forged of golden praise and whispered honours. Here, a man is bound fast by his good name; there, a lady is caged by the slender bars of courtesy. Every smile is a duty, every word a careful measure, lest they tumble from the high perch of their station into ruin. Wherefore do we call this state a freedom? Perchance the wretch who sits in a dark cell, with naught but his own soul for company, hath a wider and more boundless kingdom in his mind than all these lords possess.
