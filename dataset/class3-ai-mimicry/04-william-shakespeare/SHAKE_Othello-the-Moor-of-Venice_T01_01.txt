Hark, see how his hand doth linger upon her arm! The world itself spins with the sight, and a black bile rises in my throat, for this green-eyed fiend hath made its home within my soul. It whispers madness on the wind; it turns a simple glance into a lecherous vow and a soft word into a confession of foulest sin. My reason, once my truest captain, is thrown overboard, and I am left to pilot this wretched vessel through a tempest of my own making! I wouldst seize that man by his collar, and with these hands, wring from him the truth of his intent, though it damn me to the deepest pit! Let the merchants gawk and the children point; they know not the burning forge wherein my peace is hammered into a dagger's sharp and bitter point.
