I sit within this small demesne, its stone embrace a gentle warden. Beyond, the wild heath stretches forth, a boundless sea of green and gold, inviting the soul to wander without let or hindrance. A fool might call this garden a gaol and that wild land true liberty; for what man doth not yearn to cast off his fetters and run where he will, with only the heavens for his roof? Yet I have seen the lone hare hunted on that plain, and the winter winds show no mercy to the unsheltered tree. Here, within these very walls some name a prison, the tender rose is shielded from the blast.
