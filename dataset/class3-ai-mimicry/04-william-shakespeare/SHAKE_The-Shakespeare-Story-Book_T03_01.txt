Hark, see how the goodly bailiff struts along the causeway,” muttered Thomas, pulling his thin cloak tighter against the wind. “One would think by his fine doublet and pious frown that he dines on nought but righteousness and drinks only the tears of repentant sinners.” John chuckled, a dry and rasping sound. “Aye, a fair cover doth oft conceal a rotten book. Last night, I saw this same virtuous man, his face flushed with wine and his hands far too familiar with the tavern maid, cursing his luck at dice. He who presents a saint to the sun may well show a devil to the moon. Take heed, for the world’s judgment is a foolish thing, easily beguiled by polished boots and a solemn tongue, whilst true worth, like a humble violet, is crushed unseen beneath them. Many a soul is but a whited sepulchre: most comely on its face, yet full of naught but decay and dead men’s bones within.
