He stood upon the carter’s stump, this man of sudden consequence, and from his throat poured forth a honeyed thunder that didst remake the very world before our eyes. He spake not of our empty larders nor the coming winter’s chill, but of a dawn wherein each man might be a lord, his labour but a pleasant game. And what a fever did he raise! I saw neighbour turn from neighbour, his eye alight with some new and selfish dream this orator had planted; I saw men who yesterday did curse their meager lot now pledge their final coppers to his airy cause. He built for them a castle in the clouds, and with such cunning skill he laid each stone of breath that they forgot their feet stood planted in the mud. Alas, they were so starved for hope, they did not question what meat this stranger served, nor why he offered such a feast for free.
