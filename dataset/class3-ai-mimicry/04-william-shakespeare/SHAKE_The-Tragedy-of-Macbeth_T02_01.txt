Let lesser men, with fearful, downcast eyes, speak of a destiny writ in the cold and distant heavens, a course from which no mortal soul canst swerve. Such is a coward's comfort. For I do mark it here, amidst this noble company, that the stars themselves seem but pale lamps to light the stage whereon our own resolve doth play the chiefest part. What is this grand design if not a thing to be challenged, to be met with a spirit that fashions its own glory from the raw clay of circumstance? Nay, the future is no iron-bound book to be read with trembling; 'tis a sea of boundless prospect, and we are the captains of our own small vessels, steering not by what is fated, but by the truer compass of a valiant heart.
