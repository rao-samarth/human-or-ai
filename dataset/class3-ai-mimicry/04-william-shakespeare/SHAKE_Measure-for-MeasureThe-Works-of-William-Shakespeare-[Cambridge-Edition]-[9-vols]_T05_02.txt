Hark, cousin, how our good uncle makes merry!” whispered Eleanor, her gaze fixed upon the head of the long table where the old lord held court with booming laughter. “His cup o'erflows, and his spirit with it; surely contentment hath made a fine home in his heart.” Thomas, however, shook his head, a wry shadow crossing his features. “Contentment? Nay, you see but the painted face of a mummer's mask, a clever counterfeit to fool the eye. His mirth is a shield, and those jests are but sharp darts to keep true inquiry at bay. I have seen him when the music fades and the guests are all departed; the laughter dies ere it leaves his lips, and the great hollowness in his eyes could swallow this whole feasting-hall. He performs his joy, sweet cousin, for he much fears the silence where all his true sorrows wait to claim him.
