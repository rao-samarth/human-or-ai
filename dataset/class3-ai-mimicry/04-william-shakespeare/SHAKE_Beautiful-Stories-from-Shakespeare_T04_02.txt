Behold, good sir,” the lad spake, his finger pointing toward the gloaming where the trees did melt into the sky, “how that far wood appears a monstrous beast, ready to swallow the last of the sun’s dying light. My very eyes deceive me, and I know not if what I see be solid earth and timber, or some fleeting vapour conjured by the coming night.” The old man beside him did not turn his head, but answered with a voice as soft as moss. “And what of it, child? If it be a beast, do you hear its roar? If a vapour, can you pass your hand clean through it? We live by what our senses tell us, yet our senses are but poor and foolish servants. They tell us the moon is a silver coin, yet we know it to be a world. Trust not the shape of things, but what they do; a phantom’s blade may draw no blood, though in a dream it feels as sharp as any steel.
