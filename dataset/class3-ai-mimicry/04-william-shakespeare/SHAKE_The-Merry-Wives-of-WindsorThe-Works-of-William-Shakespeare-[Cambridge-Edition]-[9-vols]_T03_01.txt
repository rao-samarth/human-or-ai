See you how the sun doth bless these fields, Master Philip?” the old farmer asked, his hand gesturing toward the rows of green shoots. “Here a man’s sweat buys him honest bread, and his toil a peaceful sleep; what greater fortune can a soul desire?” His guest, whose fine coat was now dusted from the country road, offered a thin, weary smile. “You speak a truth, good sir, that our great lords have long forgot, or perhaps did never learn. At court, they sow not seeds but whispers; their harvest is another’s swift ruin. A smile there is a painted mask for envy, and a soft-given word the poison’d dart to fell a rival. They call your coin base metal, yet their own gold is canker’d with the rust of broken oaths. Here, your hand is calloused from the plough, a noble mark; there, a soft hand is prized, for it hath done no labour save to sign a warrant or to grasp a bribe. Pray keep your sun-blest fields; their gilded halls are but a brighter sort of dungeon.
