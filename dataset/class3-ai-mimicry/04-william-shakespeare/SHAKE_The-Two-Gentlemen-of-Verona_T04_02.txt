This humble merchant's guise doth sit upon me as my very skin, and none here upon this weary road suspecteth I am other than I seem. They see but a common pedlar, his face smudged with the dust of his journey, his back bent not with years but with the feigned weight of his paltry wares. I have schooled my tongue to a rougher, simpler speech, you see, and my gait to a common shuffle; 'tis a far cry from the stride that once commanded notice in finer halls. Let them mark my threadbare coat and offer me their pity; forsooth, their ignorance is my stoutest shield, and their condescension the very key that shall unlock my passage. For who would look twice upon such a lowly creature?
