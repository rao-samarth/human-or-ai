Yestereve, by the market cross where the fishwives cry their wares, a certain puffed-up gallant, whose purse was far heavier than his sense, didst proclaim to any who had no choice but to hear him that a woman’s tongue, though nimble, serves only to echo the great wisdoms first spoken by a man. At this, a simple dame sorting beans nearby, her fingers quicker than his thoughts, did look upon him with a most counterfeit sweetness and inquire, ‘Pray, good sir, if we are but echoes, must we not wait an age for a sound worth repeating from such a hollow vessel as thee?’ A sudden quiet fell upon the stall, more damning than any laughter, and the fine gentleman discovered a most pressing need to study the very dirt upon his own boots, as if finding his own true worth therein.
