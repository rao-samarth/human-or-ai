The great hall, once loud with mirth and music, fell to a sudden, fearful hush. Lord Valerius, his veins afire with spiced wine and the heady incense of flattery, slammed his fist upon the oaken board, the very plates and goblets trembling at his new-found rage. When his father’s grey-haired counsellor did but offer a gentle caution against some rash decree, a brutish shadow passed across the lord’s visage. "Dost thou presume to temper my will?" he roared, his voice cracking the festive air. "I am the state! My whim is now the law, my pleasure the sole compass for this realm!" He snatched a jewelled dagger from his belt—not for use, but as a glittering threat—and pointed it at the old man’s throat, his eyes promising a swift and bloody lesson to any who might dare question him again. In that terrible moment, all saw the noble prince they had loved vanish, and in his place stood a tyrant, born in the instant he tasted unbridled command.
