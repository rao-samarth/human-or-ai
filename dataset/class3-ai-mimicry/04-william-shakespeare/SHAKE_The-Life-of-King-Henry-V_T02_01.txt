Hark, Humphrey, dost thou not feel the trumpet's call within thy very soul? Forsooth, I see it now: our banners snapping bravely in the wind, a thousand helms that glint against the sun, and our names sung by maids for ages hence! The young fool, whose chin had yet to find acquaintance with a razor, near skipped in the sucking mud of the king's highway. His elder companion, however, merely grunted, and with a great and sorrowful effort, sought to adjust some unseen torment within his woolen hose. "I feel a plaguey itch in a place no honest man can scratch in public view," he muttered, "and I hear naught but the squelch of my boot, which hath decided to part company with its sole. Sing of my name, you say? Let them first sing of a dry sock, or a biscuit that does not fight back with the tenacity of a cornered badger. Glory is a poor poultice for a raw-rubbed thigh, my lad; a very poor poultice indeed."
