Speak not to me of peace! Why dost thou seek these quiet pastures? Dost think the whispering reeds will grant thee pardon, or this gentle stream absolve thy grievous act? Look how the sun itself doth hide its face from thee; I see no solace in thy haunted gaze, only a hollow ruin where a soul once stood.” He turned from me then, his shoulders stooped as if by some great, unseen weight. “You mistake my purpose, friend,” he answered, his voice a rasp against the evening’s calm. “I come not for comfort, but because this very stillness makes the clamour in my heart the louder. The lowing of the kine sounds a judgment in mine ears, and every rustling leaf doth whisper of the deed. There is no fleeing from this court that sits forever in my breast, where I myself am judge and hangman both.
