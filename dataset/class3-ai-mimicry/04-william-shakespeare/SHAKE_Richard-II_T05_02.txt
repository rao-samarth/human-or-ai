The Duke raised his cup, its ruby glitter a foul mockery of the blood so lately shed, and forced a smile that felt a stranger to his lips. Must he play the gracious host when all his heart was tomb'd with his dead son? These flattering lords, with their easy jests and flowing wine, saw naught but the sovereign's placid brow; they could not spy the tempest that did rage within, nor feel the icy hand of grief that clutched his very soul. To let one tear escape, to show the fissure in his spirit, were to invite the jackals to his gate, to prove the state as vulnerable as the man. And so he must sit upon this gilded chair, a statue carved of duty, and let the merriment wash over him like a tide of poison, praying for the dark when he might cast off this heavy crown and be but a father, alone with his unending loss.
