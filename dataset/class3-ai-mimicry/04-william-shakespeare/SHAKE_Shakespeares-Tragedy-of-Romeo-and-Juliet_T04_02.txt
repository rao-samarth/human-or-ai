"Cease this talk, I pray you," the elder man hissed, his voice a dry rasp beneath the market’s clamour, though his eyes darted about to see who might attend their words. "Would you have all the city know you prize a fickle passion above your sworn purpose? Your heart is a small and private country, oft given to rebellion, but your name is the kingdom you must govern for all to see." The younger man’s face was a mask of pale resolve. "And what good is such a kingdom, sir," he countered, his voice low but clear, "if its king be a stranger to himself? Must I forever chain my soul's own true desire to the cold iron of another's expectation? I tell you, such a life is but a gilded cage, and the songbird within perishes for want of its own sky." A passing merchant slowed his step, his ears pricked by the heat of their exchange, before his wife pulled him onward into the throng.
