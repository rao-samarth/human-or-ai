Across the workshop, my master's two sons stand like statues hewn from opposing stones, their silence a far louder clangour than any hammer-fall upon the anvil. The elder grips a chisel so his knuckles show like bone, his eyes fixed upon some ancient grievance; whilst the younger turns his back, feigning study of a new design, yet the rigid set of his shoulders doth betray the festering malice within. May heaven curse this wretched strife, for their father's legacy hath become a canker that corrupts their blood and sours the honest labour of all who toil here. Now I must ask one of them for the key to the strongbox, but to which should I direct my speech? To approach the one is to slight the other, and by my simple need, I am made a pawn in their bitter game, forced to declare for a side in a war whose origins I scarcely comprehend
