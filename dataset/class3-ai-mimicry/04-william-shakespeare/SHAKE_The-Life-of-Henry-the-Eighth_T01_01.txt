Look you there, upon that barren hill, how the bones of a great house stand stark against the grey-weeping sky. A lord of some repute once held his revels in those halls, I warrant; a man whose command was the swift breath of life or death, and whose favour was a sun to warm the ambitions of lesser men. And for all his might, what is he now? A forgotten name, his sovereignty a feast for crows and creeping ivy.
