Alas, what devilry hath Cupid played upon this quiet town, to turn my soul's own comfort into such a source of torment? For years, his company was as the gentle sun, a steady warmth that asked for naught but honest fellowship; our laughter echoed from the baker's wall to the old stone bridge, a simple and a blameless music. But now, a sudden and a wicked change is wrought! His very presence, once a balm, is a sharp and yearning ache within my breast. Each friendly touch doth burn me like a brand, and every glance I steal feels like a wretched theft from the trust we built. To give this passion voice would be to slay the friend I cherish most, to murder what was pure; yet to keep it silent is a slow and private death. O, cursed state, to be so caught betwixt two loves, where either path I choose leads straightaway to sorrow's door
