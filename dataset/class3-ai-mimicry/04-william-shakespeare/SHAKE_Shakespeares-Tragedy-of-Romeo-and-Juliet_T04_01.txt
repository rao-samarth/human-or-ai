Behold this room of kin, where every smile is a calculation and each toast a pledge not to the heart, but to the house. I must play my part in this grand pageant, nodding to the talk of dowries and dominions, whilst my own spirit, a restless ghost, doth pace the confines of my ribs. What is a man but two contrary selves locked in a single breast? One is the public figure, the name that is spoken with reverence or with fear, whose every action serves the greater weal. The other is a creature of shadow and silence, who yearns for simple suns and treasures things of no account to the wide world.
