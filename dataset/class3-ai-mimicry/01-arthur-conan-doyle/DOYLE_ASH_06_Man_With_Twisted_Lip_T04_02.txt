One gazes upon this teeming river of humanity, this relentless metropolitan crush, and despairs at the lamentable indolence of the average intellect. Each man is a walking puzzle, a veritable encyclopaedia of his own follies and professions, yet his neighbour perceives nothing beyond the cut of a coat. It is a spectacle of singular blindness.
