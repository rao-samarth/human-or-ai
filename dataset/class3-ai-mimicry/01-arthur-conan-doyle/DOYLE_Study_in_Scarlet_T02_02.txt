His companion stared, utterly incredulous, as the tall man gestured with his chin towards a gentleman now crossing the green. “A first violinist, and recently from a rather strenuous rehearsal, I should venture.” He spoke with the cool, detached air of a lecturer demonstrating an elementary principle. “You perceive, of course, the pronounced calluses upon the fingertips of his left hand, yet you failed to connect them with the faint, almost powdered, stain of rosin upon his right coat-sleeve. Observe the slight, habitual elevation of his left shoulder, a posture ingrained by countless hours cradling his instrument just so, beneath the chin. These are not disparate facts thrown together by coincidence; they are, to the trained eye, merely sequential chapters in a man’s daily history. Most men see; they do not, however, trouble themselves to observe, and therein lies the entire distinction.”
