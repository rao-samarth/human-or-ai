"Faster, man, faster!" I cried, gripping the side of the lurching hansom as we clattered over the cobblestones. Ahead of us, our quarry’s carriage had come to an abrupt halt, blocking the narrow thoroughfare. The fellow himself was now upon the street, a picture of flustered indignation, gesticulating wildly at a staged collision with a costermonger’s cart—an incident too perfectly timed to be mere chance. His cries of outrage, his feigned concern for a spilled basket of apples, it was all a most audacious piece of stagecraft. “Do not be taken in by it,” my companion muttered, his eyes narrowed to slits. “Observe the men now gathering at the alley-mouths. This is no accident; it is a carefully laid snare, a ruse designed to halt our pursuit and draw us into the open.” The entire scene, a frantic tableau of civic disorder, was nothing more than a villain’s curtain, raised to conceal the far darker play being set just beyond our view.
