As I navigated the teeming thoroughfare that evening, a profound melancholy settled upon my spirit, for it was a city of ghosts living alongside men. One could not but observe the stark dichotomy: here, the grand and imposing facades of our own administration, their gas-lamps casting a civilised glow upon the pavement; and there, a mere stone's throw away, the wretched, unlit warrens wherein the city's original inhabitants were now confined. What grand pronouncements of progress and enlightenment could possibly justify this chasm of human experience? It struck me then with a dreadful and undeniable certainty that we had not, in truth, brought light to this place.
