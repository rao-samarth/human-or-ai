Mr. Albright cornered his nephew by the hearth, his voice a low and venomous thing beneath the distant chatter of the parlour. "Do you imagine for a single instant, Arthur, that the rumours of your scandalous conduct have not reached these walls?" he began, foregoing all pleasantry. "It is not your person I seek to chastise, but the grievous wound you have inflicted upon our very name—a name which, until this week, stood for integrity and sound judgment." The younger man’s jaw tightened, but he met the furious gaze without flinching. "I spoke only what was true, Uncle." "Truth!" the elder man hissed, striking the mantelpiece with his fist.
