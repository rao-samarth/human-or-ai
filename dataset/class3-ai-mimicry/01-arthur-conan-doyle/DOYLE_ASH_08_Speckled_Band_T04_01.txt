His opponent was his own son, a slight, pale youth whose victories were found not upon the rugby pitch but within the dusty pages of forgotten tomes. It was a lamentable sight, in its way; the old lion, whose roar had once shaken boardrooms and hunting lodges alike, was now utterly cornered by a series of quiet, deliberate movements upon the chequered field.
