He closed the ledger with a quiet finality that seemed to echo in the silent counting-house. I confess a certain disquiet had settled upon me at the swift and utter financial ruin we had just engineered for our former chief accountant, Mr Abernathy. My senior partner, noticing my expression, laid a hand upon the book. “The law, you see, is a blunt instrument, and wholly impotent in a matter such as this,” he said, his voice low but firm. “Here was a scoundrel who, with all the cunning of a practised cheat, had stripped a junior clerk of his modest inheritance, yet had done so within the precise letter of the statutes.
