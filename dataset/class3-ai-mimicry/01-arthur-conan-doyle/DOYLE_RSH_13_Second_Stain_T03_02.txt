I often fancy that one can read the secret histories of this city upon the faces of the passing crowd. Just this afternoon, I observed a man of considerable means, I should judge, whose features were nonetheless contorted by an anxiety so profound it was painful to witness. This was not the fear of the common cut-purse, for his eyes darted not to the shadows of an alley but to the face of every stranger, as if searching for a single, dreaded accuser.
