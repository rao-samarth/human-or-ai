The rhythmic clatter of the carriage wheels upon the track had for an hour been the only sound between us, but he broke his long silence at last, his gaze fixed upon the dark, fleeting landscape. “You condemn my actions, I have no doubt,” he began, his voice little more than a murmur, “and by every conventional standard, you are right to do so. I was compelled, you see, to lay bare the entire ruinous state of my brother’s affairs—an act directly contrary to the solemn oath I had sworn upon our father’s very deathbed to preserve the family name at all costs. It was a perfidious business, I grant you, and one which shall stain my own honour henceforth; yet, what choice had a man when faced with the utter dissolution of our entire house at the hands of one man’s reckless folly? I saved the inheritance for the others, it is true, but in so doing, I have made of my own blood an implacable enemy and rendered my father's final wish a hollow mockery.”
