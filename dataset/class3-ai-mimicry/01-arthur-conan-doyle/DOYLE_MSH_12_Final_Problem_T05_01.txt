For every wretch who is brought before the magistrate, a hundred more glide past in their hansom cabs, their crimes being of a nature so subtle, so intimately cruel, that no law ever framed could possibly contain them. These are the scoundrels of the spirit: the purveyors of false hope, the architects of financial ruin who remain technically solvent, the social tyrants whose weapons are but a raised eyebrow and a whispered calumny. Yet, who is to say that a final reckoning does not await them? There is, I have long suspected, a more primal ledger in which these debts are recorded; and its accounts are settled not in a courtroom, but in a darkened alley, a sudden reversal of fortune, or the slow, eating poison of a deserved infamy. It is a rougher justice, to be sure, but one which perchance restores a balance that the statutes alone cannot.
