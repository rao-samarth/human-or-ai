You appear unsettled, Mr. Harding," she observed, her voice as crisp and devoid of warmth as the winter glass of his office window. He had been on the very point of dismissing her, of delivering some final, patronising platitude, but her placid demeanour gave him pause. "You believed, I think, that your promises were as easily erased as your ink upon a faulty contract. A most imprudent assumption." From her reticule, she produced not a letter of resignation, but a thin sheaf of papers—the original architectural drafts he had claimed were lost in the fire, each one bearing his signature alongside that of the junior partner he had systematically ruined. "I have already despatched a notarised copy to the Institute’s governing board, along with a full account of your chicanery. They should be opening the envelope at this very moment. You built your career upon another man’s genius and my foolish devotion; it seems only just that you should now witness it dismantled by the facts.
