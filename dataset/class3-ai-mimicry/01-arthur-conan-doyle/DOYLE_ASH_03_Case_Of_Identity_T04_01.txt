You defend him still?” the old doctor exclaimed, setting his teacup down with such a clatter that it nearly cracked the saucer. “Pray, what more evidence is required? The man is a charlatan of the first order, a scoundrel who has preyed upon the goodwill of this entire parish, and yet you speak of his misunderstood intentions!” Miss Albright stiffened, her hands twisting the worn fabric of her gloves in her lap. “You cannot comprehend, sir, the terrible burden of a solitary existence; it drives a man to actions he would not otherwise consider. His spirit was crushed by it.” The doctor leaned forward, his spectacles glinting in the firelight, and his voice dropped to a low, pitying murmur. “My dear woman, you are not describing his affliction, but your own.
