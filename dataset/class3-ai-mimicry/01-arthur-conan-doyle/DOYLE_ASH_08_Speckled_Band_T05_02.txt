She sat by the window overlooking the quiet lawn, her needlework lying quite forgotten in her lap, and in her eyes there was a stillness which spoke not of peace, but of a long and hopeless siege. The malady which had hollowed her cheeks and stolen the light from her countenance was of a most singular and cruel design, for it was a sickness with no name and a wound that left no scar. It was not a matter of a harsh word or a sudden blow, for such things, however brutal, furnish a grievance which the world might understand. Rather, it was a campaign of chilling artifice, a thousand tiny, pin-pricking slights inflicted with such scientific precision that no single one could be named as the cause of her decline. Against such a foe, whose every weapon was a shadow and whose every attack was a whisper in an empty room, a gentle spirit possesses no armour, and an appeal for justice finds no sympathetic ear, for what charge can one bring against a torment that cannot be seen?
