It has always been a peculiar pastime of mine, when confined to the close quarters of a railway carriage, to study the faces of my fellow travellers and to deduce from them what I can. Here sits a man whose frayed cuffs and weary countenance bespeak a life of clerical drudgery, yet I perceive in the keenness of his eye a mind that has grappled with problems far beyond the simple balancing of ledgers. It is a fundamental error, and a singularly common one, to mistake the shell for the kernel; to judge the contents of a mind by the cut of a coat. The most unprincipled scoundrel I ever brought to justice possessed the open, guileless face of a country curate, whilst a man of genuine philanthropic spirit was cursed with a physiognomy so forbidding that children would fly from him in the street.
