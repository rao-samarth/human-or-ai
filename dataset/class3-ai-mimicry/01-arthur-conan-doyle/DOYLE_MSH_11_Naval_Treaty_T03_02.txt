He sat quite still in the dim lamplight, his face a study in feigned composure, though a tell-tale tremor in his hand betrayed the agitation within. I allowed the silence to draw out, for it was a more potent accuser than any words I might muster. “You must think me a fool,” I said at last, my voice preternaturally calm. “Indeed, you must have counted upon it. That the Calloway documents would vanish from a locked safe, to which only two men possessed the key, was a conundrum of the first order; yet, the solution becomes elementary when one considers that only a single man—a man I have called brother for twenty years—stood to gain from their disclosure to our rivals.” I placed the forged letter upon the table between us, its damning ink a black stain upon our history. “Your single, solitary error, my dear fellow, was in underestimating not my locks, but my logic.”
