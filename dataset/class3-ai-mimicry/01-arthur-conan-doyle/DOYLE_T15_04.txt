For heaven’s sake, Morton, moderate your tone! You are making a spectacle of yourself, and we have attracted the most unwelcome attention from those upon the promenade.” I laid a restraining hand upon his arm, but he shook it off with a convulsive energy that was quite alarming in a man of his usually placid disposition. “Let them look!” he cried, his voice rising to a shrill pitch that caused several heads to turn. “It is you who are blind! Do you imagine for a single moment that such a precise sequence of chimes from that clock-tower could be accidental? Three short, one long—it is a signal, man, a dire warning!” I attempted to reason with him, to point out the utter absurdity of a conspiracy communicated through a common timepiece, but his singular notion had taken such complete possession of his faculties that he was beyond all appeal.
