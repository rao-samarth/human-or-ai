That you could harbour such a thought—that is the wound which pains me most profoundly. Yes, the man laid before me a most glittering inducement; a full restoration of my family’s name and a sum which would have secured my future beyond any doubt. The price? A mere whisper of confirmation, a simple nod to affirm his venomous suspicions regarding your venture in the Americas. He presumed that a man’s fidelity was a thing to be purchased, a commodity with a quantifiable value. But he misjudged his man entirely, for there are alliances forged in shadow and strife which cannot be dissolved by the clinking of coin. He believed our years of shared peril and mutual confidence could be bartered away like common stock—a base and contemptible calumny against the very nature of our fellowship.
