My dear Aunt Mildred," proclaimed my cousin, gesturing towards the empty pedestal where her prize-winning marmalade had lately stood, "pray do not despair. The problem, though vexing, is one of pure observation, and its solution is elementary." He paced before the hearth, ignoring the exasperated sighs of the family. "We may at once eliminate the serving girl, for the faint scent of lilac on the air does not accord with the carbolic soap she is known to favour. Young Peregrine, though mischievous, has a tell-tale stickiness about his person which is presently absent. No, the true villain is a creature of habit and opportunity." He stopped abruptly before our venerable grandfather, who was ostensibly asleep in his wingback chair. "A man who takes precisely two lumps of sugar in his tea would find this particular preserve irresistible; and only a man feigning slumber could have executed the deed unobserved, leaving behind, as he has, a single tell-tale crumb upon his bristling moustache!"
