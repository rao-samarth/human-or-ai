The weight of the calumny which had driven him from the city seemed, for a time, to have bowed his very shoulders here in the quiet solitude of the downs. One saw him at a distance, a solitary figure moving against the grey sky, and one understood at once that this was a man engaged in a most singular and desperate battle—not with any physical foe, but with the phantom of his own past. He spoke little, but his actions, ah, his actions were an eloquent and ceaseless argument against the slanders that had undone him. Every repaired gate, every hour spent aiding a troubled farmer with a lame horse, was a carefully placed stone in the reconstruction of a shattered edifice. It was a labour of immense and heartbreaking patience, for what is more difficult to rebuild than a man’s good name once it has been utterly demolished by the cruel tongues of others?
