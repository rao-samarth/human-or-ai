From his vantage on the upper deck, Mr. Alistair Finch observed the Port Commissioner, a man whose starched collar and imperious gaze seemed a direct challenge to the humid languor of the tropics. The Commissioner, with a curt flick of his wrist, had gestured for the local deckhands to clear a space for his voluminous trunk, a command usually met with immediate, if grudging, compliance.
