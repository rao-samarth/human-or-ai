To have presented the poor girl with the unvarnished facts of her father's final, ruinous disgrace would have been an act of singular cruelty; a blow from which her gentle nature might never have recovered. Some truths, I have come to perceive, serve no higher purpose than to shatter what little peace a soul has managed to secure against the brutalities of the world.
