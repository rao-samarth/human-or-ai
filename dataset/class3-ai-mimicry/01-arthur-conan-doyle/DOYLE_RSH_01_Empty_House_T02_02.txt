My companion had taken up his station by the mullioned window, his entire formidable intellect focused upon a single, desiccated oak leaf held to the light. Any casual observer might have deemed his absorption a mark of profound eccentricity, or even of a mind unhinged; yet I, who have known the man through a score of years and a hundred crises, perceived in it something altogether different. For him, this silent communion, this shared solitude amidst the rolling downs, was an expression of trust far deeper than any easy platitude or common sentiment. He has ever been a man of singular habits, one to whom the niceties of social intercourse are as alien as the customs of the Hottentot.
