You brandished your intellect as a king might his sceptre, Ashworth, an instrument of dominion before which all lesser minds were expected to grovel. I observed it in every calculated gesture, I heard it in the insufferable cadence of your voice—that absolute and unshakeable conviction in your own powers. Yet where is that conviction now? That grand edifice of your self-conception, which you constructed upon such lofty and precarious heights, has been dashed to pieces by a single, inescapable truth of your own creation. It is a dreadful thing, I grant you, to witness a man’s spirit so utterly broken; but tell me truly, is it the failure itself that torments you so, or the simple, mortifying shame of having the entire world witness the full measure of your magnificent blunder?
