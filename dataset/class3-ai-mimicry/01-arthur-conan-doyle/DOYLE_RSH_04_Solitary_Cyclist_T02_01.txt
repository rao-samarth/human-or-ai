The grandfather clock in the hall struck two, its chimes echoing through the otherwise silent house where she sat alone by the hearth. There was a time, not so very long ago, when such profound stillness would have sent a chill of apprehension through her; when every creak of a floorboard or whisper of the wind in the chimney would have seemed the footfall of some approaching menace. Yet tonight, a most singular and welcome peace had settled upon her spirit. The solitude was not an emptiness to be feared but a quiet space in which to cultivate a newfound fortitude. The firelight danced upon the book in her lap, its pages unread, for her thoughts were turned not to fictional adventures but to the very real and promising chapter of her own life, which she now felt, with a certainty that was both logical and profound, was about to begin. The world outside might be fraught with its own peculiar dangers, but within these walls, she had at last discovered that the most reliable guardian was not a locked door, but a steady and determined heart.
