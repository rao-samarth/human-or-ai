I watched young master Hemlock cross the cobbled square, his shoulders already stooped with the weight of his father’s failing business, and I was struck by a most dreadful conjecture. It is the easy wisdom of the prosperous to declare that each man is the sole architect of his own fortune; that with stout will and clever industry he may climb to any height. Yet, observing that poor lad, one could not help but wonder if some souls are born into a battle already lost. His path seemed not carved by his own hand, but rather worn into the very stones of this town by the feet of his forebears—a deep and inescapable rut. Was it some cruel cosmic sport, or simply the inexorable machinery of consequence, that one man’s spirit might burn with such ambition, only to be hemmed in by the iron walls of circumstance long before he has ever had a chance to truly fight?
