It was a damnable business, Hemmings, a truly damnable business. To think that a fellow like Croft, a man with whom we have shared our ledgers and our confidence for a decade, would sell the Crown’s naval schematics for a mere handful of sovereigns is a notion to curdle the very blood. Yet I feel a curious sense of clarity in the air this morning, as if a fever has broken. The rogue has been exposed, his perfidious network dismantled; and what remains is the solid, unshakeable core of this enterprise—good men such as yourself who value their honour above all coin. The structure was tested, it is true, but its foundations have proven sound. Let this be a lesson to us all: the avarice of one weak link only serves to reveal the formidable strength of the chain.
