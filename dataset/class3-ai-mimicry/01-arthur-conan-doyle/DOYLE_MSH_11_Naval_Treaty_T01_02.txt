I gazed upon the dispatch-box, a simple thing of leather and brass, yet its contents were of such a singular and perilous nature that my hand trembled to touch the lock. Within lay not mere correspondence, but the distilled anxieties of an Empire; a few scrawled words, a ciphered cable from some distant legationâ€”these trifles could, if mishandled, set the Continent ablaze or send good men to their graves without their ever knowing why. To possess such foreknowledge is a damnable thing, for it isolates a man from his fellows more completely than any prison wall. They chat of markets and the weather, their minds untroubled by the precipice upon which they so blithely stand, whilst I must weigh each casual word, lest some unguarded syllable betray the colossal secret I am condemned to carry. It is a burden, indeed, which presses upon the soul until one fears it might crack under the strain.
