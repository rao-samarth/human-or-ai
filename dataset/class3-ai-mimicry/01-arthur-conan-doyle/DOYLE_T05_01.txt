The great river of humanity flowed on, a torrent of bobbing hats and anonymous faces, each swept along by some private and urgent current. Yet I remained fixed upon the pavement, an unmoving stone in the stream, for my gaze was arrested by a single figure alighting from a carriage across the thoroughfare. It was she, her expression animated by some light jest, a fleeting smile that the city's grime and clamour could not diminish. A gentleman of considerable standing, to whom she was promised by a pact older than our own brief acquaintance, took her arm with an air of proprietorship. What use would there be in crying out, in bridging that chasm of cobblestones and sworn duty that lay between us? None, I knew.
