It was a peculiar thing to observe, amidst the ceaseless river of humanity that flowed along the gas-lit thoroughfares, how two men might stand so entirely apart from the throng, engrossed in their own quiet discourse. The world, with its frantic commerce and its fleeting passions, seemed to wash around them like a tide against a pier of granite. Here was no mere casual acquaintance, but a fellowship cemented by trials which the common man could scarcely comprehend; a profound and unspoken compact of mutual reliance. In an existence so often fraught with uncertainty and deception, where the ground beneath one’s feet might prove at any moment to be treacherous, such an unwavering alliance becomes the one fixed point of navigation. It is, indeed, the ultimate logic of the human heart—a steadying hand in the darkness, a mind to which one’s own can turn with the absolute certainty of finding a perfect and loyal echo.
