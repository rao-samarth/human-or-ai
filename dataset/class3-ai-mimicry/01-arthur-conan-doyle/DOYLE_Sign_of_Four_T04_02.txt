The chamber was a veritable cauldron of human passion, with accusations hurled like stones across the grimy, gaslit space and the press of bodies lending a suffocating heat to the proceedings. One might have thought the very truth itself had been torn to shreds amidst the frantic gesticulations and weeping fits which dominated the hour. Yet, in the eye of this tempest stood a single, unmoving figure whose mind, I could well surmise, was a fortress against the onslaught of sentiment. He sifted through the clamour, discarding the irrelevant tears and the hollow threats, isolating each salient point as a botanist might isolate a specimen upon a slide. He arranged them, not according to their emotional weight, but to their immutable, logical sequence, until the whole sordid affair lay plain. When he finally spoke, his voice cut through the din not with volume, but with the sheer, cold force of a perfectly constructed case, and the hubbub died at once into a stunned, uneasy silence.
