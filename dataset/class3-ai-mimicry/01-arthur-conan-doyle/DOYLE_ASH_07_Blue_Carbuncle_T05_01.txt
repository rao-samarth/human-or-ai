I sat perfectly still as he spun his damning web of supposition, each point delivered with the cold precision of a surgeon’s blade. To hear his version of events was to witness a monstrous caricature of the truth, a thing so twisted and yet so horribly plausible that I felt the very air thicken with its poison. He laid out the timeline, the motive, the opportunity—all neat little soldiers in a row, marching towards my inevitable ruin. And I, who knew the simple, unadorned facts of the matter, could offer no defence that would not sound like the desperate fabrication of a cornered fiend. My own truth was a plain and clumsy thing next to his intricate mosaic of lies; it had no power, no artifice. I saw in his eyes not malice, but the dreadful conviction of a man who believes he is serving justice, and in that moment, I knew I was truly lost.
