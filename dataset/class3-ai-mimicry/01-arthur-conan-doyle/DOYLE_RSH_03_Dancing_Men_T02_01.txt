The frantic gallop of hooves upon the quiet country lane was the first sign that the long-held silence of the estate had been irrevocably shattered. A carriage, pursued by two riders of a most desperate aspect, careened towards the old stone bridge with a recklessness that bespoke utter panic. Here, then, was the inevitable result, the final, explosive payment for a truth that had been buried for a generation; it had festered in the dark, you see, gaining a venomous potency until it could no longer be contained. The subsequent crash—a dreadful splintering of wood and the shriek of a horse—was not merely an accident upon a rustic byway. It was the catastrophic eruption of a family's hidden life, a poison so potent that its concealment had guaranteed only a more violent and ruinous escape, laying waste to all in its path with the swiftness of a thunderclap.
