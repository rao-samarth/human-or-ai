You have no proof!" the man spat, his knuckles white upon the arms of his chair. "You build this entire fantasy upon a foundation of mere air!" I leaned forward, my voice low, for a quiet tone is often more effective than a shout. "On the contrary, the foundation is as solid as the very ground beneath us, though its components are too small for your eye to appreciate. Consider the singular abrasion on your left boot, consistent only with the sharp edge of the brass scuff-plate on the minister's private carriage. Add to this the minute fleck of red sealing wax beneath your fingernail, of a composition used for but one class of government dispatch. These are not fantasies; they are facts. Each is a trifle, a nothing, but when you assemble a thousand such nothings in their logical sequence, they become a single, undeniable certainty which points, I am afraid, directly to you. There is no other conclusion.
