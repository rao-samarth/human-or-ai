The scene within the firm’s private accounts office was one of singular chaos, a tempest in a teacup that had nonetheless wrecked a twenty-year partnership. Young Abernathy, his face a mask of disbelief, pointed a trembling finger at the falsified ledger; the ink was scarcely dry upon the figures that condemned him. It was a swift and brutal piece of work, a coup de main against his mentor, old Mr. Finch, who now stood white-lipped by the hearth. For years, the junior partner had played his part with admirable diligence, only to cast it all aside in a single, audacious gamble. The motive was as old as commerce itself—a desperate lunge for a fortune he had not the patience to earn. He had seen an opportunity, a momentary lapse in his superior’s oversight, and for the promise of a few thousand pounds, had sold a man’s reputation, and his own soul along with it, in one fell swoop. The whole affair, from its cunning conception to its calamitous discovery, had transpired in less than a fortnight.
