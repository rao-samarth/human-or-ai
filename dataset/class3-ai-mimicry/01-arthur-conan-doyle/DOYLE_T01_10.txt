He leaned forward in the rattling hansom, his face a grim mask in the fleeting gaslight that sliced through the evening fog. "Do not speak to me of progress, Atherton, not when the very stones of this city cry out against you. Your grand new railway terminus—an edifice of iron and glass, I grant you—is cemented with the ruin of a hundred families, a fact you dismiss with the wave of a hand. You have procured your monumental legacy, and your name shall doubtless be etched upon its cornerstone for all of posterity to admire. But consider this, my friend: for every brick you have laid in triumph, you have dismantled a home, and for every cheer your venture earns, you have purchased the undying enmity of an honest man. Tell me, when you stand upon that platform to receive your accolades, will the weight of such a bargain not prove heavier than any medal they can bestow?"
