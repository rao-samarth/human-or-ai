As the carriage rocked us through the deepening twilight, he turned from the window, his eyes reflecting a fire that the fading landscape could not account for. You speak of the courts, my dear fellow, and of their dispassionate scales, but what of the man whose heart has been torn asunder by some singular evil? The law offers a cold and measured penalty—a set number of years, perchance—when what the very soul cries out for is a reckoning that is immediate and absolute. To surrender one's most profound grievance to a panel of indifferent jurors is to ask a starving man to be satisfied by the mere description of a feast. Nay, I tell you, there are certain outrages against the spirit of man for which the only true balm is administered by the hand of the wronged. It is a terrible and primitive justice, I grant you, but one which, in the face of monstrous iniquity, feels more righteous than any verdict read aloud in a dusty, impersonal hall.
