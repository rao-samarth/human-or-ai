You affect the air of a gentleman of means with some considerable skill, I will grant you,” I remarked, stepping before him and halting his progress amidst the clamour of the thoroughfare. He started, his smile a manufactured artifice of polite surprise. “But a man of substance, sir, does not possess a watch-chain of fine gold whilst the cuff of his shirt, peeking from that well-tailored coat, is frayed by a hundred washings. Nor does his walking-stick, for all its silver filigree, bear upon its ferrule the unmistakable grey dust of the pawnbroker's back room. These are not the trifles of a man enjoying the city's diversions; they are the desperate signals of a man clinging to the last vestiges of his former station. You are not waiting for a carriage—you are waiting for a final, ruinous collapse.
