He slammed his palm down upon the mahogany desk, the sound a sharp report in the otherwise silent library. "You dare to speak of a simple error in judgement? A fleeting moment of carelessness?" His face, a mask of pale fury in the flickering gaslight, was turned full upon me, his eyes burning with an intensity that I found myself quite unable to meet. "That one casual remark of yours, sir, uttered without thought and overheard by precisely the wrong ears, has brought about a complete and utter ruin. It has poisoned a well from which we both have drunk for a decade, and in its consequence, it has sundered a fellowship which I had believed, in my folly, to be as immutable as the stars themselves. There can be no mending this; the wound is too deep, the betrayal too profound for any apology to now redress."
