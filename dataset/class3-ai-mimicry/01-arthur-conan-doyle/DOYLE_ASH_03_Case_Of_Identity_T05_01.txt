“So it was all a fiction,” I whispered, the words catching in my throat as I stared at the man before me. Before my very eyes, the gentle, afflicted scholar I had so admired—a soul of quiet suffering and profound intellect—underwent a transformation so complete it verged upon the grotesque. The melancholic slump of his shoulders vanished, replaced by an erect and insolent posture; the sad, thoughtful gaze in his eyes hardened into a flinty, triumphant glint that chilled me to the very bone. Every shared vulnerability, every anecdote of his tragic past that had forged our supposed bond, now stood revealed as nothing more than a carefully selected tool, a key shaped with infinite patience to unlock my trust and my fortune. He had not merely deceived me; he had constructed a man for the express purpose of my ruination, and I, a fool captivated by the artistry of the performance, had willingly handed him the dagger.
