The dispatch lay before me upon the polished mahogany of my desk, its crisp official heading a stark mockery of the lie contained within. My superiors, men for whom I held the highest professional regard, had presented their case with a grave and compelling logic: my signature was but a small, necessary sacrifice upon the altar of national security. They spoke of stability, of averting a crisis which could plunge the continent into chaos, and painted my private scruples as a dangerous form of self-indulgence. What, after all, was one man’s reputation when weighed against the safety of the Crown’s subjects? And yet, as my hand hovered above the inkwell, a cold dread seized me. To knowingly certify a falsehood, to betray the very principle of truth upon which my entire career—nay, my entire character—had been founded, felt like a treason more profound than any they sought to prevent. It was a simple stroke of the pen, but in it, I saw the damnation of a lifetime’s integrity.
