Pray, consider the matter from a different angle," my companion remarked, his gaze fixed not upon the fleeting countryside but upon some inner problem. "We are trained, you and I, to observe the trifles—a smear of mud upon a trouser-cuff, the particular ash of a cigar, the faint indentation upon a carpet. From these, we construct our chain of reasoning. Yet, what is to prevent a man of sufficient cunning from constructing the chain himself, link by false link, for us to find? It is a simple matter, I assure you. A borrowed pipe, a boot scuffed against a particular brick wall, a ticket for a destination never visited—all these may be procured and artfully placed. The result is a most convincing narrative, perfectly logical in its every detail, save for one fatal flaw: its entire foundation rests not upon fact, but upon a carefully contrived deception designed to lead the keenest of minds down a path of another’s choosing.
