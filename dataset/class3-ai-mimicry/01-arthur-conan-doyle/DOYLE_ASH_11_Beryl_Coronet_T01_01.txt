My companion gestured with his chin across the crowded waiting-room. “See there,” he murmured, “that stout gentleman by the ticket-office. A finer example of public virtue I have rarely witnessed; he has given alms to three separate beggars in the last ten minutes alone.” I had, however, been observing the fellow myself, and noted details which had evidently escaped my friend’s more charitable eye. Whilst his coat was of an excellent cut, the collar was subtly turned to hide a worn patch, and the glint from his signet ring came not from gold but from polished brass. More damning still was his method: with a grand flourish for the public, his right hand would dispense a penny, whilst his left, in the same motion, would deftly retrieve a sixpence dropped by a previous benefactor.
