The whole house was as quiet as a tomb, and my heart commenced to hammering a regular blacksmith’s tune against my ribs. There on the polished desk it lay, a folded paper that wasn't mine to touch, but which held the key to untangling every last one of my troubles. A feller’s common sense whispered it wasn’t hardly stealing; it was just seizing an opportunity the good Lord himself had laid out plain as day. I took three steps toward it, my hand half-raised, my breath caught tight in my chest. But then my conscience, which I’d supposed had packed its bags and left town years ago, woke up and started raising a most awful fuss. It hollered about the decency of my host, about the shame that would stick to me slicker than tar, and I tell you, I spun on my heel so fast I nearly fell over. My insides felt like a sack full of fighting cats, one clawing for escape and the other for some scrap of honor, with the grandfather clock in the hall just ticking away the seconds I had left to choose.
