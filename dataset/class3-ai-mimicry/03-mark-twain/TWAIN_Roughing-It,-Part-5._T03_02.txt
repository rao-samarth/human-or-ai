There it sat in the ledger, plain as a wart on a deacon’s nose—a figure so plump and prosperous it had no more business in my column than a hog in a parlor. The company, a great and wheezing beast of commerce, would likely never miss such a trifle; its pockets were so deep you could lose a whole town in 'em and never hear the splash. I felt a curious warmth spread through my chest, a feeling altogether different from honest wages. It was the kind of warmth that makes a man forget the ten long years his elbow has been warring with the very same spot on this dusty desk. A man could buy a good deal of respectability with that kind of cash. He might even purchase a whole new set of principles, shinier and more flexible than his old ones. I looked from the impossible number to the window, where the sun was shining on other men's carriages, and I reckoned that Providence itself had made a generous accounting error, and who was I to trouble the Almighty with a correction?
