He’d been marching along that dusty track for a good hour, the starched collar a regular garrote at his throat and his Sunday suit collecting burrs like a tax-man collects debts. There was a meeting to get to, a solemn affair concerning property lines and the moral turpitude of a neighbor’s rooster—the kind of high-minded business that sucks the very marrow from a man’s bones. But then the creek winked at him through the willows, a cool, green invitation to righteous ruination. He stopped dead. He looked at his polished shoes, then at the gurgling water. A man of his station had no business doing what he was thinking, not a lick. Well, to thunder with his station! In a flash the coat was off and flung over a branch, the tie was a silken snake in the grass, and those durned shoes were sent sailing after each other into the cattails. He hit the water with a whoop that scattered a congress of frogs and sent his responsibilities clear to the other side of the county.
