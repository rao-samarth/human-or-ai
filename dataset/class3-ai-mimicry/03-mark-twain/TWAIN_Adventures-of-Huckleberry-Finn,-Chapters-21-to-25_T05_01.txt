The old man squinted at me over his spectacles, the lamplight making his face a geography of shadows. “So, you reckon Judge Morrison is a fine, upstanding man on account of him funding the new orphanage?” he grumbled, polishing a lens with his thumb. “Well, I suppose a man does need a place to store the children of the fellows he’s ruined. It’s a peculiar brand of Christian charity, ain’t it? He’ll foreclose on a man’s farm for a piddling debt on a Tuesday, leaving a family to the wind, and then get praised to the heavens on a Sunday for donating a bag of coins to shelter the very consequences. The town applauds the magnificent generosity of the fire-starter for tossing a bucket of water on the blaze he lit himself. They call it civic virtue, but it seems to me a scoundrel with good penmanship and a fat checkbook can purchase himself a saint’s reputation for pennies on the dollar.
