They had me pinned, you understand, dead to rights and with the ink still wet on the accusations. The papers were howling for my hide, and a half-dozen righteous-looking fellas with spectacles were convinced they’d see me breaking rocks by Christmas—and I reckon they would have, too, if it weren’t for the simple fact of the matter. You see, there are contracts written on paper, and then there are understandings etched on a man’s character. A fellow from the old society, a man I hadn’t seen in twenty years, simply took a long lunch with the right sort of judge; another sent a quiet telegram that persuaded a key witness to take an impromptu fishing trip to parts unknown. It ain’t a conspiracy, son, it’s just a better and more reliable grade of family, the kind that remembers its own long after the public and the Almighty have commenced to forgetting.
