You stop right there, Elias,” I said, my grip on his arm firm enough to halt him against the river of humanity flooding the thoroughfare. “You listen to me. That man back there, with his silver tongue and his bottle of bottled moonbeams, has sold you a bill of goods so fraudulent it would make a congressman blush. And you swallowed it—hook, line, and the consarned fishing pole besides! You’ve got a mind, Elias, a perfectly serviceable one, but you rent it out for free to any scoundrel with a good story and a loud voice. You would believe a cat could bark if it was written in a fancy enough hand. It is a peculiar brand of human vanity to think one’s own belief is sufficient evidence for a thing being true, to trade the solid ground of doubt for the quicksand of a comforting lie. Now go on, run back and buy his patented air; I hear it cures a heavy purse.
