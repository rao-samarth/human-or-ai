I will not deny it, sir; the evidence of my displeasure is scattered across the floor in a thousand glittering pieces. That machine, that miraculous little box of wires and lightning they brought in last Tuesday, was a fine and polished liar. It promised to shorten the workday, to grant us leisure and simplify our accounts, but it did nothing of the sort. No, it merely found newer and swifter ways to pile on the misery, demanding a man’s attention with the incessant chirp of a nervous bird and the tyrannical glow of a captured firefly. It didn’t save time; it subdivided it into a million anxious little seconds
