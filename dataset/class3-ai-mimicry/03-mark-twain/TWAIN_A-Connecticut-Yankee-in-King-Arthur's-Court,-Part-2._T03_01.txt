You see, a man's mind is not a railway depot; you cannot simply lay down a track and compel the train of his convictions to run upon it at your own schedule. No, sir. You can show him the timetable, you can polish the engine till it gleams like a new dime, and you can toot the whistle till the cows come home, but he will not board that train until his own heart tells him it is bound for a worthwhile destination. And there lies the grandest hope of all! For when he does step aboard, by his own free will and after a good deal of chin-scratching, he won't be a mere passenger on your journey; he will be its most steadfast and true-hearted engineer.
