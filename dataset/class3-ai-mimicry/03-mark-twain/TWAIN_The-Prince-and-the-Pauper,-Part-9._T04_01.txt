You can't print that story, son," the old man said, not even bothering to look up from his proofs, his spectacles perched on his forehead like a second, wiser pair of eyes. "You can't print it because Mr. Abernathy owns the mill, and the bank, and for all I know he holds the patent on the very air we're breathing in this cursed office." He finally set the papers down with a sigh that seemed to carry the weight of a dozen bad winters.
