Well, a man gets accustomed to another soul on a long journey, same as he gets accustomed to the infernal rocking of the ship or the cook’s criminal notions about what constitutes a boiled potato. At first, her company was just a pleasant enough distraction from the boundless tedium of the sea; a fellow sufferer to exchange grimaces with when the captain launched into another one of his interminable stories. But a curious transformation took place somewhere between the Azores and the dreary coast of France—a slow-dawning fact that I was entirely unprepared for. One evening, watching her laugh at some fool thing I’d said, it struck me with the force of a stray wave that the whole blasted trip would have been a monument to misery without her. Her countenance had become the only reliable landmark in that whole watery wilderness, and I found I had, without any planning on my part, navigated myself into a state of affection so thorough and permanent it felt more like a geographical feature than a passing sentiment.
