So they've got me on the carpet, demanding I account for the high crime of moving the founder’s portrait—a gentleman with a face so sour it could curdle milk clean across the state line. For forty years, I’m told, that dusty oil painting has hung over the water cooler, a kind of sacred cow casting its holy shadow upon any soul thirsty enough to approach. It’s bad luck to move it, they whisper; a calamitous disruption of the ‘company spirit,’ which is a thing no one has ever laid eyes on but which everyone is mortally afraid of offending.
