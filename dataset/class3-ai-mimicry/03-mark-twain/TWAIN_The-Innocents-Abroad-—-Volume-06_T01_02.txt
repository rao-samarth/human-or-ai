To hear old Mister Abernathy tell it, the accounting department on the third floor was a kind of Olympus, a celestial sphere he spoke of with a reverent hush. He would have you believe that numbers were not merely tallied but *divined* up there; that great financial destinies were forged in the silent ticking of adding machines by men of profound and sober intellect—a race of beings altogether separate from us common ink-slingers in shipping. Well, I had occasion to venture into that hallowed hall myself, on an errand concerning a lost crate of widgets, and I found no such grandees. I found only a roomful of pale men in spectacles, fretting over a misplaced decimal point with all the panicked gravity of a ship's captain who's discovered a leak in the hull. The most pressing matter of the hour, it seemed, was a furious, whispered debate over whose turn it was to purchase the communal milk. It is a curious feature of our species that we will always invent a kingdom of gods, even if it’s just one flight of stairs away.
