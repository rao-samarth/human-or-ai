Mr. Abernathy, a man whose face had more creases than a poorly folded roadmap, leaned across his vast mahogany desk and offered me the keys to my own personal kingdom—or, as he called it, the Junior Vice President position. He spoke of perpetuity, of a guaranteed place in the firmament of the company ledger; a proper final compensation for an industrious soul. I looked past him, out the window, at a flock of pigeons pecking at some discarded bread—a feast, I reckoned, they enjoyed without a single quarterly report to justify it.
