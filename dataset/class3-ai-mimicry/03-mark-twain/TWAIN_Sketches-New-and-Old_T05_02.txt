To navigate a city pavement is to engage in a peculiar and maddening form of waltz with a thousand partners, none of whom has heard the music. A gentleman sets his course with the determined air of a ship's captain, only to find his path blocked by a congregation of citizens examining a crack in the stone as if it were a geological wonder. He sidesteps, veering perilously close to a vendor of roasted nuts, and is immediately confronted by a phalanx of youngsters marching abreast, a human wall of such impregnable ignorance that a cannonball might sooner turn aside. You swerve, you duck, you halt so abruptly that your own hat threatens to abandon your head in protest.
