I found myself planted at one of those grand suppers where the silverware outnumbers the honest opinions, and every soul in the room is wearing a face they borrowed for the occasion. You look around at the assembled company, and you can see as plain as day the notions they were raised on; notions handed down from their grandfathers like a rusty pocket watch—ideas about the world, about their neighbors, about what a body ought to be. For most, that inheritance is the whole of their map, and they’ll follow its faded lines right over a cliff if the old markings point that way.
