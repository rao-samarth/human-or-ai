I was over by the pickle barrel, whittling a stick to no particular purpose, when the gentleman in the silk vest started in on his proposition. He was selling shares, you see, in an enterprise designed to harness the very lightning from the sky—bottle it up, he said, and sell it by the jar for household illumination. Now, you’d reckon a town full of men who know which end of a mule to stand behind would have the sense to laugh such a fellow right back to the train depot. But bless their hearts, they leaned in closer. Old Man Hemlock, who still corresponds with his brother by slow-moving barge, was nodding so hard his jowls shook, already figuring how many jars he’d need for the barn. The cooper started sketching diagrams on a crate with a piece of charcoal.
