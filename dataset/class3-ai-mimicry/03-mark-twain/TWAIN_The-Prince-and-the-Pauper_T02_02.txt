My dear fellow, you get a look on your face like a parson who's just discovered a mouse in the collection plate," he said, swirling the amber liquid in his glass. "You speak of a 'fair share' as if it's some sort of natural law, when the only law I've ever observed is that a big fish eats a little fish, and he doesn't pause to inquire about the little fish's family or prospects. It is the grandest, most damnable foolishness to believe that Providence—if such a thing is paying us any mind at all—intended for the man who sweats in the sun to dine on the same fare as the man who owns the sun, or at least owns the land under it. We are not called upon to fix the world's leaky architecture; we are only asked to find a comfortable room within it before the rent comes due. Now, are we to secure our dividend, or are you going to fret over the cost of another man's bread?
