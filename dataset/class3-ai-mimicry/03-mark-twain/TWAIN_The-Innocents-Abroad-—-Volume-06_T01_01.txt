You speak of that port as if it were some gilded Eden torn from a storybook,” the old man grumbled, setting his cup down with a clatter that turned three heads at the neighboring table. “Well, I have seen your sun-drenched paradise, and its ‘emerald waters’ carried a distinct perfume of fish offal and hot tar, which is not what the poets generally have in mind. As for the ‘enchanted, labyrinthine streets’ you wrote home about—they were just cramped, crooked alleys full of stray dogs and smelling powerfully of cheap cabbage, a proper breeding ground for cholera and bad ideas. You went looking for romance and so you found it, slapping a coat of pretty words on a common-as-dirt fishing town. You saw a songbird where I saw a chicken, and while I admire the power of your imagination, I will not stand for you passing it off as geography.
