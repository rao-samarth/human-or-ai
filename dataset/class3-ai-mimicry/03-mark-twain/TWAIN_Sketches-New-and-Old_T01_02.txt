Silas, I says, gripping the arms of my chair so as not to leap clean over the table and throttle him, “you have been sold a bill of goods so fraudulent it would make a patent medicine peddler blush.” He puffed his chest out like a pouter pigeon. “It ain’t poppycock, it’s *Metaphysical Cranial Sonancy*!” Metaphysical poppycock, then, I shot back. “You paid a man—a man with a suspiciously clean collar and the shifty eyes of a tax collector—to tap your jawbone with a dinner fork and predict your fortune?” “It was a *calibrated* fork!” he insisted, his voice cracking with piety. “And he declared my mandibular angle to possess the precise vibratory signature of a future senator—a senator, mind you! He says all I lack is the initial capital, which he's willing to manage.” I tell you, a man will sooner believe a stranger who promises him the moon for a dollar than trust his own brother who tells him, for free, that the dollar is counterfeit.
