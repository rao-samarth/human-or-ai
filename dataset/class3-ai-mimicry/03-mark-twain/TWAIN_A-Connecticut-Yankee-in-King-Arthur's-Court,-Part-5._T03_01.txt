A marvel of Christian charity, that Reverend,” whispered the widow beside me, dabbing her eyes as the man in question held his crystal goblet aloft to catch the chandelier’s light. “To dedicate one’s entire life to tending the flock—it is a sacrifice of which common men are not capable.” I watched the Reverend, whose flock seemed to consist mainly of well-fed merchants and their wives, take a delicate sip of wine that cost more than my horse, and I considered her proposition. His sacrifice, so far as I could tell, involved enduring a four-course meal and the terrible burden of a velvet-cushioned chair. “Madam,” I said, offering her a sympathetic smile, “you are quite right. It is an uncommon fortitude that allows a man to guide the poor toward the sparse comforts of Heaven while he himself bravely suffers the very best accommodations Earth has to offer. A lesser soul would surely crack under the strain.
