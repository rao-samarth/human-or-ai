I laid the whole blasted affair out for her, plain as a fence post and just as logical. You take the scoundrel by the collar, you point him toward the county line, and the problem, you see, is thereby solved and tidied away. A man recognizes a simple piece of arithmetic when he sees it. But she—well, her mind ain't a straight road, it's a whole confounded territory full of winding lanes and curious footpaths that don't seem to lead anywhere in particular. She commences to worry about the disposition of the scoundrel’s second cousin, and whether the sun was in his eyes when he made his blunder, and what the parson’s wife might say if she caught a whisper of it.
