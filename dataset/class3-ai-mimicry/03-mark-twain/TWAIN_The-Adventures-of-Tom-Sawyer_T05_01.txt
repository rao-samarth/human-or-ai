I was settled in my chair, nursing a respectable throbbing in my ankle and a far less respectable one in my pride, and considering the general injustice of the world. A man lays out a plan of such elegant and daring simplicity—a midnight campaign of the heart, worthy of a knight in a storybook! The idea was to scale her trellis, deposit a sonnet of my own invention upon the windowsill, and be gone like a whisper; a phantom of pure devotion. A fellow could picture it: the moonlit silhouette, the flutter of curtains, the astonished delight come morning.
