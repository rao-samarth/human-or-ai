“You go on now, Jedediah,” my brother said, nudging the boy toward the fireplace, “tell us again about that fearsome catamount.” Young Jed was halfway through the yarn—a story that had sprouted more claws with every Sunday retelling—when Aunt Martha rose from her rocker without a word. She returned a moment later carrying old General Beauregard, the corpulent housecat, who looked about as ferocious as a sleeping biscuit. And there it was, dangling from the General’s collar for all the world to see: a scrap of plaid cloth, a perfect and damning match for the conspicuous rip in Jedediah’s trousers.
