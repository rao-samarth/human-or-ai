“You call that holy work, Abner?” The lamp flickered, throwing Martha’s face into a mask of righteous fury. “Bargaining with those savages over a bottle of corn liquor and a deck of greasy cards—is that the godly example we are meant to set in this wilderness?” He merely grunted, stretching his legs toward the hearth, and a slow, aggravating smile crept onto his lips. “My dear woman, you seem to labor under the misapprehension that we crossed an entire ocean for the sole purpose of spiritual edification; a grand missionary picnic, as it were. The truth of the matter is that while the good deacon is up front composing sermons about our sacred covenant, the rest of us are busy ensuring this venture doesn’t collapse into a pile of debt and starvation—and if that requires a bit of what you might call worldly persuasion to separate a man from a beaver pelt he doesn’t rightly appreciate the value of, then I consider it a service to both commerce and the Almighty.
