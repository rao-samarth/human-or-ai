I found myself cornered at the mayor’s reception by a visiting Count with a mustache so grand it seemed to have its own ancestral sorrows. He was complaining, in that weary way of his, about the newfangled electric lamps lining the hall, declaring they lacked the “soul” of a proper gaslight. Well, it seemed to me the only soul a gaslight ever had was the melancholy kind that flickers out at the most inconvenient moment. He spoke of ancient stone and the dignity of decay, of glories a thousand years behind him; all the while, I watched the clean, unwavering light from a glass bulb fall upon the face of the engineer who’d strung the wires.
