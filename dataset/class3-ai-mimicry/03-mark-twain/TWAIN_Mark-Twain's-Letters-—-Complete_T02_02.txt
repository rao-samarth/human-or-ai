Henderson, a man whose heart was as large as his brain was small, decided my particular brand of quietude needed immediate and vigorous correction. He clapped me on the shoulder with a force that could fell a small mule and declared that what I needed was a dose of “team spirit.” It’s a curious feature of the human animal, this powerful instinct to mend another man’s spirit with the most unsuitable tools imaginable; they’ll bring a hammer to fix a watch, or in this case, a company picnic itinerary to mend a fracture of the soul. He shoved a flyer under my nose advertising a three-legged race and a hot-dog eating contest, as if a stomach full of cheap sausage was the proper antidote for a mind full of emptiness.
