I stood upon the hearth, my belly full of my uncle’s wine and my head full of mine own wit. Forsooth, I sought to fashion a toast, a thing of such surpassing cleverness that all my kin would mark me for a man of letters, a true orator amongst them. I had practiced it in secret, a perfect jewel of a speech meant to sparkle and astound. But when I opened my mouth to speak, what issued forth? Not the gilded words I had so carefully rehearsed in my mind’s own chamber, but a foul and clumsy stutter, a jumble of sounds that made my young nephew snort with laughter into his cup. My countenance, which erewhile had been puffed with such high consequence, did burn with a fire of shame as could have cooked the goose upon the table. The silence that followed was a heavier blow than any jeer; in it, I heard naught but the hollow echo of my own foolishness.
