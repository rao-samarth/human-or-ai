The house lies still, and even the moon doth hold her breath; yet I alone keep vigil with this cursed parchment, wherein is writ a tempting peace. A peace bought with the coin of my good name, a quietude that blackens the memory of my sires. They promise ease, a soft and gentle passage through my remaining years, if I but swallow this gilded falsehood and let the lie take root. But what contentment can there be in a feathered bed when one's own report is sullied in the mouths of men? What legacy is this to leave my sons, save that their father chose a coward'
