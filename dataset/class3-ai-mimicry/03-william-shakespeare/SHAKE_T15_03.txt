Again that sound! A mouse, perchance, or the house groaning 'neath my very fury. It must be found, this single, cursed proof which haunts my waking thoughts and poisons every dream. The floorboards I did pry apart with these raw hands, splintering the good oak ere I cast it to the flame. Hence went the great chest, its foolish contents hurled across the chamber like so much refuse upon the wind! Wherefore doth it hide from me? This devil in my blood commands my hand, and I obey, for reason hath long since fled this wretched skull. I strike the very walls now, my fists bloody against the cold plaster, for surely it lies entombed within, sealed up like some foul and traitorous secret. Let the coming dawn find me a ruin amongst these ruins, for I care not, so I may but possess it
