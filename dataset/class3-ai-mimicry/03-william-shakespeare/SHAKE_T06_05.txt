Mark you how yon poetaster doth hold court, as if his very breath were perfumed with ambrosia for these gaping fools to inhale. His latest string of couplets, so limp they could not hang a flea, are lauded as the finest words since tongues first learned to wag. A pox on such cheap renown! For when one man is lifted so high upon the shoulders of the crowd, his shadow falls like a chilling blight upon all others who stand below, making their own modest light seem a pitiful flicker.
