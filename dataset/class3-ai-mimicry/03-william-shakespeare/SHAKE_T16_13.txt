But master, look ye to the sky; it bleeds a sickly grey, and the wind doth speak a prophecy of shattered masts,” the lad stammered, his knuckles white upon the railing as dockhands bustled past. The old salt, whose face was a map of forgotten storms, merely spat upon the quay. “Aye, it doth,” he grumbled, his voice a gravelly surf. “And what of it? Doth the ground not promise its own cold embrace, by fever or by falling stone? This tremor in thy gut, this palsy of the spirit, it is a poison worse than any tempest. For the sea may claim thy body, true, but to stand here frozen whilst your soul yearns for the horizon—that, boy, is a damnation of another sort entirely. So choose: will you die a thousand deaths upon this shore, or live one life, however brief, upon the waves?
