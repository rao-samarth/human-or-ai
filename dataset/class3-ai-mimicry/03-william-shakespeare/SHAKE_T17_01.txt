Dost thou truly think my words were born of such base malice? I spake of caution, a simple and plain-spoken care for thy good name, and in thy fearful mind thou hast twisted it into a blade. A blade, sir? Nay, for your letter was a serpent, coiled prettily in loving phrases, yet its intent was ever to strike. What poison is this, save for the venom your own suspicion brews? You call my honest fellowship a crime, my private conference a secret treason. Oh, what folly to believe a bond of loyalty could be so undone by a whispered rumour, a meaning misbegotten! Hence, let this field be the final ground betwixt us; for the bridge of amity, which a single ill-chosen word hath set ablaze, is now but ash, and I shall not foul my hands to build it new.
