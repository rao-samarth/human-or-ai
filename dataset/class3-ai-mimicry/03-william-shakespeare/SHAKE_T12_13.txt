You sit in judgment, sirs, and ask why my hand did shift the ancient stone, that grey-bearded marker of my neighbour’s plot. Was it for a mere foot of sullen earth I risked my good name? I tell you, nay. But in that small and barren patch, my mind’s eye saw a future harvest; a golden promise that whispered of a winter with no want, a life unburdened by the creditor’s sharp gaze. A venomous thought did then uncoil within my breast, arguing that fortune’s scales were wrongly tipped and I was but the hand to set them right.
