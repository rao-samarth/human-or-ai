Hold thy hand," the elder whispered, his grip a bony cage upon the younger's arm. "You speak of forging your own fortune, of seizing the hour as if it were a common purse to be snatched by the swiftest rogue. Yet I have seen too many such bold men brought low by a stumbling horse or a sudden fever, their grand ambitions undone by a thing of no account. We fancy ourselves the master builders of our days, laying stone upon careful stone with sweat and cunning; but tell me, what man can truly account for the sudden tempest that lays his work to ruin? Nay, the heavens have their own grand design, and we but foolishly scribe our own small plots in the margins, believing the whole great book to be of our own making, until the final page is turned for us by a hand that is not our own.
