Judge Abercrombie possessed a voice constructed for the express purpose of rattling courthouse windows, and he was employing it now at the dedication of the new town clock, speaking of civic virtue and unwavering progress as if he had invented both. He paused, his chest puffed out like a robin's in springtime, waiting for the customary explosion of applause that always followed his pronouncements. But what came was a silence so profound and so complete that a man could hear his own conscience fidgeting. It was a solid thing, this quiet, built brick by invisible brick from every farmer, blacksmith, and shopkeep who had decided, without a single word passing between them, that they had heard quite enough.
