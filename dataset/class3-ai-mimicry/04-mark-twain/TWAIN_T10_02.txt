He was prowling the parlor again, the last candle sputtering and making his shadow leap about like a hangman in a hurry. He shoved the ledger sheet under my nose—a document smelling of ink and desperation—and jabbed a trembling finger at some ghastly figure swimming in a column of others. “There!” he hissed, his voice the dry rustle of impending calamity. “Ruin, plain as the nose on your face!” His great, meaty thumb obscured every line above and below that singular, damning number; he was a showman presenting the rabbit he’d pulled from the hat but hiding the whole contraption up his sleeve. He painted a picture of fire, foreclosure, and the debtors’ prison with such furious speed that my own good sense couldn’t get a word in edgewise. He built the whole conflagration out of that one little brick of a fact, and by thunder, he nearly had me smelling the smoke and scrambling for the bucket brigade myself.
