You see, a body can get used to most any discomfort—a leaky roof, a pesky neighbor, even the parson’s Sunday sermons—but he can’t ever seem to get accustomed to the racket inside his own head after a piece of shabby business. There’s a whole committee in there, you understand, holding court day and night without recess. One of 'em is a prosecuting lawyer with a voice like a rusty hinge, and he never sleeps a wink; he just keeps presenting the evidence of your own foolishness over and over. Every time I pass Widow Abernathy’s fence, the one I might have been a little too clever about in that land survey dispute, I feel that whole jury staring out from behind my eyes.
