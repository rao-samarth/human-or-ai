You can imagine the scene, old friend—the parlor so thick with the drone of relations and the smell of boiled ham you could hardly draw a Christian breath. And there, holding court by the piano, is Miss Annabelle, the very picture of respectable promise my mother has been peddling since I was in short pants; a fine woman, they all say, a sensible match. The whole assembly expects a certain outcome, for they have planned my future with more care than they've planned this supper, and a man feels the weight of it like a misplaced anvil.
