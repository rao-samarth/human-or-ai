It is a peculiar and public sort of trial when a man’s mettle is tested not by cannon-fire, but by a common housecat—in this case, the widow Gable’s famously ill-tempered creature, General. The beast was perched on the apothecary’s roof, looking down upon its subjects with the serene indifference of a king, while below, the instrument of its salvation stood waiting: a contraption of wood and prayer that seemed to have been retired from service shortly after the Flood. Young Silas Pritchard, whose primary acquaintance with peril involved miscalculating the heat of a stove lid, swallowed a lump of sawdust that had taken up residence in his throat. He could feel the judgment of a full jury of loafers and busybodies assembled
