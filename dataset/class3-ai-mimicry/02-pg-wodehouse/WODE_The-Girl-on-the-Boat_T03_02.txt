The clock on the mantelpiece struck two, each chime landing on the profound stillness of the library like a biscuit dropped in a cathedral. It was a time for deep thoughts, and I was giving the matter the full treatment. Before me, shimmering faintly in the moonlight, stood the root of the problem: a frightful construction of twisted steel that my Uncle Gerald, in a moment of madness, had labelled ‘Essence of Being’. Beside it, looking sternly disapproving, was the hefty marble bust of some Roman cove which my Aunt Agatha had commanded me—using a tone one usually reserves for instructing the firing squad—to put in its place.
