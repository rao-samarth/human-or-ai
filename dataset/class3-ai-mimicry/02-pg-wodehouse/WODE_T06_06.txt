Horace, polishing the rosette on his prize-winning marrow as if it were the family silver, turned to find Percival looming over him with an expression not unlike that of a vicar who has just bitten into a bad egg. "I say," Percival began, his voice acquiring a certain reedy quality that always betrayed a mind in turmoil, "a word in your ear, old man. Touching on this vegetable." He gestured vaguely at the colossal green object. "One has heard whispers. Rumours, if you take my meaning. Tales of certain… imported fertilisers and nocturnal visitations to the allotment sheds, all frightfully hush-hush. I'm not saying a chap would stoop, you understand, not for a moment. But when a fellow who has hitherto struggled to cultivate a respectable radish suddenly produces a gourd of this magnitude—well, it gives the discerning observer pause for thought, what?"
