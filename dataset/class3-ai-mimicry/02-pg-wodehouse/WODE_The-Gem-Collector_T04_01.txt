My dear Bertram, you simply cannot be serious," Reginald hissed, cornering the blighter behind a potted fern that had seen better days. "To wager your new automobile—the one with the silver-plated duck on the bonnet—that you can swim the length of Lord Hemlock's ornamental lake is not merely eccentric; it is the sort of thing that gets a chap's allowance snipped. Have you considered the temperature? The probable goose-related unpleasantness? And for what, precisely?" Bertram, looking entirely too pleased with himself, merely adjusted his bow tie, a rather vacant grin plastered across his face. "Well, what's a fellow to do, old bean? The string quartet was playing something frightfully funereal, and a chap gets tired of bidding on signed portraits of racehorses. One must find one's own amusements, you know. A quick dip seemed just the ticket to stir the blood. Besides," he added, his eyes twinkling with a dangerous lack of sense, "think of the splash it'll make.
