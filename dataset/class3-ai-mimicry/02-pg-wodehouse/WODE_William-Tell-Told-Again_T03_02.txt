Percival Milbank had always considered himself a man of mild temperament, one who would sooner offer a burglar a cup of tea than raise a protesting peep. Yet, as he stood by the ornamental pond in the public gardens, a sort of red mist, not unlike that which precedes a bilious attack, swam before his eyes. The cause of this disturbance was a smirking little fiend in a sailor suit who had just informed young Algernon Milbank that his cap was ridiculous and, for a follow-up, had given Algernon’s prized sailboat a shove with a patent-leather shoe, sending it out into the murky depths. For a ghastly moment, Percival found himself rooted to the spot, paralysed by the code that forbids a chap from interfering in the squabbles of the younger set.
