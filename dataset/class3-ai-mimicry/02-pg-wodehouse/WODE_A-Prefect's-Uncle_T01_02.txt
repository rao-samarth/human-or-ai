Standing there in the thick of the midday London crush, with omnibuses hooting and pedestrians jostling like so many agitated microbes under a microscope, a fellow couldn’t help but feel a profound sense of gloom settle upon the soul. It’s a dashed tricky business, this business of being looked to. One moment, you’re floating through life with no more pressing concern than the proper angle at which to tilt one’s hat; the next, you find yourself the sole repository of family secrets, the designated handler of troublesome younger brothers, and the man generally expected to sort things out when the soup is well and truly spilled. The whole thing weighs on a chap, you know—it's like being asked to carry a grand piano up a spiral staircase. It rather makes one long for the simple days when the worst crisis one faced was a shortage of marmalade at the breakfast table.
