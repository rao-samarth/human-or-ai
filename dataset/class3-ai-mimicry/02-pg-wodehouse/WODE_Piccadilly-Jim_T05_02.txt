Fiskin, a large cove from Cincinnati who seemed to have been constructed entirely of pep and rare steak, was becoming rather a blister on the soul. He leaned forward, his eyes gleaming with the sort of high-octane fervour one usually sees in zealots or fox-terriers that have just located the family cat. “See here, old sport,” he boomed, clapping me on the shoulder with a force that nearly sent my collar stud shooting into the next county, “we gotta actualize! Monetize! You fellas on this side of the pond seem to think a red-hot business proposition is something to be looked at down a microscope for a fortnight before being filed away with the receipt for last year’s dog licence.” I confess I recoiled a trifle.
