I must confess I had always held the private view that old Bingham was making a fearful hash of raising his boy, Freddie. The lad had been so coddled, so shielded from the slightest inconvenience, that one rather expected him to dissolve into a puff of scented smoke if a train so much as ran three minutes late. So when our continental express did just that—not three minutes late, but stopped dead in its tracks somewhere in the wilds of Picardy with the guard chuntering about a ‘catastrophe formidable’—I braced myself for the worst.
