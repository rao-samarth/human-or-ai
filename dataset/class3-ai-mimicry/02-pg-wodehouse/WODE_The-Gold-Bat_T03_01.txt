Dash it all, Reggie, the jig is well and truly up," murmured Percy, poking miserably at a scone that had clearly lost its will to live. "Mrs. Pringle at the post office just cut me dead—not a flicker of recognition—and I have it on good authority that the vicar is preparing a sermon on the subject of pilfered prize petunias. My name is mud in Little Snodbury." It was, one had to concede, a sticky wicket, and Percy looked for all the world like a jellyfish that had just received a telegram with bad news. But a pal is a pal, even when he’s ankle-deep in horticultural scandal. "Buck up, old bean," I said, applying a bracing slap to his shoulder that nearly sent him into the tea things. "Tosh and piffle. I know for a fact you wouldn’t know a petunia from a potato. We're in this together, through thick and thin. I'm with you to the bitter end, even if we have to face a firing squad of irate gardeners.
