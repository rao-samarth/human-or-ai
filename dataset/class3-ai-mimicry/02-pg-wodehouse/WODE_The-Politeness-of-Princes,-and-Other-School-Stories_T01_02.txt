Reginald stood by the mantelpiece, a ghastly smirk playing about his lips, the sort of smirk that would have got him a sound kicking behind the fives-courts in the old days. I, for my part, clutched my glass of brandy-and-soda, feeling the blood of my ancestors boil in a manner that was both unpleasant and likely to cause indigestion. “So it was you,” I said, my voice a low growl that might have belonged to a dyspeptic bulldog. “You, who swapped my prize essay on the Roman forum for a page of lurid limericks.” He did not deign to reply, merely flicking a speck of dust from his dinner jacket, an action more infuriating than a thousand taunts. The air between us, thick with the smoke of his beastly cigars and years of curdled animosity, crackled with a palpable zing; it was the Lower Fourth all over again, only this time there was no housemaster to break things up before a chap reached for the nearest poker.
