It seemed to young Reginald a scheme of the most cunning and water-tight construction, the sort of masterly ruse one hatches after a particularly good breakfast. The objective was simple: to prise a fiver from his Aunt Agatha, a woman who parted with cash with the same cheerfulness a limpet shows when being introduced to a crowbar. His plan, therefore, was to feign a sudden and overwhelming passion for the study of mosses, a dreary subject upon which his aunt was a noted, and frankly rather tedious, authority.
