A distinct chill had fallen upon the annual family dinner, and its source, one noted with a sinking heart, was Uncle Percival. It was the affair of the ivory bishop, you see—a small, carved fellow lost from a set some decade prior—and it had ceased to be a mere anecdote and become, for him, the single organising principle of existence. His gaze, which should have been attending to his roast beef, was instead combing the floral pattern of the rug, as if a ten-year-old oversight might suddenly be rectified between the soup and the fish.
