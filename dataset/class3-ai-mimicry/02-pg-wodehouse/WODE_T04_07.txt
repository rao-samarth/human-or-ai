I have to say, it gives a chap a distinctly rummy feeling in the pit of the stomach to see his oldest confederate being given the third degree by a man who resembles a disgruntled walrus. There was Cedric, blinking in the glare of Sir Reginald Pootle’s study lamp, while the old boy pressed him for details regarding my recent, and entirely innocent, nocturnal ramble through his prize-winning petunias. The stakes, you see, were dashedly high. Sir Reginald, who holds the keys to the Little Fuddleton Angling Society, was dangling a lifetime membership right under poor Cedric’s nose—a temptation of the first water, if you'll pardon the expression. I held my breath behind the arras, wondering if the sacred code that had seen us through thick, thin, and the occasional custard-pie incident at school was about to be traded for the chance to hook a forty-pound salmon. It was, to put it mildly, a bit of a test.
