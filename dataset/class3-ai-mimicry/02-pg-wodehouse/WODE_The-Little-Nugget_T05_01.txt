It was just as Aunt Agatha was reaching for a second cucumber sandwich—an act she performed with the stately, unhurried grace of a galleon docking in a calm sea—that Cousin Skip from Ohio spotted the wobble. “Hold everything!” he boomed, with the sort of cheerfulness that makes a sensitive man’s fillings ache. Before a chap could so much as murmur a protest, he was down on the Aubusson, brandishing a fearsome metal contraption bristling with more levers and doohickeys than a mad inventor's nightmare. The effect on the assembled company was not dissimilar to that of a firework let off in a library; Aunt Agatha froze mid-sandwich, a statue of petrified gentility, while young Prudence uttered a small shriek as the entire tea service lurched like a storm-tossed trawler. It was plain that the transatlantic view held that a rickety table was not an inconvenience to be borne with quiet dignity and a folded napkin, but a clear and present danger to be wrestled into submission, there and then, with maximum efficiency and an absolute minimum of decorum.
