One often finds, when observing the frenetic scurry of humanity along a bustling metropolitan thoroughfare, that the mind turns to weighty matters—chiefly, the acquisition of the stuff that pays the bills. You may take the most solid of eggs, a fellow whose principles are bolted to the floorboards of his very soul, but dangle before him what appears to be a copper-bottomed, gilt-edged opportunity to triple his capital without so much as lifting a finger, and you will observe a curious loosening of his moral fibre. Suddenly, the quiet satisfaction of an honest day’s toil seems a trifle overrated, a mug's game, in fact; while the siren song of the unearned increment begins to sound remarkably like a full orchestra. It is at this precise moment, when the conscience has been sent out for a sandwich from which it may not return, that a chap stands upon the slippery precipice, ready to take a flyer on anything from a patent newt-food to a rubber plantation in a region where rubber is famously disinclined to grow.
