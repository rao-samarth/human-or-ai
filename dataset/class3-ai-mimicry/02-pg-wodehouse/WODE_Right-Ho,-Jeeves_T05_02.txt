It has been well said by those who know that the heart, when subjected to the sort of pressure one finds in a bustling metropolis, is apt to behave like a two-shilling watch in a thunderstorm—which is to say, erratically. Young Alistair was discovering this principle firsthand as he stood rooted to the pavement, a distinctly hunted expression on his face. Not twenty feet ahead, sailing towards him under a full spread of fashionable hat, was Penelope, to whom he had sworn undying devotion only yesterday over tea cakes. The issue, and a deuced sticky one it was, arose from the fact that he was at that very moment en route to meet Prudence, a girl with the disposition of a hornet and a father who owned half the county, to explain why the engagement ring he’d posted to her had somehow ended up on the finger of her cousin, Mildred. To complicate an already richly complicated scenario, the enormous box of chocolates tucked under his arm was a peace-offering for a fourth party entirely, a certain Honoria, whose prize-winning terrier he had accidentally squirted with a soda siphon at a garden party.
