As we found ourselves deposited with what I can only describe as marked enthusiasm onto the platform of some godforsaken hamlet whose chief exports appeared to be dust and despair, Cuthbert had the unmitigated gall to beam. This latest imbroglio, stemming from a rather spirited debate with the train guard over the precise travel-worthiness of a prize-winning mongoose he’d acquired in Naples, was merely another Tuesday in our shared, benighted existence. A lesser man—a saner man, I might add—would have legged it for the hills years ago, leaving the blighter to his chaotic fate. But there is a peculiar, almost morbid fascination in watching him greet each fresh calamity not as the soul-crushing nuisance it so clearly is, but as a sporting new challenge laid on by a benevolent providence.
