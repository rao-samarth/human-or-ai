While fortifying myself with a scone in a bustling tea-room, my eye fell upon a chap at a nearby table who was, to put it mildly, a blot on the landscape. He had the sort of face that could curdle milk at twenty paces and a moustache so aggressive it looked as if it might leap off and savage a passer-by. One instinctively pegged him as a fellow who’d foreclose on a widow’s mortgage for a bit of a lark. Then, a small child—a mere snippet—let out a wail as her jam tart made a break for freedom and landed face-down on the linoleum. Before anyone could so much as blink, the human thundercloud had risen, retrieved the fallen article, and, after a brief and surprisingly gentle negotiation with the tot’s mother, returned from the counter bearing a fresh pastry of dazzling magnificence.
