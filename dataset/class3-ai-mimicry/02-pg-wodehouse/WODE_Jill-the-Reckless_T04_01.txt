Reggie, looking for all the world like a spaniel that’s just been told there are no more biscuits, was making mournful sounds into his teacup. “But Freddie, old man,” he bleated, “the whole bally investment! Gone! A total wash-out!” Freddie, far from being a crumpled heap, was attacking a slice of Battenberg cake with the gusto of a conquering general. “Nonsense, Reg,” he chirped, sending a pink square flying perilously close to the hat of a dowager at the next table. “A mere blip on the cosmic radar; one must simply brace the sinews, stiffen the upper lip, and all that sort of thing. When life hands you a mouldy lemon, you don't complain to the grocer—you jolly well find a new recipe that calls for mould. It keeps the grey matter whirring, what?” He beamed, completely oblivious to the fact that the dowager now looked as if she’d swallowed a wasp, and that his own financial ship had, to all intents and purposes, sunk with all hands.
