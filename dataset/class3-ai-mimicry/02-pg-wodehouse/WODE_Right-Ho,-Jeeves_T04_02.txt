I know it looks peculiar, Uncle, hiding amongst the geraniums when the Admiral was expecting a tour of the grounds, and I'll be the first to admit that the potting shed is not, by and large, the chosen salon for entertaining naval top-brass. But you must understand the sheer, paralyzing gravity of the dilemma I faced. Young Freddie Fitzwilliam had insisted, with the sort of certainty a chap usually reserves for the Derby favourite, that grey flannels were the only conceivable leg-wear for a mid-morning constitutional. On the other hand, old Bumbershoot—who, as you know, practically invented the countryside—had sent a wire that morning stating, in no uncertain terms, ‘TWEED OR SOCIAL OBLIVION.’ What was a fellow to do?
