The stillness of the morning was a bitter mockery, a calculated affront to the churning maelstrom within my very soul as I hid behind the yew hedge. I had done it again, you see. To have claimed, in a moment of sheer, beef-witted bravado, an intimate and scholarly acquaintance with the migratory habits of the lesser spotted songbird was one thing; a forgivable, if idiotic, conversational flourish. But to now find myself facing a formal summons from my host—a man who collected birds' eggs with the fanaticism of a Roman emperor collecting provinces—to identify a specimen he had just discovered nesting in the east gable, well, that was quite another.
