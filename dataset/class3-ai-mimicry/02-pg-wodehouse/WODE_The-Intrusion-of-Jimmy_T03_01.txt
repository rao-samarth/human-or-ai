Let me be entirely clear, Pilbeam,” rumbled Mr. Fotheringay, his jowls quivering with the sort of restrained indignation usually reserved for a badly poached egg. “The guiding principle of this establishment—the very bedrock upon which our quarterly dividends are built—is the avoidance of what you so blithely refer to as ‘a bit of a flutter’.” He paused to allow the sheer lunacy of the concept to sink in, adjusting his pince-nez as if aiming a cannon. “We are in the business of steady, predictable solvency, not in strapping the company coffers to a rocket and firing them at the moon on the off-chance they might return with a pocketful of stardust. Your suggestion that we divert the entire marketing budget to sponsoring a man who intends to tightrope-walk across the chasm at Crudely Gorge is, to put it mildly, the sort of notion that gives solid, respectable ledgers a fit of the vapours. Stick to the tried and true, my boy, or you’ll find your own position here becoming frightfully adventurous indeed.
