Well, if a chap's ever landed himself more squarely in the mulligatawny, I'd like to meet the blighter and shake his hand. The whole ghastly business had sprung, as these things so often do, from a basic inability to refuse a heartfelt plea, particularly when the plea is delivered by a pretty girl on a sunny afternoon. My promise to officiate the Little Wibbley pet show—a task requiring the iron nerve of a lion-tamer—was now locked in a sort of death-grapple with my subsequent, and rather more foolhardy, agreement to assist young Angela in smuggling her three-legged terrier, Puddles, *into* said show, despite the creature having been banned for life by the organising committee. One simply cannot be the stern, impartial judge and the wily accomplice simultaneously; the two roles cancel each other out like a pair of mismatched socks. It was a proper Gordian knot of my own tying, and my brain, which had been taking a well-earned holiday, felt precious little up to the task of finding the scissors.
