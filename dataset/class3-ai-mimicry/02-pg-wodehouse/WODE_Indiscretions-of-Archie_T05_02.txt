Honestly, Cyril," she whispered, her voice possessing a distinct wobble that caused the man at the adjacent table to lower his newspaper with a rustle of profound interest, "I'm not entirely certain I was built for this level of sustained astonishment. When one pictures a shared future, one imagines a gentle rhythm, a certain placid progression of days. One does not, as a rule, budget for one's larder being commandeered by a distant cousin for an ill-advised mushroom-canning enterprise, nor for the vicar to ring up at all hours to complain that our dog has taught his prize-winning parrot a series of unspeakable sea shanties. It all seems to be a relentless sequence of small, bewildering catastrophes, Cyril, and I must confess, with the postman now refusing to approach the house on account of what he calls ‘the incident with the ferret,’ my nerve is beginning to fray. Is this it? Is this simply what it is to be us?
