It is a rummy business, when all is said and done, how the simple and pastoral pleasures of life—the drone of a bee in the hollyhocks, the gentle clinking of ice in a tall glass, the amiable presence of one for whom the heart does a bit of a foxtrot—can be so utterly scuppered by the mere thought of a bank manager. The conversation, which ought by rights to have been gambolling about like a spring lamb, instead limps and stumbles, weighed down by the invisible lead weight of the quarterly accounts. One finds that a remark on the loveliness of the weather is met not with a chirpy concurrence, but with a silence so profound you could drop a pin in it and hear the echo of your overdraft.
