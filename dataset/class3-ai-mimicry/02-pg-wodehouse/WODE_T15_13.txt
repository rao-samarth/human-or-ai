It was shortly after the train rattled through a particularly bleak-looking junction that Reggie’s fixation on the other passenger’s valise took a turn for the kinetic. For the better part of an hour, he had been staring at the thing, a monstrosity of purple leather, convinced it was ticking. I’d assured him it was probably just a particularly robust travel clock, but the notion had taken root in his brain-pan and was now sprouting in the most alarming fashion. He began to fidget, then to mutter, and finally, with a strangled gasp that suggested a man who’s just found a beetle in his consommé, he shot from his seat. Before I could so much as bleat a protest, he had vaulted over a picnic basket, seized the offending luggage, and was attempting to hurl it from the window, all the while yelling something about anarchists and imminent doom.
