It was a moment when a chap’s spirit, if not actually at rock bottom, was certainly exploring the sub-basement with a view to taking a long-term lease. Percy prodded a stray pebble with the toe of his boot, his brow furrowed like a newly ploughed field that had just received notice of foreclosure. Matilda, ever the little sunbeam, attempted a chirrup. “Isn’t it a lovely day for the ducks, darling?” she ventured, indicating the local waterfowl who were, to be fair, having a fairly decent time of it. Percy emitted a sound not unlike a deflating tyre. “They aren’t worried about the quarterly accounts, are they?” he muttered, the words emerging as if dragged from him by a team of reluctant oxen. “Or the fact that Uncle Cuthbert’s sure-fire tip on Paraguayan tram-lines has turned out to be so much floating bilge-water.” Matilda’s smile faltered, her sunny disposition clouding over as if a particularly large and ominous raven had just flown across its face. The ducks paddled on, blissfully unaware that the foundations of a promising romance were wobbling precariously on the brink.
