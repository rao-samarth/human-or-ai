Aunt Matilda, having reduced a perfectly innocent rose bush to a state of quivering nudity, finally turned the full force of her displeasure upon my recumbent form. “Algernon,” she boomed, the words practically rattling the teacups, “I have been informed by a reliable source that you described my new hat to Lady Pouncefort as looking like a ‘startled octopus.’ Explain yourself.” I considered the matter from my deckchair, for it required a delicate touch. “A ‘startled octopus,’ Auntie? Good heavens, no. Such a crude image. My words, I believe, were that it possessed the sort of dynamic, multi-limbed effervescence one might associate with a particularly vivacious cephalopod surprised in its prime.” A brief, baffled silence descended upon the lawn, during which a passing bee seemed to wobble in its flight path. She stared, her secateurs giving a weak snip in mid-air, evidently finding no firm ground from which to launch a counter-attack. It’s astonishing, really, what a well-oiled phrase can do to spike an enemy’s guns.
