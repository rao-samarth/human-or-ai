The whole regrettable affair, I should mention, kicked off with a most frightful lurch of the twelve-fifteen to Market Snodsbury. The larger of the two blighters—a specimen built on the lines of a bulldog that had been crossed with a wardrobe—had just lunged for poor Pilkington’s attaché case when the train executed a sort of shimmy, rather like a flapper doing the Charleston after one too many cocktails. The wardrobe-esque chap found his feet embarking on an independent expedition northwards while the rest of him proceeded south with some velocity. His confederate, a weedy cove with spectacles, produced a chloroformed rag with the air of a conjuror, only to have the self-same rag, thanks to another jolt, apply itself firmly to his own prominent nose. He went down like a sack of well-buttered muffins.
