It is, I’ve often thought, a rummy business, this whole question of a chap’s fundamental composition. Here in Little Wibsey, where a man cannot so much as misplace a collar-stud without the affair being debated in the snug of The Goose and Gander by teatime, one gets a ringside seat to the spectacle. It is a simple matter, of course, to be a staunch confederate when the sun is beaming and the dividends are rolling in; any number of fellows will slap your back and call you ‘old fruit’ then. But let the skies turn a bilious shade of grey—
