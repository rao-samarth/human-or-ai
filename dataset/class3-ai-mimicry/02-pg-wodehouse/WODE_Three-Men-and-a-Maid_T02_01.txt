I suppose a thinking man, a genuine crumpet-soaking philosopher, would find some cosmic irony in the way the wheels of this train kept time with the throbbing in my head. The scheme had seemed so dashedly simple, you see. A mere trifle of a fib—a twisted ankle, communicated via telegram—designed solely to extricate me from a weekend under the gimlet eye of my Aunt Penelope, who holds the view that a young man’s character is best forged by three solid days of competitive hedge-trimming. But one had failed to account for the woman’s formidable benevolence. Instead of leaving a fellow to recuperate in peace, she had mobilized with the speed of a striking cobra, insisting I be transported to her rural seat for a forced convalescence.
