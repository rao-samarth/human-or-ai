It’s all perfectly simple, Aunt Agatha, if one just applies a spot of logic," bleated young Reginald, backed into a corner between a rubber plant and a tray of salmon puffs. "You see, I never actually intended to become affianced to Gloria. My objective, from start to finish, was Penelope. The note—the one mentioning starlight and a lifelong yearning—was meant for Penelope's spaniel, as a sort of preliminary overture. Gloria, you see, intercepted the dog. How was I to know she believed a man's most profound sentiments would be delivered via a cocker spaniel named Pongo?" My aunt, who had been listening with the grim patience of a creditor examining a bankrupt's books, took a slow, deliberate sip of her sherry. "Reginald," she said, her voice possessing the chilling quality of ice cubes clinking in a tomb, "you are a world-class imbecile. You will un-affiance yourself from Gloria before the fish course, or I shall have your allowance stopped.
