To suggest, Algernon, that you intend to scale the north face of the village church, armed with nothing but a ball of twine and what you call ‘pluck’, is not merely foolish—it is an act of calculated lunacy!” boomed Aunt Matilda, her trowel pausing menacingly over a bed of innocent-looking pansies. “A chap of your standing ought to be pursuing sensible hobbies, like stamp-collecting or worrying about the state of his investments, not risking his neck for a view of Mrs. Higgins’s prize-winning cabbages. There is a time for caution, for settling down and allowing a bit of moss to grow on one’s north side, so to speak.” Algernon, who had been prodding a worm with the toe of his boot, looked up with a bright and guileless smile. “But that’s just it, Auntie. Where’s the fizz in a life lived entirely on the level?
