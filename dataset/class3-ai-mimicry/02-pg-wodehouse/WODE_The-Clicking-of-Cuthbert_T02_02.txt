It was a dashed rummy thing, the way young Percival started mooning over Miss Angela Fitzwilliam of the Accounts department. He'd always been a bit terrified of her, you see, viewing her as a sort of human glacier whose sole purpose was to freeze the very marrow in a chap’s bones if he misplaced a petty-cash chit. Then came the day she caught him sketching a rather passable likeness of a Goliath Birdeater tarantula on a memo pad. Instead of ringing for the men in white coats, she peered over his shoulder and remarked, quite calmly, that he’d made the carapace a trifle too convex for a mature male of the species. Well, you could have knocked Percy over with a feather. It was as if a thick fog had suddenly lifted from the landscape of his soul, revealing not a forbidding arctic waste but a sunlit glade teeming with a kindred spirit. One moment, he was a mere cog in the machine of commerce; the next, he knew with a dizzying certainty that the foundations of a truly lasting alliance were being laid, not on whispered sweet nothings, but on the far sturdier bedrock of correctly identified arachnids.
