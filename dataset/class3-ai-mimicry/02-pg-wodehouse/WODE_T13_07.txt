The human condition, my dear chap,” old Biffen was booming to a slumbering clubmate, a pronouncement clearly intended for the room at large, “is a most curious affair.” He prodded the air with a half-smoked cigar. “A fellow spends years constructing what one might term the glittering edifice of his own consequence, brick by painstaking brick. He stands upon its highest battlement, surveying the world below with a benevolent—if slightly patronising—eye, utterly convinced that the universe has at last seen sense and arranged itself entirely for his convenience. And it is precisely at this dizzying apex, mark you, that Fate, a cosmic practical joker with a truly deplorable sense of humour, places a metaphorical banana skin beneath his polished shoe. In an instant, the whole shimmering structure comes a-tumbling down, and the architect finds himself sitting amidst the ruins with nothing but a bruised sense of dignity and the distinct, chilling realisation of having been an irredeemable ass.
