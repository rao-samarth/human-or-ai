To watch old Bickerstaff present the quarterly figures was to witness a species of high art, rather like one of those chaps who can juggle live eels while balancing a fish kettle on his nose. The plain, unvarnished facts of the matter, you see, were enough to make a strong man reach for the smelling salts, but Bickerstaff was not a man to be cowed by anything so trivial as reality. He orated on “strategic repositioning,” waved his hands about “proactive realignment,” and had a peculiar genius for seizing upon the one solitary Tuesday in August when sales had briefly perked up.
