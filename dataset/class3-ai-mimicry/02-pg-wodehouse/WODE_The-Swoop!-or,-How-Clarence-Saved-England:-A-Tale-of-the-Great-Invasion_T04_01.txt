What the readership demanded, as Porson knew with the grim certainty of a man who has studied his circulation numbers, was not the sturdy beef of civic improvement but the fizzy champagne of human folly. Give them a duke who had absconded with a barmaid, or a society pet whose diamond collar had gone missing in mysterious circumstances, and they were happy as clams. This stuff about pipes, however, wouldn't shift a single extra copy; it was good, honest journalism, and therefore completely useless.
