Now, look here, Clarence," said my uncle, polishing his spectacles with an air of profound wisdom, "I grant you the initial scheme had a certain dash, a certain, if I may put it this way, buccaneering spirit. One admires the verve. The trouble is, one can't, so to speak, scale the glittering peaks of commerce by enthusiastically removing the pitons of one's fellow mountaineers. It tends to create a spot of bother, not only for the chaps below, but for one's own sense of equilibrium when one pauses for breath at the summit. But buck up, my boy! That same vim you applied to this—this tactical misstep—can be pointed in a more sportsmanlike direction. Think of it not as a permanent blot on the old escutcheon, but merely as a practice swing before you address the ball properly. The game is far from up; in fact, I’d wager the truly interesting part is just about to begin.
