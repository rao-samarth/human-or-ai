The thing that was giving young Percival Spofforth a touch of the old furrowed brow, as he stood contemplating Major Grimsby’s prize-winning marrow, was a simple matter of ethics. It was a perfectly corking marrow, one had to admit—a green behemoth that seemed to sneer at Percy’s own, more modestly proportioned entry. And in Percy’s pocket, nestling like a guilty secret, was a small but potent syringe filled with a solution guaranteed to introduce a spot of catastrophic wilting. The silver cup for 'Vegetable of the Year' was practically within his grasp. Yet, as he eyed the path to the Major's allotment, a frightful stickiness seemed to settle upon the enterprise.
