Though young Percy had been comprehensively routed in the annual club debate, flattened as neatly as a top hat under a steamroller by a cove whose rhetorical skills were as slick as they were, frankly, a bit much, a rather rummy thing began to happen. Instead of the chilly silences and averted gazes he had braced himself for, old General Fortescue—a man who typically regarded him with the fascinated horror of a botanist discovering a new and particularly malevolent species of fungus—actually clapped him on the shoulder. “Good show, m’boy,” the old buffer boomed, rattling a nearby tray of cocktail sausages. “Went at it with gusto. No beastly tricks. Went down with flags flying, what?” And as Percy blinked in the sudden sunbeam of approbation from the assembled members, it dawned on him with a pleasant jolt that while the silver trophy was lost, he had somehow pocketed a currency of far greater value; for it seems there is a certain quiet dignity in taking a proper, gentlemanly pasting.
