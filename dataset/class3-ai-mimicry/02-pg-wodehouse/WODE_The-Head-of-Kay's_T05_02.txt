I say, Cuthbert, old man, we're for it," mumbled Percy, dabbing at a brow suddenly bedewed with a fine, anxious perspiration. "Old Man Grimsby has requested the quarterly reports an hour ahead of schedule, and my figures, I fear, have the sort of structural integrity one might associate with a blancmange in a heatwave." He slumped in his chair, a picture of pure, unadulterated desolation. Cuthbert, who had been methodically sharpening a pencil to a point of quite surgical ferocity, merely raised an eyebrow. "Grimsby? Pull yourself together, my dear chap. This is small beer. Do you not recall that frightful business with the Headmaster’s toupée and the pot of glue? We weathered that storm. You will create a diversion—perhaps a spirited coughing fit, or a sudden, passionate inquiry about his prize-winning dahlias. While he is thus engaged, I shall slip in with the corrected ledger. It’s the old Lower Fourth Gambit, slightly modified for the corporate clime. We stood firm then, and we'll stand firm now. Chin up, what?
