It was only when the dowager duchess—or she was a duchess, at the very least; a chap doesn’t encounter that sort of gimlet eye and feathered hat outside the highest echelons—fixed him with a glare that could have frozen hot toast at twenty paces that Cyril suspected a certain wires-crossedness had crept into the proceedings. He had, as instructed by his pal Binky, sought out the formidable female relative and, cornering her between the potted ferns and the cake trolley, had broken the news.
