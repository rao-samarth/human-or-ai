It was a simple enough matter, you’d have thought, to ascertain who had propelled the cricket ball through the vicar’s prize-winning cucumber frame. But when I cornered young Crispin by the gooseberry bushes, the lad simply sealed his lips and gave me a look that a cornered stoat might have envied. There’s a frightfully solemn protocol that seems to govern these small blighters—a sort of 'thou shalt not sneak' affair which apparently trumps all logic, reason, and the imminent threat of being deprived of pudding for a fortnight.
