The normally placid high street of Little Biddlecombe had, you see, come down with a bad case of the jitters, the root of which was a singularly fatuous affair involving polished stones and a length of string. One could hardly navigate to the tobacconist’s without tripping over some blighter in a profound state of concentration, flicking his pebble with a verve that bordered on the alarming. The air, usually thick with nothing more exciting than the scent of Mrs. Higgins’s prize-winning petunias, now crackled with the electric thrill of it all. Colonel Fanshawe, a man not known for rapid movement since the relief of Ladysmith, was seen positively pelting past the duck pond in a desperate dash to secure a superior bit of flint, while the vicar, a mild soul, got into a frightful fracas with the butcher over the proper wrist-action. It was a rummy business from top to bottom, and the whole town had quite lost its collective head—a spectacle that was doing absolutely no good for a chap’s digestion.
