It was only after cornering the chap by the potted ferns and holding forth for a solid ten minutes on the mating rituals of the common toad that Percival began to suspect a snag in the proceedings. He had been given to understand that the fellow with the pronounced Adam’s apple was his fiancée Agatha’s uncle, the noted herpetologist, and Percy, having swotted up on the subject, was rather letting himself go. “And the sheer tenacity of the little blighters, what?” he babbled enthusiastically. “Positively inspiring!” The individual in question, who had the general air of a provincial cleric who has just witnessed something unedifying in the vestry, simply blinked, his teacup rattling a small tattoo against its saucer. It was at this juncture that Agatha materialised at Percy’s elbow. “Darling,” she hissed, her voice a low thrum of imminent doom, “that is the Bishop of South Molling. Uncle Hubert is the stout gentleman over there currently attempting to retrieve an olive from his trouser cuff.” Percy froze, a man staring into the abyss, and the abyss, wearing a clerical collar, stared back with profound misgiving.
