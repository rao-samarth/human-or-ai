Mr. Filbert, the cove who managed our humble corner of the Consolidated Flange and Grommet Corporation, was a man who believed fervour in his work was the key to a chap’s soul. It was therefore with a troubled eye that he observed young Reginald twittering over a stuck ledger. The volume, a beast of a thing bound in what looked like rhinoceros hide, had refused to open, and Reggie, whose experience of brute force was limited to uncorking the occasional bottle of bubbly, was stumped. After prodding it weakly with a letter opener, he brightened suddenly. “I’ve got it, Filbert, old top!” he announced, with the air of a scientist who has just cracked the atom. “Remember that splendid chap who opened father’s safe last summer when the butler forgot the combination? A dab hand with gelignite, absolutely first-rate. I’ll give him a tinkle—he could have this bally book open before luncheon, I’m dashed sure of it.” Filbert simply closed his eyes, his expression that of a saint contemplating a very, very long martyrdom.
