My dear chap, I scribble this to you from the smoking room at an hour when only owls and butlers are meant to be abroad, and I must confess the old brain is reeling somewhat. The entire edifice, you see, has crumbled. It turns out that the chap we knew as Clarence, the rugged explorer returned from the wilds of Borneo with a sun-weathered face and a fund of stories that could charm a snake out of a basket, is in reality a fellow named Gus from just outside Bognor Regis. He was unmasked not half an hour ago by young Amelia, who recognised him from a theatrical production of *Charley’s Aunt* she’d seen last winter. The sun-weathered face, it transpires, was little more than a judicious application of boot polish, and his harrowing escape from a python was lifted wholesale from a tuppenny dreadful he’d read on the train.
