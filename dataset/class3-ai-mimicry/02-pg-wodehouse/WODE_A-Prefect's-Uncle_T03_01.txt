I had just embarked upon the rather delicate operation of buttering a crumpet—a task, I’ve always maintained, which separates the chaps who Get Things Right from the general riff-raff—when the deluge broke. In they swept, a family of such staggering untidiness that one felt they must have been assembled by a committee with a grudge against civilization. The paterfamilias, a large cove with the bewildered air of a man who has just been hit by a sandbag, stood blinking while his platoon of miniature hooligans fanned out with strategic precision. One small fiend instantly overturned a pot of ferns, seemingly on principle, while another began testing the acoustic properties of the room by striking a cake fork against a water carafe. It struck me then, as I clutched my imperilled crumpet, that this was the whole bally show in a nutshell: the eternal struggle between those of us who appreciate a neatly arranged tea-tray and the legions of chaos who would rather use the teapot for a game of catch.
