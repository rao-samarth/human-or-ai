One had to hand it to young Biffy Bickersteth; the fellow possessed a tenacity that would have impressed a limpet. All evening, at this ghastly charity ball for the Preservation of the Moth-Eaten Settee, he had been orbiting the glacial Miss Annabel Drake with the dogged determination of a planet that has decided it rather fancies a particular sun. Rebuffed at the canapés, side-stepped near the string quartet, and met with a look that could freeze sherry at twenty paces, a lesser man would have thrown in the towel and sought solace in the punch bowl. Not Biffy. He merely straightened his tie, took a fortifying breath that puffed out his chest like a pouter pigeon’s, and popped up again to inquire if she might be in the market for a glass of lemonade. And you know, a rummy thing happened. Just as he launched his fifth or sixth sortie, I saw the faintest glimmer of a smile touch her lips. The ice was cracking, by Jove, proving that a stout heart can, in the end, melt even the most formidable of arctic fronts.
