It had seemed a frightfully clever wheeze at the time, you understand, what with the moonlight glinting on the deck of the steamer and the girl—a topper named Cynthia—gazing at me with those big, soulful eyes. She had mentioned, in passing, a profound admiration for chaps who lived the life of adventure, so before I could stop the bally thing, my tongue had run away with the game and painted a rather vivid picture of myself as a celebrated explorer, fresh from wrestling giant pythons in the Congo. This went down like a spoonful of the finest jam, right up to the moment she clapped her hands with girlish glee. It was, she chirped, the most topping coincidence imaginable, for her father—that large, red-faced cove currently steaming towards us like a determined tugboat
