It was, take it for all in all, a beastly sort of afternoon. A persistent drizzle had settled over the city, lending the already grim facades of the buildings a positively funereal aspect, and the pavements were a seething tide of damp, irritable citizens. It was at just such a low-water mark in the day’s affairs that a gust of wind—a low, ruffianly brute of a gust—performed a smart piece of larceny upon poor old Algy’s new bowler. While a chap of lesser fibre would have simply sighed and let the matter drop, Bertram, bless his loyal heart, was off the mark in a flash. He plunged into the thick of the crowd with the vim of a salmon heading upstream, sidestepping a perambulator here and shouldering past a disgruntled merchant there, emerging a moment later, triumphant. He presented the rescued headwear, a trifle damp but fundamentally sound, with a grin that did more to banish the gloom than a sudden appearance by the sun itself ever could.
