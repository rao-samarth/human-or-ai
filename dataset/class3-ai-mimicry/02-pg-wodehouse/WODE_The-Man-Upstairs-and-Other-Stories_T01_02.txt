Young Boffin, who manned the fourth desk in the exports department, had finally screwed his courage to the sticking-place, as the Bard has it, and was murmuring a hopeful suggestion involving a theatrical revue and a small table for two in Angela’s receptive ear. He had just reached the bit about the pre-dinner sherry when a sound like the snapping of a particularly frosty twig caused him to leap a clear six inches. It was Miss Platt, the head of invoicing, a woman who looked as if she had been chiselled from granite by a sculptor with a grudge against the human race. "Mr. Boffin," she said, her voice a low-temperature gargle, "while I find your impromptu proposal... bracing, I am afraid my schedule does not permit for 'a quick bite with a fascinating companion.' Perhaps if you were to formalise the request on the appropriate stationery?" Boffin’s jaw dropped, his face taking on the exact shade of a distressed blancmange. He had, of course, been addressing Angela, but the formidable Miss Platt had evidently intercepted the broadcast and mistaken the intended recipient, leaving the poor chap utterly flummoxed and Angela hiding a snort behind her blotter.
