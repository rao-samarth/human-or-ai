It is one of life’s more baffling phenomena, if you ask me, the instinct that drives a perfectly sound young fellow to go charging into the fray. Take young Bingham, for instance, currently squared off against the notoriously ill-tempered swan on the village pond, all on account of Penelope, the vicar’s niece, having dropped her glove. A sane man would have fetched a long stick; Bingham, however, apparently felt that the situation called for a sort of wild, aquatic wrestling match. One has to suppose there is some deep-seated, primeval logic to it all—that the female of the species is biologically programmed to swoon at the sight of a chap getting his collar thoroughly chewed by an enraged bird. It seems a dashed silly system for ensuring the continuation of the race, suggesting that the Powers That Be have a rather slapstick sense of humour, but there it is.
