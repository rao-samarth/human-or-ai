I say, Freddie, pipe the cove by the potted fern,” Archie murmured, nudging his companion with an elbow that nearly sent a Battenberg cake flying. “Isn't that young Biffy Middleton?” Freddie squinted. “By Jove, so it is! Last I clapped eyes on the blighter, he was attempting to invent a silent trombone and had the general demeanour of a jellyfish that had been passed through a wringer. A complete wash-out. And look at him now—straight-backed, clear of eye, positively gleaming with the sort of vim one only sees in chaps who’ve just remembered where they left their keys.” Archie took a thoughtful sip of tea. “It was the ship-in-a-bottle business, you know. Everyone laughed, but he just sat there, day after day, fiddling with tweezers and glue, refusing to be downed by the sheer fiddliness of it all. Stuck at it like a limpet to a rock, and now he’s the king of miniature marine craft. It just goes to show, what? A spot of dogged persistence, and even the most unpromising specimen can turn into a regular pillar of society.
