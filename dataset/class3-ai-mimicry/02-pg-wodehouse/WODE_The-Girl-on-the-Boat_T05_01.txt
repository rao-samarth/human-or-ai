The absolute bottom had dropped out of young Alistair’s world, leaving him suspended in a ghastly void composed chiefly of cold dread and the scent of floor polish. The sensation was not unlike that of an oyster realizing, a moment too late, that it is destined for the luncheon plate of a hungry bishop. It was all on account of the quarterly report. A slip of the thumb, a moment of calamitous inattention while fumbling for the correct pneumatic tube canister, and now the draft containing his blistering, confidential, and frankly libellous caricatures of the board of directors was hurtling towards the chairman’s office, while the chairman's actual report—a dreary document about profit margins—was presumably on its way to Alistair’s chum in marketing. A great, silent scream built up in Alistair’s chest; his heart hammered against his ribs like a trapped bird, and he could only stare at the brass mouth of the tube system, a portal which had, in one puff of compressed air, sealed his doom.
