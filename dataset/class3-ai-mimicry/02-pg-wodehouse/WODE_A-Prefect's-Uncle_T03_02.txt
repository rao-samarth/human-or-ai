I’ve always maintained that a chap’s day should proceed with the gentle, predictable ticking of a grandfather clock; a steady, reliable rhythm, you know. But Little Drooping, when seized by a festive spirit, chucks the whole concept of a sensible schedule into a cocked hat. One moment, I was striding purposefully toward the post office, my simple objective being the acquisition of a stamp for a letter to my Aunt Penelope. The next, the high street had erupted into a positive maelstrom of what appeared to be the annual Morris Dancing championship, complete with clashing sticks, flying handkerchiefs, and a concertina wheezing like a dying grampus.
