As I swirled the last of the soda in my glass, the whole sticky business seemed to clear up a bit, like a London fog deciding to pack its bags and head for the seaside. It was all very well for Uncle Percival to go on about her people not being precisely our sort of people—a sentiment he’d delivered with the grim finality of a judge donning the black cap—but when you get right down to the brass tacks, what does it all amount to, what? A dashed fine girl is a dashed fine girl, whether her pater is a belted earl or a chap who knows his way around a potted plant. The thought struck me with the force of a well-aimed bread roll. These so-called gulfs, which look like the Grand Canyon from a distance, shrink to a mere puddle when you're standing on the brink, ready for a running jump. All a fellow really needed was a spot of nerve, a clean collar, and the heart of a lion—or, at the very least, a reasonably determined terrier. It bucked a chap up no end, I can tell you; I felt suddenly quite braced for the coming fray.
