It was a rummy business, all in all, watching young Alistair Finch navigate the city pavements with a new and quite alarming sense of purpose. Only last season, Alistair’s chief object in life seemed to be lowering the collective tone of the Drones Club and perfecting a method for avoiding his tailor. He was, to put it squarely, a Grade-A chump whose daily programme rarely extended beyond lunch. Then, however, came the business with Miss Petunia Dalgleish. It appeared the young person held certain views on the subject of gainful employment, and the effect on Alistair was nothing short of revolutionary. One now saw him striding through the crowds as if headed for an actual office, a grimly determined set to his jaw, the sort of expression usually reserved for a fellow who’s just remembered he owes you a fiver. It was like watching a particularly sluggish caterpillar suddenly decide that the butterfly business was the thing to get into; a baffling, yet undeniably potent, transformation.
