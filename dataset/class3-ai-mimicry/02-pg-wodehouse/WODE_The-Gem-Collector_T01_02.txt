Well, if you absolutely must have the unvarnished truth of the matter—and the expressions on your various faces suggest you most emphatically must—the entire sticky affair began with a simple case of mistaken identity in a crowded Lyons Corner House. One moment I was minding my own business, wondering if a second crumpet was altogether wise, and the next a stout fellow was clapping me on the shoulder and hailing me as ‘Finch, the celebrated newt-fancier!’ It seemed frightfully rude to disabuse the man, you see, particularly as he was already steering me towards a table of expectant-looking personages. Before a chap could so much as bleat a protest, I found myself holding forth on the mating habits of the crested salamander, a subject on which my entire fund of knowledge could be comfortably written on a postage stamp with room to spare. And once you've started down that path, you find it's a dashed tricky business to turn back, isn't it?
