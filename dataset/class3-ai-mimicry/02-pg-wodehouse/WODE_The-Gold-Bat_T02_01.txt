I must confess, when I first clapped eyes on Sir Roderick Finch-Hatton at the annual Drones Club ball, I mentally filed him under ‘Menaces to Society, sub-section: Glacial.’ The fellow had a jaw that looked as though it had been hewn from granite by a sculptor with a personal grudge, and his eyebrows performed a sort of permanent, disapproving V-for-Vexation over a nose that could have opened a tin of sardines. You’d sooner expect a friendly wink from a gargoyle. And yet, when young Algernon managed that spectacular spill with the tray of champagne flutes, what did this fearsome specimen do? Did he roar?
