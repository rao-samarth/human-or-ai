Liam lingered by Maya’s desk long after everyone else had filtered out for the day, the office lights humming into the quiet. “Heard anything?” he asked, his voice low, trying for casual but not quite making it. The rumors about the upcoming "restructuring" were suffocating the entire department. Maya kept her eyes on her monitor, the spreadsheet a blur of names and metrics she wished she could unsee. “Just the same chatter as everyone else,” she replied, her own voice tight. He leaned in a little closer, a friend appealing to a friend. “Look, just… should I be updating my resume? Seriously.” The question hung in the air between them, a heavy, tangible thing. She finally looked up from the screen, her expression unreadable. “It’s never a bad idea to keep it polished, right?” The answer was a masterclass in non-commitment, a carefully constructed wall that told him everything and nothing at all, and he knew in that moment their easy camaraderie had just hit solid rock.
