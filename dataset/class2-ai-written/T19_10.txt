Chloe swiped through the photos on her phone, settling on one of them both, grinning against a backdrop of sun-drenched cliffs. “Send that one to your mother,” Liam said, his eyes fixed on the highway stretching ahead. “She’ll be thrilled we’re working things out.” The screen went dark as her thumb pressed the lock button. "It's a nice picture, Liam." His laugh was a short, bitter sound that filled the small car. “It’s a lie,” he shot back, his knuckles white on the steering wheel. “It’s a perfect, well-composed lie just like this entire trip. We pose, we smile for ten seconds, and then we get back in this car and sit in a silence so thick I can barely breathe.” She stared out her window, refusing to let him see the sting in her eyes. Outside, the miles of pristine coastline blurred into an indifferent smear of green and blue, a beautiful postcard from a place they’d never really been.
