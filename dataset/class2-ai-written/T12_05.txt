Alex stared at the preliminary figures on his monitor, a document his manager had carelessly left open on the shared drive. They weren't just numbers; they were a premonition of a stock price ready to explode, a forecast far more optimistic than anything the public analysts were predicting. A cold knot of professional ethics formed in his stomach, a textbook reminder of company policy and federal law. But it was quickly warmed by a flush of pure, exhilarating opportunity. He’d worked here for six years, always following the rules, while others took shortcuts and climbed faster. This wasn't stealing from the register; it was simply acting on a piece of information that had fallen into his lap. Who would it even hurt? The question circled his mind, each repetition chipping away at his resolve. It was just a small trade, a chance to finally get ahead, to correct a cosmic imbalance he felt every time he looked at his meager savings account. His hand drifted from the mouse to his phone, the brokerage app’s icon a silent, inviting beacon.
