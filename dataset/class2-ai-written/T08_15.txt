Elias watched the rain streak down the windowpane, feeling not triumph, but a cold sense of completion. To him, what he’d done wasn’t an ending but a correction, a realignment of a world knocked violently off its axis. He had simply made things even. “So that’s it, then?” Anna’s voice was quiet from the armchair, but it sliced through his private certainty. He didn’t turn around. She saw no balance in his actions, only a new wound opened to spite an old one. It wasn't about fairness anymore; the scales hadn't been reset, they'd just been tipped in the other direction with equal, calculated cruelty. She couldn't understand how he failed to see the distinction between a debt being paid and a punishment being inflicted purely for the satisfaction of the punisher, a line he had appointed himself the sole authority to draw in the sand.
