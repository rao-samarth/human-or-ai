The cursor blinked on the half-finished report. Across the aisle, he watched her pack her bag, a small, methodical ritual that had become the quiet focal point of his afternoon. She caught his eye and gave a faint, tired smile, the kind they had shared over a dozen deadlines. His phone buzzed on the desk—a text about picking up milk on the way home, a simple request that felt like an anchor pulling him back to the life he’d chosen, the promises he kept without thinking. There was a whole conversation in that smile, a hypothetical story he could never let himself write, filled with possibilities that would forever remain just that. He turned back to the screen. The words on the page were clear, logical, and had a definite purpose, which was more than he could say for his own thoughts.
