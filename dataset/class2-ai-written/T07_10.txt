You must understand,” the Minister said, leaning forward, his voice a carefully modulated instrument of reason. “Your movement is a ghost, a whisper in the static that we can erase with a single command. All you are accomplishing with this silence is ensuring that your followers will have a martyr, but they will not have a future.” He watched the prisoner’s face for any flicker of doubt, any crack in her composure. There was none. She simply met his gaze, the exhaustion in her eyes overshadowed by a resolve that felt ancient and unbreakable. "A future you define," she replied, her voice raspy but clear, "is just a more comfortable cage." The Minister sat back, the smooth veneer of his patience wearing thin. He saw then that he wasn't speaking to a person he could break, but to an idea he couldn't possibly bargain with, and it was an idea that had already escaped these four walls.
