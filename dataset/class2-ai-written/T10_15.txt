Senator Croft’s voice boomed across the ballroom, a perfectly calibrated instrument of sincerity. He gestured toward the screen, where a stark red line plunged downwards. “And under my opponent,” he declared, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial hush, “that line represents a thousand jobs lost. A thousand families.” The room erupted in applause. My thumb flew across my phone's screen, pulling up the original report from the Bureau. The number was there, yes, but it was buried on page forty-two, surgically removed from the paragraph explaining the massive industry-wide downturn that caused it. He hadn't fabricated a thing. He had simply isolated a single, verifiable data point and forged it into a spear, hurling it at his rival while the crowd cheered his devastating accuracy. He was a master not of the lie, but of the perfectly aimed, lethally incomplete truth.
