Lungs burned. Feet slapped the packed dirt of the lane, kicking up small puffs of dust with every desperate stride. Faster. He had to go faster. The image of her face when the words left his mouth—that sharp, splintering look—kept perfect pace beside him. It flickered at the edge of his vision, superimposed over the silent, swaying wheat fields. He pumped his arms, gasping for air, trying to physically out-muscle the memory. But the quiet countryside offered no escape, no competing noise to drown out the relentless replay in his head. There was only the empty road, the sound of his own ragged breathing, and the unshakable feeling of his own voice, a phantom echo twisting the peaceful afternoon into a frantic, looping accusation.
