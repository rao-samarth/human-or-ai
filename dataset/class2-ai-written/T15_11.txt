“Please just lower your voice,” he whispered, his knuckles white around the handle of his coffee mug. The couple at the next table had stopped pretending to read the menu. “I’m not lowering my voice until you explain this.” She slid her phone across the table, the screen showing a satellite view of a neighborhood gridded with colored lines. “These are her running routes, Liam. You’ve logged every street, every turn, for the past six weeks. Who does that?” He didn’t seem to notice the other patrons, his eyes locked on the screen with a frightening intensity. “It’s not what you think. It’s for her safety. I’m just making sure she’s okay.” “From what?” she hissed, leaning forward. “From a jog? This isn’t protective; it's a playbook. You’re scaring me.” He just shook his head slowly, a faint, unnerving smile forming as if she were the one who simply couldn’t see the logic.
