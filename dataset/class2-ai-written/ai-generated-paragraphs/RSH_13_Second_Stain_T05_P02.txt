The floorboards groaned under his frantic pacing in the otherwise silent house. It had to disappear. All of it. He ripped the ledgers from the hidden compartment behind the bookshelf, the brittle pages tearing under his slick grip. He refused to read the names, the incriminating numbers; he couldn't bear to look. Twenty years of careful construction, of community awards and respectable dinners, all threatened by this single, damning stack of paper. He shoved the books into the cold fireplace, sloshing accelerant over the pile with a dangerously unsteady hand. The match flared, a sudden, violent bloom of light in the dark study. For a second, he watched the ink bubble and blacken, the signatures of his ruin curling into ash. A car passed on the street outside, its headlights slicing across the room, and he instinctively dropped to a crouch, his heart seizing in his chest.
