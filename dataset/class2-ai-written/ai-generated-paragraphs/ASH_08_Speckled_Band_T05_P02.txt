He had done it again. Standing there in the aisle of the grocery store, clutching a carton of eggs, she saw the flyer tacked to the community bulletin board. There was her name, in a cheerful bubble-letter font, listed as the lead vocalist for the Founder's Day Hootenanny. Her husband, that angel, had apparently volunteered her at the town hall meeting she’d missed due to a ‘migraine’ he had thoughtfully diagnosed. He knew she couldn't carry a tune in a bucket—the whole town knew, ever since her catastrophic attempt at karaoke at the VFW hall years ago. But he’d framed it as a beautiful gesture of encouragement. How could she say no now? Refusing would make her seem like a poor sport, ungrateful for his boundless belief in her hidden talents. She was trapped, destined to screech out a patriotic medley in front of everyone she had ever met, all because her loving partner had built her a cage out of public expectation and called it a stage.
