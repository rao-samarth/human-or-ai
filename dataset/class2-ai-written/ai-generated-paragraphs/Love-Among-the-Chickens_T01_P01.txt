The train lurched, but the man beside me didn't miss a beat. He was all frantic energy, jabbing a finger at his tablet as the French countryside blurred past the window. "It's not just an app," he insisted, his voice a low, urgent hum over the click-clack of the rails. "It's a paradigm shift in interspecies communication!" On the screen, a stock photo of a dolphin swam in a loop while garbled audio warbled. He swiped, and a terribly formatted block of text appeared. "See? That click pattern? It's a perfect sonnet about the existential angst of the abyssal plain." He was already talking about venture capital, about scaling the platform for whales, then squirrels. A flicker on the screen revealed a loading icon that never finished spinning. He didn't seem to notice, lost in a vision of a future where hedge fund managers paid a premium to hear porpoises recite Hamlet.
