“Don’t you dare talk to me about their approval,” she said, her voice a low, sharp thing against the capital’s ceaseless roar. “You think that noise makes you right? I stood on this very spot and watched them burn an effigy of the man they called their savior six months ago. These people?” She swept a hand towards the chaotic river of commuters and tourists, their faces a blur of passing indifference. “Their loyalty is a weather vane, spinning with whatever gossip blows hardest from the morning feeds. You've traded everything for the admiration of a crowd that would forget your name for a new spectacle before lunchtime. Their praise isn't a shield; it's a paper crown that will dissolve in the first rain.”
