He holds the room so easily, a king in his casual kingdom of relatives and old friends. I watch him move, the practiced laugh, the firm hand on a cousin’s shoulder, and a coldness settles deep in my stomach. I remember when that smile used to reach his eyes. Now it’s just a polished performance, an investment in future favors. Each person here is a rung on a ladder he’s still climbing, even at his own mother's birthday. They see the brilliant man who built a world from scratch, but they don’t see the scorched earth where our world used to be. He talks about his next great venture, his eyes gleaming with a fire that once warmed me but now only threatens to burn everything down. He hollowed out our home to build his monument, and I am left living in the empty, echoing space he left behind.
