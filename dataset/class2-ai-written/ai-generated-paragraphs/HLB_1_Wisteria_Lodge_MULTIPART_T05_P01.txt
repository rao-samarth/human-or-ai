The server hummed, a monotonous sound that usually faded into the background of the finance floor. Not today. Today it was a countdown timer. Alistair kept his eyes locked on the quarterly projections, his fingers clattering on the keyboard in a rhythm that matched his colleagues'. But his left hand was sweating, hovering millimetres from the emergency data-wipe key under his desk. Across the glass partition, the two "auditors" from the Frankfurt office were speaking rapid, low German, their focus entirely on his terminal. They weren't looking for accounting errors. The network access light on his PC blinked green, then green again. The transfer was almost complete. One of them glanced up, his eyes narrowing slightly. A question. Alistair gave a tight, professional smile and felt the key click under his thumb just as the man started to rise from his chair. The screen went black. Too close. He had the package, but now he had to get out of a building with more cameras than windows.
