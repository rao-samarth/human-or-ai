The sprint began, inexplicably, over a spilled crate of oranges. One moment he was trying to steer a yapping terrier away from a street performer, the next he was being pursued through the packed square by two separate groups of very serious people. He ducked behind a falafel stand, the dog’s leash tangling around the proprietor's legs and creating a spectacular, sizzling mess. A man in a sharp suit shouted, “He’s more agile than the dossier suggested!” He wasn't agile; he was panicking. He vaulted a row of newspaper boxes, nearly losing his grip on the small, heavy bag in his hand. They were obviously after the bag. A rival team cut him off by the subway entrance, yelling about a "package." He clutched it protectively, weaving through the startled commuters. Why on earth would international criminals be so desperate to steal a limited-edition, artisan-made dog grooming kit?
