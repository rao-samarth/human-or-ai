The marketing launch for the “Synergy-Pro Max” was imploding in real-time, a beautiful supernova of missed deadlines and broken links. Everyone was scrambling, fueled by lukewarm coffee and sheer panic. Everyone, that is, except for Gerald in accounting. He seemed to move with a strange, deliberate molasses-like quality whenever a task originated from Sarah’s team. A crucial spreadsheet would get "corrupted" just before a meeting. A vital budget approval would be "stuck in his queue" behind a request for more paperclips. People were getting suspicious. Finally, a junior analyst cornered him by the water cooler and asked what the deal was. Gerald just stared into the middle distance for a long moment, adjusted his glasses, and muttered, “Some people don’t respect company property. They think they can just take what they want from your desk, and it all started with a cherry-red Boston Bulldog stapler back in 2018.” The analyst blinked, realizing their entire Q4 forecast was being held hostage by a five-year-old stationery dispute.
