The conference room door slammed open, a shockwave of sound that ripped through the afternoon quiet. She stood there, not in a business suit but in running clothes, her face a pale, tight mask. Her eyes scanned the glass-walled room and found them instantly: him and the junior analyst, leaning over a spreadsheet, their hands casually clasped in a small, damning gesture of intimacy. No one moved. The air thickened. Then, she was in motion, a blur of focused fury that bypassed shouts or tears. Her hand shot out, snatching the heavy crystal sales award from the credenza beside the coffee machine. The air split with a sickening crack as she brought it down on his head. He crumpled without a sound. The analyst shrieked, scrambling back as the woman turned, the award still gripped in her hand, her expression now utterly blank. The silent, stunned office was suddenly a crime scene.
