Another handshake, another practiced smile that never quite reached her eyes. The room was a symphony of competing interests, a low hum of ambition set to the clinking of crystal glasses. She scanned the crowd, a quick, sweeping assessment. The finance minister from the eastern province, looking too pleased—a problem for tomorrow. A journalist with a predatory gaze near the bar—avoid. There. The trade delegate, cornered by a rival, looking for an exit. That was the play. With a slight nod to her aide, she began to move, a shark gliding through shimmering water. Every step was a calculation, every polite laugh a carefully deployed weapon. The real battles weren't fought with armies in fields but here, under the glare of chandeliers, with a firm grip and a perfectly timed word. The weight of it all settled not on her shoulders, but behind her ribs, a cold, hard knot of responsibility.
