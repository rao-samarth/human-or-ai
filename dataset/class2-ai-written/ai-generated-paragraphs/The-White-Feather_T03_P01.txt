The traffic light bled red against the wet asphalt, and the crowd on the corner swelled, a collective sigh of impatience. She stood near the edge, her expression unreadable, just one more face among dozens. A canvas tote bag, heavy with groceries, dangled from her hand. No one could see the frantic calculations running behind her eyes, the replaying of a conversation that had ended only an hour before. Her entire world had been knocked off its axis, a fragile structure threatening to collapse. To the man checking his phone beside her, she was a static part of the urban landscape. But inside, she was a fortress under siege, holding every stone in place through sheer, silent force of will. The signal flashed to a white walking figure. The herd surged forward. And so did she, moving with a practiced steadiness that betrayed nothing.
