He had moved to the small stone cottage for isolation, a place where the loudest sound was the wind rustling through the sycamore trees. His life in the city had been a cacophony of failed relationships and demanding work, and this was his quiet escape. He certainly hadn't counted on her. She lived on the adjacent farm, a place of muddy boots and early mornings, her hands perpetually smelling of soil and rain. They first spoke over a shared fence line, arguing about a wandering sheep. The argument, however, quickly softened into a conversation, then into a shared cup of tea on her porch as the afternoon sun dipped low. He found himself noticing the way she laughed, a genuine, unforced sound that seemed to chase the lingering silence from the valley. She, in turn, was drawn to the gentle weariness in his eyes, a history she somehow wanted to understand. Neither had been looking for anything, yet here it was, growing as quietly and surely as the wildflowers in the meadow between their properties.
