The woman sat on the worn bench, her coat frayed at the cuffs and a simple scarf covering most of her gray hair. Commuters rushed past, their eyes sliding over her as if she were just another piece of the station's grimy furniture. A few feet away, a young man in a pristine suit checked his watch, the picture of polished importance, his impatience a palpable force field. It would have surprised them all to know the truth. The woman was not destitute or lost; she was a celebrated linguist, fluent in six dead languages, calmly absorbing the modern symphony of the station while waiting for a fellow academic. The man, however, was not the captain of industry he appeared to be. The suit was borrowed, the watch a cheap replica, and beneath the composed exterior, his heart hammered against his ribs in a frantic rhythm, terrified of failing the job interview that felt like his only chance.
