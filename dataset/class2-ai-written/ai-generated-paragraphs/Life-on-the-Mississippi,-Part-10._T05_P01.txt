There was a time when every movement felt rehearsed, a clumsy imitation of another's skill. But today, something was different. His hands moved with a quiet certainty across the smooth grain of the wood, the tool an extension of his own will rather than a foreign object to be conquered. He wasn't thinking about the lessons anymore, or the countless failed attempts that came before; the constant, questioning voice in his head had finally fallen silent. He paused, wiping a fine layer of dust from his knuckles, and saw not a practice piece, but a creation that was wholly his own. A breeze stirred the long grass in the meadow outside the open workshop door, carrying the scent of damp earth and possibility. It felt less like an ending and more like learning to breathe for the first time.
