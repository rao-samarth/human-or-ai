The large man leaned over the small cafe table, his voice a low rumble meant only for the person opposite him, though several nearby patrons suddenly found their laptops fascinating. “So, we have an understanding,” he growled, cracking his knuckles. “You pay the weekly ‘insurance’ fee, and nothing unfortunate happens to this nice little place.” The smaller man, who had been meticulously reviewing a spreadsheet, looked up over his glasses. He didn’t seem scared, merely thoughtful. “I’m not sure we do have an understanding,” he replied calmly. “Your proposition, legally speaking, constitutes extortion under Penal Code 518. Furthermore, your implied threat of property damage is a separate offense. Given the three security cameras in here, one of which has a clear view of your distinctive tattoo, your expected value from this venture is overwhelmingly negative when factoring in probable legal fees and custodial sentencing. Would you like me to run the numbers for you?” The large man just blinked. The only sound was a barista dropping a spoon.
