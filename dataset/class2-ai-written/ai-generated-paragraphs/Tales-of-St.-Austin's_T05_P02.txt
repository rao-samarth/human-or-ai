I’m not angry, I just need you to be straight with me. The words hung in the air, heavier than any shout would have been. It was right there, on the tip of my tongue—the easy excuse, the plausible story about how it must have happened before I ever touched it. A simple version of events that didn't include the frantic search for the keys, the stupid idea to just “move it for a minute,” or the sickening scrape of metal against the brick pillar. But looking at their face, I saw it wasn't about the damage anymore. That was just a problem to be fixed. This was about the choice I made right now, in this quiet room. The lie felt cheap and hollow, while the truth, as terrifying as it was, had a solid, undeniable weight—a weight I suddenly understood was mine to carry.
