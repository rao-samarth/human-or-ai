Across the living room, illuminated by the low fire, a familiar contest was underway. Richard was describing the superior square footage of his new lakeside cabin, carefully omitting the crushing mortgage that came with it, his voice a little too loud in the post-dinner quiet. His cousin, Sarah, not to be outdone, countered with a detailed account of her recent promotion, peppering her story with terms like "strategic acquisition" and "scaling verticals," as if these phrases were tangible trophies she could place on the mantelpiece. They spoke of owning, of expanding, of conquering these tiny, personal empires built of equity and job titles. An elderly aunt, watching from her armchair, simply stirred her tea. She had seen generations of her family perform this same ritual, each convinced their particular scramble for a slightly better patch of earth was a uniquely noble pursuit, never noticing they were all just running in the same small, gilded circle.
