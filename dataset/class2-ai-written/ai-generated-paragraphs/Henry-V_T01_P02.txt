The truck rumbled over the uneven dirt track, its headlights cutting a weak path through the deepening twilight. Liam pointed a grime-covered finger past the cracked windshield. “Look,” he said, his voice brimming with a youthful hope that felt like a relic from another time. “You can see the settlement’s lights. We actually made it.” He turned, expecting a shared smile, but found only the familiar exhaustion etched on Anya’s face. She stared at the distant pinpricks of light, her gaze unfocused, seeing not a destination but the faces of those who hadn’t survived the journey. She was calculating dwindling supplies and feeling the phantom weight of every single choice that led them to this precarious new beginning. “Every one of those lights is a responsibility,” she murmured, her voice barely a whisper above the engine’s drone. “It just means the real work is about to start.”
