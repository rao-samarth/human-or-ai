The flashbulbs were relentless, turning the ballroom into a staccato burst of white light. “Smile, darling,” he murmured, his voice a low hum against her ear, lost in the din of polite chatter. “The press loves us tonight.” She adjusted the diamond necklace, her fingers cold against her skin. “How much longer do we have to play this part?” Her own smile felt like a mask hardening on her face, a fragile piece of porcelain. “Just until the main course is served. Mr. Abernathy is watching from the head table.” His hand tightened on the small of her back, a possessive gesture that looked like affection to anyone more than three feet away. “He needs to see a united front. Remember what’s at stake.” “I remember,” she said, her gaze fixed on a point over the photographer’s shoulder. “I’m just not sure it’s worth the price anymore.” He simply squeezed her hand in response, raising it to his lips for a perfunctory kiss as another camera flashed, capturing the perfect moment for the morning papers.
