The only light came from the small lamp on the kitchen table, casting a harsh glow on his father’s face. “You think this is a game,” the old man said, his voice low and frayed. “You think the world out there is waiting to hand you a prize for being clever.” He gestured around the quiet room, at the worn but solid furniture. “Everything I broke my back for, you just want to walk away from it.” The son shifted the weight of the pack on his shoulder. “This is your dream, Dad. It was never mine.” A deep, unbridgeable quiet settled between them. “I saw what that dream cost you,” the son added, his own voice barely a whisper. “I won’t pay that price.” His father’s face crumpled, not with anger anymore, but with a profound, shattering bewilderment, as if the foundation of his entire life had just been declared worthless by the one person it was all built for.
