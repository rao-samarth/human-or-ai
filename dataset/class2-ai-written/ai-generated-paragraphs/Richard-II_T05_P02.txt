The roar of the city was a physical thing, a wave of sound that beat against the armored glass of the motorcade. She kept her hand raised in that slow, familiar arc, a gesture practiced to perfection. Each face in the endless crowd was a bright, demanding blur, their adulation a currency she no longer knew how to spend. Someone held up a photograph, a faded image from years ago when his laugh was still the loudest sound in any room, and for a breathtaking second, the world fractured. Her gloved fingers tightened on the velvet seat, a desperate anchor against the undertow of memory. But the smile remained fixed, a mask of serene strength meticulously painted on for the world to see. The procession had to continue. The crown, and all its crushing weight, had to be borne.
