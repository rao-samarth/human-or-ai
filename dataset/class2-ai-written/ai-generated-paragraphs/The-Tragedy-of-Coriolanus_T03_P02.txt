"You have to see the bigger picture," she hissed, her voice barely cutting through the clatter of the station cafe. He slammed his coffee cup down, sloshing dark liquid onto the saucer. "The picture is a lie! We built this entire campaign on one clear promise, and you're asking me to sign off on its exact opposite." She leaned across the tiny table, her eyes intense. "It's not the opposite, it's a foothold. We get this win, we get a seat at the table. Power. You can't change a thing from the outside, yelling into the wind." A train announcement blared overhead, but neither of them flinched. "What good is a seat if you've sold your spine to pay for the chair?" he shot back, pushing his own chair from the table, its metal legs scraping harshly on the tile. "You call it a foothold. I call it a leash. They'll own us." Her face hardened. "So your grand strategy is noble irrelevance? We lose everything, but at least our principles are intact?" He just grabbed his coat from the back of the chair and turned away.
