The conversation drifted over from the next table, a quiet murmur against the clink of porcelain. "The therapist says Barnaby’s ennui might stem from the summer in Provence," a woman said, her voice a low, tired hum. "Apparently, the lack of structured adversity has stunted his resilience." Her friend made a sympathetic sound. "We had a similar issue with Clementine after the sailing trip. Her riding instructor said she'd lost her competitive edge entirely. We’re thinking a carefully curated philanthropic trip, perhaps to Guatemala, might… recalibrate her." They both fell silent then, stirring their identical, untouched cappuccinos, their gazes fixed on some middle distance beyond the rain-streaked window, as if searching for a genuine hardship they could never quite purchase.
