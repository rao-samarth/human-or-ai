The train rattled on, blurring the countryside into a smear of green and grey. Every few miles, another town, and every town had its spire. A cross here, a crescent there, each one a silent, rigid finger pointing at a different, exclusive sky. I felt a wave of dizzying despair wash over me. Were we all just born into a story, a map drawn by our ancestors, and told to follow it or be lost? The idea that your eternal fate could be decided by the random chance of your birthplace seemed like the most monstrously unfair proposition imaginable. Billions of people, all whispering different prayers into the same indifferent wind, each utterly convinced their whisper was the only one being heard. The sheer, deafening volume of all that certainty felt like the most profound silence I had ever known.
