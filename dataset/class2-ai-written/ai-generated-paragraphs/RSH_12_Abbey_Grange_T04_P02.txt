“You're certain?” The question was sharp, an accusation in itself. He watched her across the small desk, his gaze boring into her as if it could excavate the truth. She held his stare, her hands folded calmly in her lap, a perfect picture of composure she did not feel. Inside, her heart hammered against her ribs, a frantic drumbeat against the steady, quiet lie she had just constructed. “He packed a bag this morning. Said he was going to visit his cousins up north for a while.” Every word was a carefully placed stone in a wall she was building around the boy, who was at that very moment huddled in the attic room, listening to the floorboards creak. The man across from her leaned forward, his suspicion a palpable force in the dim light. She didn't flinch. This untruth was a bitter thing, but it was also a shield, and she would hold it up until it broke.
