He watched the crane hoist another steel girder into place, a clean, powerful motion against the flat blue sky. From his desk, the construction site was a puzzle assembling itself into a logical future for the town—commerce, structure, a necessary step forward. He admired the precision of the crew, the straightforward physics of it all. This was how things were built, how they improved. She, however, saw it differently from her window above the bakery. For her, the raw earth and rising frame weren’t a beginning but an ending. They had torn out the old community garden for this, that small patch of green where conversations grew as readily as tomatoes. She thought of the long afternoon shadows the new building would cast, a permanent cooling of the sunniest spot on the square. They were both observing the same grid of metal and men, yet one saw a foundation being laid while the other felt a space being lost.
