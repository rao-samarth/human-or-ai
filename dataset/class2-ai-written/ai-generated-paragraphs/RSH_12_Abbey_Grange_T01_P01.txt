The champagne flutes chimed together, a delicate sound that couldn't quite mask the hollowness in the room. On the stage, a man was being celebrated for his shrewd business acumen, lauded for navigating complex regulations to achieve unprecedented success. The official citations spoke of his genius, his adherence to every statute. Yet, scattered among the applauding guests were a few who knew the real story. They remembered the small competitor he had crushed, not with illegality, but with a perfectly legal, soul-crushing exploitation of a loophole. They knew the "cost-saving measures" were just firings of loyal employees right before their pensions vested. The law had been followed meticulously, a shield for actions that were fundamentally wrong. The real crime, it seemed, wasn't breaking the rules, but following them so perfectly that you broke people instead. And for that, there was no court, only the silent judgment of a few witnesses in a gilded room.
