He was carefully snipping the deadheads from the lavender bushes, the silver clippers making a soft, rhythmic sound in the afternoon stillness. The air smelled of warm earth and the low hum of bees. A sudden crunch of tires on the gravel driveway announced her arrival, and moments later she burst onto the stone patio, a gust of city energy disrupting the quiet. Her phone was already held aloft as if searching for a signal. “The connection out here is impossible,” she stated, not as a complaint but as an immediate problem to be solved. He straightened up slowly, wiping his hands on his trousers, and gestured with the clippers toward a pitcher of iced tea sweating on the wicker table. “There’s lemonade,” he offered. She barely glanced at it, her thumb already swiping furiously across the screen. “No time,” she said, her voice sharp. “Just have to fire off this one email, then we can strategize the quarterly projections.” He simply nodded, turning back to his lavender.
