The delay was announced with a weary chime, and I found myself staring through the smoked glass of the first-class lounge. Inside, a man in a crisp, uncreased shirt laughed silently into his phone, swirling a dark liquid in a heavy-bottomed glass. A woman across from him gracefully turned a page of a magazine, her posture perfect. Beyond the glass, out here in the main concourse, the light was harsher, the sounds a chaotic blend of rolling suitcases and tired children. A young family was trying to consolidate three bags into two, their faces etched with stress. It was strange to think that the only real thing separating these two worlds was a specific sequence of letters on a boarding pass. A simple code, printed by a machine, had sorted everyone into different realities, granting one peace and the other a frantic, fluorescent-lit wait.
