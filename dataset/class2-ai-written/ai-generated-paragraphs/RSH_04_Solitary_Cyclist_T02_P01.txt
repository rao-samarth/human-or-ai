The chandeliers cast a fractured, brilliant light over the swirling crowd, but for her, the room felt dim, muffled. Each burst of laughter from the polished floor was a sound from another world, a frequency she could no longer tune into. She smoothed down the silk of her dress, a pointless, repetitive gesture against the sudden chill that had nothing to do with the air temperature. Her smile was a carefully constructed thing, a fragile piece of architecture she feared might collapse if someone looked too closely. She was an island in a sea of glittering tuxedos and shimmering gowns, the current of celebration parting around her, leaving her untouched. A single, cold tear escaped, tracing a hot path down her cheek before she could catch it, a tiny, silent betrayal of the poise she was so desperately trying to maintain.
