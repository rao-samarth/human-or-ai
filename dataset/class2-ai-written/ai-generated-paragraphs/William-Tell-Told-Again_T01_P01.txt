A single lamp cast long, dancing shadows across the cramped room. "They're putting up the new banners in the square tomorrow," he said, his voice a low murmur that barely disturbed the silence. She didn't look up from her tea, just continued tracing the rim of the chipped mug with her finger. "Let them," she said flatly. "What are you proposing we do about it?" He leaned forward, the faint light catching the intensity in his eyes. "We're going to give people something else to read." Her hand froze. "You can't be serious. They have eyes everywhere, you know that. You'll end up on a list." He finally managed a grim smile. "It's better than being invisible while they erase everything we are. If we stay silent, we've already lost."
