Sarah’s voice was a low hiss, barely audible over the clatter of ceramic and the barista calling out an order. “We have to tell her.” David stared into his cooling latte, refusing to meet her eyes across the small table. “Tell her what? That her entire life, our entire childhood, was built on a lie?” He finally looked up, his expression pleading. “It would destroy her, Sarah. What is the point of that?” She shook her head, a sharp, angry motion that made a nearby woman glance over from her laptop. “The point? The point is that the lie is already destroying us. It's why you can't hold a relationship, it's why I have nightmares. We are living with a ghost. Don't you see? We're not protecting her; we're just choosing a slower poison.” He flinched, slumping back in his chair as if her words were a physical blow. The silence that followed was heavier than any argument.
