He was speaking in a low voice to his companion, but the words carried across the quiet cafe with a certain weight. “We are so often taught to admire the ascent, the relentless climb toward some self-defined sun. People will burn every bridge to feel that warmth, convinced that the peak is the only place worth being. They ignore the strain on the wings they’ve built, the quiet fraying of the feathers, because their eyes are fixed on the light. The fatal flaw isn't the desire to fly high, but the forgetting of gravity. The fall, when it comes, is never a surprise to anyone but them. It isn’t the sudden impact that destroys a person; it is the long, terrifying drop in which they have time to realize they have built their entire world in a place they could never remain.”
