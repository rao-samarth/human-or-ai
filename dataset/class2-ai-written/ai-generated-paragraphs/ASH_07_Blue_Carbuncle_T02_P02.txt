In Oak Creek, everyone agreed that Mrs. Gable was the absolute picture of grandmotherly virtue. Her prize-winning petunias were a local legend, and she was never seen without a flour-dusted floral apron and a gentle, crinkly smile. She was the kind of woman who'd hand-deliver a pie just because it was Tuesday. That was the established narrative, anyway. The story cracked when young Leo from next door was called over to fix her "sluggish internet." He expected to find her knitting by the window. Instead, he found her study plunged into darkness, illuminated only by the frantic, colorful glow of three massive computer monitors. A formidable headset crowned her perfectly coiffed silver hair. With the intensity of a battlefield commander, she leaned into her microphone and hissed, "Flank them, you fools, or I'll turn your virtual kneecaps into dust!" Leo quietly backed out of the room, deciding her internet was probably fine.
