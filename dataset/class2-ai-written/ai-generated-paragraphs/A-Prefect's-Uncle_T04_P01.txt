The old wooden box sat on the coffee table, its varnish dulled by decades of dust. Inside was the letter, still sealed after all this time. He had promised his grandfather he would deliver it, but only after twenty years had passed, and only if the circumstances were right. Well, twenty years had come and gone, and the circumstances were anything but right. Delivering it now would unravel carefully constructed lives, stirring up old pains for a family that had finally found some measure of peace. He could burn it. He could simply let it sit here, another forgotten relic in a quiet house. No one else knew of its existence. But the memory of his grandfather's firm, calloused hand on his shoulder was too strong, too real. A promise was a structure you built your life on, brick by heavy brick, and this one, difficult as it was, had to be laid in place. He picked up his keys from the bowl by the door.
