“So that’s it? You just dismiss it?” Sam’s voice was a low hiss, but heads at the next table still turned. “Because it doesn’t come with a glossary? You sit there with your impenetrable texts, feeling so clever for understanding a footnote, but you don’t get this. You don’t understand what it is to push your body until it screams, to trust someone completely without a single word. It’s not an 'abstraction,' Alex. It's the most real thing there is. There’s no theory to hide behind when you’re out of breath and the clock is running down. It’s just you. Your effort. Your failure. Your victory. It’s honest. God, it’s so much more honest than sitting in a quiet room debating the meaning of a meaning, pretending that’s the same thing as actually living.” A cup clattered somewhere in the suddenly silent coffee shop.
