My uncle was at it again, holding court by the potato salad, explaining how a certain type of energized water, if sipped at dawn, could cure literally any ailment. The conviction in his voice was absolute, and a few of my cousins were nodding along, completely captivated and already asking where they could buy a case. I just sat there, swirling the ice in my drink, feeling that familiar, quiet exhaustion wash over me. It’s not just him; it's everywhere. People seem so desperate for a shortcut, for a single, hidden piece of knowledge that will solve everything, that they’ll accept the most absurd stories without a single critical question. They just want to believe. It’s so much easier than facing the messy, complicated reality that most problems are difficult and require actual work. They trade a healthy dose of skepticism for the cozy comfort of a simple answer, and nobody seems to notice what a terrible bargain that really is.
