The only light in the kitchen came from the laptop, casting long shadows across Ben’s face as he traced the rim of his cold coffee mug. “We could lose everything for this. Prison, even.” Across the small table, Sara didn’t look up from the files she was organizing into a single encrypted folder. “They're knowingly selling a faulty part, Ben. The official reports are buried, and people could die in those cars.” He shook his head slowly, his voice barely a whisper. “But this is illegal. It’s theft.” She finally met his gaze, her own eyes tired but firm. “I saw the internal emails. They called the potential victims ‘acceptable statistical losses.’ The law is protecting the corporation that wrote that. So, you tell me, who is supposed to protect the people who buy their cars?” He fell silent, his gaze dropping back to the glowing screen where the anonymous “send” button waited.
