“Don’t you dare tell me it’s just a suit,” Julian said, his voice low and tight. He gestured at the crisp lapels, the subtle sheen of the silk tie knotted perfectly at Mark’s throat. To him, it wasn’t fabric; it was a uniform for a different kind of person, a declaration of allegiance. Mark felt a familiar weariness settle in his shoulders, the weight of trying to explain the unexplainable. But Julian saw only the shell. He saw how waiters now rushed to their table, how strangers held doors with a deferential nod. People don't interact with who you *are*. They interact with the man in the thousand-dollar suit. That man gets the promotion, the respect, the benefit of the doubt. After a while, how could you not become him? The old friend was just a ghost haunting a much more impressive, and much emptier, new frame.
