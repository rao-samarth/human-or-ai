He squeezed my hand and pointed towards the old oak. “That’s the one you told me about, isn’t it? Where you built the tire swing?” The question landed with a soft, dreadful weight. My mind, a frantic archive of the city noise and concrete I actually came from, scrambled for the right detail to add to the fiction. I could see the whole narrative reflected in his eyes: the wholesome girl, the simple past, the perfect fit for this quiet life he loved so much. It would be so easy to nod, to invent a memory of scraped knees and summer afternoons. Or I could let the silence stretch, let it break this beautiful, fragile thing I had so carefully constructed between us. A breeze rustled the leaves, and for a moment, it sounded like a whisper, urging me one way or the other.
