You have a remarkable talent for appearing benevolent,” she said, her voice a low counterpoint to the polite murmur of the gala. He turned from the abstract painting, his smile a practiced, political thing that didn't quite reach his eyes. “I find philanthropy to be its own reward.” He lifted his glass in a mock toast. “Unlike you, I prefer to build things up, not tear them down.” She didn't flinch. “You build monuments to your own ego with the money you stole from my community's future. You poison our water and then host fundraisers for clean air. That isn't philanthropy; it's a performance. And I will not applaud.” His face hardened, the veneer of civility cracking just enough to show the cold fury beneath. He gestured subtly to a guard across the room, but her gaze remained locked on his, unwavering. She had already decided she would not be the one to look away first.
