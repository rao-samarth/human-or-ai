Dusk settled over the fields, turning the long grass a deep, hazy blue. He listened patiently on the creaking porch swing as she spoke of her father, her voice catching on memories that were still sharp-edged and new. He nodded at all the right moments, a practiced, gentle incline of his head. He knew this part well: the careful silence, the soft murmur of understanding. It was a foundation, built from her grief, upon which he could later construct his requests. While she looked out at the fading light, he wasn't seeing the sunset. He was mapping the path forward, seeing the moment her guard would finally crumble completely, the precise second he could lean in and ask for the one thing he'd wanted all along. He placed a hand on her shoulder, a gesture of pure, calculated comfort.
