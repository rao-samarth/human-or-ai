“You still don’t see it,” he said, his voice barely a whisper in the quiet living room, the glow from the streetlights outside casting long shadows across the floor. “You think it was just a misunderstanding, a few poorly chosen words.” She sighed, turning from the window. “They weren’t poorly chosen. They were aimed. I’ve heard them before, just never from someone you called a friend.” He threw his hands up in frustration. “What was I supposed to do, start a fight in their home?” His voice cracked. “Yes. Or leave. Or just look at them and tell them they were wrong. Anything but stand there nodding while they explained my own life to me. They weren’t just insulting me, they were trying to put me back in a box they’d built, and you just held the lid open for them. You watched them make me small.”
