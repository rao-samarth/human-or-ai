The familiar drone of the family get-together was momentarily pierced by his uncle’s booming laugh. He was launching into *that* story again, the one from ninth grade that always made him feel about twelve years old and foolish. A familiar heat crept up his neck as he saw his cousin smirk. He was bracing for the punchline, the same one he’d heard a dozen times, when a calm voice cut through the noise from beside him. It was his oldest friend, who had known him since they were scrawny kids sharing a lunch table. Without even looking at him, she smoothly redirected his uncle's narrative with an innocuous question about his new car, a topic she knew he could talk about for hours. The danger passed. No grand defense was needed, just this quiet, immediate intervention. It was an old reflex, a silent pact forged across countless school hallways and whispered conversations, an instinct to protect one of their own before the blow even landed.
