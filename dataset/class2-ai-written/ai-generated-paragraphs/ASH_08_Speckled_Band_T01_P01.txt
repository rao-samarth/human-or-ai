You just couldn't leave it alone, could you? We had it. A clean deal, enough for both of us to disappear, and you had to push. His knuckles were white on the steering wheel, the highway signs blurring into streaks of green and white in the twilight. She didn't turn from the window, just watched the barren landscape roll by. "Enough was never going to be enough for you," she shot back, her voice low and sharp. "You saw that last-minute opening, that little extra percentage, and you got that look in your eyes. The one that always ends with us running." He slammed a hand against the dashboard, the plastic cracking under the force. "Don't you dare put this on me. You didn't say no, did you? You saw the money, too. We're in this mess together, driving back with nothing because you got just as hungry as I did."
