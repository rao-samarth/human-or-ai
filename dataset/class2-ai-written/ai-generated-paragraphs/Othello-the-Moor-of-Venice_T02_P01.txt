The email landed like a grenade: “Execs on the floor in ten.” Instantly, the placid hum of the department shattered. Keyboards clattered with a new, frantic energy as Sarah shoved a teetering stack of overdue reports into a filing cabinet, slamming it shut just as a binder slid off the top. Across the aisle, Mark was frantically killing browser tabs—the half-finished article, the vacation planning, the job search sites—replacing them with dense spreadsheets full of impressive, if meaningless, data. Whiteboards were wiped clean of panicked diagrams and replaced with neat, approved project timelines. Smiles were plastered on, posture straightened. They were a well-oiled machine for precisely nine minutes, a flawless performance for an audience that would see only the gleaming, polished surface, never the frantic, sputtering chaos churning just beneath.
