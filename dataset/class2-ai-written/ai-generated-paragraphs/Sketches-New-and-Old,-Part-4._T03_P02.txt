The argument started over a scrawny hen, which, as the general store owner knew, belonged to neither man. It began as a low grumble in the street, a matter of wounded pride more than poultry. Silas, a man whose hands were perpetually stained with something dark, just laughed a dry, rattling laugh that scraped the air. The laughter was the final insult. A fist flew, clumsy and wide, and then a knife appeared, glinting in the afternoon sun. Before anyone could properly intervene, or even decide if they wanted to, a single shot cracked the thick silence. Jedediah slumped against a hitching post, a look of profound surprise on his face.
