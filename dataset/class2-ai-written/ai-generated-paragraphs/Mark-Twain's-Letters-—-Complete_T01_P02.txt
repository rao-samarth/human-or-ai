The only light came from the laptop screen, casting a pale, clinical glow across the counter and the neat stacks of inventory he’d spent all day arranging. He scrolled through the spreadsheet again, as if the numbers might rearrange themselves into a more favorable pattern this time. They didn’t. The initial investment, a figure that had once felt like a bold step towards freedom, now just seemed like a deep hole. Outside, the town square was quiet, the last car having rumbled past an hour ago. He thought of the encouraging smiles from neighbors, the local paper's small feature on the grand opening. It all felt like a different lifetime. Now, there was just the hum of the old refrigerator and the stark reality of the balance sheet, a constant, low-grade hum of worry that had become the soundtrack to his ambition. This was the part no one talked about: the silence after you’ve risked everything.
