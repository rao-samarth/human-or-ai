She spoke about the project with such conviction, her words painting a scene of my catastrophic failure. In her version, I was reckless, a rogue element who ignored every warning. She described meetings that never happened and quoted emails I never sent. I just sat there, clutching the arms of the cheap visitor’s chair, my own memories beginning to feel like faint, unreliable ghosts. The person she was describing wasn’t me, but the file on her desk—the one that would follow me—would say that it was. The official record was being written right in front of me, a meticulous work of fiction, and my own truth felt like a scream trapped behind glass, completely silent and completely irrelevant. What do you do when the story told about you becomes more real than you are?
