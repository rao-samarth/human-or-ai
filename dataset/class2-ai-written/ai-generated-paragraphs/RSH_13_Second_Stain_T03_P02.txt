The coffee shop buzzed with a life he no longer felt a part of. His phone vibrated on the table, a familiar, sickening pulse against the wood. He didn't need to read the text; he knew the demand. With a mechanical precision that bypassed thought, his thumb moved across the screen, opening the app and authorizing the transfer. Another piece of his future was shaved away, sent to an anonymous account that held his past captive. Outside the window, a river of pedestrians flowed under the grey afternoon sky, each person a universe of their own choices and destinations. His own world had collapsed to the size of this single, recurring transaction. It wasn't the money that was killing him. It was the slow, methodical erasure of every decision, every possibility, he had once believed was his alone to make.
