The clatter of trays faded into a dull roar as their voices cut through the lunchtime chaos. They had him cornered at the small table by the window, his shoulders hunched as their quiet accusations flew like poison darts. He wouldn't fight back; he’d just sit there and take it, letting the lie about the missing client files cement itself into office lore. A hot, decisive anger surged through my chest. I pushed my chair back, the screech of it drawing a few glances. Three long strides and I was there, standing over their little tribunal. “Looking for who to blame?” I asked, my voice ringing a little too loud in the sudden quiet. Every head turned. I met their surprised eyes, one by one, before letting my gaze fall. “It was me. I messed up the transfer and tried to cover it. It was my mistake, all of it.” A split-second decision, a lie sharp as glass, and I could already feel the brand of their judgment settling on my skin as I turned and walked away.
