The train rattled through the darkening countryside, blurring the familiar fields into a gray smear. He turned the shard of black pottery over and over in his palm, its edges still sharp enough to bite. He’d found it in the dust of a forgotten ruin, a place where kindness was a weakness to be exploited. Before this trip, he would have seen it as a simple souvenir. Now, he felt a kinship with its cold, broken nature. He thought of the person waiting for him at the station, a person who still believed in the gentle man who had left weeks ago. A hollow ache settled in his chest. That man was gone, buried under the rubble of what he’d seen and what he’d been forced to become. He closed his fist around the shard, welcoming the sting. He was bringing home a stranger, and the wreckage was only just beginning.
