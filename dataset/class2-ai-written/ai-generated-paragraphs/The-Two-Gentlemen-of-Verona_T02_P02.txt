He watched them from across the plaza, their laughter a bright, sharp sound against the city’s drone. They were kissing for a photograph, a brief performance of permanence for a digital audience. It reminded him of a similar afternoon, the sun feeling just as warm on his own skin, when he’d believed such moments were anchors, heavy enough to stop the world from spinning. He tried to recall the exact weight of that certainty now, the absolute conviction that this feeling, this person, was the final destination. But the memory was thin, a watercolor impression left out in the rain. The couple disappeared into the river of people flowing toward the subway, and the space they occupied was instantly filled. The warmth he'd felt a moment ago was just the sun on the back of his neck, and soon the shadows would lengthen and that would be gone, too.
