The clock on the mantle marked three, each tick a tiny hammer against the silence. Sleep was a distant country tonight. He stared into the fire's dying embers, seeing not flames but fractured images of the future. The boy was clever, yes, but kindness was a poor shield against ambition, and the court was filled with wolves who smiled. He had spent a lifetime patching the cracks in the foundation of this nation, holding it together with sheer will. What would happen when that will was gone? He pictured borders fraying, old grudges flaring into new wars, the careful balance he had maintained for decades shattering like glass. A tremor ran through his old hands. It wasn't death he feared, but the loud, chaotic dawn that would follow his final sunset.
