“You keep going back,” he said, his voice quiet but sharp. “It’s like you’re watching the same awful movie over and over, somehow expecting a different ending.” She flinched, turning away from his gaze. “It’s not that simple. You don’t see everything that happens.” He sighed, the sound heavy with frustration. “I see enough. I see you shrinking. Every time you talk to him, a little less of you comes back to us. He just consumes all the oxygen, even when he’s not in the room. I’m not telling you what to do, but you have to at least acknowledge that the fire alarm is screaming and you’re still sitting there telling everyone you just like the warmth.” She didn’t answer, just stared at a crack in the plaster on the wall, her silence saying everything he feared.
