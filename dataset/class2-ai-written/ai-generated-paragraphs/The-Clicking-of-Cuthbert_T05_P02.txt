Julian slammed his fork down—a deliberate, theatrical gesture—to deconstruct the problematic origins of the humble turnip. Before he could launch into his thesis, his sister Beatrice cut him off, announcing her recent microdosing journey had revealed the true interconnectedness of root vegetables and late-stage capitalism. She gestured wildly with her wine glass, sloshing pinot noir dangerously close to the white sofa. Meanwhile, their cousin Mark was trying to show everyone a video of his cat falling off a chair, his phone held aloft like a forgotten relic. Julian countered Beatrice by invoking a philosopher he’d clearly only read the Wikipedia entry for, just as their uncle, bless his heart, asked loudly if anyone had seen the remote. The entire conversation was a high-speed collision of performative intellect and utter nonsense, a frantic ballet of egos fueled by merlot and a desperate need to be the most interesting person in the room. I just watched the gravy congeal on my plate.
