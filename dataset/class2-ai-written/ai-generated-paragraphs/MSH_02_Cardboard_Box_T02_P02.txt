It wasn’t the lipstick on his collar that did it, nor the late-night texts from “Steve at the office.” No, it was the methodical *thump-thump-thump* from the back garden that truly signaled the end. He stood at the kitchen window, watching his wife systematically annihilate his life’s work. She wasn’t crying or screaming; she was just calmly, almost peacefully, swinging a pickaxe into the swelling orange belly of Bartholomew, his frontrunner for the county fair. Splinters of gourd flew like shrapnel across the manicured lawn. Bartholomew, who had been nurtured with organic compost and whispered encouragement for months, was now just a pile of seedy guts. Next, she moved onto Gourd-on Ramsay. He knew then, with a sinking feeling far deeper than any marital guilt, that the affair was the least of his problems. He had lost his legacy.
