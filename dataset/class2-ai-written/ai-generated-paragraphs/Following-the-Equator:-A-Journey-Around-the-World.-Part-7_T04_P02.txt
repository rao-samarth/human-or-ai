The usual afternoon traffic had stalled, not for an accident, but for something else entirely. A human river flowed between the canyons of glass and steel, their voices rising together in a song that seemed to push back the city's relentless hum. An old woman, her face a map of joy, held a candle whose small flame burned impossibly steady against the breeze. Beside her, a young man sang with an earnestness that felt both ancient and new, his eyes fixed on some point beyond the skyscrapers. Even the detached onlookers, paused on the sidewalk with their phones forgotten in their hands, seemed to lean into the sound. For a few minutes, the gridlock and the blaring horns were silenced by a current of shared conviction, a tangible warmth that connected strangers in a moment of profound, uncomplicated grace.
