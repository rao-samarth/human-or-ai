He sat in the quiet hum of the office, the beige walls and standardized desks offering no hint of the documents on his screen. The reports were not just information; they were alternate histories, narratives of events that the public would never know. Knowing these things felt less like holding power and more like carrying a ghost, a constant, low-grade fever of awareness that tinged every normal interaction with a sense of the unreal. When colleagues discussed the evening news, he could only nod, his silence a carefully constructed wall. How could you speak of weather or weekend plans when you understood the silent, tectonic shifts happening just beneath the surface of the world? The truth, he was learning, was not a light to be shared but a heavy, indivisible object you were tasked to hold alone, forever changing the shape of your own solitude.
