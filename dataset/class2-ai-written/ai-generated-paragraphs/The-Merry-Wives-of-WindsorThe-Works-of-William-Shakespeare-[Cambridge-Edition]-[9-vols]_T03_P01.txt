He leaned back in his leather chair, casually explaining how the multi-million dollar contract had simply “slipped through the cracks,” a term that suggested a minor, blameless accident. A faint, unapologetic smile played on his lips as if he were recounting a charming social blunder. Across the polished table, the department head felt a familiar, cold dread settle in her chest. She had built her career on meticulous planning and relentless follow-through, qualities that seemed almost quaint in his presence. For him, this was a temporary inconvenience, a problem his father's influence would eventually smooth over. For her, it was a failure that would mean late nights of damage control, difficult conversations with her team, and the gnawing anxiety that her careful, hard-won stability was ultimately subject to the whims of someone who had never known a single consequence.
