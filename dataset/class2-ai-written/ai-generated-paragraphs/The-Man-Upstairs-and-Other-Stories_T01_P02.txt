The email was gone, sent not to her sister but to the entire marketing department distribution list. Her heart hammered against her ribs as she imagined everyone reading her dramatic recap of a disastrous date, which unfortunately segued directly into a far-too-detailed paragraph about the new analyst’s charming smile and how his sweaters always looked so soft. She spent the next seven hours avoiding all human contact, certain her professional life was over. Just as she was packing up to flee, he appeared at her cubicle, leaning against the partition. She braced for impact. "For the record," he said, his own smile just as charming as she’d described, "I would never order your dinner for you." He paused, a hopeful glint in his eye. "And my sweaters are, in fact, incredibly soft." A wave of relief so potent it made her dizzy washed over her, replacing mortification with a brilliant, blooming possibility.
