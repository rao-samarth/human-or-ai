He walked into the boardroom for the quarterly review, just as he always did. The air was thick with our unspoken sympathies, a heavy silence that the hum of the projector couldn't quite cut through. But he wore his suit like armor, his voice a steady, measured thing as he spoke of revenue streams and growth potential. It was the small things that gave him away. The way his hand rested on the table, fingers perfectly still, not drumming or gesturing as they usually would. The brief, vacant look in his eyes when the projector screen changed, a flicker of disconnection before the focus returned. He was ruling his little kingdom of spreadsheets and deadlines while a much larger one had crumbled. We all sat there, participating in the charade, pretending we only saw the commander and not the man hollowed out behind the eyes.
