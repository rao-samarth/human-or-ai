In a town like Mill Creek, the paths were worn deep before anyone was even born. You could see it in the way the high school quarterback, once a local legend, now stocked shelves at the only grocery store, his shoulders stooped from a different kind of weight. He never left. Why would he? Here, he was somebody. His best friend, however, had been different. She was the quiet one who had spent her summers working at the dusty library, absorbing stories of worlds beyond the county line. An out-of-state scholarship was her one-way ticket, a fissure in the predetermined landscape. Now a researcher in a distant city, she rarely thought of the boy who peaked at seventeen. For him, the horizon had always been the familiar silhouette of the water tower, a permanent, unmoving fixture against the sky. For her, it was just the first thing she had to drive past.
