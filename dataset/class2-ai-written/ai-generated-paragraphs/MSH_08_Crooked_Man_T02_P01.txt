The only light came from the monitor, painting the study in cold blue tones. Every accolade he’d received, every easy laugh he’d shared at a party while I stood in the corner, seemed to coalesce into the file icon on the screen. It was so simple, really. Just a drag and a drop. My own breath was a stranger in the room, shallow and quick. The cursor blinked over the "Send" button, a tiny, pulsing heartbeat before the final act. I pushed the mouse forward, the plastic cool beneath my trembling fingers. The resulting click was obscenely loud, a crack in the foundation of the night. There was no rush of triumph, only a sudden, chilling stillness. A floorboard settled upstairs with a groan, and for a moment, it sounded like something vital had finally broken.
