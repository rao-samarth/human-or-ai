The old orchard was a wasteland of stumps and churned earth, silent under the pale afternoon sun. Maria kicked at a shard of shale, the sound sharp in the quiet. "You remember what it was like?" she asked, not looking at him. "Before." Leo stuffed his hands in his pockets, his gaze fixed on the horizon where the new processing plant coughed a thin grey line into the sky. "It was supposed to be a sure thing," he muttered. "A fortune. We could have bought ten orchards." She gave a short, mirthless laugh. "A fortune," Maria repeated, her voice hollow. "And now we have poisoned soil that won't grow a thing for decades.
