He kept talking, his voice a low, reasonable hum in the quiet office. The words made a kind of sense, a chessboard logic of sacrifice and strategy, of lesser evils and long-term gains. Each syllable was a small, smooth stone dropped into the well of my convictions, and I could feel the water level rising, threatening to drown what I once thought was immutable. He called it pragmatism. I just felt a hollowing out, a quiet erasure of the person I was supposed to be. He wasn't asking for my understanding, only my complicity. The silence that followed his explanation felt heavier than any argument, filled with the ghost of a principle we had both just agreed to bury. Out the window, the city lights began to blur, each one a distant, unwavering star I could no longer navigate by.
