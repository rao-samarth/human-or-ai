He moved like a phantom through the throng, a whisper of motion against the city's roar. The bump was textbook, the wallet a smooth transfer from their pocket to his. Easy. He grinned, melting into the human river flowing down the crowded avenue. A quick turn into a grimy alleyway, the stench of rain and refuse a familiar comfort. He pulled out the leather prize, a satisfying weight in his palm. Flipping it open, his eyes scanned past the cash and cards, catching on a small, blinking red light tucked into a hidden sleeve. A tracker. His blood ran cold. He fumbled to pry it out, his fingers suddenly clumsy, but it was too late. Heavy footsteps echoed from the alley's mouth, blocking the only escape. The very thing he had stolen had become his beacon, shining a spotlight on his crime for all the wrong people to see.
