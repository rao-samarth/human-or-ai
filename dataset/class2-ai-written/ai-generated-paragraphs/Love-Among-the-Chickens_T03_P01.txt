I just can’t do this anymore. Kevin’s voice was a strained whisper, cracking under the sterile fluorescent lights of the conference room as he stared at the whiteboard, a frantic web of acronyms connecting “synergistic mind-share” to “leveraging our core competencies.” His knuckles were white where he gripped the table’s edge. Back home, a crisis meant the well pump was broken or a coyote was circling the livestock—real, tangible problems you could fight with sweat and grit. Here, the world was ending because their Q3 brand sentiment analysis showed a 0.8% dip in positive-to-neutral keyword association. Brenda from marketing was actually dabbing her eyes with a tissue, genuinely distraught. A wave of dizzying, hysterical absurdity washed over him. They were weeping over hashtags, treating a spreadsheet like a casualty report, and all he could think about was the solid, honest feel of a shovel in his hands and dirt under his fingernails.
