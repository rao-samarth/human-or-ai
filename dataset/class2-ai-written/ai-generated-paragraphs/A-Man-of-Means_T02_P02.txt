He watched the presentation unfold, his coffee growing cold in his hands. There were his late-night diagrams, now rendered in sleek corporate graphics. He heard his own turn of phrase, the one he’d been so proud of, spoken from his manager’s mouth as if it were a casual thought. His name was never mentioned. It wasn’t theft, not in a way you could prove; it was absorption. He recalled the encouraging smiles and the casual invitations to "share whatever you’ve got," seeing them now not as collaboration but as mining. A senior colleague caught his eye and gave a faint, knowing smile that offered no comfort, only confirmation. The open-hearted enthusiasm he had brought to the job just weeks ago felt like a phantom limb, an ache for something that was no longer there. In its place, something brittle and small began to form, a lesson learned in the quiet hum of the projector.
