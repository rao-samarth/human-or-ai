Kael slammed the datapad on the polished desk, the sound sharp in the quiet room. “The council’s decision is final, Lena. The reclamation project proceeds. It's for the greater good.” She didn’t flinch, her voice low and steady. “Whose good, Kael? We're paving over the last wild marsh, displacing a whole community that has lived in its rhythm for centuries, all for another sterile block of habitation units. They have songs for that place, you know. They have stories woven into the reeds. What do we have?” He scoffed, turning to look out at the sprawling, ordered city lights. “We have medicine. We have infrastructure. We have security.” She finally stood, her chair scraping against the floor. “We have cages. Beautiful, well-lit cages. We’ve traded everything that felt real for predictable comfort and called it advancement. Look at them out there. Do they even know what an unobstructed horizon looks like anymore?”
