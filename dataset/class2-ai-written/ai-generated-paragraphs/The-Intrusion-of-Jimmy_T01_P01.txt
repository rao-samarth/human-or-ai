“You see that man by the bar?” Eleanor murmured, tilting her champagne flute discreetly. “The one in the slightly-too-tight suit?” Julian followed her gaze to the newcomer, a man whose energy seemed too bright for the room’s gilded dimness. “I heard his family has no real history here. Made his fortune in something dreadfully common, like shipping.” She took a delicate sip. Julian smiled faintly. “Perhaps,” he replied, turning back to her. “But I saw him last week, after the community fundraiser downtown. It was late, raining, and he was helping an elderly woman whose car had broken down, holding an umbrella over her while he changed her tire in the dark. He got completely soaked. He didn't have to do that; no one he wanted to impress was around.” Eleanor was silent for a moment, her polished critique faltering as the string quartet began a new, softer melody.
