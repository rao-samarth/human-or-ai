The polite murmur of the gala was a world away from the venom in her voice. She cornered him by the ice sculpture, her smile a thin, sharp line. "You're quite the performer," she said, her voice a low hum beneath the string quartet. "So charming, so attentive. It must be exhausting." He adjusted his tie, his own smile feeling like a mask he couldn't remove. "I'm not sure what you mean." Her eyes, cold and unflinching, raked over his tuxedo. "Oh, I think you do. Juggling all those appointments, all those promises. Remembering which lies belong to which life. Does your wife know what a talented actor she married? The sheer effort of it all... I can almost see the cracks from here." He felt the warmth drain from his face, the weight of her words pressing down harder than any public expectation.
