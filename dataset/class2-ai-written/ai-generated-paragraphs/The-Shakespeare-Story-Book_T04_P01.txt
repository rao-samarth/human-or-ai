He watched her across the small, cluttered study, her fingers tracing the spine of a book he'd given her years ago. "So you just told him?" The question was quiet, stripped of the anger he felt coiling in his gut. She wouldn't meet his eyes, focusing instead on the worn leather cover. "It just... came out. We were talking, and the opportunity was there." An opportunity. He tasted the word like bile. It wasn't an opportunity; it was a choice. A deliberate dismantling of something they had built together, brick by painstaking brick. She saw it as a simple confession, a moment of weakness, but he saw the blueprint of her ambition laid bare. He knew, with a certainty that chilled him, that every shared secret, every whispered dream, had been an asset she was simply waiting to cash in. The trust between them had not just been broken; it had been leveraged.
