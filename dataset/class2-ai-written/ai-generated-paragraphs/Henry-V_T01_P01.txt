The kitchen was dark except for the single light over the sink, illuminating the condensation on his glass. He traced a line through it, watching the water bead. "You're still up," she said softly from the doorway, her voice gentle in the late-night quiet. He didn't turn around. "Just thinking." She came and stood beside him, her presence a familiar warmth. "About what?" He sighed, a heavy, tired sound that seemed to carry the weight of the entire day. "Every choice I make… it’s not just a line on a spreadsheet. It's someone's job. It's a family's rent. I close one door and a hundred others slam shut somewhere else, and I'm the one who has to pretend I don't hear them." He finally looked at her, his eyes shadowed. "They all look to me for the right answer, and I'm not even sure there is one."
