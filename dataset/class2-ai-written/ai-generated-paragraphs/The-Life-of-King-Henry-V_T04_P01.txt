The first notes cut through the polite murmur of the hall, silencing everything. It was a new anthem, born not of ancient myth but of recent memory, its melody carrying the ache of our shared wounds. I saw the hardened face of the old commander across the room, his eyes glistening, and next to him, the young diplomat who had argued for peace, her posture rigid with a fragile pride. We were a collection of ghosts and survivors, of rivalries and reluctant alliances, all held captive by this sound. This was not the music of simple victory. It was the sound of a difficult bargain, the sound of choosing to build something on fractured ground. The strings climbed, weaving the sorrow of generations into a chord of defiant hope, and in that moment, the air itself seemed to change. We breathed it in together. For the first time, it felt like we were breathing the same air.
