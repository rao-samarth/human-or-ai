The fluorescent light above the kitchen table hummed, the only sound besides their strained breathing. “So you've made your decision,” the old man said, his voice flat as he traced the rim of a cold mug. His son stood silhouetted in the doorway, a packed bag slumped at his feet like an exhausted pet. “It was never a decision, Dad. It’s just what has to happen.” The father finally looked up, his face a roadmap of disappointment. “Everything we built. For you. And you just throw it away for a feeling?” The son’s voice was quiet but sharp. “I can’t live your dream for you. It isn’t mine.” A terrible, final silence descended, heavier than all the arguments that had come before. The old man just shook his head, looking back into the empty ceramic, a stranger to the boy he had raised. The front door clicked shut, severing the night.
