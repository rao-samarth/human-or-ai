They’re saying he used a tire iron. The old man at the counter swirled the dregs of his coffee, not looking up. “Found it in the ditch this morning.” His companion just shook her head, staring out the diner window at the quiet main street. “And all because he saw them walking home from the cinema. Just walking.” The man grunted. “He couldn’t stand it. Never could stand seeing anyone else even look at her.” A long silence stretched between them, filled only by the hum of the refrigerator. “He’ll be locked up for a long time,” she finally whispered, her voice tight. “And for what? A shadow on the wall, a shared laugh he wasn’t part of. Now everything’s broken.”
