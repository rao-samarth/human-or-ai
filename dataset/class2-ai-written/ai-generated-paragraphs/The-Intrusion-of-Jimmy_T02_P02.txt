"All of it?" My voice was barely a whisper, yet it felt like a scream in the quiet living room. His silhouette was a stark cut-out against the blue-black square of the window, and he didn't even turn. He just let the question hang in the air between them, thin and sharp as a shard of glass. Finally, he moved, not with the panic or shame I’d expected, but with a slow, deliberate grace that was suddenly terrifying. He walked to the mantelpiece, trailing his fingers along its polished surface. "You saw the perfect house, the charming couple, the whole beautiful picture," he said, his tone conversational, almost bored. "You loved the frame. You never once asked what was holding it all up, or what we’d wallpapered over." He picked up a silver picture frame, looking at our smiling faces, then set it down with a soft, final click.
