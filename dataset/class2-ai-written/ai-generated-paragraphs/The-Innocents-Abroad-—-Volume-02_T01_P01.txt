From the next table, the conversation was impossible to ignore, carried on the scent of coffee and burnt sugar. “It just wasn’t what I imagined,” one of them said, pushing a croissant crumb across her plate. “You see all the pictures and build this whole movie in your head. Cobblestone alleyways at dusk, profound thoughts while staring at ancient art, finding some hidden truth about yourself.” She paused, taking a sip of water. “But the reality was mostly just… logistics. And sweat.” She gave a small, humorless laugh. It was the crushing weight of navigating a foreign transit system, the disappointment of a famous landmark swarmed with tour groups, the low-grade anxiety of a dwindling budget. She hadn't found some deeper version of herself; she’d just found that her luggage was too heavy and her phone's data plan was inadequate. It was the slow, sinking realization that the perfect, effortless experience she had so carefully curated in her mind had never planned on showing up.
