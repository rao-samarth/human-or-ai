The last of the tea had gone cold in their mugs, but the conversation was just warming up. He had tried to explain the intricate, unwritten rules of queuing, a subject she found both baffling and hilarious. She, in turn, had recounted a story of such unbridled, earnest optimism about a community bake sale that he almost choked on his own reserved cynicism. It was always like this. One of them would launch a missile of pure irony, only to watch it sail harmlessly over the other's head and land in a field of literal interpretation. Or a statement of genuine feeling would be met with a squint of suspicion, as if searching for the hidden punchline. Yet, in the quiet glow of the lamp, these misfires weren't frustrating. They were small, brilliant sparks. Each misunderstanding became a bridge they built together, laughing, one plank of absurd clarification at a time, until they could meet in the middle, still different, but closer than before.
