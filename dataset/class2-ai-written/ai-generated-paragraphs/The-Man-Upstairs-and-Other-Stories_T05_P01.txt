The train jolted, and the cart rattled past their knees again. He flinched, pulling his legs in. “Are you sure you don’t want anything?” she asked, her voice tight with an impatience he knew all too well. He shook his head, eyes fixed on the blur of green outside the window, avoiding the price list taped to the cart's side. He'd seen it the first time. The numbers swam in his head, a frantic calculation of exchange rates and the shrinking balance he’d just checked on his phone. Another alert had come through an hour ago. He could feel her watching him, her sigh a small, sharp puff of air in the cramped carriage. He just needed to get to the next city, find the cheap hostel, eat instant noodles. But she wanted this, the experience, the overpriced coffee from a moving train. He shoved his hands in his pockets, the metallic clink of a few coins a pathetic reminder. Just keep moving, he thought, as the landscape accelerated into an incomprehensible streak of color.
