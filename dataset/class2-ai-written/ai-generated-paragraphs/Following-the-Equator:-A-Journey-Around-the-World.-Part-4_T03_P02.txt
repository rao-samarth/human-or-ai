The snag was nothing, a jacket button caught on a purse strap for half a second. But a shriek sliced through the street noise. Then a man’s voice barked, “Hey! Grab him!” Suddenly, the world tilted. Every face in the rushing crowd became a judge, a jury. Eyes locked onto him, cold and certain. There was no time to explain, no space to breathe. Panic surged, hot and sharp, and his legs were already moving, pumping against the pavement. He was just a guy going for a train. Now he was prey. The city’s indifferent hum became a hunter’s pursuit, every shadow a threat, every distant siren a final, damning verdict written before he’d even committed a crime.
