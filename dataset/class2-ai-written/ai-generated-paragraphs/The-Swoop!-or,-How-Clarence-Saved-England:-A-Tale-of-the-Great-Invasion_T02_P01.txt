“Just look at them,” he hissed, gesturing out the passenger window at the monotonous suburbs scrolling by. “The same cars, the same houses, the same blank faces. Don’t you see it? It’s not right.” I gripped the steering wheel, my knuckles white. “What’s not right? It’s a housing development. People live here.” He turned from the window, his eyes wide and frantic. “They’re not people anymore. Not really. It’s a quiet replacement, a mimicry. They look like us, they sound like us, but something fundamental is gone. They’re just… empty.” I took a slow breath, the air in the small car suddenly feeling thick and used. “Or maybe,” I said, keeping my voice level, “they’re just tired from their commute.”
