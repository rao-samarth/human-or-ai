“I don’t understand how you can look me in the eye,” he said, his voice a low tremor that barely contained its rage. “Everything we built for you. The connections, the reputation. You just… tossed it aside for a handout job in some forgotten corner of the city.” He gestured vaguely, as if the place itself was an insult. “Do you have any idea what people are saying? You had a name, a future.” She finally met his gaze, her own expression unyielding. “I still have a name. It’s mine. And my future doesn’t depend on a corner office or the approval of your golf partners.” She took a short, sharp breath, the sound loud in the quiet room. “What I do matters more than what people think I am. It seems you’ve valued the label on the bottle more than what’s actually inside it for so long you can’t tell the difference anymore.”
