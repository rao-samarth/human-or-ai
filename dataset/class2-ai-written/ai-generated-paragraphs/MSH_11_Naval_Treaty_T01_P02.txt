He sat in the dark, the only light a pale stripe from the streetlamp cutting across the rug. The whiskey in his glass remained untouched. For decades, his mind had been a locked archive, its contents shaping the histories of nations while he couldn't even explain to his wife why he sometimes woke up shouting. The information wasn't abstract; it was a physical presence, a constant pressure behind his eyes. He could still see the faces of assets whose names were never spoken aloud, hear the crackle of transmissions that had redrawn maps. Every casual news report about some distant conflict was an echo of a ghost story he helped write. The profound silence of the house was a lie. It was filled with the unheard warnings and silent agreements that had defined his career, a collection of realities too corrosive for any one person to carry alone, and yet, here he was.
