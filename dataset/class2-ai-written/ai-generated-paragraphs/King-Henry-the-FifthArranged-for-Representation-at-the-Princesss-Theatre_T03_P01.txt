Her gaze swept over the valley, where dusk was settling and lights began to flicker on in distant farmhouses. "You see lines," she said, her voice low but cutting through the silence. "I see the hands that built these walls. I hear the songs we all learned as children, the exact same ones our grandparents sang. This isn't about borders or the people in charge; it’s about *us*. All of us, in those houses, bound by something more than just geography. It’s the unspoken promise we make to each other, to hold this place together, especially when it’s breaking. You think that's nothing? That shared resolve, that quiet understanding between millions of strangers… that’s the only thing that's truly real.
