She navigated the room with a practiced grace, her laughter echoing just loud enough over the clinking glasses. Compliments washed over her like a warm, familiar tide, praising her poise, her recent triumph. They saw a figure carved from marble, unbreakable and serene. But they couldn't feel the tremor in her hands, hidden by the clutch of her small purse. They couldn't hear the frantic, desperate beat of her heart beneath the designer gown. This composure was a borrowed thing, a fragile armor she'd painstakingly assembled just hours before. Each smile was a stitch holding it all together. With every polite nod, she felt another thread snap, wondering how long she could possibly stand here, a beautifully decorated ruin, before the whole magnificent illusion shattered into dust at her feet.
