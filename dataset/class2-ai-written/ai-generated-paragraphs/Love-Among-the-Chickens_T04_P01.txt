When the all-staff email announced the project’s cancellation, a familiar wave of groans and frantic keyboard clicks filled the office. He didn’t even need to look over; I just caught the slight nod, the signal we'd refined over years of similar debacles. Our meeting point was, as always, the sputtering office coffee machine. There was no point dissecting the latest failure, as the formal post-mortems would inevitably handle that with clinical detachment. Instead, he asked about my weekend, and I listened to him describe his daughter’s chaotic science fair project. The coffee was, as usual, profoundly bitter. But for those few minutes, standing in the quiet space between the frantic energy on the floor and the inevitable emergency meetings, we found our equilibrium again, ready for whatever came next.
