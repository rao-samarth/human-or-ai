He leaned against the brick, trying to look essential to the performance, a practiced nod keeping time while his thumb scrolled endlessly through his phone. But no one was looking at him. All eyes were on her. Her fingers didn't just play the strings; they attacked them, a furious blur conjuring a melody that cut straight through the downtown clamor of sirens and shouting. She wasn't performing for the growing crowd—her eyes were shut tight, lost inside the sound pouring from the amplifier. A bike messenger skidded to a halt. A businessman lowered his phone. The city’s relentless current of people eddied around her, a momentary vortex of pure, unfeigned energy, leaving her companion looking like a forgotten statue on the shore.
