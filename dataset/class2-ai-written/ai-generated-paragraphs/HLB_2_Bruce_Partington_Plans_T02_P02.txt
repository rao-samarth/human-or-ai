“You can’t just walk away from this,” he said, his voice barely rising above the wind rustling the tall grass around them. “It isn’t a file you can just burn.” She kept her back to him, staring down at the patchwork of green fields in the valley below, her hands jammed into her pockets. “It’s not a file,” she agreed, her tone flat and cold. “It’s a trigger. It’s a key that opens a door and lets something terrible walk through. You think I don’t know that? I live with the weight of it every single second.” He took a step closer, his boots crunching on the dry soil. “Then give it to us. Let us carry it.” A bitter, hollow laugh escaped her lips. “You don’t carry it. You use it. You’re just another finger hovering over the button, telling yourself it’s for the greater good. I’ve seen what happens next. There is no greater good at the bottom of that hole, only silence.”
