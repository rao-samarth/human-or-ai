Yuri, now masquerading as Gary from Accounts, tried again. “The sparrow has migrated south,” he whispered urgently over Brenda’s cubicle wall, smelling faintly of cheap cologne and desperation. Brenda didn’t look up from her spreadsheet. “That’s nice, Gary. Can you just initial the Q3 expense reports when you get a moment?” He leaned closer, hissing the words with more emphasis. “The *sparrow*,” he stressed, “has migrated *south*.” Brenda finally peered over her monitor, her expression one of mild irritation. “Is that what we’re calling the server consolidation project now? A bit poetic for IT, isn’t it? Just make sure the package is delivered to the Reading office by five.” Yuri’s eye twitched. The package. Of course, the package. “The package is secure,” he confirmed through gritted teeth. “Excellent,” Brenda said, turning back to her screen. “Just leave it with Janet for the internal mail. And it’s your turn on the tea rota, don’t forget.” He stood there, defeated. His primary contact was a project manager so deeply embedded that she had mistaken a critical activation phrase for corporate jargon.
