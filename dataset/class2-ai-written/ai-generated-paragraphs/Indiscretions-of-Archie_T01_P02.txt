He stood stiffly by the floor-to-ceiling window, refusing to look at her as the city lights glittered below. "There was an agreement. A process." She didn't bother looking up from her tablet, the cool glow illuminating her faint smirk. "Your process involves a three-course lunch and a handshake. My process got the signature in under an hour." He finally turned, his voice low and tight with a fury she refused to acknowledge. "You burned a bridge we spent a decade building. That trust is worth more than this one contract." She finally set the tablet down with a soft click. "Trust doesn't appear on a balance sheet. That bridge was a toll road, and they were bleeding us dry with pleasantries. I just built a freeway right past it. Welcome to the fast lane."
