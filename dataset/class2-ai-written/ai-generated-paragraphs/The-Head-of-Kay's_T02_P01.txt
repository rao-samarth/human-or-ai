The memo about the new ‘Uniform Mug Policy’ landed in everyone's inbox with the thud of a truly terrible idea. Brenda from Corporate, in her infinite wisdom, had decided personal mugs were 'detrimental to brand synergy' and that all employees must henceforth use the sterile, company-issued white ones. The response from the junior analyst pod was not outright rebellion, but something far more insidious and coordinated. By noon, every official mug on their desks had been creatively modified. Chloe’s now sported a tiny, hand-knitted sweater. Mark had used a permanent marker to draw a surprisingly detailed portrait of his cat, Mr. Sniffles. Sam, ever the minimalist, had simply glued a single googly eye to the bottom of his, so it stared up blankly with every sip. Brenda patrolled the aisles later that day, her lips a thin line of suppressed fury, but she couldn't say a thing. Technically, they were all following the rules.
