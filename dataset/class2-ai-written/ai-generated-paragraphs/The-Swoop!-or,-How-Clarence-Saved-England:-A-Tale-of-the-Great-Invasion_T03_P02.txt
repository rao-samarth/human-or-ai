“Can you please put down the piping bag for one second, Clara?” His voice was tight, stretched thin across the kitchen island. She didn’t look up, her entire focus on the delicate sugar lattice she was constructing. “The nationals are in two days, Leo. Two days. This has to be perfect.” He slammed a stack of mail onto the counter, making her flinch. “Perfect? The final notice from the bank is in here. The mill is officially laying off another hundred people, and our neighbors are packing up their lives. But all you can think about is whether your marzipan roses will impress a panel of celebrity bakers?” She finally met his gaze, her own eyes hard. “This competition is the only good thing happening! It’s on every news channel. It’s giving this town something to believe in, something other than shuttered windows and failure. This is what people care about now.” He just stared back, shaking his head slowly. “They care about a cake show while their town dies.”
