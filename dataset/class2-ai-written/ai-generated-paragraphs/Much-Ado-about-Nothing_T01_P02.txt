In team meetings, his contributions were always concise and impeccably researched, presented with an air of unshakable confidence. His digital calendar was a masterpiece of organization, and his project updates landed in everyone’s inboxes with a punctuality you could set your watch to. Yet, a few of us who stayed late had caught glimpses of the other side: the weary sigh when he thought no one was looking, the way he’d endlessly rearrange icons on his desktop, or the stack of empty coffee cups that accumulated by Thursday. It wasn't a malicious deception, but a carefully maintained professional armor. The flawless executive summary he produced didn't betray the twenty discarded drafts or the gnawing uncertainty that preceded it. We were all interacting with the polished final product, a curated professional identity that bore only a passing resemblance to the person shouldering the effort.
