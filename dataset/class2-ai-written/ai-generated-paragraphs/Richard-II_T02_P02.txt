He sat in the chair reserved for visitors, a stranger in the room he had designed. Across the expanse of his own mahogany desk, the younger man spoke with a placid, rehearsed sympathy about a "new chapter." The words were meaningless, smooth stones skipping over the deep, silent chasm that had opened inside him. He felt an odd lightness, as if his bones were suddenly hollow. For years, every command, every signature, every difficult choice had added to his substance, building him into someone who mattered. Now, that person was being dismantled in a quiet conversation. He looked down at his own hands resting on his knees. They seemed useless, disconnected from the will that had once moved industries. He was no longer the architect of this place; he was just a ghost haunting its memory, listening to the terms of his own disappearance.
