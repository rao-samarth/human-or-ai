The train doors hissed shut just as he skidded to a halt on the platform, his briefcase swinging wildly. “Told you,” he gasped, leaning against a pillar. “It wasn’t our path.” The woman beside him slammed her tote bag onto a bench. “Path? Don’t you dare. You insisted on that second espresso. You stopped to tie your perfectly-tied shoe. You fumbled for the tickets that were in your front pocket the whole time.” She jabbed a finger toward the departing train, its red lights shrinking into the tunnel. “That wasn't the universe making a decision for us. That was you, making a dozen tiny, stupid ones.” He just shook his head, a wry smile playing on his lips. “And what made me do all those things, hmm? It all leads to the same place.” The screech of metal on metal faded, leaving only their argument hanging in the suddenly quiet air.
