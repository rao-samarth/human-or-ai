She used to stay late because the projects excited her; now, the clock on her screen just seems to hold her hostage until five. He still stops by her desk, of course, a little too often, with that same paternal smile that everyone once found so encouraging. Few people know the full story of the promotion she was offered at the downtown firm, the one that evaporated after a single confidential phone call. He had built her career, he’d tell anyone who listened, polishing a rough stone into something brilliant. But his pride had become a gilded cage. Now, her brilliance is a quieter, more careful thing, a light kept under a lampshade so it only illuminates his own corner of the world, never threatening to shine anywhere else. He calls it mentorship. She doesn’t call it anything at all.
