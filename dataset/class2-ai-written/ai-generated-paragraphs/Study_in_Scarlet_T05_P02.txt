He ripped another drawer from the old desk, its contents—pens, faded receipts, a single silver earring—spilling across the floorboards. It wasn't there. Nothing. A choked sound escaped his throat as he slammed his fist on the splintered wood, the impact rattling a nearby lamp. He could still see her smile, the way she’d tucked a stray hair behind her ear right before telling him where she’d hidden it, a secret just for them. "If anything happens," she'd said, her voice a low whisper against the hum of the city outside. Now everything had happened. He kicked aside a pile of books, his breath coming in ragged gasps as dust motes danced in the moonlight slanting through the window. The promise he’d made echoed louder than the frantic thumping of his own heart. He had to find it. Spinning around, his eyes scanned the cluttered room, landing on the loose brick in the fireplace. Of course.
