From the next table over, her voice cut through the afternoon haze and the clatter of plates. “They all wanted a roadmap, a guarantee, a meticulously charted course from here to there,” she said, swirling the ice in her glass. Her companion nodded, listening intently. “But some of the best things are born from a total disregard for the map. There’s a crucial, beautiful energy in the moment you just decide to swerve, to take the unmarked path simply because it feels right. You can analyze an idea to death, dissecting every risk until the original spark is just a cold ash. Sometimes you have to protect that initial, wild impulse from the tyranny of your own good sense. It’s not about being careless; it’s about trusting that a powerful gut feeling is its own form of navigation, one that logic can’t always replicate.”
