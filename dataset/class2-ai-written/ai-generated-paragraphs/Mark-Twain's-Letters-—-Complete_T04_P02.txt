He nudged his companion with an elbow, gesturing with his chin toward the lamppost across the green. Another one. “Progress for the People.” He grunted. “They’re running out of words for ‘the same’.” His friend watched a car carefully navigate a crater-sized pothole before answering. “Oh, I don’t know. This one promises to listen. The last one promised to act. Before him, it was all about ‘heritage.’” She sighed, a small, tired sound. “They’re just renting the big chair for a few years, making sure their cousin’s construction company gets the contract to patch a few sidewalks. The real problems? They’re permanent fixtures, like that statue. They just get a new plaque screwed on every decade.”
