“So this is your grand strategy?” she hissed, keeping her voice low enough to be lost in the drone of her uncle’s toast. “You bring her here, to my cousin's wedding, as what, an audition? You told me we were figuring things out.” He shifted his weight, looking desperately toward the bar as if it were a life raft. “It's not like that. We aren't exclusive. I didn't think you'd even come.” The excuse was so flimsy it was insulting. It wasn't a plan; it was a catastrophic failure of one, a spreadsheet of social obligations where he'd put two names in the same cell. Every smile he'd offered her, every late-night text, now felt like part of some ridiculous, poorly managed algorithm for keeping his options open. He just wanted to have everything at once, and now here it was, all colliding next to the gift table. She just shook her head. “Right. Not exclusive. Just exhausting.”
