He sat by the window, watching the dry-stone wall that separated the long pasture from the woods. For years, he had admired its construction, the patient fitting of one stone against another without any mortar to bind them. But he now understood that a slow, persistent pressure, applied in just the right place, could compromise the entire structure. It wasn't a single, violent blow that had brought everything down; it was the quiet, methodical work of envy, a chipping away at the foundation until the slightest nudge was enough for a collapse. The most stable-looking arrangements, he realized, were often the most vulnerable to the patient treachery of a friend. The stones now lay scattered in the grass, indifferent to the design they once held.
