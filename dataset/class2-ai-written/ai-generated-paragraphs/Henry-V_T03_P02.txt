He walked the line of the new stone wall, his boots silent on the damp grass. The younger man followed a few paces behind, his hands clasped, his gaze fixed on the older man’s back. The landowner didn’t speak. He simply trailed his bare, gnarled fingers along the top course of stones, feeling for looseness, for imperfection. He paused, his head tilted, listening to something only he could hear in the wind that swept across the open pasture. Then he stopped and pointed with his chin towards a low section near the oak tree. It was a subtle gesture, barely a movement at all, but it carried the weight of a decree. The younger man nodded quickly, a silent acknowledgment of the flaw and the unspoken command to fix it. The authority wasn't in a raised voice; it was in the quiet, absolute certainty of that small gesture.
