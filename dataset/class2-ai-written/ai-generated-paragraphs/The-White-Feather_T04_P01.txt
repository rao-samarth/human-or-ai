The green-gold fields blurred into a single streak outside the train window. He turned from the view and studied her profile. “It’s funny,” he said, his voice low over the hum of the wheels. “I look at you now, and I can barely remember the person you were five years ago.” She didn't look away from the passing landscape, a small smile touching her lips. “That person is still in there,” she replied softly. “She just got tired of her own excuses. There wasn’t a lightning strike or some grand revelation. It was just the thousandth time I chose to get up instead of stay down. It was the brutal, boring discipline of showing up when every part of me screamed to quit. For the longest time, it felt like digging a tunnel with a spoon, absolutely pointless. Then one morning, a little bit of light gets in, and you realize you haven't been digging in place at all.”
