Outside the cafe, the city roared its usual chaotic symphony of sirens and hurried footsteps on pavement. A woman in a cream-colored linen suit, seemingly insulated from the noise by her own concerns, leaned across the tiny marble table. She lowered her voice, a confessional whisper against the din. “It’s been an ordeal, truly. The architect simply doesn’t understand the energetic difference between limestone and travertine. He called my concern an ‘aesthetic preference’.” She took a delicate sip of her oat milk latte, her brow furrowed with genuine distress. Her companion, a man with a watch that cost more than the delivery bike chained to the lamppost nearby, reached over and patted her hand. “You have to protect your peace,” he murmured, his gaze distant, as if contemplating a similar, earth-shattering dilemma of his own.
