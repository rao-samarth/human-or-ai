He had done it again, leading them in a perfect, hopeless circle with a map that might as well have been a dinner napkin for all the good it was doing him. The silence was heavy. Around him, shoulders slumped under the weight of backpacks and his own incompetence, the air thick with unspoken frustration. He was about to offer another useless apology when he saw it—not a landmark or a sign, but the tiny, bright blue flowers he’d been pocketing all morning, a scattered trail of them leading off through a break in the trees he’d made on his first, clumsy pass. He pointed, a wordless gesture that nobody wanted to trust, but they were too tired to argue. And there it was. The path opened up to a view that stole their breath, and for the first time all day, the looks they gave him weren't filled with pity, but a dawning, bewildered respect that felt warmer than the sun on his face.
