He delivered the project on time and under budget, a feat lauded in the quarterly review. From the outside, it was a flawless execution, a clear path to his next promotion. Yet within the team, a different reality had taken root. The relentless drive had burned through goodwill like fuel, leaving behind a trail of exhausted colleagues and fractured professional relationships. He seemed to view their fatigue not as a human cost but as an acceptable operational expense, a necessary sacrifice for the goal. While he was already strategizing his next ascent, the department he was leaving behind began dealing with the fallout: increased turnover, a palpable sense of distrust, and a creative silence where collaboration once thrived. The numbers on the final report told a story of triumph, but the quiet hallways and empty chairs told a much more complicated one.
