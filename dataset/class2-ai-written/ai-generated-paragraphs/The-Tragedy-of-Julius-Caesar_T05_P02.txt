He kept his gaze fixed on the horizon, where the last light bled from the sky. “It was for the greater good,” he insisted, his voice barely a whisper against the rustling grass. “A cleaner world.” She let out a short, bitter laugh that died in the twilight. “Whose world? Go tell that to the family down the road, if you can find them in the ash. You speak of a glorious new dawn, but all your philosophy brought them was fire in the night.” He finally turned, his face a mask of rigid certainty. “They chose their side. It was a necessary price for progress.” “A price you were happy for others to pay,” she shot back, her own voice cracking with a fury that surprised them both. “Your beautiful ideas don't build anything. They just leave graves.”
