The grandfather clock in the hall chimed two, each strike a hammer blow against the suffocating silence of the house. Moonlight cut a sharp, silver line across the polished floor, illuminating a single letter lying by the door. She stared at it, her reflection a pale ghost in the dark windowpane. They couldn't know. Not now, not after everything she had built. All those years of careful construction, of forced smiles and hollow pleasantries, would turn to ash over this one, careless truth. The whole pristine edifice would collapse. She knelt, her fingers trembling as they brushed against the crisp paper. It felt heavier than stone. Burning it would be easy, but the words already existed somewhere else, in someone else's mind. That was a fire she couldn't extinguish with a match. The world they saw, the woman they believed in, had to be preserved. It was the only thing left that mattered.
