He replaced the rotted fence post with the same deliberate slowness he applied to everything. Out here, where the fields stretched into a hazy, indifferent horizon, time had a different texture. It was not a progression, but a loop. Each swing of the mallet was a faint echo of a gavel's fall from forty years ago, a sound no one else remembered. The verdant land he stood on was just a sliver of what should have been his, the rest signed away on a piece of paper he never touched. People in the village saw a quiet, resilient old man. They did not see the boy who still stood frozen in a dusty courtroom, his whole life rerouted by a single, casual lie. Anger had burned out long ago, leaving behind only the cold ash of routine and a watchfulness that never slept. He packed the earth around the new post, making it solid, unbreachable. It was a small, pointless victory, but it was his.
