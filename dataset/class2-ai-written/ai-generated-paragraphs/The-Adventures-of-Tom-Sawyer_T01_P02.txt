You see how they interact with the space entirely differently? To them, that low concrete border isn't just an edge for a flowerbed; it’s a castle wall, a balance beam over a chasm. Every object is assessed for its potential—a pinecone becomes a grenade, a fallen branch is a rifle. Their world is governed by a logic of imaginative utility. But we adults see it through a lens of designated function and potential liability. We see a border that could be tripped over, plants that could be trampled, a stick that could poke an eye out. It’s not a conflict of malice versus order, but a collision between two incompatible operating systems: one built on what things *could be*, the other on what they are *supposed to be*. And in the end, the rules of what is supposed to be almost always win.
