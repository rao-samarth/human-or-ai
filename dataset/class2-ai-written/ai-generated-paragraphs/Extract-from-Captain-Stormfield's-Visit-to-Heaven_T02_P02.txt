A flicker in the periphery. I flinched, snapping my head toward the lamp where a colossal shadow danced. It wasn't a shadow. It was a moth, a furry behemoth with frantic, dusty wings beating a desperate rhythm against the shade. My space. My light. The violation was absolute. I snatched a rolled-up magazine from the coffee table, the perfect weapon for this sudden, necessary war. It launched itself into the air, a chaotic blur against the ceiling. I swung, a wild miss that sent a gust of wind rattling the blinds. It swooped, a kamikaze dive toward my face, and I scrambled backward, tripping over the rug. The lamp wobbled. My heart hammered against my ribs. All of this—the silent house, the sleeping city, the vast, star-filled dark outside—had narrowed to this single, frantic contest between my will and the mindless flutter of this insect. My world, for this one moment, conquered.
