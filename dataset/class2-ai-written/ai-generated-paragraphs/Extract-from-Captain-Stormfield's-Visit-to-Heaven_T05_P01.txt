He threw his hands up, the gesture sharp and sudden in the dim kitchen light. “What don’t you get? It’s about an end to all this. An end to the struggle, the uncertainty. A final, perfect peace.” She didn’t move from her chair, her voice quiet but cutting through his frustration. “A perfect peace? For eternity? You’d be bored in a week. You think you want some flawless, unchanging paradise, but what you’re describing is a museum. A beautiful, silent room where nothing ever happens again. I don’t want that. I want a tomorrow. I want the possibility of a better conversation than the one we’re having right now. I want the chance to be wrong and learn something new. Your heaven sounds like the end of a story, and I’d rather have one that never stops being written.”
