“Honestly, just think about the stability,” she urged, leaning forward so her voice wouldn’t carry across the crowded train station. “You wouldn’t have to worry about rent, or bills, or anything, ever again. That’s a kind of freedom, you know.” He watched a departure board flicker with changing destinations, anywhere but here. The convenience was undeniable, a life curated and cushioned from every sharp edge. He could have anything he wanted, except for the one simple thing he saw in the faces of strangers holding hands on the platform: an easy, uncalculated warmth. He finally turned back to her, the rumble of a distant train underscoring his quiet words. “I know, but when he puts his arm around me, it doesn’t feel like an embrace. It feels like an audit, like he’s checking to make sure his investment is still there.”
