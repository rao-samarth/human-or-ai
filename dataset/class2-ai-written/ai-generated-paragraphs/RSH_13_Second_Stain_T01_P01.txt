The wind whipped across the open field, but it did nothing to cool the fury in her voice. “You sat on it for twenty years,” she said, her words low and tight. “You knew what that file contained, what it would do if it ever got out.” He didn’t look at her, his gaze fixed on the distant line of grey hills as if they held some kind of answer. “I made a calculation. The immediate chaos of exposing it was a greater threat than letting it lie dormant.” She took a sharp step closer, the dry grass crunching under her boot like shattering glass. “It wasn’t dormant, it was gestating! This isn’t some old ghost you could lock away. It’s a network, a trigger, and now they know we’re looking. Because of your silence, the whole fragile structure is about to collapse on top of us.” He finally turned, his face a mask of tired resignation. “Then let it collapse.”
