She watched him from her usual booth, the one with the cracked red vinyl. In a town where every face was a story she’d already read, he was a blank, beautiful page. She’d convinced herself that the restless energy he carried wasn't a warning, but a sign of a vibrant spirit suffocated by the town’s slow pace—a spirit just like her own. He smiled, and she felt it was a private sun meant only to warm her. She ignored the way that same smile was given just as easily to the waitress, the mailman, and the stray cat by the door. Loneliness had been such a constant, heavy coat that she was now willing to see a bonfire in a single, fleeting spark. He was not a person so much as a cure, and she was too eager for the medicine to ever question its ingredients. The quiet hum of her own life had become unbearable, and his loud, careless confidence sounded, to her, exactly like a song of salvation.
