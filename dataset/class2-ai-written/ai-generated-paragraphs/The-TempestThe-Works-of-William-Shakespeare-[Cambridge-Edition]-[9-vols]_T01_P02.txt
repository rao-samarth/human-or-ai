Frank had the bucket of potent fish emulsion ready. Midnight. Henderson, his neighbor and sworn horticultural nemesis, had definitely sabotaged his prize-winning petunias, and this was the perfect, stinking revenge. Creeping across the dewy grass, Frank felt like a suburban ninja on a deeply personal mission. But as he neared the property line, he saw a dim light. Henderson was out there in his pajamas and slippers, gently prodding a wilting zucchini plant. “What’s wrong with you?” he whispered to the vine, the sound of a man who had been truly defeated by a vegetable. Frank paused, the bucket suddenly feeling heavy and ridiculous. The righteous anger just fizzled out, replaced by a strange, inconvenient wave of pity. The next morning, Henderson found a bag of premium compost on his porch with a small, anonymous note that simply read: “They like more sun.”
