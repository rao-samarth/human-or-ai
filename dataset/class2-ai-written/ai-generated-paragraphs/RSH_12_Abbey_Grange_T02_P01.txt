He was holding court at the head of the dinner table, recounting an anecdote from their recent trip. Laughter filled the room, warm and genuine. When his wife tried to add a small, funny detail about getting lost, he simply placed his hand over hers on the tablecloth. To everyone else, it was a gesture of casual affection. A loving touch. But she felt the slight, deliberate pressure of his thumb, a silent command to stop talking. Her own story died on her lips. She smiled and took a sip of water, the movement a carefully constructed piece of theater. His correction was so smooth, so perfectly integrated into the performance of a happy marriage, that no one else noticed the shift. He continued his tale, his voice booming with confidence, leaving her in the quiet wake of his authority. The air around her seemed to thin, each breath a little harder to draw in a space that had suddenly, invisibly, shrunk to the size of her chair.
