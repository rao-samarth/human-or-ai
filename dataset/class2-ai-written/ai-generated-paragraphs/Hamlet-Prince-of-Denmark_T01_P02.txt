The city hummed with a life that felt impossibly distant. Rain slicked the pavement, turning the streetlights into blurry constellations on the ground. He stood by the window, watching the anonymous shuffle of umbrellas below, and felt a familiar coldness settle in his chest. For years, the thought of retribution had been a fire, a singular point of heat in the void. It had given him purpose, a reason to draw the next breath. But the flames had long since died down, leaving only ash. He could no longer clearly picture the face that had started it all, not really. It was just a shape now, a placeholder for his own bitterness. The people he once loved, the simple joys he used to know—they were like faded photographs from someone else’s life. He had hollowed himself out to make room for this single, gnawing objective, and now that he was close, he realized the space was just an echo. He was an empty room, waiting for a guest who would never arrive, while the whole vibrant world carried on without him, indifferent to the ghost he had become.
