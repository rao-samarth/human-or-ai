The official letter arrived on a Tuesday, its crisp envelope a stark contrast to the muddy boots by the door. It stated, in no uncertain terms, that his privet hedge was found to be encroaching upon the public footpath by a margin of three inches, a violation of Subsection C, Paragraph 4 of the Rural Walkways Act of 1987. The consequences, laid out on page two, were not a simple fine. Instead, a 'Specialist Foliage Abatement Team' would be dispatched at his expense. He watched them arrive the following Monday: four men in full white hazmat suits, carrying what looked suspiciously like laser-guided secateurs. They spent an hour setting up warning cones and cordoning off the area with yellow tape before one of them, with the theatrical gravity of a bomb disposal expert, snipped off the offending twigs. The invoice for their services, which included a 'Biohazard Containment Surcharge,' arrived a week later.
