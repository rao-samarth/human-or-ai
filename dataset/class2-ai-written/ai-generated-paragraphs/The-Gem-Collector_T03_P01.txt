They brought in these planning experts from the capital, a whole team with laser pointers and glossy binders full of traffic-flow projections for the town square. For weeks, they talked about rerouting, signal synchronization, and a one-way system that would have sent half of Main Street into a fit. Then, during the final presentation, Maria—who just runs the little bookshop by the creek—quietly raised her hand. She said the whole jam only happens on Saturdays because of the market, so why not just have the produce vendors set up in the unused lot behind the post office instead of right on the corner? The lead consultant gave her this patient little smile, the kind you give a child, and explained the 'intricate logistical matrix.' Well, we tried her idea the next weekend anyway, just to shut her up, really. And it worked. Perfectly. I guess sometimes you just get lucky.
