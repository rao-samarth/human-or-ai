They had arrived with a shared fantasy of life in the metropolis, envisioning clandestine meetings on rooftop gardens and spontaneous trips to late-night jazz clubs. Their romance was meant to be a story written against the backdrop of iconic skylines and the kinetic energy of bustling streets. Yet, the narrative quickly became fractured by the practical demands of urban existence. The thrilling anonymity of the crowd soon felt like profound isolation. Instead of whispered secrets over candlelit dinners, their conversations became negotiations over shared expenses and whose turn it was to deal with the perpetually leaking faucet. The grand adventure had dissolved into a series of logistical problems, and the city, once a symbol of infinite possibility, now seemed to be a constant, humming reminder of all the things they couldn't afford, both financially and emotionally. The skyline was still there, of course, but they rarely looked up anymore.
