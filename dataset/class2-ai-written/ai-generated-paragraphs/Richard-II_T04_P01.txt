The man at the next table was holding court, his voice a smooth, confident hum that easily carried over the clatter of ceramic. He spoke with such earnest passion about community, about the simple, profound responsibility we have to one another. His friend nodded, captivated. He painted these beautiful pictures with his words, sketching out a world where empathy was the only currency that mattered, and I found myself leaning in, almost convinced. Then the bell above the door chimed, and a man, shivering in a thin jacket, shuffled inside. He approached their table, his hand outstretched, his request a barely audible whisper. The speaker didn’t miss a beat. He simply angled his shoulder, creating a physical barrier, and continued his monologue on human decency, his voice rising just enough to cover the quiet sound of the man’s retreat back into the cold. All those eloquent sentences suddenly felt like hollow shells scattered on the floor between us.
