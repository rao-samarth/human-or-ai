“You just don’t want to believe it,” she said, kicking a loose stone from the path, her frustration a sharp contrast to the quiet hum of the fields around them. He stopped, turning to face her fully. “Believe what? That this time it's different? That this new savior with his carefully rehearsed sincerity is going to fix a system designed to be broken? It’s a performance. A comforting little drama staged every few years to make everyone feel involved. They let you wave your signs and cast your vote, feeding you just enough hope to keep you from burning the whole thing down. While you're busy feeling righteous, they’re just swapping out the faces at the table, making the same backroom deals that ensure nothing of substance ever really changes for people like us. You're not fighting for a cause; you're just cheering for your preferred brand of disappointment.”
