The air grew cool as the sun bled into the hills, painting the fields in a soft, hazy light. "It’s so quiet here," Elara said, leaning back in her wooden chair. "You forget what real peace sounds like." Her brother, Simon, barely seemed to hear her, his gaze sweeping the valley with a familiar, unsettling focus. "It’s an asset," he murmured, more to himself than to her. "The silence is a commodity. You could build exclusive lodges just over that ridge, market the tranquility. People pay a fortune for an experience like this, to escape the noise they helped create." He took a sip of his wine, a flicker of excitement in his eyes. "That farmer has no idea what this land is actually worth." Elara just watched the last sliver of sun disappear, feeling a quiet she knew he could no longer understand.
