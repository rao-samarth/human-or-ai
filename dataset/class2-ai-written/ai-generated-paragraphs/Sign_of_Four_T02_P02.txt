He stood on the corner of Market and Fourth, the city's pulse a frantic drum against his ribs. For years, that beat had been his own, a rhythm of fury driving him forward, one step after another down a narrowing alley of his own making. He’d hunted shadows, memorized faces in crowds, and let the vibrant chaos of a million lives blur into a meaningless backdrop for his singular, grim purpose. But today, the smell of roasted nuts from a street cart, the sound of a saxophone wailing a bluesy tune, the sight of two kids chasing pigeons—it all pierced the veil. He felt the heavy cloak of his quest slip from his shoulders. What if he just stayed here? What if he bought some of those nuts, listened to the whole song, and simply became one of the anonymous, smiling faces in the crowd? The thought was terrifying, and then, suddenly, it was liberation. The path ahead was no longer a straight line to a bitter end, but a thousand branching streets, each one a possibility.
