He slid the folder back across the polished mahogany, his reflection momentarily distorted in its gloss. “Think of it as a necessary adjustment,” he said, his voice a low murmur that barely disturbed the quiet of the top-floor office. “A rounding error in the grand scheme. The markets stabilize, the jobs are saved, and your own future becomes significantly brighter.” He leaned forward, the expensive fabric of his suit rustling, his confidence an almost physical pressure in the room. “All you have to do is sign off.” My gaze drifted past him to the sprawling city lights below, a galaxy of tiny, unknowing lives. I pushed the folder back toward him, my hand steady. “Some errors you can’t round off,” I replied, my own voice quiet but clear. “They just grow.” A cool stillness settled between us as his smile finally vanished, replaced by something hard and unyielding. “Then you grow with them, or you get crushed by them. The choice has always been that simple.”
