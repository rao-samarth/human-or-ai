Sam pressed the end-call button with more force than necessary, staring at the black screen of his phone as if it were to blame. “Everything alright?” Maria asked from the next cubicle, her voice soft. He let out a long breath. “My dad,” he said, turning his chair slightly. “He found out I signed the lease for that apartment downtown. Now he’s emailed me three other listings he thinks are ‘more suitable’ and insists I break my lease. He even offered to cover the fee.” Maria winced. “That’s… intense.” “It is. But you know what? I just told him no. Flat out. The decision’s made.” A small, unfamiliar smile touched his lips. “The silence on his end was incredible. For the first time, it feels like my own decision is actually the final one.”
