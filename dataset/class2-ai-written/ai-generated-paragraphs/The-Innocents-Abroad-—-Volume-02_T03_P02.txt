The roar of scooters and the clatter of café tables filled the narrow street. “You see that corner building?” Liam had to raise his voice. “The one with the boarded-up windows? Such a waste of prime real estate.” Anja followed his gaze, her expression softening. “Waste? But look at the ironwork on those balconies. No one makes anything like that anymore. It’s seen generations come and go.” He scoffed, gesturing with his coffee cup. “Generations of leaky pipes and bad wiring, probably. Tear it down, put up something clean and functional with good plumbing. People could actually live there, safely.” She just shook her head, a small smile playing on her lips. “And they would never hear the stories this one tells,” she said, almost to herself.
