A woman's shriek cut through the afternoon hum of the city, sharp and accusing. A circle of onlookers was already forming, their phones held high, a digital jury instantly convened. In the center, a young man in a worn-out jacket stood frozen, his hands half-raised in a gesture of surrender or confusion as the woman pointed a trembling finger at him. But I saw it. Just a moment before, I’d watched a different figure—quicker, cloaked in the crowd's anonymity—bump into her and vanish down a side street. Now, every instinct screamed at me to stay quiet, to let the current carry me past this messy, unfortunate scene like everyone else. To speak up would be to invite hassle, suspicion, maybe even danger. Yet, the kid’s terrified eyes met mine for a split second, and the simple truth caught in my throat, a heavy, unformed word waiting to either save him or condemn me to a long, complicated evening.
