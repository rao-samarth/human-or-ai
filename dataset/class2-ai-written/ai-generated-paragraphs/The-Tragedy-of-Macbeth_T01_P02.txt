He stood before the immense window, the city a silent grid of distant gold below. This view, he reminded himself, was the entire point. It was the prize. Yet, the reflection staring back from the dark glass wasn’t a victor, but a hollowed-out version of a man he once knew. The memory of his old partner’s face—the initial confusion hardening into a quiet, wounded understanding—surfaced unbidden, as it often did in these late hours. He had called it a necessary sacrifice, a strategic severance required for the climb. Everyone who reached this altitude had a similar story, he reasoned. But the rationalizations felt thin in the heavy silence of the house, offering no warmth against the chill that had little to do with the night air. The empire was his, but the cost echoed in every empty room.
