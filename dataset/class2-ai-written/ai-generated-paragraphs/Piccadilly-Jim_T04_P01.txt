I spent six months on this, six entire months. The plan was flawless, cinematic even. I had it all timed to the sunset. I trained him, you know. A real seagull! The signal was a specific three-note whistle, and he was supposed to swoop down, this graceful little agent of destiny, and drop the velvet pouch right into my outstretched hand. But do you know what I didn't account for? A child, not ten feet away, with a squeaky rubber duck that made the *exact same sound*. So my majestic bird, my meticulously coached courier of romance, veered sharply left and delivered a two-carat diamond into a bucket of half-eaten French fries. The kid’s father just shrugged at me and said, ‘Finders keepers.’ My entire future was traded for a greasy potato.
