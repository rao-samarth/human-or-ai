The shadow in the corner of the living room had definitely moved. I was sure of it. One moment it was just a vaguely coat-shaped lump draped over the armchair, and the next, it had shifted, grown taller, and possibly sprouted a menacing hat. My heart hammered against my ribs. This was it. This was how I'd end up on some true-crime documentary. Grabbing the heaviest thing I could find—a ridiculously oversized art history textbook—I crept forward, my breath held tight in my chest. I flicked on the lamp with a sweaty thumb, ready to confront the specter. There it was, in all its terrifying glory: my laundry pile, which I'd left on the chair. A rogue pair of jeans had slid off the top, creating the illusion of movement, and a stray sock perched on top formed the “hat.” I stood there, clutching a book on Renaissance sculpture, feeling profoundly foolish.
