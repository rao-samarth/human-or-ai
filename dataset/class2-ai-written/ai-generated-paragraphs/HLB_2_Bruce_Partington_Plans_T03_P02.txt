He occupied the corner booth at the diner, a worn vinyl seat that had become his unofficial throne during the town’s slow-burning crisis. Around him, anxious faces leaned in, finding a fragile anchor in his steady voice as he pointed to locations on a laminated map. He offered calm reassurances and sensible plans, his suggestions met with grateful nods from neighbors who’d known him since childhood. No one noticed the slight, almost imperceptible pressure of his finger on the unmarked ridge north of the river, a place he insisted was irrelevant. He smiled, a familiar, comforting expression that didn't quite reach his eyes, and slid the last doughnut across the table to a worried mother. He was their solid ground, their unwavering constant in a time of fear, and all the while, he felt the cold weight of the second, folded map in his jacket pocket, the one that told a completely different story.
