He occupied the high-backed chair by the fire, a still point around which the evening’s warmth and laughter eddied. His family saw the patriarch, the steady hand that guided a nation, and they offered him their stories and their smiles. He returned the gesture with an automatic, gentle curve of the lips, a practiced reassurance. But inside, he heard not the anecdotes of his grandchildren, but the faint, persistent echo of petitioners’ pleas and the dry rustle of unsigned documents waiting on his desk. He watched them as if from a great distance, this small circle of people he was meant to protect, feeling the cold weight of the signet ring on his finger more acutely than the heat from the hearth. Each loving glance cast his way only carved the chasm between the man they saw and the exhausted strategist within a little deeper.
