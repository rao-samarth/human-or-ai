He was still talking on his phone when I sat down on the bench, but his voice had dropped to a quiet, focused tone. "No, I know, I'm still waiting. But listen. There's this tiny dandelion, right here, forcing its way through a crack in the concrete. The sun just came out from behind that big grey building, and it's lighting up all the little seed heads. They look like a hundred miniature fiber-optic lamps. It’s… I don’t know, it’s just absolutely perfect. The way the light catches them. The sheer stubbornness of it growing here, of all places. It’s ridiculous, isn’t it? To get so worked up over a weed? But I can’t look away. The whole universe seems to have shrunk down to this one little explosion of light and fluff on a dirty sidewalk, and honestly, it’s one of the most incredible things I’ve ever seen." He fell silent, just looking, and for a moment, so did I.
