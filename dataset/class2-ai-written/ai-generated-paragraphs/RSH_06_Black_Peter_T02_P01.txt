The only light came from the digital clock on the stove, its red glow doing little to push back the shadows clinging to the kitchen corners. He stood motionless by the back door, listening. “It’s nothing,” she whispered from the table, her teacup rattling softly as she set it down. “Just the house settling.” He didn’t turn, his silhouette a rigid line against the moonlit glass. “It’s the same sound as last night. The gravel crunching.” A long silence stretched between them, thick with twenty years of unspoken fear. “No one knows we’re here,” she insisted, her voice lacking any real conviction. “We were careful.” He finally moved, his hand coming up to trace a crack in the windowpane. “You can’t just bury something like that and expect the ground to stay flat forever. Sooner or later, a stone works its way to the surface.”
