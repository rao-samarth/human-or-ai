The train hissed, a final sigh before departure, and his connection was gone. Panic flared hot in his chest, a frantic bird beating against his ribs. The departure board was a cascade of incomprehensible symbols, the loudspeaker crackling in a language he couldn't grasp. His first instinct was to bolt, to retreat back to the anonymous safety of his hotel room and surrender the day. But then her voice, not a memory but a current running through his veins, cut through the noise: *Stop flapping. Find the person in charge.* It was the same tone she’d used when he'd crashed his bike, the one that left no room for hysterics. He scanned the rushing crowd, his eyes locking onto a woman in a crisp blue uniform behind a glass partition. He took a deep breath, straightened his shoulders—her gesture, not his—and started moving.
