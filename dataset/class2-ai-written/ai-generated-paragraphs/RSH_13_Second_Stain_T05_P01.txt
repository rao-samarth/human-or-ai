The rhythmic clatter of the train over the tracks did little to soothe him as he reread the message on his phone, the screen’s cold light illuminating a future crumbling into dust. The lie that had launched his career was about to be exposed at the very conference he was speeding towards. All those years of work, of public accolades, were built on a foundation of sand, and he could already picture the faces of his peers—the shock, the pity, and the quiet, satisfying scorn. There was no explaining it away. His gaze fell upon the steaming cup of coffee beside his laptop, the one device holding the only complete copy of the original, fraudulent data. A sudden lurch of the carriage was all the excuse he needed. His hand jerked, sending the dark liquid flooding across the keyboard. A brief hiss, a flicker, and then blackness. He stared at the blank screen, the smell of burnt sugar and chicory filling the air. It wasn't a solution, merely a stay of execution, but in the suffocating confines of the train car, it felt like breathing again.
