Mark was carefully placing his favorite mug into a brown cardboard box when Sarah walked over, her own coffee forgotten on her desk. “What are you doing?” she whispered, nodding toward the box. He didn’t look up, just continued wrapping the mug in a sheet of bubble wrap from the mailroom. “Position’s been eliminated,” he said, his voice flat. “Part of the new Q4 efficiency drive.” Sarah stared, bewildered. “But just yesterday, in the all-hands, Henderson went on for twenty minutes about how we’re a family here. He said people are our greatest asset.” Mark finally looked at her, a small, tired smile on his face. “Oh, he was very professional about it. Wished me the best. Even sent a link to a webinar on ‘Navigating Career Transitions.’ Very civilized, right up to the end.” He taped the box shut with a loud, final rip.
