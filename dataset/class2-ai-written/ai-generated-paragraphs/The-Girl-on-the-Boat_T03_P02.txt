Cynthia sighed, a sound of theatrical despair that suggested a far greater tragedy than the one she was about to describe. The heirloom tomatoes for the fundraiser’s canapés, she explained, had arrived merely 'vine-ripened.' She said the words as if announcing a chemical spill, her perfectly sculpted eyebrows raised, awaiting a shared sense of outrage. She was looking directly at me. My mind raced through the acceptable responses. A sympathetic frown was the safest bet, a low-risk investment in my continued employment. A noncommittal hum might also work. All I could think about, however, were the instant noodles in my desk drawer, which suddenly felt like a profound philosophical statement. I felt the muscles in my face trying to arrange themselves into a mask of genuine concern over tomato pedigree. It was the most crucial performance of my week, and I was forgetting all my lines.
