It started with a simple hi-vis vest and the title of “Chief Barbecue Placement Officer” for the annual block party. For the first hour, he was a pillar of the community, a helpful neighbor ensuring every grill was a safe distance from the inflatable slide. But by noon, something had shifted. A glint appeared in his eye, the kind reserved for minor despots and overly ambitious HOA presidents. He began enforcing rules that didn't exist, declaring a strict "no-kebab zone" near the prize-winning petunias and demanding a "charcoal tax" payable in potato salad. He even requisitioned a garden gnome, which he placed on a folding chair to serve as his silent, unblinking deputy. We all just watched, mesmerized, as a man who once organized carpools transformed into a miniature tyrant, all for the brief, intoxicating authority over a well-organized cul-de-sac.
