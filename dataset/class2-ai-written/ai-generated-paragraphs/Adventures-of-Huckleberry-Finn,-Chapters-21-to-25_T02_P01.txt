The shift began not with a shout but with a shared glance across the ballroom, a subtle, synchronized turning of heads. A single whispered comment had multiplied, replicating itself at table after table until it became an unspoken consensus. Individually, few felt any genuine malice toward the man now standing alone by the iced sculptures. Some even privately questioned the story's source, its sudden, vicious certainty. But to voice such a doubt would mean breaking from the pack, inviting the same cold scrutiny for oneself. It was so much easier, so much safer, to simply adjust one's posture, to angle a shoulder away from him, to join the low murmur of disapproval. In the warm, chandelier-lit room, a powerful chill radiated from the newly formed collective, a solidarity built not on conviction but on the quiet, mutual fear of being the next one left standing alone.
