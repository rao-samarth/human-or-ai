The only light in the study came from the green-shaded lamp, pooling over stacks of municipal reports and zoning proposals. He traced a line on a demographic chart with his finger, the numbers blurring into meaninglessness after so many hours. His gaze drifted upward, past the cluttered shelves, to the worn world map pinned to the corkboard. A single, thin red line, drawn years ago, snaked from their small city across oceans to a remote coastline he had only seen in pictures. It was a foolish, youthful dream of escape, of anonymity in a place where no one knew his name or depended on his signature. A distant siren wailed, a sharp reminder of the sprawling, needy city just outside his window. He let out a slow breath, the sound loud in the silent room, and pulled the top report back into the circle of light. The map would have to wait.
