Across the table, my brother laughed at a joke, the sound swallowed by the cheerful din of the holiday meal. I watched the performance of normalcy, a participant in every way except the most crucial. There is a strange, immutable physics to a past action; it cannot be un-spoken or un-done, but instead settles into the foundation of the present, altering everything that is built upon it. The person they saw raising a glass was a stranger to the one who endlessly re-examined the moment of betrayal, searching for a different path that no longer existed. It wasn't a question of their forgiveness, not really. It was the cold realization that a part of you can become a permanent exhibit in your own mind, a silent monument to a choice from which you can never fully walk away. Someone passed the potatoes, and I smiled.
