"Don't look at the door like it's your only way out," she whispered, and the quiet words landed with the force of a physical blow. He kept his gaze fixed on the water-stained patch of ceiling above her bookshelf, tracing its shape as if it held some vital answer. Every instinct screamed at him to turn, to pull her close and erase the last hour with a promise he couldn't possibly keep. But his father’s weary face swam in his mind, the memory of that final, desperate request made years ago still sharp and binding. That single promise was the architecture of his entire life, the one load-bearing wall holding up his family’s expectations and his own sense of honor. To stay here, in this small, warm room with her, would be to choose himself. It would also mean bringing everything else crashing down. The space between them grew thick with the things he couldn't say, a silence broken only by the low hum of the refrigerator.
