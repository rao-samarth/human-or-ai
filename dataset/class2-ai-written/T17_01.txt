The drone of the cicadas was the only sound between them for a long time. Finally, Liam broke the silence on the porch steps. “I still don’t get why you’re so angry. I told you I had car trouble.” Chloe stared out at the darkening fields, refusing to look at him. “You sent a text,” she said, her voice flat. “Four words. ‘Can’t make it, sorry.’ For Dad’s anniversary.” He threw his hands up in frustration. “To the *bakery*, Chloe! I couldn’t make it to the bakery to get the cake. The axle on my truck snapped on the county line. I sat in a ditch for two hours waiting for a tow, thinking you all knew.” She finally turned to him, her eyes glistening. “We thought you just didn’t care. We thought you chose not to be there.” The unspoken months of resentment hung in the humid air, a ghost born from a single, misunderstood message. The moment was gone, and they both knew it couldn't be reclaimed.
