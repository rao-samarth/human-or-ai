The key made a small, sharp sound as it landed on the granite countertop, a final, metallic punctuation mark. His father, a large silhouette against the pale light filtering through the kitchen window, didn’t move from his position by the door. “You really believe you can just walk out?” It wasn’t a shout but a low rumble, the same tone he had used for years to quell arguments and end discussions before they began. This time, however, the familiar vibration of command found no purchase. She met his gaze across the dimly lit room, the hum of the refrigerator the only other sound between them. “It’s not a matter of believing,” she said, her voice steady. “It’s what’s happening.” He took a single, deliberate step, a solid obstacle blocking the path to the hall, a last assertion of his rule over this space. She didn't flinch or look away, simply stood there, her packed duffel bag a heavy weight at her side, her silence a more potent challenge than any scream could ever be.
