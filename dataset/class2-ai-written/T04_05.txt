The light from the open refrigerator cast a holy glow on it. There it sat, a perfect, glistening cheesecake on a ceramic pedestal, a silent monument to everything we had sworn off two weeks ago in a solemn, broccoli-fueled ceremony. I could, in theory, close the door and pretend this never happened, returning to the couch where Mark was blissfully unaware, deep in some terrible reality show. But could I live with the knowledge, the sweet, creamy secret fermenting in my soul? This was more than just dessert; it was a crisis of conscience. Waking him would mean a shared failure, a mutual descent back into sugar-fueled chaos, but facing this glorious temptation alone felt like a betrayal of the highest order, an act unbecoming of a true comrade-in-arms. I quietly grabbed two forks.
