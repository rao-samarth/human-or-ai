The cursor blinked with an infuriatingly steady rhythm on the employee evaluation form, the only light in the otherwise dark office. On one side of the screen were the hard numbers—Ben’s missed sales targets, the two client complaints, the late project submission. The objective data was clear, and Alex’s responsibility as department head was just as unambiguous. The path forward was a straight line, dictated by company policy and professional integrity. Yet, Alex’s mind kept drifting to the previous weekend, to the easy way Ben had smiled across a cluttered cafe table, completely unaware of the report they would have to write. He'd spoken of his frustrations with the new software, his voice full of a hope and vulnerability he never showed under the office's fluorescent lights. How could they reduce that person to a series of negative data points? To be fair to the company was to be cruel to him, but to be kind to him was to compromise everything Alex had worked for. The cursor continued its patient, silent pulse.
