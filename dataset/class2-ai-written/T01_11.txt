The air smelled of damp earth and the coming rain, but Chloe wasn't looking at the darkening hills. Her gaze was fixed on her brother. "You talk about the empire you built, Liam, but all I remember is the fire." He flinched, his expensive coat suddenly looking out of place against the splintered fence post he leaned on. "It was a necessary step. A foundation." Her laugh was short and brittle, a sound that didn't belong in the quiet pasture. "You call bulldozing Dad's fields a foundation? You sold off every piece of this place, every memory, for a down payment on your grand vision. You didn't just leave; you paved over the road back. So don't stand here and tell me you did this for us. You did this for you, and we were just the price you were willing to pay." He opened his mouth, but no sound came out as the first drops of rain began to fall.
