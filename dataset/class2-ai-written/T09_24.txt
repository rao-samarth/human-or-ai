From the balcony, the city was just a grid of pinpricks and distant noise. He replayed the conversation again, not the words themselves, but the silence that followed. That silence had a weight to it, a physical presence he carried around like a smooth, heavy stone in his pocket—something he’d find himself unconsciously touching throughout the day, just to confirm it was still there. Down below, the traffic flowed in silent streaks of red and white, each light a life he couldn't comprehend, a story he wasn't part of anymore. A siren wailed somewhere to the east, but the sound felt thin, swallowed by the vastness between the buildings. He pressed his forehead against the cold metal of the railing, wishing the chill could seep inside and numb the one part of him that felt too warm, too loud in the quiet of his own mind.
