A woman’s voice, dangerously low, cut through the quiet of the international foods aisle. “I specifically told you to use the *fancy* bottle.” The man beside her, looking utterly bewildered, threw his hands up, nearly knocking over a display of artisan crackers. “Your text said, ‘Give Bartholomew a proper wash with the good stuff.’ How was I to know you didn’t mean the imported, first-press olive oil?” An elderly man froze mid-reach for a jar of sun-dried tomatoes, his head cocked. “It’s for his coat, Darren, not to marinate him! He’s a Persian cat, not a chicken cutlet! He now looks like he’s been lacquered, the vet’s bill for degreasing him is going to be astronomical, and my signature vinaigrette is ruined for the dinner party tonight!” Darren just stared at the shelf of balsamic vinegars, muttering to himself, “But his coat has such a remarkable sheen.”
