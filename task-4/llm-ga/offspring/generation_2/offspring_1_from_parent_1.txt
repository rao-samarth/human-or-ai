The kind of man who returned borrowed things promptly and supported every local bake sale. One afternoon, I mistakenly opened a letter addressed to him. Inside wasn't a bill. It wasn't a newsletter. It was a government file, thick with blacked-out lines, and a photograph of a much younger man with his face and chillingly detached eyes. The envelope felt leaden in my hand. Standing there on my porch, I finally understood that his mild-mannered nature wasn't a personality at all. It was an elaborate, perfectly constructed front, concealing a past that had no right to be tucked away in a place like this.
