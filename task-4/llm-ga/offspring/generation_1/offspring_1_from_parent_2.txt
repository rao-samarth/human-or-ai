GENERATION 1 - OFFSPRING 1
Parent: Individual 2
================================================================================

Text:
My whole life, the steady pulse of our street had been the soundtrack to safety, a dependable cadence where the biggest commotion was an overturned trash can. I was utterly convinced I had a complete read on the fellow in the yellow bungalow across the street. He was the very picture of ordinary kindness, the sort of person who would bring back your hedge trimmer refueled and buy out the entire bake sale table himself. That whole image shattered the day an accidental delivery prompted me to open a piece of his mail. It contained no cheerful note or utility statement, but an official government file, heavy with redacted sentences and a photograph of a much younger him, his expression a look of cold determination. The envelope became impossibly heavy in my grasp as the reality of it began to sink in: his amiable, soft-spoken demeanor wasn't who he actually was. It was an impeccable performance, a masterful concealment for a past that was frighteningly at odds with our tranquil pocket of the suburbs.
