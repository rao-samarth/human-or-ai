Occupying the butter-yellow bungalow across the street, he was the very embodiment of unassuming decency: the type of fellow who returned loaned items without delay and patronized every neighborhood fundraiser. But that whole reality was shattered by a simple mix-up with the mail. One afternoon, I unintentionally tore open an envelope addressed to him. Inside was not a utility bill or a local circular, but an official-looking dossier, dense with blacked-out text, and a snapshot of a significantly younger man with an unnervingly vacant gaze. The envelope felt impossibly heavy in my hand as I stood on my doorstep and the truth finally crystallized. His gentle disposition wasn't a genuine character trait; it was a sophisticated, flawlessly crafted disguise, masking a history that was utterly out of place, tucked away in a community like ours.
