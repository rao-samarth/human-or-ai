I was all but certain I had my neighbor's measure. Living in the yellow bungalow across the way, he was the very model of quiet decency; the kind of man who returned borrowed things promptly and supported every local bake sale. Indeed, so reliably pleasant was he that he was almost part of the sceneryâ€”a perfect fit for the comforting hum of our street, where the worst infraction was a poorly maintained hedge. That entire world, however, was shattered by a simple postal error. One afternoon, I by chance opened a letter addressed to him. Inside was not a bill or a newsletter, but a government file, thick with redacted lines, and a photograph of a much younger man with chillingly detached eyes. The envelope felt leaden in my hand as I stood there on my porch. I finally, truly, understood. His mild-mannered nature was no personality at all, but rather an elaborate, perfectly constructed facade, concealing a past that had no business whatsoever being ensconced in a place like this.
