I was all but convinced I had the measure of my neighbor. The man in the yellow bungalow across the way presented, in all aspects, the very model of quiet decencyâ€”the kind of fellow who returned borrowed things promptly and supported every local bake sale. So reliably pleasant was he that he became part of the scenery, a perfect fit for the comforting hum of our street, where the gravest infraction was a poorly maintained hedge. That entire world, however, was shattered by a simple postal error. One afternoon, I mistakenly opened a letter addressed to him. Inside: no bill, no newsletter, but a government file thick with redacted lines, and a photograph of a much younger man with chillingly detached eyes. The envelope felt leaden in my hand as I stood there on my porch. It was then I truly understood. His mild-mannered countenance was no personality at all, but rather an elaborate, perfectly constructed facade, concealing a past that had no business whatsoever being ensconced in a place like this.
