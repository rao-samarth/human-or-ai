I was thoroughly convinced I had a complete read on my neighbor. Residing in the yellow bungalow just opposite, he was the epitome of unassuming virtue; the sort of fellow who returned borrowed items with haste and chipped in for every community fundraiser. In fact, his unfailingly cordial nature made him blend into the backgroundâ€”ideally suited for the peaceful rhythm of our lane, where the most serious transgression was an unkempt hedge. That whole perception, however, was obliterated by a mere mix-up in the mail. One afternoon, I inadvertently opened an envelope meant for him. Inside was neither a bill nor a circular, but an official dossier, heavy with blacked-out sentences, and an image of a much younger man with an unnervingly vacant gaze. The envelope felt impossibly heavy in my grasp as I remained there on my stoop. At last, the truth dawned on me. His placid demeanor wasn't a personality, but a meticulous and flawless charade, masking a history that had no right to be tucked away in a place like this.
