I was practically convinced I had a handle on my neighbor. The resident of the yellow cottage across the street was, seemingly, the epitome of unassuming respectability; the sort of gentleman who brought back borrowed items without delay and patronized each neighborhood fundraiser. He was so dependably good-natured that he blended into the backgroundâ€”seamlessly suited to the tranquil pulse of our lane, where the gravest transgression was an untidy hedgerow. However, that entire perception came crashing down because of a simple mix-up with the mail. One afternoon, I mistakenly opened an envelope intended for him. Inside was no invoice, no circular, but a federal dossier, heavy with blacked-out sentences, and a photo of a far younger man with an unnervingly remote gaze. The letter felt unnaturally weighty in my grasp as I stood there on my front steps. In that moment, the truth dawned on me. His placid nature was no personality at all, but a sophisticated, flawlessly built mask, concealing a history that had no right whatsoever to be tucked away in a community like this one.
