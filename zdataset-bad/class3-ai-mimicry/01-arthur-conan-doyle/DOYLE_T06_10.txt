The fire had sunk to mere embers, casting long, wavering shadows across the study where my companion sat, his head buried in his hands. He confessed not to a physical ailment, but to a far more insidious affliction—a canker of the spirit that had taken root upon witnessing a rival’s recent, and quite public, success. Each word of praise directed at this other man was, to his fevered mind, a personal slight; every professional advancement a direct impediment to his own. He spoke of a constant, gnawing suspicion, a sleepless vigilance which interrogated every friendly gesture for some hidden mockery or condescension. It is a most singular form of self-torture, this bitter preoccupation with another’s good fortune, for it transforms an otherwise rational man into a haunted creature of the night, forever measuring his own substance against a shadow that lengthens with every dawn.
