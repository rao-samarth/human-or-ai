The profound stillness of my study was shattered by a most singular and persistent scraping, a sound which seemed to emanate from the very bowels of the house itself. My first thought, that of a common housebreaker, was swiftly discarded; there was a certain methodical quality to the noise, a rhythmic rasping entirely inconsistent with the furtive work of a cracksman. For a long minute I remained frozen in my chair, my mind a theatre for all manner of dreadful possibilities, from some monstrous rodent gnawing upon the joists to a more subtle and ghastly mechanical failure in the plumbing. It became clear, however, that to sit there in a state of growing trepidation was an untenable position for any man of sense.
