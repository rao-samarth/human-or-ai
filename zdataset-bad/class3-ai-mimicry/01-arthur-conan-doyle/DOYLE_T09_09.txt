The glittering chandeliers cast a thousand brilliant points of light upon the assembly, and the music of the orchestra swelled with a gaiety that was, to my own soul, a most exquisite form of torture. I moved amongst the smiling couples, a spectre at a feast, for in every friendly countenance I fancied I could detect the shadow of an accusation. How could these good people converse and laugh with such unburdened hearts? They did not know—they could never know—of the singular and wretched betrayal I had perpetrated; a deed which had fractured a sacred confidence and left upon my character an indelible mark. Each polite inquiry as to my health felt like a surgeon’s probe upon a festering wound, and I knew, with a certainty that chilled me to the very marrow, that I was henceforth a man apart from all such innocent revelry.
