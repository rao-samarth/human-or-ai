It was a grim and perplexing business to observe how the entire edifice of their thirty-year partnership was now crumbling to dust, and all, it seemed, upon the foundation of a single, carelessly worded telegram. Mr. Abernathy, a man not given to effusive explanations, had intended the message as a simple statement of fact—a means to spare his colleague a tiresome journey. Yet in the mind of Mr. Croft, a gentleman of a more sensitive and, it must be admitted, suspicious temperament, the blunt words had landed as a calculated slight. From this poisoned seed of doubt, a forest of grievances had sprung. Every subsequent action was viewed through this distorted lens; courtesy was mistaken for condescension, silence for judgement. And so they stood, two ordinarily reasonable men, on the precipice of a permanent rupture, each convinced of the other’s malice, and neither willing to re-examine the trifling spark that had kindled such a ruinous conflagration.
