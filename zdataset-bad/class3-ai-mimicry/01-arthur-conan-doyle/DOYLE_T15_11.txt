Good God, man, pull yourself together!" I exclaimed, placing a firm hand upon his shoulder, for he was trembling with a terrible agitation. "You have not slept, you have barely eaten, and for what? To stare out at the squalls lashing this dreary village street? This entire business concerning the old church bell is a phantom of an overwrought mind." He whirled upon me then, his face a pale mask in the gloom, and his eyes held a feverish light that I found truly alarming. "You call it a phantom?" he hissed, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper that was more unnerving than any shout. "I tell you, it is the only real thing left! I hear its tolling not with my ears, but here"—he tapped his temple with a bony finger—"a constant, silent summons. It is a logic purer than any you could devise, a command I am utterly powerless to disobey, and I shall follow it, even if it leads me to damnation.
