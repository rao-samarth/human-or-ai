I seized him by the arm as he made to plunge into the afternoon throng of Threadneedle Street. “Penhaligon, I implore you, contain yourself!” I hissed, for his face was a mask of wild agitation. “Do you not see him?” he cried, his voice cracking. “The fellow in the drab overcoat—he has followed me for a fortnight! He mocks me with his every step!” My gaze followed his trembling finger to a most unremarkable individual: a portly gentleman of middle years, clutching a leather satchel and looking for all the world as if his most pressing concern was the next omnibus. “My dear fellow,” I reasoned, “what possible interest could such a man have in your affairs? The notion is a phantom.” But my appeal was lost upon him; he tore his elbow from my grasp with a cry that was half-sob, half-snarl, and was instantly swallowed by the human tide, leaving me to ponder how swiftly a fine mind can be utterly consumed by a single, poisonous idea.
