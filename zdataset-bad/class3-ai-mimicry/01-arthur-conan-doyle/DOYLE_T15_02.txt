He paced the length of my study, his hands clasped so tightly behind his back that the knuckles showed white against his skin. "You must see that it is the only way," he insisted, his voice a low, strained thing, quite unlike his usual boisterous tone. "To expose a scoundrel, one must employ a scoundrel's tools; the man's reputation is built upon a lie, and I shall answer it with one of my own devising!" I could perceive in his glittering eye and the tremor in his speech a singular agitation, a fever of the mind which had quite consumed his better judgment. This was no longer a question of justice, for justice is a cold and impartial thing. It had become a personal crusade, a monomania which threatened to destroy the very honour he claimed to be defending, and I knew with a dreadful certainty that no argument of mine could now sway him from his perilous course.
