At the Lord Mayor’s banquet, amidst the clinking of crystal and the low hum of self-important chatter, Mr Abernathy, a man whose fortune was built upon a foundation of scrupulous, if uninspired, investments, listened with a fixed placidity. His companion, a fellow of dubious origin but undeniable persuasive charm, spoke in low, conspiratorial tones of a venture in the Americas—a railway scheme of such breathtaking audacity that it verged upon the fraudulent. A casual observer might have seen nothing more than polite interest upon Abernathy’s countenance; yet a keener eye would have noted the slight tremor in the hand that held his wine glass and the faint glistening upon his brow. Here was a serpent in this gilded garden, offering a fruit of such prodigious financial reward that it threatened to topple a lifetime’s edifice of caution and respectability. Every principle he had ever held dear seemed to shrink and recede before the colossal, glittering vision of what might be gained, and for that one perilous moment, the dull, safe path of rectitude appeared an altogether fool’s errand.
