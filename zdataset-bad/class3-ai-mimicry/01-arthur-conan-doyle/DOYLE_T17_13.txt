Mr. Albright, a man not given to elaborate instruction, had merely remarked that the north-facing elevation lacked a certain "vigor," gesturing vaguely toward the blueprints before moving on to other matters. To his mind, it was a trifling observation, a thought to be mulled over at a later date. Young Hemlock, however, whose entire professional future seemed to him to hang upon just such pronouncements, received the words as a direct and urgent command. He toiled through the night, his mind ablaze with visions of promotion, fundamentally altering the façade with a series of bold, quite unsolicited, classical columns. The catastrophic result of this earnest misunderstanding was not revealed until the following morning, a mere hour before the client’s arrival. Albright stood aghast before the redrafted plans; the foundation of a promising partnership had been fractured beyond repair by a single, ill-defined word and an excess of youthful ambition. Henceforth, a chasm of cold formality would exist between the two men, where once there had been the potential for mentorship
