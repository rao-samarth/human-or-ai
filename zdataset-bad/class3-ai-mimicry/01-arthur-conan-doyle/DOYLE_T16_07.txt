Amid the drone of polite conversation and the glitter of crystal, I perceived the terrible gravity of the moment with a clarity that was itself a torment. The General, a man whose reputation was built upon the ruins of others, was preparing to speak; and I, by some cruel twist of fate, was the sole person in that gilded ballroom who understood the catastrophic lie he was about to unleash. A cold paralysis seized me, for to challenge him here would mean my own immediate and public disgrace, an utter shattering of the life I had so carefully constructed. The faces around me, placid and unsuspecting, swam in a haze of candlelight. Every fibre of my being cried out for silence, for the simple, blameless act of letting the blow fall upon another. Yet, as his voice began to rise, some deeper, unbidden mechanism took control, and I found myself on my feet, the expectant hush of the room turning upon me like a physical force.
