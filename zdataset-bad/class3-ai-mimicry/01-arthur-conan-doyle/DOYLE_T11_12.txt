It is a curious study, is it not, to observe the subtle gradations by which men arrange themselves upon this floating microcosm of our society? One need not consult a directory of the peerage to perceive the unspoken chasms which separate one passenger from the next. See that industrialist, whose coat is of the finest cut; he nonetheless betrays his origins in the very manner he grips his cane—a tool to him, and not a mere accessory. Now, contrast this with the languid ease of the young baronet yonder, whose shabby tweeds somehow command more immediate and genuine respect from the ship’s purser than all the manufacturer’s crisp banknotes. It is a silent, intricate dance, governed by a thousand trifles of accent, posture, and an inherited assurance utterly distinct from acquired wealth. Here is a code written not in ink, but in the very marrow of their bones, and it is this which determines their place in the firmament far more than any earthly fortune.
