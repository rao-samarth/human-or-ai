From my corner of the Duchess’s crowded drawing-room, I observed the celebrated Lord Ashworth. He was, as is customary upon the announcement of some new public triumph, the very centre of a vortex of congratulation, and yet his countenance bore the unmistakable stamp of profound fatigue. I found myself pondering the true mechanics of his ascent; was this man, in truth, the masterful architect of his own grand fortune, or was he merely the fortunate passenger upon a current of circumstance he had the wit to board but not the power to direct? His jaw was set, to be sure, with the hard line of resolute intention, a feature which spoke of a will not easily bent. And yet, a certain look in his eye—a distant, almost surprised expression—suggested a man who, having aimed his vessel for a specific port, now finds himself washed ashore upon some altogether different, though no less magnificent, coast.
