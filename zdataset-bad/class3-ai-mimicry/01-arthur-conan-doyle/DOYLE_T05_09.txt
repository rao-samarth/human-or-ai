The Governor’s ballroom presented a veritable crush of society, and the orchestra laboured through a waltz whose cheerful strains grated upon my sensibilities. I stood beside Miss Albright, my intended, her animated discourse upon some trivial matter of the season passing by my ears entirely unheeded. For my gaze, and indeed my whole consciousness, had become fixed upon a figure standing near the far colonnade—a woman whose quiet presence made a mockery of all this gilded pageantry. There, my reason told me, was ruin; a path leading away from all that was expected and arranged, a defiance of the solemn vow which now felt less like a promise and more like a carefully wrought iron cage. My position was fixed, my arm linked with that of the woman to whom my future was pledged; yet every fibre of my being strained across the polished floor, drawn by a current far stronger, and infinitely more perilous, than any mere social obligation.
