I have made a discovery of some gravity concerning the clock upon the mantelpiece. It began as an innocent enough tick-tock, a pert, insolent little sound that was, for a time, a tolerable punctuation to the long hours after midnight. But it has since grown in its office, you see, promoting itself from a simple timekeeper to the grand marshal of my private vexations. A man might think himself a fool for waging a silent war against a conspiracy of wood and brass, and in the light of day I would likely be that man’s most ardent supporter. Yet here, in the dead of the house, its rhythmic impudence is a physical thing—a steady, hammering force that chops my very thoughts into useless little bits before they can properly form.
