Over by the depot's dusty ticket window, a gentleman of considerable girth and self-satisfaction was propounding his theories to a lean, sun-cured fellow who looked as if he’d been wherever the wind blew him. "A man is the architect of his own fortune, sir," the large man declared, "and don't let any soul tell you different!" The traveler just squinted. "Is he now? And I suppose the lightning bolt consults the architect before it strikes the spire he just built?" The portly man puffed up like a toad in a puddle, entirely convinced he’d personally arranged for the sun to rise that morning and was now doing the world a favor by explaining the mechanics of it.
