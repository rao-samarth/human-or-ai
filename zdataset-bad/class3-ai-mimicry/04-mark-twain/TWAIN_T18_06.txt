He accosted me on the corner of Fourth and Elm, a blustering cannonball of a man in a waistcoat that seemed two sizes too small for the sheer volume of his vexation, and accused me—with a wagging finger that nearly poked out the eye of a passing delivery boy—of having absconded with his personal equilibrium. Now, this was a commodity I had no more use for than a second shadow, and I said as much, but the man was not to be reasoned with.
