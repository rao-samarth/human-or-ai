I found myself settled on a bench in the main depot, where a man can get a fine education in human nature for the price of a few minutes' patience. Nearby, a pair of otherwise respectable-looking gentlemen were concluding a piece of business—a small matter of a disputed property line, it seemed—and one of them was holding forth on the flexible nature of survey stakes. The other fellow, who had begun the conversation with a face full of pious reservation, was now nodding along with the enthusiasm of a new convert. You could practically see the ledger in his mind where the column for "virtue" was being erased to make more room for "profit."
