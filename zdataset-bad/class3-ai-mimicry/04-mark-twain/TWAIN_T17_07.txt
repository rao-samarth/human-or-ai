We hadn't been rattling along in that devil's own cracker-box for more than an hour when young Mr. Abernathy, a man whose constitution was plainly better suited to a parlor settee, was flung sideways by a particularly vicious lurch. His hand, flying out for purchase, connected with the brim of a slumbering giant's hat, knocking it clean over the man's eyes. The giant awoke with a bellow that would have frightened a locomotive, seeing the offense but not the cause. The poor fellow’s frantic apologies, a torrent of stammered “beg-pardons” and “my-good-sirs,” were taken for the rankest sort of mockery by the aggrieved party. Before a sensible soul could intervene, there was a roar, a scramble, and the distinct sound of a valise full of prayer books being used as a bludgeon.
