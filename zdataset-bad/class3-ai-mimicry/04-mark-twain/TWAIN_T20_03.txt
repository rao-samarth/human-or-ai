Mr. Finch delivered a most pious lecture this morning on the mortal sin of extravagance, a sermon he reserves for those occasions when the company books are looking a trifle lean. He spoke with the fire of a genuine prophet about the sacred duty of conserving paperclips and the soul-damaging wastefulness of using a fresh sheet of foolscap when the back of an old one would do. It was a fine and noble speech; it nearly brought a tear to the eye. Then, not an hour later, I happened to pass his office and saw four stout men wrestling with a prodigious new desk made of some dark, imported timber—a veritable monument to commerce—which they were installing to replace the perfectly sound oak one he’d used for years.
