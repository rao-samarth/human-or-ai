I planted myself on a crate to watch the human river flow by, and I declare it’s a more peculiar spectacle than any paid-for theatrical. You see a man whose coat is worth more than my yearly wages get a deferential nod from a policeman for blocking the whole walkway, while a half-starved urchin trying to sell papers gets a sharp rap on the ear for the same offense, only smaller. The whole arrangement is a sort of silent agreement, a grand bit of make-believe where everyone knows their part without a word being spoken. A grand lady will sail through the thickest crowd, and a path just seems to melt away before her, as if the common folk were made of steam; but let a working fellow with soot on his cheek try the same maneuver, and he’s met with a wall of shoulders as solid as a brick kiln. It’s a powerful sort of magic
