The whole town had tucked itself in for the night, leaving a quiet so deep you could hear a mouse sigh in the parsonage attic. But for me, a little private court was in session right behind my eyeballs, and it had no intention of adjourning. There ain't no jury, and no judge in a powdered wig, mind you; just the plain, unvarnished recollection of the look on Silas’s face when I let him down with that easy lie. It was a small, mean thing, the kind that don't cause a public scandal but instead takes up residence in a man’s chest and refuses to pay rent.
