I was walking down a thoroughfare the other day, a river of humanity so thick you could hardly spit without christening some gentleman’s top hat, when I saw the whole miserable business play out plain as day. A fellow—a perfectly ordinary sort of clerk by the look of his worn cuffs—was staring at another man climbing into a polished cab. Now, the man in the cab wasn't a king, mind you; he just had a better coat and a smile that suggested his dinner wasn't going to be boiled cabbage for the third night running. But the look on that clerk's face! It was a masterpiece of human misery. You could see him calculating the cost of the coat, the shine on the boots, the price of the cigar, and subtracting it all directly from his own soul.
