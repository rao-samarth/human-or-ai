That young Abernathy fellow, who only got his position by virtue of his father's name and not an ounce of his own wit, took a notion to claim Miller's accounting improvements as his own. Well, the higher-ups, in their infinite and often misplaced wisdom, called a meeting—which is their cure for all mortal ills—and had the young scamp issue a dry, pre-written apology. But you could see plain on Miller’s face that this neatly packaged settlement didn’t settle a thing in his soul. The company called the books balanced, a fair and equitable trade—a man's stolen labor for a minute of public humiliation.
