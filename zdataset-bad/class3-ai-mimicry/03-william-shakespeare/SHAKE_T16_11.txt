What devil's scratching from the floor below doth so conspire to murder sleep? A sound most unnatural, like bone dragged slow on stone. My heart, that craven bird, doth batter its frail cage and plead for me to play the statue, to be but breathing stone until the dawn. And yet, what manner of man am I, to let some miscreant or night-born phantom hold dominion in mine own house? Fie on such thoughts! 'Tis but the wind, or a loose shutter vexing the frame. So I told myself, again and yet again. Still, I took up the heavy fire-poker, its iron cold a comfort to my palm, which did not tremble—nay, not with fright—but with a keen and eager choler to confront this rude disturber of my peace. Every board did cry out under my foot, a chorus of complaint, as I began my descent into that waiting, ink-black maw.
