In the great press of the market square, where merchants hawked their wares and common folk did jostle for a better view of some passing lord, a sudden groan of timber went unheard by most. But one man, a humble carter whose eye was ever turned to strain and weight, looked up to see the stone-wrought balcony give a sickening shudder. A tremor, born not of the cold but of a deeper dread, did take hold of his limbs; for upon that failing precipice, a small girl played, heedless of the chasm that yawned to claim her. His blood ran to ice, and his feet, those leaden servants, would fain have carried him to safety with the shrieking mob. What was this child to him? A stranger's issue, no more. Yet, as the dust began to fall in a grim, grey shower, some truer spirit rose within his breast, a fire to quench the watery fear that held him fast, and with a cry that was half prayer, he plunged headlong into the chaos, his own life a paltry wager against the soul of innocence.
