The candle-flame doth dance and mock the stillness of this chamber, wherein he lies bound fast to the oaken chair. My own hand, which now holds the steel, trembles not from fear but from the great and terrible question that doth grip my soul. To grant him the swift end he hath so richly earned by his treachery—is that the satisfaction my heart demands? Or is it a baser hunger, a foul craving that mimics the face of fairness but is, in truth, its darkest shadow?
