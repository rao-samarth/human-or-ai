He did but spy the folded parchment pass from her pale hand to the waiting boy, yet in that moment, a venomous fancy did possess his mind and banish all his reason. With a voice made rough by this new choler, he spake of treason, of a faithless heart, and of a love betrayed by secrecy’s dark cloak. Her protestations, though they sprang from truth’s own well, did sound to his corrupted ear as hollow, practiced lies; for jealousy, that green and ghastly sickness of the soul, had made him deaf to all but its own serpent whispers. Thus the breach was made, not by a sword or by some great and willful offence, but by a simple glance misread. A harmless message, meant to bind their fortunes fast, now served only to unloose the knot of all their amity, proving that a doubt, once planted in a noble mind, grows faster than the most pernicious weed.
