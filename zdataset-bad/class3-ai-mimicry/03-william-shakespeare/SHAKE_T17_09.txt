Judge me not harsh, for you did not see it! There, in the market square, stood he I called my brother, his head inclined to that serpent's ear, their whispers thick as thieves'. A shared flagon, a sudden laugh that seemed to mock my very fortune—what need had I for more? Should I have played the simpleton and asked the meaning of such congress? Nay, for truth is a shy creature before a liar's face, and his would have been a mask of innocence. I turned upon my heel ere they could spy me, and what was sworn between us in good faith I have now unsworn, sending back his token with a sharp word for the messenger. Let him now wonder at the cause, as I was made to wonder at his loyalty. He hath made his bed with vipers, and I’ll not be stung for pulling back the sheets.
