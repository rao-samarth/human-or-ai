My dear Percy, a most peculiar thought struck me this afternoon as I watched one of those fluffy thistle-down things—the sort of feathery whatnot that perambulates on the breeze—doing its stuff over the back lawn. One moment it was heading purposefully towards the rose bushes, as if on a mission of the first importance, only for a sudden puff of wind from the east to send it scuttling off towards the vicarage. And it set a chap to pondering, old man. Is that not precisely the human condition in a nutshell? We map out our lives with the care of a seasoned general, deciding to pop up to London or take up bee-keeping, when in reality we are merely so much thistle-down, buffeted hither and thither by the arbitrary whims of—well, of whatever it is that does the buffeting.
