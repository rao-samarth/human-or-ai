Look here, Daphne, old thing,” I began, feeling the sort of internal disquietude usually reserved for a chap who has inadvertently swallowed a fish-fork, “it’s not that the prospect of buzzing off to Biarritz with you doesn’t fill my very being with a certain zing. It absolutely does. The heart does a bit of a tango, and all that. But the fact is, a fellow has his code.” Her expression, which had been one of sunny anticipation, clouded over like the sky before a beastly thunderstorm. “Your code? What code, Algy?” “The code of the Chumleighs!” I insisted, perhaps a trifle more forcefully than intended. “I gave my word to Uncle Theodore that I would personally oversee the welfare of his prize-winning petunia, ‘Constance’, while he’s away. One can’t simply abandon a petunia to its fate, not when one has given one’s solemn oath. It wouldn’t be cricket. It’s a frightful bind, I grant you, especially when faced with the alternative of you, but a promise is a promise, even when it’s to a vegetable.
