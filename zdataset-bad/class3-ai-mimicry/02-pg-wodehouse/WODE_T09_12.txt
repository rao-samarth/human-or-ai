A fellow ought, by rights, to feel on top of the world when the senior partner has just described one’s memorandum on the Pringle account as ‘a work of profound and startling sagacity’. The trouble, as Bertram Woosterly discovered while staring glumly at his blotter, was that the sagacity in question belonged entirely to young Finch in receivables, a mild cove who wouldn’t say boo to a particularly timid goose. Having shamelessly presented Finch’s scribbled notes as his own, Bertram now found that the boss’s hearty approbation sat upon his soul with all the breezy lightness of a suet pudding.
