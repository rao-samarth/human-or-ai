Reggie was looking at me with the sort of damp, pleading expression you see on a spaniel that has not only chewed the new slippers but has also buried them in the rose bed. “The long and the short of it, old horse,” he quavered, twisting a button on his waistcoat as if it were the master-switch for the entire electrical system, “is that you must pop along to Aunt Millicent and explain that the porcelain frog was, in fact, your idea of a jest.” A cold dread, not unlike the sort one gets when swallowing a fish-bone, began to trickle down my spine.
