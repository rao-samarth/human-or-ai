The mater’s eldest sister had fixed me with the sort of gimlet eye a master jeweller turns upon a dubious pearl, and the silence in the drawing-room had become thick enough to slice. She wanted to know precisely where young Reggie had got to on Tuesday, the very afternoon he was meant to be taking her Pekingese for its constitutional. A simple question on the surface, I grant you, but one fraught with the deadliest of perils. I, and I alone, knew that Reggie had at that moment been engaged in a highly unsanctioned motor-race against a chap with a handlebar moustache, an escapade that would, if revealed, cause the tap from which the young blighter’s allowance flowed to be turned off with a clang. The code of the Binghams is to stand by a pal in a tight spot, of course, but looking into the terrifying abyss of Aunt Mildred’s disapproval, I found my loyalties performing a rather ghastly sort of Charleston. To squeal was unthinkable; to invent a plausible fib on the spur of the moment, with that eagle eye upon me, seemed a feat worthy of a seasoned conjurer.
