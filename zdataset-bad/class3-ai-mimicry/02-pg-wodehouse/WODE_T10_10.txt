It’s a rum business, my companion mused, stirring his tea with the air of a biologist examining a particularly tricky microbe. We were observing a young fellow at the next table, a chap with a chin you could have skied down, holding his elderly aunt in what appeared to be a state of rapt hypnosis. He isn’t lying, you see—not in the common-or-garden, out-and-out whopper sense of the word. Rather, he presents the raw material of what occurred as if it were a lump of clay, to be artfully squashed here and elegantly elongated there, until the finished article bears only a passing, cousinly resemblance to the original lump. The inconvenient bits, the angles that might cause a thoughtful frown to crease the listener’s brow
