The chap opposite me in the Dover train, a bustling little cove all bristling with ledgers and urgent-looking telegrams, had the sort of face you see on a man who has decided to Get On In The World. I seemed to recall him from my formative years as a perfectly amiable egg, the sort who could take a joke and even, on occasion, make one. But the intervening decade or so had apparently been spent swapping out his soul, piece by piece, for stock options and a seat on some bally board or other. He spoke of 'leveraging assets' and 'synergising opportunities' with a ghastly, pious gleam
