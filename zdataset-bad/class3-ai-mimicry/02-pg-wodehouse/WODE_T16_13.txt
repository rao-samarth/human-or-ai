There she was, old Prudence, gazing at me with that expectant look she gets—a sort of customs-official-inspecting-your-luggage-for-contraband-parrots expression—waiting for me to agree to bell the proverbial cat; the cat in this instance being her formidable father. It’s in these tight corners, you know, when a fellow is supposed to puff out his chest and utter a crisp 'Tally-ho!', that my own particular chest has a tendency to do the precise opposite, rather like a punctured concertina. The ganglions seize up, the tongue cleaves to the roof of the mouth as if it were a postage stamp of a particularly adhesive nature, and the vital spark simply clocks off for a brief holiday. A better man, a chap with more iron in his soul and less blancmange in his spinal column, would have said ‘Right-ho!’ and squared his jaw. All I could manage, however, was a sort of low, gurgling sound, the kind of noise one associates with a faulty drainpipe summoning up its last reserves before giving up the ghost entirely.
