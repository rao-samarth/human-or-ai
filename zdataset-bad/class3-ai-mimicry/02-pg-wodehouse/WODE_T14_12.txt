One likes to think one is the captain of one’s soul, the master of one’s own twopenny-ha’penny, but a fellow has his doubts when planted in the thick of a London afternoon. My objective, you see, was perfectly straightforward: to pop across the street and secure a rather promising spotted cravat I’d spied in a shop window. Yet, the moment I squared my shoulders for the expedition, a veritable maelstrom of omnibuses, hansom cabs, and determined-looking women with parcels erupted before me. A sudden downpour then added a certain dampness to the proceedings, propelling me sideways under an awning with all the dignity of a dropped éclair.
