Watching the drizzled fields slide by the carriage window, a fellow can’t help but get to thinking about the general scheme of things. One finds oneself wondering if one ever really steers the bally ship at all, or if one is merely a passenger, handed a ticket at the outset with a destination already printed in irritatingly small type. You make what seems a frightfully important decision—to take the 3:15 instead of the 4:30, say, or to wear the mauve tie rather than the spotted one—and you puff out the chest, feeling like a master of your own destiny.
