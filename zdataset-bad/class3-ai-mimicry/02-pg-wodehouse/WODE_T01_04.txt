I suppose, if a fellow were to take a poll of the local buttercups, the consensus would be that I had well and truly arrived. There had been a frightful amount of striving and pushing to secure this particular patch of greenery—a period during which I’d fancied myself a Napoleon of commerce, all grim jaw and decisive telegrams. The snag, which the old grey matter had conveniently overlooked during the heat of the chase, was that in breasting the tape, I’d had to give poor old Reggie a rather nasty jab with the elbow.
