There is a certain species of mental fever, a singular fixation of the intellect, that can seize upon an otherwise thoroughly sound cove and turn his entire spiritual fabric inside out, rather like a sock. One saw it at its most virulent in the case of old Godfrey Plummett of Upper Smattering-on-the-Wold, who, having determined to carry off the prize for the year's most substantial marrow, had begun to exhibit a curious reallocation of his priorities. No longer did he potter amongst his prize-winning roses; indeed, the poor blossoms were positively drooping from neglect.
