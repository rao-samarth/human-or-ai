The clatter of ceramic mugs on saucers nearly drowned out his whisper. “It's a rounding error, Maya, that's all. We adjust the third-quarter projections, land the contract, and nobody ever has to know.” Her coffee sat untouched, growing cold. “A rounding error?” Her voice rose just enough to make a man at the next table glance up from his phone before pretending to be engrossed in it again. Ben leaned forward, his own voice a low, urgent hiss. “It’s the difference between making payroll and closing our doors for good. Are you really going to let some abstract principle sink us when the solution is right there?” She looked at him, truly looked at him, and saw a stranger where her partner used to be. “That isn't a principle, Ben, it's the law. It’s the only thing that made us different. I thought we were building something real, not just finding a shortcut to a bigger payday.”
