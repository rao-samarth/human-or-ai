"You forwarded my draft to the entire executive team with the note 'a few minor tweaks needed before we can consider this viable'?" Sarah’s voice was dangerously quiet, a stark contrast to the open-plan office hum around them. She wasn't yelling; it was worse than that. Mark’s brow furrowed in genuine confusion. "I was just trying to manage their expectations," he stammered, his own volume dropping to match hers. "I thought it was a strong start, and I wanted to protect it from being shot down for not being a final version." A humorless laugh escaped her. "Protect it? Mark, you positioned my two weeks of work as a rough concept needing your final approval. You made me look like an amateur who couldn't deliver. Did you really think that’s how I would interpret your 'help'?" He opened his mouth to argue, to explain the logic he saw so clearly in his head, but the look on her face stopped him. It wasn't about logic anymore. The trust that had held their project partnership together had just evaporated, leaving only the cold, unworkable space between their desks.
