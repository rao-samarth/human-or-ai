The offer hung in the air between them, thick and slick as the exhaust from a passing bus. It was everything Liam had been working toward: the corner office, the recognition, the kind of security his parents never had. All he had to do was agree that the initial concept was his alone. Across the bustling intersection, he could see Anya checking her watch, her back to a graffiti-covered newsstand, completely oblivious. The man in the expensive suit was still talking, his voice a low, persuasive hum against the city's cacophony. It would be so easy. Just a small revision of history, a silent omission that would pave his way forward while leaving her behind on a project that would now go nowhere. A car horn blared, and Anya looked up, her eyes scanning the crowd for him. He felt the weight of her simple, unquestioning expectation settle upon him, heavier than any skyscraper's shadow.
