He traced the grain of the floorboards with his eyes. “It wasn’t just that you were certain,” she said, her voice soft but without any warmth. “It was that you needed everyone to see you be certain.” Each confident prediction he had made over dinner parties and phone calls now felt like a small, sharp stone in his throat. He had built a pedestal for himself out of his own words, a high and glittering thing meant for an audience. And now, in the quiet of the room, there was nothing left but the long drop down. He couldn’t look at her, could only stare at the floor, as if he might find some solid ground there that had vanished from everywhere else. The silence that followed her observation was worse than any accusation, heavy with the weight of her being right all along.
