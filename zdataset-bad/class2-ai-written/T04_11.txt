The afternoon traffic on Sixth Avenue was a roaring, impatient river, but inside the small cafe, a pocket of silence had formed around their table. Alex had laid out the offer letter like a peace treaty, its crisp letterhead a stark contrast to the coffee-stained wood. Maya said nothing. She just kept tracing the rim of her ceramic mug, a slow, deliberate circle that seemed to contain all the things she couldn't say about the company logo printed at the top—the very same one from her termination notice six months ago. “It’s a life-changing amount of money,” Alex finally managed, his voice thin against the backdrop of sirens and horns. He hated how it sounded, like an excuse. Maya finally looked up from her cup, her gaze steady and clear. She didn’t look angry, just tired. “I know,” she said, and that simple acknowledgment felt heavier and more damning than any accusation she could have possibly made. The city rushed on outside, utterly indifferent.
