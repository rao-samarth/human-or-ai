“Don’t open that here,” Maria hissed, her eyes darting to the couple at the next table who were now pretending to be fascinated by their lattes. Mark ignored her, unrolling the blueprint across their small cafe table, coffee cups rattling. “I told you, it’s irrelevant. The project was cancelled three years ago.” He stabbed a finger at a section of the schematic, his knuckles white. “But don’t you see? The ventilation shafts don’t line up with the final build specs. Someone signed off on this, Maria. Someone knew.” His voice was rising, a raw, frayed edge to it that made her skin crawl. He wasn't just investigating a defunct construction plan; he was re-excavating a grave, convinced a ghost was still inside. People were staring openly now, and all she wanted was to disappear into the cheap upholstery of the booth. He leaned closer, his pupils dilated, the scent of stale coffee on his breath. “We just have to find the original procurement order. I know it’s in the city archive. We go tonight.” It wasn’t a question.
