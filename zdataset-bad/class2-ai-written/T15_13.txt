His fingers tightened on her elbow, a silent, desperate plea amidst the polite murmur of the gala. “Clara, you’re making a scene.” She didn’t even glance at him, her entire being focused on a woman in a sequined dress standing near the ice sculpture. For her, the clinking glasses and muted orchestra had faded into a dull, unimportant hum. “Don’t you see it?” she whispered, her voice a low, ragged thing that scraped against the elegant atmosphere. “Every time he looks away from her, she smirks. Just for a second. It’s a message, a little victory lap just for me.” He followed her stare and saw nothing but a stranger idly chatting with a friend. “No one is smirking,” he insisted, his own voice strained with a mixture of fear and embarrassment. “Please, let’s just step outside.” Her grip on his forearm was suddenly iron, her nails digging into the fine wool of his jacket. “No. I’m not letting her win this time.”
