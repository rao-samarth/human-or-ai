The only sound in the dark kitchen was the hum of the refrigerator. Liam set his glass down on the counter, the small click unnaturally loud in the silence. “So, David was certainly impressed with you tonight,” he said, not looking at her. Chloe lowered her book, her brow furrowed slightly. “He was just being polite, Liam.” He let out a short, humorless breath. “No, it was more than that. The way he hung on every word, you’d think you single-handedly saved the company.” He finally turned, his gaze sharp. “He just seems to find you so… fascinating.” A deep sigh escaped her lips, the sound full of weariness. “We were talking about quarterly reports. Please don’t do this again.” But he couldn’t stop replaying the image of them laughing together, a tight knot forming in his chest where a simple, easy confidence should have been.
