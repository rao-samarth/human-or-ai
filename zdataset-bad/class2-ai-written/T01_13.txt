The bus hummed through the dark, a low drone that vibrated up from the floor into Liam's seat. His phone screen cast a pale, clinical light on his face. The message was from the program coordinator: a last-minute spot had opened up for the fellowship overseas, the one he’d been waitlisted for, the one that would change everything. The catch was buried in the third sentence. He would have to leave tomorrow morning, meaning he’d miss his grandfather’s funeral completely. He glanced at the rumpled black suit hanging in its plastic bag from the overhead hook. He’d promised he would be there to give the eulogy, to be the strong one for his mother. All he had to do was type a simple, one-word reply. His thumb hovered over the glowing screen, the entire weight of his future seeming to press down on that single point of contact while the empty highway signs flashed past in the window, each one a ghost of a different path.
