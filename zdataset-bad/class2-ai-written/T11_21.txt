He keeps talking, so sure of his own generosity, and I just focus on the condensation ring my glass is leaving on the polished wood. He waves his hand as if it’s nothing—just a simple call to his father’s old partner, a little nudge to get my application to the top of the pile. He can’t understand why I’m hesitating. For him, the world is a map of connections, a network where a favor is a simple currency everyone trades. For me, it’s a long, anonymous queue. What he’s offering isn't a shortcut; it's a completely different route, one that isn't even visible from where I'm standing. He thinks he's opening a door for me, but it feels more like he's just pointing out the impenetrable wall that has always separated his world from mine.
