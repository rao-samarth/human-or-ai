The cursor blinked, a rhythmic pulse in the dark study. Three a.m. Again. I clicked through the folders, dragging Marc’s final drafts into the encrypted archive—a clean cut, a surgical removal. This was the final step before the presentation that would secure everything I’d worked for. I told myself it was just business, a necessary move on the board. He was too slow, too sentimental for this kind of climb. The promotion was mine; I had earned it with these sleepless nights and a spine of steel. A sudden buzz from my phone shattered the silence. It was a text from an unknown number. Just one image: a screenshot of my original proposal, timestamped, with Marc's name clearly listed as a co-author. My blood went cold. The frantic clicking stopped. I stared at the screen, not at the files I was hiding, but at my own reflection, and the victory I had imagined felt like ash in my mouth.
