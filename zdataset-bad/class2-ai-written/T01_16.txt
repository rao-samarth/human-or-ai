The congratulatory calls had finally stopped, leaving a silence that felt heavier than sound. Elias stared at the framed photo on his desk, the one from his first campaign. A younger man smiled back, full of unyielding principles and a belief that he could change things without being changed himself. What a fool. He ran a hand over his face, the exhaustion settling deep in his bones. He had won. He held the power he had craved for years, a victory built on backroom deals and promises he knew were lies. The smiling face in the photo seemed to mock him, a ghost of the man he’d sacrificed piece by piece for this hollow, lonely triumph. The house was cold, and for the first time in a long time, he felt truly alone with what he’d become.
