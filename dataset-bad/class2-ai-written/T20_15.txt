The canvas tote bag, emblazoned with a hand-printed “Community First!” slogan, dug into her shoulder as she watched the last vendor at the street market pack up his hand-carved wooden birds. A sharp pang of guilt. Sarah’s party started in less than an hour, and she’d completely forgotten a gift. Her gaze drifted across the bustling avenue to the sterile, automatic doors of a massive chain superstore, its fluorescent lights promising a soulless but efficient solution. She hesitated for a moment, the protest chants from earlier that afternoon still echoing faintly in her memory. But what was one small, anonymous purchase, really? An act of pragmatism. Sarah would never know the difference, and besides, didn't those retail workers deserve a living wage, too? She adjusted the bag, a small, satisfied smile touching her lips, and confidently strode toward the glowing entrance.
