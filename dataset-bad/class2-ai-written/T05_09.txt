Just say it, Elias. Stop staring at the floor like it holds some secret answer.” Maya’s voice was dangerously quiet, cutting through the late-night stillness of their small kitchen. He finally looked up, his expression hollowed out. "My sister phoned this morning. The business isn't just struggling; it's failing. They need me to come back." Each word landed like a stone. "For good?" she whispered, the question hanging between them, fragile and sharp. "What about the lease we just signed? The life we planned right here, in this city?" He ran a hand through his hair, the gesture frayed with exhaustion. "They're my family, Maya. It was a promise I made long before I ever met you, that if they truly needed me, I'd be there. I can't build our future on the ruins of theirs." The choice wasn't about what he wanted, but about which vow he could bear to break.
