The low rumble of the approaching subway vibrated up through the concrete. Ahead, where the crowd was thickest near the platform edge, a man suddenly folded, his legs giving way without a sound as he crumpled to the grimy floor. A small, empty space instantly opened around him as people shuffled back, their faces a brief flicker of alarm hardening into studied indifference. A few phones were raised. A cold knot tightened in her stomach; every instinct screamed to stay anonymous, to not be the one to get involved. What if she did the wrong thing? The train’s headlamp was now a bright, fast-approaching star in the tunnel’s throat, but still, no one moved. Then, with a sharp exhale she hadn’t realized she was holding, she dropped her messenger bag and pushed forward, breaking the unspoken pact of the bystanders. The screech of the train’s brakes was deafening as she knelt by his side.
