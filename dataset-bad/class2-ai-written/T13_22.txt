My uncle held the wine glass by its stem, an orb of deep crimson catching the lamplight as he pontificated to the captive audience around the dinner table. He spoke of sun-drenched Tuscan hills and the subtle notes of leather and cherry, building a detailed narrative for the bottle he had so generously brought. He took a final, authoritative sip and announced it, with a flourish, as a rare 2016 Chianti Classico. A small silence followed, broken only by his niece Sarah, home from a season working a harvest in Australia. "It's a nice one," she said, her voice gentle, "but it's actually a Shiraz from the Barossa Valley. See the kangaroo on the cork?" All eyes darted from my uncle's suddenly pale face to the small, damning marsupial he'd proudly displayed beside the bottle. The grand edifice of his expertise, constructed over a decade of holidays, crumbled into dust in a single, quiet moment. He said nothing, simply placing his glass down with a precision that betrayed the tremor in his hand.
