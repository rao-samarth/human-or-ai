He had held the map, not because he was the best navigator, but because it made him the leader. For three days, he’d confidently ordered coffees and paid for train tickets using a handful of carefully practiced phrases, earning the impressed glances of his companions. This was his moment, guiding them through a labyrinth of unnamed alleys to a restaurant he’d found online. When the proprietor greeted them with a rapid, smiling burst of local dialect, he felt a surge of satisfaction. This was authenticity. He simply needed to respond. But the sounds meant nothing; they were a wall he couldn't climb. His friends watched, waiting for their brilliant guide to translate. He could confess now, laugh it off as a bluff gone too far. Instead, a stubborn vanity seized him. He smiled back, a wide, knowing smile, and offered his most polished phrase—a polite thank you. The proprietor’s face fell, his warmth vanishing into a mask of pure confusion. The silence that followed was immense, and in it, he felt himself shrink from a worldly expert into a small, foolish tourist.
