He adjusted his tie, the silk a whisper against his starched collar, and offered her a tight, camera-ready smile. "We should mingle. The Barclays are over by the fountain, and you know how important this is." His hand rested on her arm, a gesture that looked like affection to anyone glancing their way but felt like a vice to her. She didn't pull away, instead turning her head just enough so her next words were aimed directly at his ear, a venomous secret amidst the cheerful din of the fundraiser. "You are so good at this," she breathed, her own smile feeling like a crack in porcelain. "Playing the part of the devoted husband, the benevolent pillar of the community. They see a perfect portrait. I see the empty frame you've become, the man who polishes the glass so no one notices there's nothing left behind it. So go on, mingle. Sell them the fantasy."
