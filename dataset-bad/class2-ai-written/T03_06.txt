The house was silent, save for the low hum of the refrigerator from the kitchen. At his desk, the spreadsheet’s columns offered two distinct futures. One path promised a significant quarterly profit, achieved through a supply chain adjustment that was technically legal but deeply unethical. The other maintained the current, more expensive standard, offering only modest, predictable growth. The money was tempting, a figure that would earn him accolades from the board, but it came with the risk of exposure—a single investigative article that could undo decades of careful work. He considered not the immediate gain, but the intangible value of the way people spoke his name in the industry. That particular currency, painstakingly accumulated, was not something he was willing to gamble, no matter the potential reward. With a final click, he deleted the file containing the proposal. The financial loss was immediate and certain, a steep price for something no balance sheet could ever truly measure.
