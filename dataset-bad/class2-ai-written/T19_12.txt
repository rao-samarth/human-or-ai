The perfect evening? Is that what you called it?” Sarah gestured vaguely at the immaculate living room, its shadows deep in the single lamp’s glow. “Mark, look at this place. Every cushion is fluffed. Every book on that shelf is for show, arranged by color because you saw it online.” She took a step closer, her voice dropping to a harsh whisper. “Your laugh tonight—the one you use for your boss—was so polished I almost believed it myself. We put on such a good performance for them, didn't we? The happy, successful couple in their flawless home.” She let out a short, bitter laugh that held no humor. “Don’t you ever get tired of living on a set?
