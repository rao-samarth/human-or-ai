He wouldn't look at her. That was the first, sharpest cut. He just kept staring at a fixed point on the conference table, speaking in that hollow, detached tone about "restructuring accountability" and how the board needed a clear line of responsibility for the data breach. Every sterile, corporate word was a stone being laid on the wall between them. She could still picture them six months ago, sharing a cheap bottle of wine on her apartment floor, laughing about these exact kinds of meetings and promising they’d always have each other’s backs against the suits. Now he was one of them. It wasn’t until he mentioned the "new leadership opportunities" opening up that the final piece clicked into place, cold and heavy in her gut. The silence that followed his careful monologue wasn't empty; it was filled with the ghost of every late-night session, every shared risk, every single promise he was now methodically erasing to save himself.
