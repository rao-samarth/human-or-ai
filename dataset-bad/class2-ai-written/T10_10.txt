The projection on the wall glowed with the quarterly review slide, and David was walking the executive team through it with practiced ease. He pointed to a graph showing a steady, optimistic rise in client retention. A knot tightened in my stomach. I knew the real numbers; I had run them myself just yesterday. His version conveniently omitted the sharp downturn from the last three weeks after the system update fiasco, a detail he’d brushed off as a “temporary data anomaly” in our prep meeting. He was framing a near-disaster as a minor hiccup on the road to success. Now, he paused, turning to me with a slight smile that didn’t reach his eyes. “Anything to add, Maya?” he asked, the question hanging in the air like a dare. The CEO looked at me expectantly, and all I could feel was the frantic pulse in my wrist and the weight of the truth lodged in my throat.
