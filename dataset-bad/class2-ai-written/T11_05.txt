The five minutes before the weekly brief always felt the longest. Alistair was describing the trouble he’d had sourcing the right kind of reclaimed timber for his boathouse. He spoke with a casual weariness, as if this were a universal struggle, and the other managers nodded with knowing sympathy. I sat nursing my lukewarm coffee, tracing the rim of the paper cup with my thumb. It occurred to me, not for the first time, that there were two kinds of conversations permitted in this room. Theirs, filled with the acceptable anxieties of property and travel, were considered connection. Mine, were I to mention the stress of a looming rent increase or a car that wouldn't start, would just be noise. It wasn’t a rule written anywhere, of course. It was just in the air, thick and still as the recycled atmosphere from the vents.
