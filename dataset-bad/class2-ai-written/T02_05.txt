Trevor managed a serene smile as his girlfriend’s mother, Carol, extolled the virtues of her five-bean casserole. On the outside, he was the picture of plant-based enlightenment, nodding sagely about the texture of the quinoa and the earthiness of the lentils. He’d been playing this part for three hours. Inside, however, a primal hunger was staging a coup. His inner caveman had taken over the control room of his brain and was now using a laser pointer to highlight the glistening, herb-crusted roast chicken at the far end of the table. Carol wouldn't notice if one little drumstick went missing, would she? He could create a diversion. A sudden, passionate speech about the ethical sourcing of tempeh, perhaps? As he contemplated faking a swoon just to be carried past the carving station, he realized with a jolt of horror that Carol’s tiny chihuahua was now growling at him from under the table, its beady eyes fixed on his with an unnerving, meat-detecting intelligence.
