From my table near the window, I couldn't help but overhear them. The man stirred his cold coffee, his gaze fixed on the swirling liquid. "I just figured you'd call me back," he said, his voice barely a whisper. "The missed call *was* the message. It meant I was there, waiting." The woman across from him shook her head slowly, her expression unreadable in the dim light of the late-night cafe. "How could I know that? A missed call is just a missed call. You always said to text if it was actually important." He looked up then, and the faint hope in his eyes seemed to extinguish itself. "But this was different. I thought you would just know this was different." A heavy silence settled between them, more profound than the gentle clatter of dishes from the kitchen. It was the sound of a chasm opening, one that started with a simple, unspoken expectation and was now far too wide to bridge.
