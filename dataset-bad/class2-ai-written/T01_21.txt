The key slid into the lock with a quiet click, but the silence inside the house was louder. He stood in the dim hallway, the faint glow of the neighbor’s security light striping the floorboards, and listened to the sound of his own breathing. Every light was off, every door was closed. He had won. The deal was signed, the rivals were crushed, and the entire ascent he had dreamed of was now simply the ground beneath his feet. But as he loosened his tie, he felt a profound hollowness where triumph should have been. He thought of the missed calls from his brother, the string of unanswered texts, the anniversary dinner he’d cancelled with a brisk, unapologetic email. Each step he took up the stairs echoed like a footstep in an empty cathedral, a monument built for a congregation of one. He had climbed the mountain, only to find the summit cold, barren, and utterly alone.
