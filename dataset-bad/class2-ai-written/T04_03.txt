“So you’re actually thinking about taking their deal,” Elena’s voice was dangerously quiet, cutting through the low hum of the refrigerator. Marcus stared at the floor, unable to meet her gaze. The offer had been so simple, so clean: a quiet exit, his record wiped, a future free from the consequences they had both sworn to face together. He could just walk away. "They said my sister would be safe," he finally managed to say, the words tasting like ash. "They promised no one would ever bother her again." Elena let out a sharp, bitter laugh that held no humor. "Your sister? What about the promises you made to me, standing right here in this kitchen? We knew the risks from the beginning. We agreed it was all or nothing, for both of us." She took a step closer, forcing him to look up into her betrayed eyes. "Now, when the fire gets hot, you’re the only one who gets an escape hatch. Tell me, Marcus, how much was I worth?"
