The roar of the protest in Civic Plaza was a physical force, pressing in on all sides. On the phone, her editor’s voice was tinny but firm. “Just run the quotes from the victims,” he said. “The part about their prior business dealings with the developer only muddies the water.” She looked out over the sea of faces, earnest and angry, holding signs painted with the very quotes he wanted her to use. They deserved to know the full picture, didn’t they? The messy, complicated reality of it all? Yet, he was right about one thing: the simplified story was cleaner, more powerful. It would give these people a clear villain and a surge of righteous energy that the complete, ambiguous truth would only dilute. Her thumb hovered over the delete key on her draft, the cursor blinking patiently at the start of the inconvenient paragraph.
