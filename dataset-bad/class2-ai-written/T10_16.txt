The only light came from the single streetlamp outside, filtering through the blinds to cast long, distorted shadows across the living room floor. He watched how the familiar armchair became a hunched, waiting creature, its true form lost in the exaggerated projection. It wasn't a lie, he thought, not in the strictest sense. The chair was there, and so was the light. The deception was in the angle, in the deliberate positioning that emphasized one edge while plunging the rest into an absolute, unquestioned darkness. The most powerful narratives are constructed this way, not from whole cloth, but from carefully selected, brightly lit fragments.
