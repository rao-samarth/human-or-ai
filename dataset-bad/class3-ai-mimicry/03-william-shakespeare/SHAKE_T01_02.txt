But see you not, good sir,” the young man cried, his gesture taking in the placid stream and the grazing sheep, “how this dull peace doth mock a spirit made for greater things? I would build a name that stands a mountain to this molehill life.” The old man smiled, a sad and knowing thing, and laid a hand upon the boy’s trembling shoulder. “Aye, a mountain casts a mighty shadow, lad, and many a poor soul is lost in its long shade. For what good is a name spoken with awe in the mouths of strangers, if it turns to ash upon the tongue of thine own conscience? Each stone thou placest to raise thyself higher is taken from some other ground, perchance from the garden of thy simple joy or the foundation of thy virtue. Climb, if thy heart commands it so, but know that the air grows thin at such a height, and many a man hath found at the peak naught but a chilling wind and the echo of all he forsook to get there.
