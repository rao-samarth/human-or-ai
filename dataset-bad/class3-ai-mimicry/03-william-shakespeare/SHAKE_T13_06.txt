He stood amidst the glittering throng, his voice a brazen trumpet sounding forth his own great cleverness, his countenance a mirror to his own conceit. Forsooth, he thought himself a very sun, about which all lesser planets must needs turn, and so he raised his cup to make a jest. He began a verse, a trifle of his own making, so certain of its art that he smiled ere the final word was spoke. But then his tongue, that traitorous servant, did catch upon the phrase, and what should have been a diamond of wit came forth a lump of common mud. A stillness first, a horrid, empty space wherein his folly echoed; then, like the patter of a sudden rain, a ripple of mirth did spread across the hall. His face, which once did shine with self-regard, became a burning map of his own shame, and he saw in every smiling eye the ghost of his glorious opinion, now quite dead.
