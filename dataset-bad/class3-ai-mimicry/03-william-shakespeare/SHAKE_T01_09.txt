He strode through the clamorous market, his fine silks a stark rebuke to the rags of common folk, and pondered what poison a man must drink to purchase such a state. For this high station, what had he not ventured? He had made a villain's pact with silence when truth cried out to be spoken; he had bartered honest fellowship for a whisper in a great lord's ear. Each cobblestone did seem to mock the lightness of his purse, for his own soul, in equal measure, had grown so leaden and heavy. O, to be thus raised, yet feel so utterly cast down!
