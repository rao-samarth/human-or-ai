The great hall glimmers with a thousand points of light, and music, soft as a lover's sigh, doth weave its spell amongst the velvet-clad assembly. Yet my soul finds no comfort in this gilded cage, for I see not true joy but its painted counterfeit upon every smiling visage. Each courtly bow, each whispered pleasantry, seems but a practiced part in some weary, unending play, a fine veneer o'er thoughts of darker hue. The jewels upon a lady's throat may shine with borrowed fire, but what of the heart that beats so faintly 'neath their cold and heavy weight? Men speak of fortune and of high estate, their voices full of hollow pride; still, I perceive in their bright eyes the shadow of a spirit starved for aught of honest substance. We are but puppets in a grand design, our faces plastered with a pleasing mask, and we call this polished, empty pageant our very life.
