Forsooth, what sudden tempest did break upon that market street, where all before was but the common hum of commerce! A goose, a great white tyrant of a bird with a neck like a serpent and a most villainous eye, had won its freedom from a poulterer’s crate. And there stood Master Quibble, a scrivener of some small repute, his very soul caught betwixt two warring fears: the one, of the creature’s furious beak, and the other, a far greater dread, of the jeering laughter should he make a public fool of himself. To give chase, and risk a tumble in the mire for all the world to see? Or to stand frozen, a coward conquered by a common fowl? A moment he did dither, his face a mask of tragic indecision, ere he cast his dignity to the winds, hitched up his robes, and with a most unmanly yelp, plunged headlong after the feathered fugitive.
