You gentlemen ask why I refused the promotion, and I shall be plain. Mr Davies and I had shared a clerk’s desk for a dozen years; in that time, a man comes to know the very constitution of his neighbour’s character. The offer from the Board was, I grant you, a tempting one, for it promised not only a private office but the very seniority for which I had long striven. Yet, the condition was that I attest to certain deficiencies in his accounts—deficiencies I knew to be fabricated entirely to secure his dismissal. A man’s good name, however, is not a trifle to be bartered for advancement. The quiet faith which exists between two men who have laboured side-by-side through difficulty is a commodity of a far rarer sort
