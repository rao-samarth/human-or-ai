You ask if I felt no trepidation, and I must confess that a most abject and primal terror did indeed grip me. Any man who claims otherwise in such a circumstance is either a charlatan or a fool. The shriek of tortured metal, the lurch of the floor beneath one’s feet—it is a sensation calculated to freeze the very marrow in one’s bones. For one long, dreadful second, as the forward carriage hung upon that spider-web of steel above the chasm, I was mastered by a base instinct to do nothing, to simply cling to my own small patch of safety and await my fate. Yet it was the sight of the faces in the compartment behind, pale and aghast in the flickering gaslight, that broke the spell.
