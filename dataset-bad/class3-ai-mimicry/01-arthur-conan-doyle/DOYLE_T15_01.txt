He leaned forward across the mahogany table, his eyes burning with a light that was altogether unnatural. “You do not comprehend, my dear fellow,” he hissed, his voice a dry rasp, “but the pattern is there—unmistakable! Every tick of that infernal clock, every shadow that falls upon the pavement, it all speaks of his foul design.” His hands, I noted, were clenched so tightly upon the armrests that the knuckles shone white as bone. For a fortnight now, this singular notion had consumed him entirely, transforming a man of hitherto sound judgment into a feverish creature who saw conspiracy in the most mundane of trifles. He spent his nights pacing the study until dawn broke, charting coincidences that could exist only in the frenzied confines of his own mind. I endeavoured to reason with him, but it was like speaking to a man in the grips of a powerful delirium; my words were but a distant murmur against the clamour of his all-consuming thought.
