He believes, I am quite certain, that these late hours have rendered him transparent, that I can see past the genial host to the fretful speculator beneath. A trifling error, for he perceives only the character I have so painstakingly assembled for his benefit—a simple confidant, whose counsel is as reliable as the morning post. What he fails to grasp is that his own carefully maintained disguise is but a child’s pasteboard mask compared to the one I inhabit daily. He worries over the fate of some paltry investment, whilst my own game is played for stakes he could scarcely comprehend, and still he claps me on the shoulder and calls me his most trusted friend. One must, after all, select the proper attire for the role one is to perform, and my own costume of unassuming loyalty is, if I may say so, a masterwork of its kind.
