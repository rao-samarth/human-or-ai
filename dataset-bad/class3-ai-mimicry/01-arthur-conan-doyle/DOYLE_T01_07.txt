He pulled me from the jostling pedestrian stream near the Exchange, his grip upon my arm a thing of iron. “Look upon it, then!” he hissed, his voice a low counterpoint to the city’s roar, gesturing with a trembling hand towards the towering new shipping headquarters. “There stands the pinnacle of your life’s work—a structure whose foundations, if men but knew the truth, rest entirely upon the ruin of a trusting associate. You speak of enterprise and of the future, yet I see only the ghost of poor Davies’s signature on a document you knew to be his undoing. You have gained the whole of the Thames’s traffic, it is true; but tell me, Clarkson, what precisely remains of the man I once knew to be honourable? The applause you hear from your board of directors is but the hollow echo of a soul you have willingly bartered away for profit.”
