"You see the mud upon his boots, the tremor in his hand, and you cry ‘Villain!’ without a moment’s pause for rational thought! Pray, what of the facts? The mud is London clay, slick and grey, whilst the scene of the crime was upon the chalky soil of the downs—a distinction a child could make! His tremor? It is not the agitation of a guilty conscience, but the exhaustion of a man who has run half the length of the city to plead his innocence. You have been so dazzled by the obvious, so completely taken in by a carefully staged deception, that you have failed to perceive the simple, glaring truth of the matter. This entire performance was laid for your benefit, my good sir, and you have played your part with a most lamentable predictability. Now, let us cease this folly and speak instead of the man who truly benefits from such a convenient misdirection.
