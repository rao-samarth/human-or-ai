So you mean to tell me this is about what’s *right*? I have seen that particular brand of piety before, and it always has the same sour smell of personal grievance dressed up for Sunday meeting. A man gets it into his head that he has been appointed by Providence itself to settle a score, and he convinces himself his own hot-blooded malice is a tool of some grand and level-handed celestial court; he mistakes the fever in his gut for a holy fire. What he wants isn’t a balanced scale—he wants the other fellow’s side of the scale broken clear off and stomped into the mud.
