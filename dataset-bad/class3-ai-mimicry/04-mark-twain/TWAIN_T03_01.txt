Bartholomew looked a fright, paler than a bucket of whitewash and twice as shaky. He’d gone and printed Judge Abernathy’s prize hog auction under the headline “Fine Swine & Finer Swindle,” a simple slip of the finger that had the whole town in stitches and the Judge in a towering fury. The boy wanted to blame the ink, the press, the infernal humidity—anything but his own carelessness. “Son,” I told him, leaning against the cold press, “you can let the foreman sack you for incompetence, and folks will forget it by Tuesday. But if you let him sack you for being a liar, well, that’s a stain that don’t wash out with soap nor salvation.
