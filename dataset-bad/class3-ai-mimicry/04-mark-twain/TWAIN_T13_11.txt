We had a fellow in our office, a bookkeeper by the name of Abernathy, who considered his own handwriting to be a direct communication from some higher power, delivered by way of his own sainted fingers. He would flourish his pen like a conductor’s baton and produce letters so perfect they looked more like engravings than honest script, and he took no small amount of pains to ensure everyone noticed. The rest of us, with our common chicken-scratch, were meant to look upon his ledger as a thing of beauty, and we mostly obliged him for the sake of a quiet life. Then came the day of the annual report—a document of immense and tedious importance. Abernathy was tasked with the title page, a job he approached with the solemnity of a high priest.
