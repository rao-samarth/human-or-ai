He sat there, perfectly still, and repeated his assertion that the telegram had arrived on a Tuesday. Now, a man might let such a trifling inaccuracy pass, for the sake of peace and continued commerce; but in the set of his jaw, I perceived something more than a simple error of recollection. He was not merely stating a fact as he saw it; he was, in his quiet way, accusing me of a fundamental untrustworthiness, as if a man who could misremember a day could just as easily miscalculate a column of figures. The argument, you see, was no longer about the day of the week—a thing of no consequence whatsoever—but had become a solemn referendum on my character, and from such a high court there is seldom a favorable verdict. The silence that followed was heavier than any ledger, for we both understood that a partnership built on mutual confidence had just been declared bankrupt over a difference of twenty-four hours.
