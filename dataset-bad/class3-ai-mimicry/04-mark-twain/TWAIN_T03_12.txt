You ain’t truly fixing to set a whole barrel of your own merchandise on fire, are you, Silas?” Jebediah squinted at the cask as if it had personally insulted him. Silas Croft, whose face was already a thundercloud of indignation, only wiped his hands on his apron with a grim finality. “That Parson Higgins has spread it clear to the county line that my whiskey is mostly creek water and disappointment, and I'll not stand for it. A man's good name, Jebediah, is the only property the taxman can't get his hands on, and it's worth a sight more than a barrel of corn liquor.” He gave the bung a firm tap. “When this here spirits burns with a pure blue flame, right in the town square, the whole congregation will see Higgins for the meddling old crow he is. It's a costly experiment, I grant you, but a fellow can't very well distill a new character once the old one's gone sour.
