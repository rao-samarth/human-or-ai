I do believe the quality of a man’s spirit is best seen not when he is wronged, but when he is given the clear and open chance to wrong back. We were all packed in at the Founder’s Day Banquet, a whole flock of stuffed shirts and shimmering gowns, and the guest of honor was a railroad baron who’d made his fortune by means that weren’t always neighborly. And there, in a corner, sat Elias Finch—the very man whose small shipping business the baron had cheerfully crushed twenty years prior.
