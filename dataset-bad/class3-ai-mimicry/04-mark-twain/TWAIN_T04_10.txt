I saw Jedediah over by the feed store, chin-wagging with that slick-haired fellow from the city who smelled of cigars and easy money. Now, Jed and I had weathered seventeen years of mud, drought, and ornery livestock together; we’d shared the last of the salt pork one particularly grim winter and nursed each other’s children through the fever without a second thought given to the matter. But you put a man whose pockets are full of nothing but lint in the path of a gentleman promising a king’s ransom for his patch of dirt, and all that history has a tendency to get a little shaky.
