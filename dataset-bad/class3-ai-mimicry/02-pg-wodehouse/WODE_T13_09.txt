Algernon Finch-Potts fairly glided toward the prize-giving platform, his bosom swelling with a satisfaction so profound it threatened the integrity of his waistcoat buttons. The Silver Trowel for Superior Petunias awaited him, gleaming under the marquee lights, and he favoured the assembled gentry with a smile that was one part benevolent monarch and two parts Cheshire cat. It was this supreme confidence, this feeling of being Fortune’s favourite nephew, that prompted him to eschew the mundane business of simply walking up the three shallow steps.
