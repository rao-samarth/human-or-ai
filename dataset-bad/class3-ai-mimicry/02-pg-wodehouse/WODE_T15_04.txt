It had become increasingly clear to anyone with half an eye that young Reginald Fitzwilliam’s steering gear had developed a bit of a wobble. What had begun as a simple, wholesome quest for a particular sort of tortoiseshell button—the kind with the little amber flecks, you understand—had now blossomed, if that’s the word I want, into something altogether more sinister. He could now be observed, on any given Tuesday, weaving through the throng of Piccadilly like a rather desperate ferret, his gaze fixed not on the faces of his fellow man but on their midriffs. More than once, a startled shopper had found Reginald's nose practically grazing their waistcoat, the blighter having executed a swift, unannounced dive to inspect a promising specimen. This sort of thing, while no doubt excellent for the button-fancying soul, was doing his standing in the community no good at all, and had led to a sharp exchange with a stout party in a bowler hat who took exception to being scrutinised as if he were a prize turnip at a village fête.
