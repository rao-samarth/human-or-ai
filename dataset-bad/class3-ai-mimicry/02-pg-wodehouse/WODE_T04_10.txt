The large specimen with the unfortunate cauliflower ear, who had cornered poor Binky by the port-side railings, was pressing him for particulars as to my current whereabouts with a persistence one had to admire. It’s in these tight corners, you see, when the sea-spray is flying and a beefy party is making pointed inquiries, that you get the true measure of a cove. Some chaps will fold up like a cheap deckchair at the first sign of a squall, perfectly willing to jettison the cargo—said cargo being you—if it means a quiet life. For a moment there, as Binky’s Adam’s apple performed a rather energetic foxtrot, I confess I feared the worst. His entire aspect was that of a man who had just spotted a shark
