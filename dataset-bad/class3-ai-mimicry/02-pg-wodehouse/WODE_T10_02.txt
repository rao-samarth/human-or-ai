I was attempting to wrestle some sense from the morning’s stock prices—a task requiring the concentration of a meditating yogi—when the barrage began from the leather armchairs by the window. The booming tones belonged to old Fitzwilliam, who had young Hemlock cornered by the potted ferns, and was giving him the sort of detailed, one-sided account of recent events that would make a barrister blush. It seemed Hemlock’s new motor-car, while perfectly stationary, had been instrumental in causing a passing cyclist to wobble.
