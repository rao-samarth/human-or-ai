It’s all very well for these poet chaps to warble on about the heart wanting what it wants, but they seldom seem to factor in a chap’s aunt and her blasted petunias. Here was I, with my entire stock of earthly happiness hinging on a clandestine three o'clock rendezvous with Amelia by the duck pond—a girl whose smile, I might add, could coax a hermit crab from its shell—and what stood in my way? A sacred promise, that’s what.
