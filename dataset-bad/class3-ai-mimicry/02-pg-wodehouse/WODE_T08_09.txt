There's a queer, grey sort of quiet that settles over a place like this after one of its minor dramas has played out. One had watched the whole tedious business unfold with poor Blevins and his prize-winning pumpkins, a tale of unabashed skulduggery that had the entire village tut-tutting over its tea. And now, with the perpetrator having come a proper cropper—a truly spectacular fall from grace involving a faulty ladder and the vicarage pond—you’d think a certain communal cheer would be the order of the day. But it isn’t, you know. Blevins himself, when you pass him, merely nods, his shoulders stooped a little lower than before.
