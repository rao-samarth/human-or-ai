You know, Freddie," old Barmy Phipps was saying from the depths of his armchair, his voice carrying rather more than he probably intended, "one can’t help but feel a certain grim satisfaction that young Pilkington got his just deserts over that business with the dahlia competition. The man is, by any standard, an absolute pill. But then one’s conscience does a bit of a wobble, you see. There’s a world of difference, it seems to me, between the universe deciding to drop a flowerpot on a blighter’s head and young Reggie Bingham deliberately giving the universe a leg-up by—as I hear it—switching the fertilizer. It’s the active participation that gives one pause. It all becomes a bit murky then, doesn't it? One chap gets his comeuppance, which is all to the good, but the other chap has appointed himself chief constable, judge, and jury for the whole affair, and that, I submit, is a dashed rum state of things.
