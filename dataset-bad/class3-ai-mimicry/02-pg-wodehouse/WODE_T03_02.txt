It was, I perceived, one of those distinctly sticky moments when a chap’s entire standing in the clan hangs by a thread. The ghastly porcelain pug that had been a blot on the mantelpiece since the year dot now lay in a thousand pieces, and young Percival, its executioner, was gazing at the wreckage with the sort of frozen horror usually reserved for an unexpected tax audit. Worse, my Aunt Mildred was bearing down on the scene, her expression that of a hanging judge who has missed his lunch.
