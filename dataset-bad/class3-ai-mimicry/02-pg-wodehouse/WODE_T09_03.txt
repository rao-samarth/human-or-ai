The steady ticking of the grandfather clock in the hall, usually a sound as soothing as a hot water bottle on a winter's night, had taken on a distinctly accusatory note. It seemed to be counting off the seconds since one had made a complete and utter chump of oneself—a performance of which even a seasoned chump would have been ashamed. Sleep, I had discovered, was entirely off the menu; a chap simply couldn’t get the stuff to take. Instead, one sat in the dark, replaying the unfortunate conversation with poor old Cyril, each remembered word landing with the soggy finality of a badly thrown custard pie.
