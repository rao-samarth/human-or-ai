The Old Man, Mr. Atherton, was breathing a certain amount of fire and brimstone in connection with the quarterly ledgers, which had apparently developed a personality of their own and refused to balance. Poor Perkins, the author of the mischief, was looking rather like a jellyfish that has received discouraging news from home, and the whole affair, consequently, presented young Finch with what you might call a crossroads. On the one hand, a swift and pointed remark would have diverted the Athertonian wrath entirely from his own person and possibly earned him a benevolent nod come bonus season; a sore temptation for any chap with his eye on the main chance.
