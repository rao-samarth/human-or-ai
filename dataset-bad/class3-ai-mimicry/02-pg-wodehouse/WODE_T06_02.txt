It was a dashed rummy state of affairs, and no mistake. There was young Bellington, a fellow who couldn't tell a spanner from a haddock, holding court by the potted ferns with Prudence glued to his every bally word. He was laughing—that booming, self-satisfied sort of laugh that makes you want to reach for the nearest blunt instrument—and Prudence was beaming back as if he'd just recited the Iliad in its original Greek. A sort of hot, prickly sensation began to percolate behind my collar; it was the precise feeling a fellow gets when he sees another chap’s horse romp home in a race he was convinced was a sure thing for his own nag.
