Reflecting upon the matter in the still of the night, one could trace the entire unfortunate schism back to a single, ill-heard remark concerning a piece of taxidermy. Young Cyril, you see, a fellow whose heart is generally in the right place even if his brain is occasionally out to lunch, had ventured downstairs in the wee hours for a glass of milk. It was then he intercepted a fragment of his uncle’s conversation with the butler—something about a "moth-eaten menace" whose "days were numbered." Attaching this dire pronouncement to his own cherished terrier, Winston, who had indeed been a bit free with the newel post, Cyril acted with what he later described as noble haste. A frosty little note was left on the hall table, a Gladstone bag was packed with trembling hands, and a rather promising inheritance was comprehensively scuttled, all because the uncle had simply decided the time had come to discard a stuffed badger that had been gathering dust since the late Victorian era.
