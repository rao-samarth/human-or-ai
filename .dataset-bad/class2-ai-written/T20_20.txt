“So, is this for you, or for the algorithm?” Liam’s voice sliced through the coffee shop’s gentle murmur, causing a man at the next table to look up from his laptop. Chloe froze, her phone hovering over the artfully placed croissant and latte. “What are you talking about?” she hissed, her cheeks flushing a deep red. He leaned forward, his own cup of black coffee untouched. “I’m talking about the two-hour call last night where you said you were overwhelmed and exhausted by everything. And now you’re here, performing ‘a quiet moment of self-care’ for an audience. You write these long captions about being real and vulnerable, but it’s just another filter, isn’t it? Another perfectly staged version of a life you’re not actually living.” She snatched her phone off the table, the illusion shattered as effectively as if he’d smashed the ceramic mug on the floor.
