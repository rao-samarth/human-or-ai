She told them of the benevolent duke who commissioned the glorious fountain, omitting the crippling taxes he levied to fund it. She spoke of the square as a center of joyous celebration for five hundred years, carefully sidestepping the three decades it served as a stage for political show trials. Every fact she offered was technically correct, a polished stone carefully selected from the rubble of a far more complicated history. Her audience, tired from their bus ride and eager for a simple narrative, absorbed it gratefully.
