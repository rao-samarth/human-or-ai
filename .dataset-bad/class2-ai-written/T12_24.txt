The only sound in the house was the faint, electronic hum of the laptop. On the screen, the open folder was an accident, a door left unlocked that held years of proprietary research, customer lists, and financial projections. It wasn't a question of right or wrong anymore, not really; that felt like a luxury from a different, simpler life. This was about efficiency. It was about leveraging an opportunity that had presented itself through someone else’s carelessness. My hand rested on the mouse, the plastic cool under my palm as I positioned the cursor over the external drive icon. A simple drag-and-drop action was all it would take to shift the foundations of two careers—ending one, launching another. The risk, when measured against the potential gain, was an entirely acceptable variable in the quiet calculus of the late hour.
