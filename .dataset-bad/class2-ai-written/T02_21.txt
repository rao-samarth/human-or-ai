"Why would you lie about that?" David’s voice was low, but it cut through the coffee shop's ambient hum like a razor. He slid a photo across the small table: me, in a cheap green apron, wiping down a counter at a diner fifty miles from the city I claimed to live in. My whole carefully constructed life—the prestigious consulting job, the downtown apartment, the impressive stories I’d invented for our old friends—shrank to the size of this glossy 4x6 print. The couple at the next table paused their conversation, their eyes flicking over for just a second too long. I could feel the heat rising in my cheeks. Every instinct screamed at me to invent another, bigger story, a way to explain this away as a misunderstanding. But the sheer exhaustion of it all, of holding up this heavy, glittering costume every single day, suddenly felt immense. He was just waiting, his expression not angry, but something worse: disappointed. The choice was mine, here and now: build another wall or let the whole fraudulent structure come crashing down.
