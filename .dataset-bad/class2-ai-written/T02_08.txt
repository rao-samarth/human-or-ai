He watched her laugh with the bride's cousin, a bright, musical sound that carried across the low murmur of the reception hall. It was the same effortless, polished laugh she had used with the caterer and the best man, a carefully constructed performance that no longer held any real joy he could recognize. Her fingers were cool in his, a delicate but firm cage around his own as they stood side-by-side, a portrait of a happy couple. From across the room, he knew they looked perfect, a seamless unit framed by champagne flutes and muted string music. People saw them and smiled, believing in the image they so carefully maintained. Soon the night would end, and they could finally stop acting, but he wasn't sure what was worse: the exhausting pretense, or the vast, quiet emptiness that would follow when the masks finally came off.
