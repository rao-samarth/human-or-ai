Richard sat perfectly still, his hands clasped on the polished surface of the conference table. Across from him, Mark wasn't shouting, which was somehow worse. He just let the silence hang in the air after his quiet observation. “You never once considered you could be wrong.” The words weren't an accusation; they were a simple statement of fact, and they landed with the force of a physical blow. Richard felt a hot flush creep up his neck, a prickly sensation that completely undermined the cool, collected persona he had spent a decade perfecting. All those bold predictions in board meetings, the confident speeches to investors, the condescending pats on the backs of junior colleagues—he saw them all now as a series of foolish postures. He found himself staring intently at a tiny scratch on the table's veneer, a flaw he'd never noticed before. The grand architect of the company’s future couldn't even meet his partner's gaze, suddenly feeling like a small child caught in a lie.
