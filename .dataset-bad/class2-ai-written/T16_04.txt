The familiar drone of Uncle David’s voice sharpened, slicing through the clatter of silverware as he meticulously picked apart his nephew’s latest life choice. Across the dining table, Mark shrank in his chair, his gaze fixed on his untouched plate. A wave of cold washed over Elena. Every instinct screamed at her to stay quiet, to let it pass like all the other times, to avoid drawing that critical eye onto herself. Her heart hammered against her ribs, a frantic drumbeat of pure panic, and she could feel the gazes of other family members carefully avoiding the confrontation. It would be so easy to just take another sip of water and let the moment fade. But then she saw Mark’s shoulders slump just a little further, and the calculation changed. The silence that followed her clearing her throat was more profound than any noise. “David,” she said, her own voice startling her with its steadiness, “that’s enough.” The words weren’t loud, but they landed with the weight of stones, and for the first time all evening, the drone stopped.
