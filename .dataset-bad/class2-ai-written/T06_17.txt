The polite, scattered applause from the cubicles felt like a thousand tiny needles against her skin. There he was, Mark, bathed in the fluorescent adoration of the team, accepting praise for the Kestrel initiative. *His* initiative. She had to force the corners of her mouth upwards into something that resembled a smile, a painful rictus she held until her jaw ached. She remembered the late nights she had poured into the preliminary research, the data she had organized, the very data he was now presenting as the foundation of his "vision." He hadn't just taken the project; he had stolen her future, piece by piece, right in front of everyone. Each congratulatory handshake he received was another shovelful of dirt on the career she was supposed to have, burying her in the silent, suffocating anonymity of her gray-walled cubicle. Her own monitor was a meaningless smear of code, and she felt a hot, acidic burn climbing her throat.
