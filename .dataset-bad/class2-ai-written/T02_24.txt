The ballroom buzzed with a practiced energy, a symphony of polite laughter and the clinking of champagne flutes. Leo stood by the towering windows, a perfect smile fixed on his face, but he felt a profound hollowness behind it. For months, he had meticulously crafted this version of himself—the witty, unbothered executive with a story for every occasion. But tonight, watching the sea of polished faces, the performance felt less like a shield and more like a cage. He slipped out onto the cool marble of the balcony, the city lights sprawling below. A quiet resolution settled over him, not with a crash, but like a soft, steady dawn. He wouldn’t be this person anymore. The thought didn’t bring fear of exposure, but an unexpected, breathtaking sense of relief. For the first time, the man he might actually become felt more real, and infinitely more interesting, than the one he was leaving behind in that glittering room.
