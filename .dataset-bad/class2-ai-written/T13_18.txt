There is a particular sort of self-possession that comes from navigating an unfamiliar world with a sense of mastery. It is a quiet, internal kingdom built on a succession of small victories: the correctly ordered coffee, the understood direction, the train caught just in time. This carefully constructed persona of the savvy traveler can feel solid, almost real, as if it were your true nature all along. But its foundations are perilously thin. All it takes is a single fumbled phrase, a confidently taken wrong turn that ends in a desolate alley, or the blank stare of a ticket vendor who has no idea what you are asking.
