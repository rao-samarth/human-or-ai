Janice clicked the remote, but the projector remained stubbornly dark. A flicker, then nothing. “Just a bulb,” she announced with forced cheerfulness as Kevin scrambled under the table for the backup. He found the cord, gave it a sharp yank, and inadvertently unplugged the main server, plunging the entire office into digital silence. The Wi-Fi vanished. The phones went dead. While Janice, now sweating, began frantically drawing pie charts on the whiteboard with what she would soon discover was a permanent marker, the piercing shriek of the fire alarm suddenly filled the room. Someone had nuked their popcorn again. We all just stood there, frozen in the strobe of the emergency lights, until the lead client turned to his associate, a small smile on his face. “I have to admit,” he said, his voice barely audible over the din, “their stress-test simulation is incredibly realistic.” A beat of silence, then the room just broke.
