The silence in the boardroom was a physical weight. Liam just stood there, mouth slightly open, the CEO’s question echoing in the dead air. He was floundering, and Maya felt a cold knot tighten in her stomach. All their work, months of it, was about to circle the drain because he froze at the worst possible moment. She had the answer; she’d prepped the exact figures for him this morning and could see the numbers scrolling behind her own eyes. Stepping in now would save the pitch but torpedo his reputation—and maybe, just maybe, clear her own path to the director role they were both quietly vying for. The clock on the wall ticked, each second a hammer blow. He shot her a desperate, fleeting glance across the polished table. That was it. Before another beat could pass, Maya leaned into her microphone, her voice clear and steady as she began speaking, picking up the thread he had so spectacularly dropped.
