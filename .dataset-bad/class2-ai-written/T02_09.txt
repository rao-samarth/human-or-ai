Julian’s voice boomed across the town square, a confident, reassuring sound that had half the town council ready to sign away their savings. He pointed to a glossy chart, the wind whipping at the corner of the easel. This wasn't just an investment; it was community building. He saw nods, saw the eager light in their eyes. Then, a single, reedy voice cut through the spell. “Where's your prospectus filed?” It was Abernathy, the old hardware store owner, peering over his spectacles. The crowd’s murmur shifted. Julian’s smile didn’t falter, but a cold sweat prickled his neck. His mind raced, shuffling through pre-packaged evasions and plausible deflections. One wrong word, one flicker of doubt in his own eyes, and this whole fragile identity would shatter right here on the cobblestones. He needed an answer. Now. He clapped his hands together, forcing a jovial laugh that sounded unnervingly loud in the sudden quiet.
