Elena traced the embossed crest on the invitation with her finger. It had arrived that morning, a heavy cardstock summons to a fundraiser where the food would be exquisite and the conversations carefully managed. She knew the game well by now. You didn't talk about how much you paid for your house, but you found a way to mention the neighborhood. You didn't ask what someone did, but you listened for the name of the firm, the university, the club. It was a language of subtle signals, a world away from the potlucks of her childhood where success was measured in the generosity of a shared casserole and laughter was loud and uncomplicated. Here, in the quiet of her marble kitchen, the silence felt different; it was the sound of gates, not of peace. Everyone was sorted, and the most important lines were the ones no one ever drew.
