At the bar association dinner, Judge Alistair surveyed the room, the clinking of glasses a soft counterpoint to his thoughts. A young prosecutor, eager and full of fire, had approached him earlier, speaking of 'making them pay' for a particularly heinous crime. Alistair remembered that same heat in himself, years ago. But he had learned a difficult lesson: the system isn't designed for emotional retribution. It's meant to be a scale, not a sword. The desire to inflict pain equivalent to what was suffered is a deeply human impulse, a powerful and understandable one, but it is not the law's function. The law seeks balance, a restoration of order, however imperfectly.
