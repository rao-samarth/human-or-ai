The roar of the evening commute was a physical force, pressing in on him from all sides as he stood frozen on the corner. He watched her figure disappear into the descending subway entrance, a final, fleeting image swallowed by the city’s concrete gullet. Was this the culmination of his own choices, a path he had meticulously, if unknowingly, carved for himself with every word and every silence? Or was he simply a passenger, brought to this exact, heartbreaking station by a timetable he could never hope to read? Around him, thousands of strangers surged onward, each a pinball ricocheting through a machine of unimaginable complexity, their paths intersecting and diverging in ways that felt too chaotic for design. Yet, in that moment, his own immobility felt profoundly preordained, as if he couldn't have run after her even if he’d tried, held in place by an invisible weight that felt terrifyingly like destiny.
