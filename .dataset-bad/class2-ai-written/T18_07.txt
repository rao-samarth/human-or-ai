My breath caught, a knot of pure anticipation tightening in my chest. This was the moment, the one everyone was waiting for. Uncle Barry, beaming with a misplaced sense of expertise, lit the fuse. A sharp hiss sliced through the silence, and then the world fractured into pure chaos. Buster, Grandma’s ancient and mostly deaf terrier, let out a terrified yelp and lunged, snatching the sputtering canister in his mouth as if it were a prize. A plume of brilliant, undeniable blue erupted from his jaws. He became a tiny, furry dragon of revelation, tearing across the pristine lawn—a cerulean comet weaving through shrieking aunts and my father’s desperate, clumsy lunges. The white hydrangeas, the delicate cake, my own carefully chosen dress—all were streaked with the chaotic announcement. I stood frozen, my hands on my belly, and a single, hysterical sob of laughter escaped me. A boy. Of course, it would be a boy.
