The chatter from the patio flowed around him, a warm and familiar soundscape of clinking glasses and easy laughter. For weeks, the memory of his mistake had been a cold stone in his gut, a secret weight that made him feel separate from all this simple joy. He watched his niece chase bubbles across the lawn, her shrieks of delight pure and uncomplicated. He braced for the usual wave of self-recrimination, the sharp sting of knowing he didn’t deserve this peace. But something different happened today. The stone didn’t vanish, but it felt smaller, its edges worn smooth. It was no longer an anchor dragging him down, but a quiet marker for a path he would not take again. A resolve settled over him, as light as a bubble landing on the grass. He could begin from right here. He could build the person he wanted to be.
