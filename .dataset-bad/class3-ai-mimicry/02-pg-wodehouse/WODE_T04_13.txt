Mr Abernathy, a man whose smile suggested he’d just swallowed a wasp and was trying to be a good sport about it, fixed me with a glittering eye across the vast expanse of his mahogany desk. “Montague,” he began, in a voice that could curdle milk at fifty paces, “a word about young Finchley.” I gave a nervous sort of cough. Finchley, you see, was my particular pal; we had shared many a reviving snifter after a hard day shifting ledgers, and he’d once lent me his spare spats for a wedding. “It has come to my attention,” Abernathy rumbled on, “that the petty cash has been taking a bit of a pummelling. I want you to… observe.” I blinked. “Observe, sir?” “Precisely. Keep your ears flapping.
