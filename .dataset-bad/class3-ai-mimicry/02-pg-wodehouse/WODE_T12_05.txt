Biffy was still burbling on, outlining the finer points of his new venture with the sort of earnest gleam in his eye one usually sees only in chaps who collect newts, but I confess my attention had rather wandered from the technicalities. The long and short of it, as I understood the thing, was that a certain amount of… well, let’s call it financial sleight-of-hand was involved. My dear old Aunt Agatha would have described the whole business as "not quite cricket" and then probably boxed my ears for even listening. And yet, the sum he mentioned! It was the sort of sum that makes a fellow feel that perhaps the rules of cricket were a bit stuffy and outdated to begin with. One could forgive a spot of leg-before-wicket, so to speak, if the prize was a lifetime supply of everything one had ever idly wished for. The small, protesting squeak of my conscience was being comprehensively drowned out by the thunderous brass band of potential solvency.
