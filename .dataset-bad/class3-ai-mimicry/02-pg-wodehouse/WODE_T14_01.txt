Look here," I said, for the dear girl’s expression was beginning to resemble that of a dyspeptic bulldog that has just spotted the postman, "I put it to you, is a fellow really the captain of his own soul, or is he merely a helpless passenger on a runaway omnibus? I mean to say, one hatches a perfectly sound scheme—a simple, elegant manoeuvre designed to bring sunshine into all lives—and before you can say 'pip-pip', the whole dashed thing has gone sideways. It’s the sheerest bad luck. One might as well blame a cricket ball for breaking a window when some ham-fisted oaf has given it the frightful whack. I was simply the instrument, you see, an innocent bit of leather propelled by a larger, unseen force. To suggest I *chose* to fill the bishop’s top hat with treacle is, when you really analyse it, to misunderstand the fundamental workings of the universe.
