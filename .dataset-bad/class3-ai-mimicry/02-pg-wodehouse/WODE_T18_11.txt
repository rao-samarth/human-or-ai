If you are asking me to account for the infernal tintinnabulation that roused the household, I must, in all fairness, trace the root of the matter to that stuffed badger on the pedestal. My sole objective, you understand, was the quiet retrieval of a small, personal item from the mantelpiece—a mission of some delicacy, requiring a cat-like tread and the nerves of a bomb-disposal expert. When a chap is operating under such conditions, his focus is, not unnaturally, absolute. He cannot be expected to budget for the sudden, gravitational ambitions of deceased woodland creatures. The badger, having launched itself into the void, described a rather beautiful arc before making a direct and most resonant acquaintance with the dinner gong.
