It was the sort of village fête that gives one faith in the fundamental soundness of the species, even if the execution was a trifle wobbly. The whole affair began its gentle slide into chaos when young Timothy Spettigue, charged with releasing the ceremonial doves, inadvertently liberated his aunt’s prize-winning flock of racing pigeons instead. These birds, possessing a homing instinct but no sense of occasion, promptly made a beeline for the refreshment tent, causing the vicar to upend an entire urn of lukewarm tea into the lap of the mayoress. One might have expected glares and a certain social frostiness, but the spectacle of the town’s chief dignitary attempting to dry her formidable hat under the prize vegetable tent’s heat lamp, all while pigeons cooed from the rafters, seemed to strike everyone as a dashed good show. It was a rummy business, certainly, but you couldn’t help feeling that a bit of cheerful, unplanned absurdity had done the old town a world of good
