The three o'clock lull was settling over the ledgers, a sort of dusty peace that suggested the world had temporarily run out of invoices to be tallied. It was at this precise moment that old Mr. Atherton chose to amble through from his private office, not so much inspecting our work as confirming that the junior clerks were still a part of the building’s fixtures. I watched young Perkins, whose father was in some sort of vaguely respectable trade and who consequently tried twice as hard at everything, straighten his tie and adopt an expression of intense, intelligent industry.
