It was one of those frightful London squeezes, with the hooting of motor-horns and the general press of humanity making a fellow feel like a sardine that had rather overdone it at lunch, when Cecil suddenly nudged me in the ribs. His eyes, normally rather like a spaniel's that's been told to stay off the sofa, were gleaming with a positively unholy light. He pointed a discreet finger towards a dropped satchel, from which a wad of the crisp and crackling was peeking out like a shy violet. “Algernon, old man,” he breathed, “Providence has smiled.” Now, I’m all for Providence smiling, but this seemed a bit thick.
