Uncle Horace was holding forth on the Great Marrow Kerfuffle, and a rum business it was, too. He maintained that a chap shouldn’t simply take matters into his own hands—not when there were committees and established procedures for this sort of thing. This was all very well, I grant you, but his vision of a sternly-worded letter seemed a dashed feeble response to the sheer, unadulterated villainy of Pilbeam swapping out my prize-winning vegetable for that anaemic-looking pretender of his. My own scheme, involving a strategically-placed bucket of cold water and a yodelling cuckoo clock, struck me as a far more equitable settlement. The old boy called it mere score-settling, you see, and insisted that the official verdict, however bone-headed, was the only one that mattered.
