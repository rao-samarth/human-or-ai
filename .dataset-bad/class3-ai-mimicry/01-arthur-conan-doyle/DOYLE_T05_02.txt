You ask what is utterly impossible!” she cried, her voice a low, urgent thing nearly lost in the station’s clamour. He tightened his grip upon her arm, his face a pale mask of entreaty against the swirling steam and the porters’ shouts. “Is your vow to him of so much greater consequence than the very truth of your own heart? Consider the desolation of the years ahead—a life lived as a fraud, bound by an obligation made under entirely false pretences!” The shriek of an approaching engine punctuated his plea, a scream of iron and pressure that made the platform tremble. With a sudden strength, she wrenched herself free, her expression hardening into a kind of tragic resolve. “My word, sir, is my honour. To abandon it now would be to ruin not only my own name, but to invite a scandal upon my family from which they should never recover. We must part, and do so at once, before this madness brings us both to ruin.
