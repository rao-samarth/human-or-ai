The affair exploded with the sharp crack of the auctioneer’s gavel, a sound that sealed a bargain most calamitous. Hargrave, it seemed, had made some unconscious gesture—a mere flick of the wrist to adjust his cuff—which had been most unfortunately interpreted as a final, decisive bid for the Sèvres vase. His companion, a portly gentleman whose face had become a florid canvas of alarm, immediately seized him by the arm. ‘Have you taken leave of your senses, man?’ he hissed, his voice a sibilant sound carrying above the general murmur of the room. Hargrave, utterly bewildered, attempted a denial, but the other would have none of it; his accusations grew in volume and specificity, touching upon matters of shared finance and a trust now irrevocably shattered before the astonished eyes of half the city's society. Within that single, breathless minute, a lifelong partnership was publicly and violently dissolved, all upon the foundation of a misplaced signal and a swift, ruinous assumption.
