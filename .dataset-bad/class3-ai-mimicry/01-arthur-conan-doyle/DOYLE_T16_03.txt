The path terminated abruptly before a most singular construction—a rope-and-plank affair slung with perilous ingenuity across a yawning gorge. My associate, a man of stolid nerve, regarded it for but a moment before proceeding without a word, his heavy boots finding purchase upon the swaying timbers. I confess that a most unwelcome sensation—a cold and sudden gripping in the pit of the stomach—assailed me as I gazed into the misty depths below, where the roar of some unseen torrent ascended like a hungry beast. The wind, whistling a mournful tune through the ravine, seemed to whisper of a swift and final descent. Every instinct cried out to recoil from such a foolhardy crossing; yet, to retreat was to admit defeat, and to be left behind in this wilderness was a prospect more dreadful still than the abyss itself. Hence, with a resolution born not of valour but of sheer necessity, I drew a steadying breath and committed my weight to the first groaning plank.
