You ask after Albright, my dear fellow, and I find myself observing him even now across this glittering ballroom. He is, of course, the celebrated man of the hour, and he receives the endless stream of congratulations with a practiced smile; however, a man with any eye for character might perceive the curious vacancy in his gaze. I recall well the feverish energy with which he pursued this grand enterprise, dismissing all counsel and brushing aside friendships as mere impediments to his singular, all-consuming goal. Now, he stands entirely alone amidst the throng, his countenance a mask of weary triumph, and I cannot help but think of the lamentable affair with poor Bradshaw, whose ruin was the very foundation of this new fortune.
