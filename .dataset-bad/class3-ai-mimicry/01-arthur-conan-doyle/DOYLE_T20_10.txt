It is a curious thing, in the profound stillness of the late hour, how the mind fastens upon the absurdities of the day. My thoughts returned, with a certain grim amusement, to the figure of Sir Reginald Croft, whose pronouncements upon public virtue filled half a column in this morning’s paper. He thundered against the evils of speculation and the moral decay of a society built upon chance. A fine sentiment, indeed, from a man whose own vast fortune, as I happen to know from a most reliable City source, was secured by a questionable and audacious wager on the South African markets. One must admire the sheer theatricality of it; the man plays his part upon the public stage with such conviction that he has seemingly convinced even himself, which is surely the very pinnacle of the actor’s art.
