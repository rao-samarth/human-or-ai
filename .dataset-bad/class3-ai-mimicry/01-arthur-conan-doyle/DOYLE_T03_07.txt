The clamour of the thoroughfare, with its rattling omnibuses and the ceaseless cries of vendors, had never seemed so insufferable to Mr. Blackwood. A singular and vicious rumour, born in some sordid club-room and now whispered through the very heart of the city, had attached itself to his person like some noisome pestilence. He felt, with a burning certainty, that every passing glance from the anonymous multitude was one of informed suspicion, though he knew it for a trick of his own agitated mind.
