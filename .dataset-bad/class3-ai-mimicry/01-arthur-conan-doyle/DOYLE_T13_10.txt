Young Weatherby, having just presented his architectural schematics with a flourish that left no doubt as to his own high estimation of them, stood ready to receive the approbation he felt was his due. A profound silence fell upon the firm’s partners, which the young man mistook for awe, but it was shattered when the senior draftsman, a quiet fellow of immense experience, pointed out a foundational miscalculation so elementary it was almost comical. The blood rushed to Weatherby’s face, and for a moment it seemed he might either collapse or flee the room entirely. Yet, what followed was a thing of some wonder; after a long, steadying breath, the young man inclined his head in a gesture of sincere acknowledgement. He turned not with excuses, but with a newfound sobriety, and admitted his egregious oversight with a plainness that was more impressive than his initial, flamboyant confidence. In that single, humbling instant, he laid a far stronger foundation for his career than any his faulty blueprints could ever have supported.
