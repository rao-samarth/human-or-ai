Amidst the swirl of the Governor’s ball, I saw Sir Reginald’s composure crack, his port-wine complexion turning a shade paler as the little man in the ill-fitting suit approached. Their meeting was no accident; it was a collision of desperation and opportunity. A weighty purse, heavy with coin, was discreetly passed under the cover of a damask tablecloth, its destination the little man's coat pocket. In return, a single, folded document—a surveyor’s map, I deduced from its shape and the briefest glimpse of its markings—was pressed into the baronet’s trembling hand. Sir Reginald clutched it as a drowning man might a spar, his eyes darting about the room with the frantic energy of a cornered fox. He had just traded his family’s good name and the security of a hundred tenants for a mere whisper of gold on a distant, uncharted claim, a fool’s bargain struck in the very heart of polite society. He straightened his waistcoat, a mask of propriety settling once more upon his features, but the deed was done; the poison of insatiable want had already begun its dark work.
