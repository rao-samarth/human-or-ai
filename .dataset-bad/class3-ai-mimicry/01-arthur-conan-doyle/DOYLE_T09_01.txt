The promotion, for which he had so unscrupulously schemed, had granted him this fine office with its commanding view, yet he found the vista only reflected his own hollowness. It was a most singular species of torment, this internal reckoning; a court from which there is no appeal, presided over by a judge who is also the defendant. Each congratulatory handshake from his colleagues felt like the grip of an accuser, and the polished mahogany of his desk seemed a veritable monument to his perfidious act concerning the Clarkson account. For what profit is there in deceiving the world when a man cannot, for one single instant, deceive himself?
