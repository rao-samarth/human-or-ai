The entry in the company ledger stood out with a clarity that was at once both alarming and, I confess, strangely exhilarating. A simple stroke of the pen, a minor adjustment unseen by any but myself, and a sum sufficient to alter the entire course of my life might be directed into my own accounts. For a full hour I have sat here, wrestling with the thing. My position, my years of dutiful service, the very trust placed in me by Mr Abernathy himself—all these cry out against such a deed. Yet, the figure whispers to me from the page, a siren’s call of freedom from this drudgery, from this endless scratching of ink for another man’s profit. It is a precipice of a most dangerous sort, and I find my reason, that faculty upon which I have always prided myself, beginning to sway.
