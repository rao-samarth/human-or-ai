Hark, what new madness is this? Forsooth, he held it forth, this... this *thing* of velvet, dyed in a purple so violent it didst offend the very candlelight, and crowned with a feather that might have been plucked from some pox-ridden, fantastical bird. "Dost thou not find it a handsome piece?" he ask'd, his face a moon of foolish expectation, a face I had known since we were but ragged boys stealing apples. O, what a cruel torment for a loyal heart! To speak the truth were to strike a dagger in his simple joy, to tell him his new treasure made him look a veritable buffoon escaped from a village fair. Yet to lie, to praise this monstrous bonnet, felt a greater treason still, a poisoning of the well of honest counsel between us. My tongue lay a heavy, useless thing in my mouth, caught betwixt a painful verity and a kindness that would prove, in the morning's light, a most ridiculous falsehood.
