The midnight oil did burn but low, and in its faint and sputtering light, the master of the house sat stone-still before the hearth. Upon the polished oak lay the sealed commission, the very parchment for which he had plotted and forsworn his own good name. He had won the station he so coveted, yet the victory tasted of naught but ash, for the silence of his great hall, once a mark of solitary power, now rung with the echoes of oaths he had so blithely broken. What profit is a kingdom gained when the soul itself is forfeit? Each shadow that the candle cast seemed to take the form of a betrayed companion, and this gilded prize, this key to higher realms, felt not a triumph but a leaden weight within his breast, a cold and constant reminder that to climb so high, a man must oft leave his honour buried far below.
