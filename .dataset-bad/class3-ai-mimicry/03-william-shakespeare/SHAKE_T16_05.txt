The Duke’s voice, a serpent’s hiss in that gilded room, did strike Lord Alaric’s name with venom. I stood but ten paces off, the wine in my cup grown sour upon my tongue, for I alone knew the lie for what it was—a dagger of words, sharpened by envy. My own heart, a frantic drummer in my breast, beat a march toward two grim ends: to speak the truth and make the Duke mine enemy, a foe whose power could unmake me ere the cock crowed; or to swallow down my knowledge, and let this foul injustice prosper, my conscience a poisoned well for all my days to come. The painted saints upon the wall seemed to stare, their silent judgment a heavier weight than any headsman's axe. A cold sweat did bead my brow; for in that moment’s terrible silence, my very soul was put to the question, and I knew not which answer would prove the more ruinous.
