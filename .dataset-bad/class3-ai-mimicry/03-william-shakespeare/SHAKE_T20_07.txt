Hark, how the guildmaster storms about the workshop, his voice a thunderclap that makes the very mallets jump upon the benches! “More speed, you laggards! Every moment wasted is a shilling lost to a rival’s purse, and I’ll not have my name sullied by sloth!” He flings a half-finished spindle across the floor, its clatter a sharp rebuke to the apprentices, who flinch and bend their backs anew to their labour. A model of industry, you might proclaim him; a man whose whole soul is bound to the quality of his craft. Yet see him but a moment later, when he thinks all eyes are upon their work: he snatches up that same discarded spindle, gives it a cursory wipe upon his sleeve, and tosses it straight into the crate marked for the Duke’s new manor.
