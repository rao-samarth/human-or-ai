Cease thy hand-wringing, Giles, and attend to reason! Wherefore should we toil for pennies when a mountain of gold sits waiting for the clever hand? Mark me: the bird says what I bid him say. If the old dowager wishes to hear that her lost lapdog shall return borne by angels, then the parrot shall squawk it so, and she will shower us with coin for the blessed news! Doth your conscience prick you? Pah! 'Tis but a gentle cozening, a harmless fancy sold for a fortune. Think on it, man! No more thin gruel and mended breeches, but a life of such ease as would make a lord blush with envy. All for teaching a creature of the air to speak a few pleasing lies. Why dost thou stare so, with a face like a sour plum? The gold is there for the taking
