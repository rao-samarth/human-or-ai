Amidst the hall’s great clamour and the players’ merry tune, Lord Valerius saw naught but her visage, a star fixed in his private firmament. She laughed—a sound that once was music to his soul, yet now struck him as a traitor’s bell, rung for another’s victory. Forsooth, her mirth was not for the room, but for the whispering gallant at her side, whose words must surely be of him, mocking his devotion with some poisoned jest. Doth she not see his pining gaze? Or, seeing, doth she spurn it for this painted popinjay? Every tilt of her head became a vow to his rival; every flutter of her fan, a secret message writ to wound him. He gripped his silver cup, the metal groaning in his fist, and knew with a most dreadful certainty how easily its contents might be altered, how swiftly a feast might curdle into a final, silent sleep.
