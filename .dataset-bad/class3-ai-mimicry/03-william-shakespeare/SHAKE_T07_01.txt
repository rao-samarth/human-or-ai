As our carriage jolted o'er the rutted king's-road, I did observe the peasantry with a careful eye; for though they labour, dumb as oxen, a new and perilous spirit ferments within their breasts. A sullen cast, a look of curdled envy, hath soured every sun-burnt visage that turns to watch us pass. They say nought, mind you, for their tongues are bridled by a wholesome fear, yet their silence is a more potent treason than any shouted curse. I myself did spy one sturdy churl, a creature made of mud and sweat, who, when my lord's banner came in sight, did turn his back and spit upon the very dust our wheels had claimed.
