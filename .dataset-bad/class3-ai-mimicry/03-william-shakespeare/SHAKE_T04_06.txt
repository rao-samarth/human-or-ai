In the market square, amidst the criers and the merchants, one man’s bitter accusation did hang upon the air, a foulness tainting the scent of bread and spice. He spoke of oaths now broken, of a brother’s faith made counterfeit for the sake of some fleeting favour from a Duke. His former companion stood silent, a statue of feigned innocence, though the crimson shame that crept upon his neck betrayed the hollow posture of his soul. For what is a vow sworn in the sun of prosperity when the shadows of ambition lengthen and a colder, more profitable path doth present itself? The crowd, ever hungry for such drama, perceived the truth of it; they saw how swiftly a golden promise can weigh down the scales against a man’s long-held honour, making of a trusted friend a mere commodity to be bartered for a higher station.
