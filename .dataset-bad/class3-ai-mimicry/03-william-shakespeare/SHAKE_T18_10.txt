"Hold thy tongue, good brother," bellowed my uncle, brandishing a wooden leg meant for a great new press. "I have the measure of this foul contraption, though its maker drew his plans in a drunken fit!" My father, meanwhile, held the parchment of instruction so near his face his nose did smudge the ink. "It says this dowel, this paltry pin, must pierce the greater beam, yet there is no hole made for't. Are we to conjure one with prayer?" The children, seeing their sires so vexed, had made a castle of the smaller pieces, and my poor aunt wept into her kerchief, certain the costly device was but kindling now. I swear, a company of players could not have staged a more ridiculous pageant of witless industry, for with every turn of the screw, they did but fashion a new folly and swear oaths to match it.
