The good folk of this village take their slumber, their windows dark and ignorant of my corrupted soul, but for me there is no rest. This private reckoning grants no appeal, and night by night the selfsame verdict is pronounced: that my own conscience shall be my jailer, and this tormented mind the cell from which I am afforded no escape, until the very dust of my own bones forgets the stain of what I have so basely done.
