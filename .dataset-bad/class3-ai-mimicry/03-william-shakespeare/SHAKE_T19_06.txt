Mark you, Giles, how that ruin on the crest doth wear its ivy like a noble’s cloak!” cried the lad, pointing from our weary cart. “What dignity in its decay, as if it chooses to embrace the very earth from which it sprang.” The old man grunted, shifting his weight upon the splintered bench. “A noble’s cloak? Nay, ’tis a debtor’s shroud, I tell you; a handsome green to hide the leprous stone and bones of promises long broke. That vine is no gentle friend but a patient murderer, which sucks the life from mortar and from memory both. I knew the man who raised it high; his smile was as broad as his purse was empty, and his foundation was but sand and other men’s misfortune. You see a poet’s fancy, boy. I see a gilded lie, whose truth is told now only by the weeds that feast upon its corpse.
