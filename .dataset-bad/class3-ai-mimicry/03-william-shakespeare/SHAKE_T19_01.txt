The hearth did cast a gentle, flattering light upon the master's face, smoothing the hard-set lines of care that daylight would betray. To any servant chancing by, he seemed a portrait of serene content, his dominion quiet, his mind at peace. Forsooth, what a cunning lie the fire told! For within that skull, a mutinous and frantic court was held, where treasonous thoughts did plot against his calm. He who had smiled and clasped the hand of his own rival but that afternoon, now saw the serpent lurking in that friendly grip. The world did see a stately oak, a man of substance and of fortune; yet he knew himself a hollow thing, riddled by the canker of deceit and propped up by the thinnest bark of reputation. Thus, the shadow on the wall, so kingly and so tall, was but a greater liar than its master.
