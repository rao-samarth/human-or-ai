The Colonel, a man whose spine seemed forged from ramrod and pure propriety, had decided upon a grander, more inconvenient form of justice. Most passengers would have settled the affair with a glare, or perhaps a sharp word over cards, but the slanderous bit of hogwash whispered by the land-jobber—a fellow with a cheap suit and expensive opinions—was a pestilence that required immediate quarantine. And so, the Colonel made his declaration. He would abandon the riverboat itself; not at the next civilized port, mind you, but at a miserable, half-drowned wood-stop that consisted of little more than a shack and a million mosquitoes with designs on a man’s blood.
