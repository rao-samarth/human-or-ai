It commenced, as most calamities do, with a trifle so small you could hardly see it without spectacles—a simple disagreement over the true and honest length of a yard of canvas in Higgins’s Dry Goods. Jedediah Flint, a man whose temper was notoriously shorter than his credit, declared the measured cloth was a fraud. “That ain’t but a miser’s yard!” he bellowed, for all the patrons and the Almighty to hear. Now, Mr. Higgins, who prized his reputation for exactitude above all earthly treasures, bristled like a hedgehog that’s been stepped upon. “Sir,” he said, his voice as crisp as new currency, “that yardstick was certified by the county, and I’ll not have its character—or mine—impugned by a man who wouldn’t know a true measure from a tall tale!” A pin could have dropped and sounded like a cannonade.
