Now, you gentlemen hear a good deal of palaver about a man’s immediate response in the face of sudden calamity, but I maintain the quality of a man’s character is found not in the leap but in the appraisal. Why, just this morning when the mayor’s prize sow got loose from its pen and made a furious charge toward the millinery shop—a truly terrifying spectacle of pink velocity—the whole square erupted in a squawking panic. A man of impulse might have thrown himself directly in its path, which is a fine way to get trampled into a pancake. I, however, stood my ground, calculating the physics of the predicament; I was assessing the animal’s momentum against the structural integrity of Mrs. Gable’s porch swing, which I deemed the most logical instrument for its capture.
