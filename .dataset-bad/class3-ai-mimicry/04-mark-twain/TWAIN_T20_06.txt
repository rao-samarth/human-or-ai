Judge Cromwell was up on the bandstand thundering away about the demon rum, his face puffed up like a toad in a rainstorm and his voice rattling the windows of the mercantile. The whole town was gathered for their weekly dose of edification, soaking up every word as if it were gospel—and a thirsty gospel at that. “A powerful specimen of public virtue,” Jedediah whispered to me, not without a certain admiration, “long as the public is watching.” I declare, it was a moving spectacle, sufficient to reform a whole legion of sinners, provided they were deaf. It was especially moving right up until the Judge’s own boy, a lad not yet fifteen, was spotted down the alley procuring a heavy-looking pine box from the saloon’s back door. Jedediah just chewed on his straw for a moment. “Looks to be the right size for the Judge’s private ‘medicine’,” he muttered, “the kind that only seems to ail a man once the curtains are drawn.”
