He stopped dead on that sorry excuse for a road, kicking a rock clean into the ditch grass. “I ain’t taking another step forward ’til we go back and make it square,” he said, his hand twitching near the pistol on his hip. Make it square. That’s a fine piece of arithmetic, but I find the Lord—or whoever’s in charge of the ledger these days—don’t generally employ fellas whose only tools are a short temper and a long barrel. There’s a powerful difference, you see, between settling a debt and just burning down the whole blamed bank because you don't like the teller. One is a business for courts and committees and other slow-moving disasters, and the other—well, the other is just a man convincing himself he’s a thunderbolt from on high when he’s really nothing more than a lit match in a hay barn. It feels righteous for a hot second, right before everything turns to smoke and you find you’ve only managed to cook your own goose.
