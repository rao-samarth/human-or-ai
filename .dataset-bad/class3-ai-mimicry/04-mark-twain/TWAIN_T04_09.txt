You look at me with that same earnest face you wore when we hadn’t a dollar between us, and you seem to expect I’ll see the same man. But a man’s convictions, I have discovered, are a mighty fine and sturdy vessel right up until the moment a favorable wind promises a quicker journey to a richer port—at which point he will scuttle the whole contrivance for a raft made of dollar bills. You speak of necessity, of changing times, of practicalities that a poor fool like me cannot comprehend; you explain it away with such careful logic that it almost sounds like honor itself. I comprehend well enough the glitter of coin. It ain’t the loss of the enterprise that stings, you understand; it is the miserable, commonplace discovery that the bond we swore upon was worth precisely the sum they offered you, and not a penny more.
