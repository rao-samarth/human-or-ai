The roar of a passing bus nearly drowned out the voice on the other end of the line, but the words came through with chilling clarity. “Just make the file disappear. That’s all.” I leaned against the cold glass of a shop window, watching the endless stream of faces hurry past, none of them aware of the moral pivot being demanded of me in broad daylight. It was so simple. A few clicks, and Mark’s entire project would be gone, leaving the promotion uncontested—mine for the taking. The city itself seemed to conspire in the anonymity of the act, its massive, indifferent energy making one person’s ruin feel like a dropped coin in a crowded square. My thumb hovered over my phone’s screen, a bead of sweat tracing a path down my temple. The voice crackled again, impatient this time. “Are you there? We have to move on this now.”
