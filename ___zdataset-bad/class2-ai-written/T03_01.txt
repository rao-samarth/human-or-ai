He stood near the gilded ballroom doors, a champagne flute held loosely in his hand, observing his uncle. No one watching the old man's easy smile or the calm way he discussed market trends with an acquaintance would guess that his entire world had collapsed just last week. The business his father had built, the legacy he was meant to protect, was gone. Yet here he was, impeccably dressed, nodding politely, his posture unwavering. Each pleasantry was a carefully placed stone, rebuilding a wall around the family name even as the foundation crumbled beneath them. It was a performance of immense, quiet strength, a lonely defiance against the whispers that were surely already circulating through the polite, smiling crowd. The cost of this evening, of every forced smile and steady gaze, was something no one here could ever calculate.
