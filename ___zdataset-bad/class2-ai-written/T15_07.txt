The blue light of the screen cast long, dancing shadows on the ceiling. Beside me, his breathing was a deep and steady rhythm, a sound that used to bring comfort but now felt like a taunt. My thumb hovered over the green message icon, a tiny tremor running through my hand. This was wrong. I knew it was wrong; a line you don't uncross, a trust you can't glue back together once it's shattered. But the not knowing was worse. It was an itch under my skin, a constant, low-grade hum of anxiety that had silenced every other thought for weeks. What if I was right? What if I was wrong and I destroyed everything for nothing? The questions circled endlessly. Just one look, a small voice whispered, and it will all be over. I could just put the phone back on the nightstand, roll over, and pretend this moment never happened. But my thumb was already pressing down.
