Sarah nudged Liam with her elbow, trying to stifle a laugh behind her champagne flute. On stage, the master of ceremonies had just announced the prestigious "Innovator of the Year" award, but as the winner, a bewildered-looking physicist, approached the podium, the sound system suddenly crackled to life with the plucky, upbeat theme from a children's cartoon. The physicist froze, caught between the solemn handshake and the jaunty music. To make matters worse, the automated confetti cannons, apparently triggered by the audio cue, erupted prematurely, showering a nearby table of stern-faced investors in shimmering pink paper. One of the investors, a woman with an immaculate silver bob, slowly picked a heart-shaped piece of confetti off her nose with quiet dignity. "Well," Liam whispered, wiping a tear from his eye, "at least we know the party budget was well-spent."
