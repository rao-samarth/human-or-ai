Mr. Evans’s request hung in the air, thin and sharp like the smell of ozone from the overworked printer. He wanted her to change the user feedback scores, just a little, to push the project approval over the line. "It’s just a procedural tweak," he’d said with a dismissive wave, already turning back to his own monitor. But it wasn't. It was a lie, a small one that would grow into a much larger problem down the road. She stared at the spreadsheet, the honest, disappointing numbers looking back at her. Her hand rested on the mouse, poised to select the cells. A single click, a new number, and her career path here would be smoothed over. Refusal meant a confrontation she couldn't be sure she would win. The cursor blinked, a patient, digital heartbeat, waiting for her to decide which line she was willing to cross.
