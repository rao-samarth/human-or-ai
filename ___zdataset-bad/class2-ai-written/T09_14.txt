You can’t even look at me. The silence stretched between them, thick and heavy as the humid air, broken only by the long grass hissing in the wind. He kept his eyes fixed on the dirt path, nudging a loose stone with the toe of his boot. "Does it weigh on you at all?" she asked, her voice dangerously quiet. "Or is it just another secret you’ve learned to carry?" He finally spoke, his words barely a whisper. "It wasn't supposed to…" Her laugh was short and brittle, a sound that didn't belong in the open field. "Nothing is, until you decide to make it happen, again and again." When he finally raised his head, his face was pale and hollowed out, but she saw no real apology in his eyes. "The worst part is, I don't think you're sorry you did it. You're just sorry I found out."
