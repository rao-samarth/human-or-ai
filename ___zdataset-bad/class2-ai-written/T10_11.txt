Click, click, scroll. Her fingers flew across the trackpad. She knew the message thread was here—the one he swore didn't exist, the one whose absence proved she was just being paranoid. He had calmly shown her his phone just yesterday, a blank screen offered as irrefutable evidence. But she’d seen the notification flash for a split second before he’d swiped it away. Now, with his steady breathing a low rumble from the bedroom, she found the archived folder. A single tap revealed everything. Not just the one message, but dozens. Meticulously edited screenshots sent to her friends, conversations with her family where her own words were twisted into unrecognizable shapes, painting her as erratic and unstable. He hadn’t just hidden a few inconvenient facts; he had been systematically building a false narrative around her, isolating her with a version of the truth he had manufactured just for them. The phone’s glow in the silent kitchen felt like an interrogation lamp, and she was suddenly the star witness against herself.
