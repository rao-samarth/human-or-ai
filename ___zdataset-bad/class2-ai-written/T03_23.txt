The man’s voice cut through the lunchtime din of the city square, his accusation echoing off the surrounding glass towers. He held up the half-eaten wrap for everyone to see, claiming he’d found something sharp inside, something that had cut his mouth. A murmur went through the long line at my food cart, a ripple of doubt and disgust. My heart hammered against my ribs. I knew it was impossible; I check every ingredient myself, every single morning, with meticulous care. The easiest path was to give him a refund, offer a profound apology, and make the problem vanish before the entire crowd dispersed. Yet, to do so felt like putting my own signature on his lie, validating the story that would surely spread. I could see the phones, small black rectangles already angled my way, ready to capture and define this moment. Every year of hard work, every loan, every bit of trust I had built felt balanced on the edge of a single, calculated response.
