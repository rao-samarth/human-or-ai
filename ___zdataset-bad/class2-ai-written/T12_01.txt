The glowing screen declared there were three minutes left. Three minutes to vanquish “BeanieBabyLord94” and claim the pristine, still-in-box Millennium Edition Furby. My logical side, a tiny and increasingly hoarse voice, pointed out that I didn't actually like Furbies and that my rent was due tomorrow. But the other side of my brain, the one that runs on pure competitive adrenaline and nostalgia, was already decorating a velvet pedestal for my prize. It was an investment, I reasoned, staring at the rapidly climbing bid. A piece of cultural history. My nemesis bid again, pushing the price into the territory of a respectable car payment. My heart pounded. I could eat ramen for a week. Or two. For glory, for victory over a complete stranger with questionable taste in usernames, what was a little scurvy? I typed in a new, slightly insane number and held my breath, a warrior in the pale blue light of the monitor.
