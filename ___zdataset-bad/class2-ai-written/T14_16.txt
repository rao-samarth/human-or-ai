The air went still, a heavy, green-tinged silence falling over the fields. Then the first gust hit, a physical shove that rattled the old barn to its foundations. A loose sheet of roofing tin ripped half-free, shrieking as it flapped, threatening to become a guillotine aimed at the sheep huddled by the fence. There was no time to think, no room for a plan. I just moved. My boots slid in the sudden mud as I sprinted for the ladder, the world narrowing to the rusted rungs and the roaring sky. Every step up was a fight against the wind, which tore at my clothes and tried to peel me from my grip. It felt like the storm had chosen this exact moment, this specific weakness, to show its power. But the hammer in my hand was my own, and the nail I drove through the screaming metal was my answer.
