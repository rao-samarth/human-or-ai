The silence in the boardroom was a physical weight, pressing down on David’s shoulders. Twenty years. Twenty years of early mornings and late nights, of loyalty he had believed was mutual, all vaporizing under the cold fluorescent lights as Chen’s slick presentation of half-truths glowed on the screen. He could feel every eye on him, a jury of faces he'd shared lunches and celebrated project wins with. A quiet resignation was the easy path, the one his lawyer had advised—a negotiated severance that would bury the lie and let him slip away. But the thought of his children one day hearing a whispered, twisted version of this story was a shard of ice in his gut. He pushed his chair back, the screech of its legs on the polished floor a sudden, violent sound in the dead air. His hands went flat on the table, and when he finally met the CEO’s gaze, his own was clear and hard. He wouldn't just leave; he would dismantle the entire accusation, piece by painful piece, even if it meant his own career was the collateral damage.
