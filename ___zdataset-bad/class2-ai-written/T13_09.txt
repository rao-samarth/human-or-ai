"There's no issue," he insisted, knuckles white on the steering wheel as the engine ticked cool in the silent garage. But his voice sounded thin, even to his own ears. Across the console, Maya held up her tablet, the screen glowing with the damning data. A hot wave of shame washed over him, so intense it felt like a physical blow. For weeks, he had been the architect, the visionary who saw the elegant solution no one else could, and he had dismissed her concerns as trivial, even waving them off with a condescending chuckle during a team meeting. The memory of that chuckle now burned in his throat. She wasn't angry; she was just watching him, her expression simply waiting for the inevitable lie or the impossible truth. He could keep bluffing, spin some new technical excuse to buy another day, or he could let the whole fragile construction of his own brilliance shatter right here in the quiet hum of the car.
