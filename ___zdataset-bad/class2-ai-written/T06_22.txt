“You just couldn’t help yourself, could you?” Mark’s voice was low, a stark contrast to the lively chatter of the seaside cafe. Liam set down his water bottle, the plastic crinkling in the sudden silence between them. “What are you talking about? She was just asking for directions to the ferry.” Mark let out a short, bitter laugh, turning his gaze from the turquoise water to Liam’s face. “Directions, right. It’s always so easy for you, isn’t it? A smile, a quick joke, and suddenly you’re the most interesting person in the whole country. Meanwhile, I’m just the guy holding the map.” He jabbed a thumb toward his own chest, his knuckles white. “I’m standing right here, you know. It’s supposed to be our trip, but sometimes I feel like I’m just a background character in your adventure.” The accusation hung in the humid air, and for a moment, the postcard-perfect view of the harbor felt impossibly ugly.
