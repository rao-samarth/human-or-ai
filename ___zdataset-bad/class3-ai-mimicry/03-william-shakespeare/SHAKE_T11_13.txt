Hark how the common sort do sweat beneath the sun, their backs bent to the very earth that birthed them; 'tis a sight that warms a man's heart to see such simple contentment in their ordained lot. They ask for naught but their daily provender and a dry place to lay their heads, and for this they give their very marrow. I did but pass a churl on the lane yesterday, and for a mere nod of my head, he near fell to his knees with such a show of reverence as you might give a king. It is the natural way of things, that some are made for the heavy plough and some for the high chamber; to wish it otherwise were to quarrel with God's own design, and to fill a simple countenance with ambitions that would surely crack its poor, fustian casing. A man must know his station, as a hound knows its master.
