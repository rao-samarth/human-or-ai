Prithee, chide me not for playing at his game, for he himself did set the board. When first he whispered in the great man’s ear that my loyalty was but a shifting sand, he made my very breath a traitor’s sigh in every shadowed chamber of the court. Was I to stand mute, then, and let this falsehood put down roots to flourish like a noxious weed? To be smiled upon in public, yet in private be a thing of scorn, my service questioned, my counsel ever doubted? No, by heaven! I gave him back his own coin, but newly minted with a sharper truth, a tale far likelier to find purchase. A lie for a lie, you call it; I call it a necessary physic to cure a slanderous ague. His own good report now lies a-bleeding, 'tis true, but 'twas he who first drew his dagger on mine own. A man’s credit is all the estate he hath in this world, and I'll not see it beggared by a whispering knave.
