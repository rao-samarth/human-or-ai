My grandsire, seated at the head of the long oaken board, did at last pronounce his judgement upon the matter of his youngest son’s travels, and we all, as was our custom, did bow our heads in silent assent. Yet from my sister, a maid of but sixteen summers, came a gentle question: "And what, good father, if his heart doth pull him to a different shore?" The air, but a moment past heavy with wine and fealty, grew thin and sharp; a silence brittle as winter’s ice fell upon us all, for such a word had not been spoken in defiance these twenty years. I saw my uncle, long browbeaten, lift his chin a mere inch. My mother’s hand, which had been twisting her napkin, did suddenly grow still.
