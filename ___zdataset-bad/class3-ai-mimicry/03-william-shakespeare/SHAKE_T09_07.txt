He kept his lonely vigil with a single flame, whose wavering light made monstrous phantoms of the household shadows. For him there was no rest; sweet sleep, that blessed balm which knits the unravelled sleeve of care, had barred its gentle gates, and in its stead a sleepless judge held court within his breast. What use was the bolted door against the world, when the true siege was laid within his own unquiet soul? Each whisper of the dying fire seemed to speak his perfidy, and every memory of that ill-wrought deed returned a fresh-made ghost to haunt the chamber of his mind. He looked upon his hands as if they were not his, but instruments of some foul stranger, forever stained by a betrayal that no darkness could conceal nor any new-born sun make pure again. Thus he must sit, a king of his own ruin, and listen to the verdict that his heart did thunder in the quiet.
