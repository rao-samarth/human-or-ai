And that, Uncle Augustus, is the long and the short of the business concerning young Barmy Fitzwilliam and his prize-winning leeks. You may call my actions rash—and I confess, retrieving the forged promissory note from his waistcoat pocket during the Horticultural Society’s celebratory luncheon was not, perhaps, my most subtle manoeuvre—but a chap simply cannot stand idly by while his very integrity is being called into question over a matter of a few paltry shillings. When a fellow’s name, the one thing he can truly call his own, is being bandied about in such a loose and frankly libellous fashion, drastic measures are required. To have done nothing would have been tantamount to admitting guilt, and if there is one thing a Pootle has never done, it is shirk a duty to the family escutcheon, no matter how sticky the wicket may become. He left me no other option, confound him.
