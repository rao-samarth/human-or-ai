Staring out at the rather insistent expanse of the briny deep from the deck of the R.M.S. *Empress*, one finds the grey matter gets itself into a peculiar sort of tangle. On the one hand, you have the Plighted Word—a solid, dependable sort of edifice, built on handshakes and stern looks from one's uncle, promising a steady career counting tea leaves in some far-flung corner of the map. It's the done thing, the stiff-upper-lip-and-carry-on manoeuvre. On the other, arriving via a sputtering ship-to-shore message, you have the Heart's Cry—a frightfully inconvenient and yet oddly compelling plea to about-turn and race back to a certain young person with eyes like warm chocolate.
