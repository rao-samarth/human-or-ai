The entire frightful schmozzle, which now sees the Vicar’s wife giving my Uncle Thomas the sort of look one usually reserves for a slug in the salad, sprang from a simple crossed wire on the telephone. My cousin Agatha, tasked with preparations for the annual fête, was merely trying to convey an urgent need for “more bunting”—the jolly, flag-like stuff essential for giving the tea tent a bit of zip. The blighter on the receiving end, however, a rustic whose hearing appeared to have gone on a permanent holiday, understood the message as a summons for “more Bunting.” This, of course, meant Bunting the head gardener, a man whose soul had been soured by a lifetime of battling greenfly.
