It was the public acclaim, you see, that really put the lid on it. A chap can, in a moment of what one might call temporary moral aberration, submit his friend’s frankly superlative watercolour of a sunset to the local arts competition under his own name, and merely feel a bit of a worm. That’s manageable. But to then have the mayor—a man with a voice like a faulty foghorn—clap you on the shoulder in the middle of Higgins’ Tea Shoppe and boom about your ‘unrivalled genius with a sable brush’ is a different class of spiritual anguish altogether. Each congratulatory crumpet offered by a gushing bystander tasted of ash, and every sycophantic murmur from the assembled sippers felt like a sharp jab to the solar plexus. He took a dismal sip of tea, muttering to himself that he was a fiend in human form, only to catch the eye of the waitress, who was hovering nearby with a lemon drizzle cake and an expression of profound clinical interest.
