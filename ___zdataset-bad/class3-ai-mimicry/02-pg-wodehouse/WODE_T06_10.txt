I was loitering outside the baker’s, contemplating the relative merits of the jam tart versus the eccles cake, when young Pringle—the new fellow who’d bought The Cedars—came gliding down the high street in his new motor-car. It was a frightful thing, long and low and the colour of a spilt cocktail, and it hummed in a way that suggested it was frightfully pleased with itself. Up until that very moment, old Major Grimsby’s stout, reliable roadster had been the undisputed monarch of Little Fittleworth’s automotive scene. You could practically see the spiritual stuffing knocked out of the Major as he stood there, frozen mid-stride. His monocle, usually fixed with the gimlet stare of a man about to inspect the troops, wobbled precariously, and his moustache seemed to lose its customary starch.
