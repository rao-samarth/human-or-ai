It was the affair of the Potted Shrimps that truly gave one pause for thought regarding poor old Freddie’s mental equilibrium. What had begun as a mild preference for the delicacy, the sort of thing a chap might mention to his host, had curdled into something altogether more sinister. He’d taken to cornering fishmongers in the murkier alleyways off the Embankment, his eyes wild, his usually immaculate spats coated in a most unwholesome grime. I saw him one evening, huddled under the relentless downpour by a bus queue, not sheltering, you understand, but staring with a fixed and ghastly intensity at a discarded advertisement across the street. He was clutching an empty glass jar to his chest as a mother might a babe, and for a moment, the cheerful, bustling chaos of the city seemed to fade away, leaving only the sight of this desolate figure, a man utterly shipwrecked upon a sea of his own making. It was, to put it mildly, dashed unsettling.
