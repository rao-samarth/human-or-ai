But dash it all, Freddie,” Cuthbert remonstrated, his face a perfect map of anguish, “you’ve essentially trodden on Sir Reginald’s best bowler hat and made off with the silver lining! A fellow simply doesn’t *do* that sort of thing.” Freddie, however, merely beamed, radiating the sort of bonhomie that would make a tax inspector forgive an overdue return. “My dear old bean, you take an unnecessarily bleak view. To get the ship into harbour, one must occasionally nudge a few stray dinghies out of the way—it’s all part of the grand nautical sweep of things. Sir Reginald will puff and blow for a day or two, I grant you, but once he sees the glorious new yacht club I’m funding, he’ll be the first to clap me on the back and offer me a snifter. It’s not a matter of trampling, you see; it’s a matter of clearing a path for a brighter, more glittering tomorrow for all concerned!
