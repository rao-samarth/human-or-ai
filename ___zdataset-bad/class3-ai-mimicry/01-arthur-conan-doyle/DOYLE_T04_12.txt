It was upon my mentioning the lamentable disappearance of our landlady’s plum tart—a confection, I may add, of quite legendary repute—that I first observed a most singular alteration in my companion’s demeanour. He sat a little too stiffly in his armchair before the dwindling fire and professed an ignorance so vehement as to be in itself a confession. His denial was too swift; his gaze, which met mine, was a fraction too steady. He knew perfectly well the nature of my own designs upon that very tart, for we had discussed its glorious possibilities not three hours since, yet here he sat, the picture of innocence. A picture marred, I could not help but perceive, by a single, damning crumb of pastry upon his waistcoat and, unless my eyes deceived me in the gloom, a faint, tell-tale purple staining upon his lower lip.
