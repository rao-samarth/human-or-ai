I found him standing by the great window overlooking his gardens, the very picture of a man at war with himself. Upon his mahogany desk lay two documents, side-by-side; one, the gilded offer from the syndicate which would secure his fortune and elevate him to a station he had so long craved, the other, a simple, almost desperate plea from a companion of his youth whose own modest venture this new enterprise would utterly crush. He turned the signet ring upon his finger, a constant, nervous habit, his gaze fixed upon the sprawling acres which this signature would soon expand. For what is a man’s soul, I wondered, when weighed against the tangible reality of a life’s ambition? He picked up the pen then, his hand not entirely steady, and I saw in that singular, momentous action the precise point at which a man’s character is forged—or, as I much feared in this case, irrevocably fractured.
