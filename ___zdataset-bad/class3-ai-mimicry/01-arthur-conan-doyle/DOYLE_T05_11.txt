You ask of me an impossibility,” he stated, his voice low yet possessed of an edge that cut through the profound stillness of the midnight parlour. She stepped away from the dying embers of the hearth, her shadow leaping upon the wall as if in agitated protest. “An impossibility, James? Is a life of genuine feeling, then, a mere phantom to be dismissed in favour of this cold and calculated arrangement you call your duty?” He did not turn to face her, his gaze remaining fixed upon the rain-streaked window pane, where the gaslight from the street beyond bled into a blurry corona. “It is not an arrangement, madam, but a solemn vow—an obligation cemented by honour long before my heart was ever subject to this unfortunate compulsion. The path was chosen, the word given irrevocably. To deviate from it now would be an act of such profound betrayal that it would poison any happiness we might otherwise snatch. We are defined by such promises, and I, for one, cannot be forsworn.
