The lamplight cast long, dancing shadows across the study, yet my gaze was fixed upon the iron-bound coffer that sat squat upon the rug. Within its cold, unyielding shell lay the Abernathy bonds—a fortune sufficient to secure a man's future, and, it must be said, to obliterate his past entirely. I had sworn a solemn promise to my ailing client that the box should be delivered untouched to his heir on the morrow. Yet, what was that promise now, weighed against the crushing reality of my own debts and the utter ruin that stared me in the face? A single, desperate act; a signature forged, a quiet journey to the continent, and I might live as a gentleman whilst his wastrel son would surely squander the inheritance on cards and horses. My hand trembled as it reached for the poker by the hearth—not for the fire, but for the heavy, unforgiving lock that stood between myself and salvation, or, as my conscience screamed in that silent room, my damnation.
