The recital had commenced under the most solemn auspices, yet a strange and terrible levity was soon to overtake the affair. The violinist, a man of ferocious temperament and no small opinion of himself, had just launched into a particularly torturous passage when his music stand, with a protesting groan, gave way and collapsed precipitously. A wave of consternation rippled through the assembled guests, but it was nothing to the paroxysm of fury which seized the performer. In his frantic effort to restore the fallen sheets, he overset a carafe of water, which cascaded directly upon the formidable bonnet of a dowager in the front row. The good lady let out a shriek of such piercing quality that it quite eclipsed the violin's lamentable squeak, and in that moment, as the musician stood frozen, his bow hanging limp and his face a mask of apoplectic horror, the entire room seemed to hold its breath before the inevitable, dreadful explosion of laughter.
