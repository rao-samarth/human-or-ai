A chill, quite independent of the night air, traced a path down my spine. Every instinct, every primitive impulse inherited from some remote and harried ancestor, screamed for concealment; to remain frozen in my bed and pray for the blessed relief of the dawn. For a long moment I did just that, a prisoner to a most palpable dread. And yet, what manner of man would I be to cower in my own chambers whilst some villain, some midnight intruder, roamed freely beneath my very roof? The question galvanised me. Though my heart hammered against my ribs, a frantic drumbeat against the absolute silence, my hand was quite steady as it closed upon the cold, heavy steel of the poker by the hearth. My course, however perilous, was set.
