Mr. Abernathy had mastered the new Electrified Accounting Engine, or so he informed every soul in the ledger office from the proprietor down to the boy who swept the floors. He gathered us all ‘round it, this gleaming brass-and-wire contraption, puffed out his chest like a pouter pigeon that’s just spied a particularly fine crumb, and declared it would calculate the quarterly earnings in less time than it takes a man to properly wax his mustache. With a flourish suited to a riverboat card sharp revealing a winning hand, he jabbed a prominent ivory key. The machine whirred, it clicked, it let out a most distressing groan—as if it had swallowed a bad oyster—and then, after a pregnant silence that stretched the length of a Sunday sermon, it spat out a single slip of paper.
