You wouldn't have believed the spectacle at the Mayor’s reception last evening, my dear sister. I witnessed poor Mr. Albright—a man whose entire fortune seems to be invested in the public’s estimation of him—receive what amounted to a cold shoulder from the visiting dignitary. A lesser man might have fetched another glass of punch and forgot the matter. But not Albright; no, he commenced a campaign of such frantic and conspicuous maneuvering to reclaim that lost sliver of regard—accosting mutual acquaintances, laughing a bit too loudly at the dignitary's distant remarks, and generally making a nuisance of himself—that he might as well have hired a town crier to announce his own triviality.
