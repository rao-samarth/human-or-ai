We were all packed tighter than buttons on a waistcoat along the thoroughfare when a dray-horse, having taken some private offense at its station in life, decided upon a sudden and violent retirement down the cobblestones. Pandemonium, of course, is the cheapest and most readily available public entertainment. As the women shrieked and the men flattened themselves against shop windows, a fellow in a coat far too respectable for the neighborhood lunged out and seized the beast’s bridle. A great roar of approval went up from the assembled gapers, who are always mightily impressed by any man who has not had the proper time to consider the consequences. They called it valor; I suspect it was merely an interruption of a particularly dull train of thought, a twitch of muscle before the brain could lodge a formal protest. Give a man a half-second more to calculate the odds, and he’ll discover a sudden and pressing engagement a block away, which is the truest sort of human instinct there is.
