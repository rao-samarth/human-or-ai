I was standing on a corner where the clamor of a thousand carriages threatened to unhinge a man’s very skeleton, when I saw him—William Hightower, a fellow I knew from our leaner days. Confound him, he was dressed in a suit so fine it likely cost more than my lodging for a year, and he had on his arm a lady of such cheerful beauty that looking at her was like staring into the sun. A miserable, acid sort of fire began to burn in my belly; it’s a peculiar affliction of the spirit, a poison you mix for your enemy but are forced to drink yourself.
