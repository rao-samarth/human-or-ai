There was a man down the lane whose whole mind was at last taken prisoner by the notion of a perfectly straight fence line. It began as a commonplace chore, the sort of thing any property owner might undertake on a Saturday, but it did not end there; it festered. Soon, you could see him at all hours, squinting down the length of the pickets with a surveyor’s transit, muttering about fractional deviations wholly imperceptible to a sane eye. He would unearth a post and reset it half a dozen times a day, hollowing out the earth around it until his yard resembled a prairie dog town after a flood.
