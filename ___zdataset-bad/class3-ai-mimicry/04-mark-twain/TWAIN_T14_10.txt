I watched the new lad at the stamping press, and you could see it plain in the set of his jaw; he moved with a certain righteous fury, as if he meant to wrestle a promotion from the very gears and levers through sheer will alone. A man gets the grand notion in his head that he is the architect of his own fortune, that every drop of his sweat is a coin paid toward a future of his own design—a future separate and superior to the clamorous present. And yet, I have seen a hundred such men, and a thousand such notions, come through that door. He does not yet see that he is only a part of the machine’s rhythm; no more the master of it than a single tooth on the great flywheel is the master of the engine. A slip of the foot, a foreman’s ill temper, a sudden sickness in the family—any stray current can throw the whole grand contrivance off its track, and all his striving amounts to no more than the frantic motion of a moth trapped inside a lantern, beating its wings against a fate
