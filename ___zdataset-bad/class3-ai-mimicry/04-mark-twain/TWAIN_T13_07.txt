On the steam-packet winding its way through some foreign and dusty territory, I made the acquaintance of a gentleman of prodigious certainty. He held court upon the upper deck, and with the unshakable authority of a man who has memorized the Creator’s own blueprints, he would identify every tedious hill and undistinguished thicket that slid past our view. Presently, he announced that a particularly celebrated cliff-face, a marvel of local legend, was soon to appear on the port bow; he spoke of its features with the fond familiarity of a man describing his own front porch. A good half-hour later, after his predicted landmark had failed to materialize, the true and actual marvel slid silently into view—on the starboard side.
