He paced the mossy bank of the stream, where the trout darted like silver shadows, yet his mind took no account of the tranquil scene. The letter in his breast-pocket—a single sheet of paper—seemed to possess a weight far beyond its substance, for it held the utter and complete ruination of his last rival. A quiet word to the proper authorities, a carefully placed hint, and the path to the coveted professorship would lie clear before him; a lifetime’s aspiration fulfilled at last. It was, by any cold calculation, the logical and most efficient course of action. But as he watched a kingfisher flash by, a jewel of impossible blue, a profound disquiet settled upon his soul. To seize such a prize by employing the methods of a back-alley blackmailer, to build one’s own monument upon the ashes of another’s good name—it was a bargain which, he suspected, would exact a toll far dearer than the glittering reward itself.
