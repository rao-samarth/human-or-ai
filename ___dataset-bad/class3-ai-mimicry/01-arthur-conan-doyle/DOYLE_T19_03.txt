From the corner of the rattling railway carriage, he observed his fellow passenger—a portly man whose expensive tweed suit and gold watch-chain proclaimed him a gentleman of considerable means and little worry. The fellow’s broad, good-humoured face seemed the very picture of provincial success, a man who might deal in grain or hold a controlling interest in some county bank. It was a most convincing performance, yet certain minute details betrayed the artist. For all his outward calm, the man’s thumb worried incessantly at the clasp of his valise, and his eyes, rather than resting upon the pleasant pastoral scenes fleeting past the window, would dart with a flicker of raw anxiety towards any new conductor or station-master who passed along the corridor. It was clear that the grand costume of prosperity was but a disguise, a hastily donned cloak to conceal the far leaner and more desperate creature who trembled within.
