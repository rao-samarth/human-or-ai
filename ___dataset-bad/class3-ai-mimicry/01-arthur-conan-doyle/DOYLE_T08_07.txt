There he is, by Jove!" exclaimed my young companion, his hand tightening upon my arm as we navigated the throng of Cheapside. He gestured with his chin towards a portly gentleman just then stepping into a cab—a notorious financier recently acquitted on some technicality. "The man is a villain, and yet the law sees him walk free. It is a damnable state of affairs." I laid a steadying hand upon his shoulder, for his face had grown quite flushed with indignation. "Control yourself, my dear fellow," I murmured, as the cab rattled away into the sea of traffic. "For what you desire is not the impartial application of a principle, but rather the very personal satisfaction of a grudge. The former, however imperfect its machinery, is what separates us from barbarism; the latter is merely a return to it under a more righteous-sounding name. When one man decides he is the sole arbiter of what is due to another, he does not serve the greater good—he merely serves his own passions, and in doing so, becomes a peril to all.
