He stood by the window, his silhouette a rigid black line against the grey twilight. “You must see that my position is an impossible one,” he stated, his voice devoid of all but a weary resignation. “The engagement was announced, the families are joined in all but the final ceremony; to retreat now would be a public disgrace of the first order, an act from which my name would never recover.” She did not weep, which somehow struck him as a far more potent form of accusation. Her hands remained quite still in her lap, her gaze fixed upon him with a dreadful clarity that pierced his carefully constructed logic. “So you would chain yourself to a life of hollow duty,” she said at last, her tone measured and low, “and call it honour? You would sacrifice what is true and vital for the mere appearance of rectitude? I ask you, sir, what manner of man is it who builds his house upon such a foundation of sand?”
