As the last vestiges of daylight bled from the sky, leaving the rolling countryside in a wash of grey and purple, my thoughts turned, as they so often did of late, to the unfortunate affair of Mr. Abernathy. It had been a wretched business, to be sure; a choice between my own good standing and the last tattered remnants of his. To have lent my name to his cause would have been to sink with him, a foolish act of charity which could not have saved the man but would most certainly have damned me in the estimation of those whose judgment I valued. And yet, the victory, if such it could be called, brought no satisfaction.
