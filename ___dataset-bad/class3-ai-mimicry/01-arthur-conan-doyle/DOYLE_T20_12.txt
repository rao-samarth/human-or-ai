We had taken our supper in a boisterous public dining-room, the air so thick with smoke and chatter that one could scarcely hear his own thoughts. At the adjacent table, however, a gentleman in clerical garb made certain his every word was heard, his voice rising in a dramatic sermon against the temptations of the flesh and the moral rot bred by idleness and avarice. It was a performance of some power, to be sure; yet I found my own attention drawn to the surreptitious manner in which his gaze repeatedly darted towards a young serving-girl, a look not of spiritual guidance but of a baser, more terrestrial appraisal. Here was a man who decried the mire with a passionate sincerity, all the while sinking to his own neck in the very same mud, and the sheer, wretched tragedy of it left a more bitter taste than the cheap claret upon my tongue.
