The clock upon the mantel had long since chimed three, yet sleep remained a stranger to the solitary figure in the study. It was not the fear of discovery that so tormented his constitution—for the unfortunate affair was his secret alone—but rather the ceaseless gnawing of a more intimate accuser. He had parted with the confidence so easily, a careless word dropped to an influential ear, assuring himself then that it was a trifle, a thing of no consequence. But consequence, he now understood, was a patient and terrible creditor, and the letter lying open upon his desk was its final, brutal reckoning.
