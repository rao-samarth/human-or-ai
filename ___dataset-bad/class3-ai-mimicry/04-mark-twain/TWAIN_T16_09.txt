The old mill creek was starting to get ideas above its station, turning from a lazy ribbon of brown water into a genuine torrent that threatened Mrs. Gable’s prize-winning petunias and, worse yet, the town’s only bridge. You could feel the whole community holding its breath, a collective pause that was heavier than the humid air. Now, a fella with no sand in his craw will stand on the bank and spectate, calculating the odds and jawing about what ought to be done—and we had a fine supply of those. But then young Silas, who was generally considered to have more freckles than sense, waded out into that rising mess without a word, a coil of rope slung over his shoulder, to lash the main support beam that had come loose. He was shaking, you could see it clear as day, but he kept going. It's a curious thing, ain't it? The world is full of loud-mouthed heroes in the making, but the real article is usually the one too busy and too plain scared to make a speech about it.
