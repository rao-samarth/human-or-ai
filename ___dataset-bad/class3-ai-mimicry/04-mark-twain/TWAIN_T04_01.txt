Sheriff Gable—a man whose notions of justice were as straight as a dog’s hind leg—got young Silas cornered betwixt the livery and the general store, right where the smell of hay and horse liniment was thick enough to chew. The lawman laid out a proposition so greasy with convenience that a fella could slide right into a clean conscience on it, without the bother of a proper confession. All Silas had to do, you see, was admit that the whole unfortunate affair with the deacon's missing cashbox was Jedediah's idea from top to bottom; a simple nod was all it would take to turn his own grievous sin into a mere boyish misstep, with a small reward to boot.
