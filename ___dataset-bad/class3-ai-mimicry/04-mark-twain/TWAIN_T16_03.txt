Mr. Abernathy had just finished laying out his grand new scheme for streamlining the department, a plan so magnificent in its foolishness that it was a genuine work of art, the kind of abomination a committee of half-wits might invent after a long lunch. A profound and holy silence fell over the conference room, the kind you’d expect in a church right after the preacher asks if anyone objects to the union. My own heart commenced a racket against my ribs that threatened to give the whole game away.
