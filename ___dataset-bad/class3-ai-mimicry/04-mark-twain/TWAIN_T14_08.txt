To hear those two fellows jawing at the next table, you'd think they personally instructed the sun when to rise and the river when to swell. Every scrap of their supposed success, which they lay out like a new suit of clothes, is credited to their own magnificent foresight and iron-willed decisions; a man would get the idea they were born with a map of their own lives already tucked in their fists. The plain fact of it is they are no more in command of their journey than a tumbleweed is in command of the wind that blows it.
