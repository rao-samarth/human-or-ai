You can observe the entire mechanics of a man’s fate laid out on the Sunday dinner table, right there between the roast and the pickled beets. My aunts, with the knowing and satisfied look of women who have successfully arranged a piece of furniture, would glance from my face to Eleanor’s, and you could see the future they’d built for us both taking shape in their minds. And Eleanor, mind you, is a fine and sensible woman—a fact which complicates the matter considerably, for there ain’t a single logical complaint a fellow could lodge against her. It is a peculiar sort of imprisonment, to be locked in a cage
