Now, that slice of apple pie was a masterpiece of Christian engineering, a regular monument to butter and righteousness. And yet, it sat on my tongue like a mouthful of dry sermons and sawdust—a consequence of my cousin Jedediah sitting opposite, staring into his plate with the sort of profound gloom usually reserved for a professional mourner. He had a notion, you see, that the south pasture ought to have been his, and he wasn't entirely wrong in that calculation; he was only wrong in thinking I'd ever admit it. The trouble with a man’s interior auditor—that fussy little bookkeeper who resides somewhere behind the ribs—is that he never sleeps.
