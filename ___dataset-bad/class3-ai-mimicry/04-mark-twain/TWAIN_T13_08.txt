There was a certain high-collared gentleman aboard our stage who had, for the better part of an hour, been holding forth on the indomitable character of his city-made boots; a marvel of polished leather and stout stitching, he assured us, altogether impervious to the sort of rustic inconveniences we were then suffering. He expounded upon their virtues with the tedious authority of a man who has never had to walk more than a city block in his life. Then the coach lurched into a particularly malevolent stretch of mire, and our man, needing to alight, stepped down with the grand air of a commodore taking a new shore.
