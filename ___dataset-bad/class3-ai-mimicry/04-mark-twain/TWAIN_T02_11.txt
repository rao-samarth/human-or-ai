Mr. Finch clapped me on the shoulder with a hand that felt as heavy as his public reputation, and the coin he pressed into my palm was of a warmer and more genuine gold than the man himself; for I had spent the better part of the morning staring at a ledger whose figures sang a song of pure thievery. He called me a “lad of sound principle,” a compliment that soured in my ears like milk left in the summer sun. Sound principle! Why, the man’s whole enterprise was built on a foundation of such rotted timber that a single honest word from me might bring the entire edifice down upon our heads—with my head, I reckoned, being the first to get flattened.
