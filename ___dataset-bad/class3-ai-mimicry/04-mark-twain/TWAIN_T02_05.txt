The collection plate, which was nothing more than the man’s own sweat-stained hat, made its solemn journey down the row of passengers on the port side of the steam-wheeler. The so-called Reverend up front was spinning a yarn about orphans in Arkansas with a voice so full of honeyed sorrow you’d think he’d buried half of them himself. It was a first-rate performance, and would have fetched a tear from a stone, had I not recognized the very same pious face from a three-card-monte table in St. Joe just two years prior, where his fortunes had been considerably less blessed.
