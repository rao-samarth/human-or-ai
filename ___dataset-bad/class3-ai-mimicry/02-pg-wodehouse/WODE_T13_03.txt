It is no overstatement to say that, as I navigated the maelstrom of Oxford Street, I was feeling rather pleased with the cut of my jib. I had just successfully executed a particularly tricky manoeuvre to avoid a perambulating vendor of roasted chestnuts, earning an appreciative nod from a nearby constable, and the whole episode had left me with the warm glow of a chap who is, in a quiet way, a master of the metropolitan arts. It was at this precise moment of self-congratulation that I spotted it: a crisp ten-pound note, nestling innocently by the kerb. The problem, however, lay in its proximity to the shoe of a large and intimidating-looking woman. A moral crossroads presented itself forthwith. To simply bend and retrieve the treasure risked her thinking I had pilfered it from her very person, yet to ask if it was hers risked the humiliation of having her say no, leaving me to scoop it up under her basilisk gaze.
