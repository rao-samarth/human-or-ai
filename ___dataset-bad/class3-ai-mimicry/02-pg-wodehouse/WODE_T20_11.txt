Percival Pootle, having just held the dining room captive with a twenty-minute oration on the sterling virtues of a simple, frugal life, was feeling rather pleased with himself; you could tell by the way he was adjusting his diamond cuff-links. He beamed at the assembled company, a benevolent monarch who has just outlawed something frightfully expensive that he himself happens to dislike. It was at this precise moment of self-congratulation that Beatrice, his cousin, glided towards him, a determined glint in her eye that spoke of choppy waters ahead for the S.S. Pootle. "Percy, dear," she began, her voice as sweet as poisoned honey, "that was a truly magnificent tribute to the humble turnip and the mended sock. So inspiring. Tell me, when you spoke of shunning all worldly fripperies, were you thinking of the new yacht before or after you had it christened with a magnum of the '04 champagne?"
