I slumped into the armchair, the silence of the late hour broken only by the mournful ticking of the grandfather clock—a sound that seemed to be counting off each second of my recent and spectacular social demise. An hour ago, I had been a titan, a colossus of the drawing-room, poised to deliver a witticism so perfectly constructed it would have made a bishop kick a hole in a stained-glass window. Instead, I had somehow managed to trip over the ottoman, launch a tray of canapés into the décolletage of a famously frosty dowager, and conclude my performance by landing face-first in the potted aspidistra.
