The little tea-room had been a haven of tranquil mastication until the chap at the next table, a beefy specimen with a tie that shrieked rather than spoke, decided to air his grievances. "And what's that supposed to mean?" he boomed, causing a nearby waitress to wobble dangerously with a tray of scones. His target, a prim-looking cove with a pince-nez and the general air of a man who irons his banknotes, merely blinked. "My good man," the pince-nez cove began, a phrase that seemed to add a dollop of petrol to the already blazing fire. "Don't you 'my good man' me!
