The absolute shock of it was the thing that truly unmanned him. He had squared his shoulders, puffed out his chest a trifle, and advanced upon their little table with all the misplaced confidence of a kitten tackling a bulldog, only to be stopped dead by the glacial stare of the elder female. It was a look that did more than merely dismiss; it vivisected, catalogued, and ultimately discarded him as an entirely unsuitable specimen, all in the space of a single, heart-stopping moment. The clatter of the tea-room seemed to fade to a dull roar in his ears. He felt the hot flush of shame creep up his neck, a ghastly, prickling sensation, as he saw the girl—the very object of his hopeful little expedition—glance away with a flicker of what looked horribly like pity. To be assessed and found wanting was one thing; to have it happen so publicly, to be rendered utterly invisible by a single, perfectly aimed aristocratic glare, was a blow from which the spirit, he felt, might never quite recover its bounce.
