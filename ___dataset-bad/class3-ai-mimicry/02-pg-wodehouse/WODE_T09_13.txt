It’s a dashed peculiar feeling, you know, to be adrift in the great human soup of Piccadilly on a Thursday afternoon while inside your personal geography a small, grim-faced cove in a bowler hat is methodically prodding your soul with a sharp stick. Every time a bus rumbled past, it seemed to be muttering ‘You let old Biffy down, you utter tick,’ and I couldn’t help but agree with its assessment. The memory of his trusting face, just before I’d administered the poltroon’s lie about a prior engagement involving a sick aunt, kept popping up like a jack-in-the-box designed by a particularly malevolent fiend. A proper stinker, that was. It makes a fellow feel like a bad egg in a world of perfectly respectable omelettes, and frankly, it rather takes the fizz out of one’s day.
