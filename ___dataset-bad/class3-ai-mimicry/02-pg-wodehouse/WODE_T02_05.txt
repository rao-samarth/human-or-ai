It was a profound and sobering thought that a chap’s entire social existence could be brought to ruin by a single, ill-advised moustache. Young Cuthbert Finch, tiptoeing past the library at a quarter past two in the morning, felt this with a poignancy that was quite unnerving. To have presented himself to his Aunt Agatha’s new ward, the spirited Penelope, as a rugged explorer just back from the Amazon was one thing; to have glued a frightful bit of badger fur to his upper lip to support the narrative was quite another. The dashed thing had begun to itch abominably during the cheese course, and he now had the ghastly sensation that it was peeling away from starboard, threatening to reveal him not as a seasoned adventurer, but as the callow, clean-shaven youth he actually was. He knew with a dreadful certainty that the moment of discovery was nigh, and when it came, the ensuing explosion would make the eruption of Krakatoa look like a child popping a paper bag.
