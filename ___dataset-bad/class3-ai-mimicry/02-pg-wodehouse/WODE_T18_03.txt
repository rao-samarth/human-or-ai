It was Smedley’s firm conviction, a belief he held with the quiet tenacity of a bulldog chewing a favourite slipper, that all knots could be improved by the addition of at least three more loops than strictly necessary. This particular theory, while no doubt fascinating to the student of advanced string-mangling, was having a rather unfortunate effect on the simple business of affixing the 'Grand Village Fête' banner above the vicarage gate. The Reverend Pootle, a man who viewed all practical matters with the nervous apprehension of a cat in a room full of rocking chairs, was offering advice that helped not a jot. Hence, at the precise moment Lady Hemlock arrived bearing her prize-winning Victoria sponge, the entire contraption—Smedley's masterpiece of a knot included—parted company with the gatepost. The banner descended not with a gentle flutter but with the purposeful swoop of a hunting owl, enveloping the grande dame in a cloud of canvas and righteous indignation.
