Algernon shifted his weight from one patent-leather shoe to the other, a manoeuvre which did little to alleviate the sensation that his collar had shrunk a size or two in the clammy city air. Beatrice, meanwhile, regarded him with the cool, analytical eye of a biologist examining a particularly disappointing amoeba. "You honestly see nothing amiss, do you?" she said, her voice cutting cleanly through the din of passing omnibuses. "You've trodden on poor old Freddie's aspirations as if they were a stray banana peel on the pavement, filched Cuthbert's finest idea without so much as a by-your-leave, and for what? A slightly plumper directorship and the dubious honour of being seen at dinner with that frightful bounder, Sir Oswald. It was all so splendidly simple in your head, I suppose, but I do wish you'd look at the wreckage, Algy. One can't simply climb a ladder by sawing off the rungs beneath one's chums; it's frightfully bad form and, sooner or later, leads to a most undignified tumble."
