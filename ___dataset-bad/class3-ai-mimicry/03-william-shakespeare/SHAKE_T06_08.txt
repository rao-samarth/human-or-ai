My cousin stands beside my grandsire's chair, and every gilded word of praise the old man speaks plants a fresh thorn within my heart. They laud his new-bought lands, his ventures blessed by chance, and the fair countenance of his bride; whilst I, who have toiled with no less sweat, am bid to stand a silent effigy, my own small gains mere shadows to his sun. How easily he smiles, how gracefully he doth incline his head to such acclaim! Perchance he cannot see the venom that turns my celebratory wine to bitterest wormwood, for his cup is ever full.
