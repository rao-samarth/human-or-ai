Lord Valerius, his doublet heavy with silver thread, gestured with a wine-filled goblet toward the throng of dancers. "Behold, young sir, the grand pageant," he mused, his voice a low rumble beneath the lute's high melody. "Dost thou truly think each one has chosen his partner, his step, his place in this great hall? Nay, for I see the hand of chance in every turn, the pull of unseen strings that make these puppets leap and bow." The young courtier beside him bristled, his own ambition a fire in his eyes.
