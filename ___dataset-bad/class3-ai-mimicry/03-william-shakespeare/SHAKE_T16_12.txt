My own blood turned to ice, and my tongue, a faithless servant, clave to the roof of my mouth. To rise against this man was ruin; a single word of defiance would be a warrant for my own end. Yet, how could I sit mute whilst such a venomous lie took root? Nay, better to perish for the truth than to live a long life nourished by the coward's portion of silence. This trembling in my limbs is but a fleeting ague, a phantom, which must be banished ere it masters me.
