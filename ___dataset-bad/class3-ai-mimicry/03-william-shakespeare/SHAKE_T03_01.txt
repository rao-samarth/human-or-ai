You ask wherefore I didst such a thing, and with such cruel dispatch. Know then, that in this world, a man possesses naught of truer worth than the good report he carries amongst his fellows, a fragile jewel that, once cracked by foul-tongued calumny, can ne'er be mended to its former gleam. The whisper 'gainst my house had grown to a clamour, a vile and pestilent noise that threatened to undo all my fathers had wrought; therefore, I struck, and silenced it. What is a purse heavy with gold if the hand that holds it be pointed at in scorn? I chose the lesser grief, to banish one man's folly that the virtue of a whole family might not be forfeit to the marketplace of gossip. Let them call me harsh in this quiet corner of the world; 'tis a far kinder judgment than to be known as the son of a scoundrel, a title he would have purchased for us all with his loose and wicked tongue.
