He did spy the plume of smoke, not from a friendly hearth, but a foul and ravenous blackness that did climb the heavens with a wicked haste. The wind, that tyrant of the open fields, did howl a dreadful song, and a cold dread did lay its hand upon his heart, making a frantic drum of it. For what awaited him there but certain peril, a furnace-maw to consume him quite? His very limbs, which ere now had served him well, felt bound by some invisible chain, held fast by that prudent fiend which whispers to all men of their own mortality. Yet reason is a poor and feeble king when set against the clamour of a soul. Within that doomed and crackling frame a life did gasp, and to stand idle whilst it perished were a death of a far fouler kind. And so, though every sinew cried for flight, he cast his doubt aside as one might cast a ragged cloak, and ran against the wind toward the flame
