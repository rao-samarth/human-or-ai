Do not be so amazed, good cousin, that such a fawning wretch should rise whilst true merit begs for alms upon the street. The world is not some open field where the strongest stalk doth win the sun, but is, in truth, a well-kept garden where the place of every bloom is set by the gardener’s hand alone. This knowledge is not writ in any book, nor spoken plain from any pulpit; ‘tis a grammar of the soul, a silent music to which all men, willing or no, must keep their step. That honest clerk you so commend, for all his nimble figures, hath the accent of the clay from whence he sprang. His virtue is a coarser cloth, you see, unfit to be fashioned for a courtier’
