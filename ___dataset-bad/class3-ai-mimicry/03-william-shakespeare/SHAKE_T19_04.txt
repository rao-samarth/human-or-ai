In this humble parish, what pleasantries are exchanged when neighbour meets neighbour in the market square; a bow here, a kind word there, the very air seems thick with goodly fellowship. And yet, what poison doth fester beneath such a comely skin? The man who grips thy hand with warmest zeal may, in his heart, be plotting thy swift ruin; the wife whose tears do flow for a lost bauble, perchance, hath a soul as dry and barren as the winter dust for her husband's true sorrows. Thus we do all perform upon this cobbled stage, each soul a player with a well-rehearsed part, whilst true substance hides its form from common view.
