Hark, what sudden tempest breaks upon this tranquil lea? The Steward, puffing with a borrowed might, unroll'd his master’s edict, its cruel ink a blot upon the afternoon. He spoke of tithes, of fealty, of service to be rendered, his voice a drone against the buzzing of the bees. A silence answer’d him, so deep and strange it prick’d the very air. Then from the crowd, a single stone, ill-aimed but bold, flew past his ear and struck the ancient parish oak. A gasp, a moment's doubt, and then another stone did fly, and then a score! They came not from one hand but all, a rattling hail of peasant's wrath. The Steward, his face as pale as whey, did stammer, clutch his precious scroll, and turn his heel; for he saw that the sullen earth itself had risen up, and that the meek, so long in harness, had at last grown teeth and would be driven forth no more.
