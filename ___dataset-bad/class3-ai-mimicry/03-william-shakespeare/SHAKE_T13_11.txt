He sat amongst his kinsfolk, a prince dethroned within his own imagining. But an hour past, his voice was the sole music in the hall, his every pronouncement a decree, his jests the cause of forced and ringing laughter. He had painted for them such a vision of his own great consequence, a man to whom fortune herself had bowed. Yet now, a single, softly spoken truth from his grandsire's lips had pricked the swollen bladder of his vanity, and all his airy boasts were spilled upon the floor for naught. He looked upon the dregs of wine within his cup, seeing therein the reflection of a fool. The quiet murmur of the others' talk went on around him, yet he heard it not; for the silence in his own soul was a roaring tempest, and he wished the very earth would swallow him and his shame together. The feast grew cold before him, for he could not eat, his throat being choked with the dust of his own fallen monument.
