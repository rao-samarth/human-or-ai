Prithee, unmask this riddle for me, for my ears do yet ring with your grand sermon on the lean joys of abstinence. A finer oration on the virtue of an empty purse I have ne'er heard. Yet what spectacle greets my eye? A chamber so laden with foreign silks and sugared dainties it doth quite overwhelm the senses. Doth this gilded cup of Rhenish wine serve only to wet a throat made hoarse from proclaiming public virtue, and are these roasted larks merely props to illustrate the sin of gluttony? Forsooth, you paint a saint with your tongue, a man content with water
