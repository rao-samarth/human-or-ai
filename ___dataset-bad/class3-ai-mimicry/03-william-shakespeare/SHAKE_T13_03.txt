He walked the fallow field, where winter’s breath had left the soil a hard and silent thing, much like the stone that now sat within his own breast. But an hour past, he had strutted like a peacock, his words gilded with a certainty that now seemed the most wretched folly. He had proclaimed his wit a blade that none could turn, his fortunes as secure as the firmament itself. Wherefore, then, did the memory of a simple farmer’s quiet smile burn him so? Aye, 'twas a hotter brand than any iron, for it had seared not his skin, but the very substance of his vanity. The wind, which once he would have hailed as a herald to his consequence, did now but whisper through the barren hedgerow the one word his soul could not abide: fool. He was a hollow drum, whose loudest boast had served only to announce its emptiness to all the world.
