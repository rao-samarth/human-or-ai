The weight of the polished gold statue was nothing compared to the pressure in my chest as I approached the podium. A thousand smiling faces blurred into a sea of glittering light, their applause a distant, roaring wave. The prepared speech in my pocket, full of polite thank-yous and benign anecdotes, felt like a coward’s shield. My heart hammered against my ribs, a frantic drumbeat of pure terror at the words that were actually forming on my tongue—the true, dangerous words. I could see the network cameras, the powerful executives in the front row, the entire future I had so carefully constructed. To say nothing was safe, an easy path back to my seat. But the silence that followed would be a condemnation of its own. My throat was sandpaper. I let the notecard slip from my trembling fingers, watching it flutter to the stage, and leaned into the microphone. A deep, shaky breath, and then I began.
