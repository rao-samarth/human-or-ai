The network-enforced quiet was supposed to settle over the house at eleven. Yet here, in the dim glow of the monitor, a single line of code rerouted the mandatory shutdown command into a harmless data loop. It was a small circumvention, creating a pocket of unauthorized activity in the dead of night where none was permitted. They called it efficiency. But he saw it as a gradual tightening, a slow removal of choice disguised as civic responsibility. The system wasn't designed to be oppressive; it was designed to be helpful, to manage resources for the greater good, which made the act of defiance feel less like a protest and more like a necessary correction. He watched the diagnostic program confirm the bypass was stable and then opened a second, unsanctioned browser window, simply because he could.
