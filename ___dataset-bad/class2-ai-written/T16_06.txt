The last light of the day bled across the fields, painting the long grass in shades of orange and purple. Elias saw the mare, Willow, caught in the old fence line separating the properties. A rusty strand of barbed wire was twisted tight around her back leg, and her eyes were wide with a terror that made his own stomach clench. He knew he should run for help, that getting close to a panicked animal of that size was foolish for someone like him. Every muscle in his body screamed to back away. Yet, he saw the blood starting to darken her white sock and the way she trembled, too exhausted to struggle further. The silence of the evening was broken only by her ragged breathing and the frantic thumping of his own heart. Taking a slow, deliberate breath, he pushed aside the image of a flailing hoof and began walking toward her, keeping his voice low and steady.
