He had been leaning forward across the small cafe table, hands gesturing expansively as he detailed the brilliance of his strategy. His voice, pitched just a bit too loudly for the quiet afternoon crowd, painted a picture of inevitable triumph, of a corporate ladder he was ascending with unstoppable force. His companion simply nodded, nursing a cup of tea, a silent audience to the performance. Then, in a lull between pronouncements, she spoke, her words no more than a murmur. “They gave the promotion to Michael this morning.” The man’s hands fell to the table. He seemed to shrink, the confident posture deflating like a punctured lung until he was just a man staring into his half-empty coffee cup. He picked up his spoon and began to stir the cold liquid, a small, repetitive motion in a sudden, vast silence that his own boasting had created.
