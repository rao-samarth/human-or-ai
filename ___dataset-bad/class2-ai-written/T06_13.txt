The only light came from the blue-white glow of his partner’s phone, illuminating the nightstand for a brief moment before fading back to black. It was enough. He’d seen the name on the screen, a name he recognized from her recent stories about the new office. A tight, cold knot formed in his stomach. He listened to the steady rhythm of her breathing beside him in the darkness and imagined her life there, a world full of inside jokes and shared lunches he would never be a part of. She was building something new, something exciting, while he felt like he was just treading water in the life they had made together. That single, silent notification felt like a stone dropping into a still pond, its ripples spreading out to reveal a chasm between them he hadn't realized was so wide. He rolled onto his back, staring at the ceiling, feeling the familiar, acidic burn of something he couldn't name.
