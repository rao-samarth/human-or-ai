I calmly pointed to the official rulebook, explaining that the system only works if we all agree to its impartial framework, ensuring a balanced and predictable outcome for everyone involved. Aunt Carol, however, waved her hand dismissively. “Oh, don’t be so stuffy,” she said, sliding him a "get out of jail free" card from her own stash. “We’re not here for cold, hard regulations; we’re here to settle scores.” It quickly became clear that one person’s idea of a structured, equitable contest was another’s golden opportunity to finally get even for decades-old grievances, with the final arbiter being whoever could shout the loudest over the rattling of dice.
