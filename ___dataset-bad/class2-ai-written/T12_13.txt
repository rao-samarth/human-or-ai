The cursor blinked rhythmically at the end of the subject line, a tiny, pulsing beacon in the otherwise dark office. Marcus shouldn't have opened the attachment. He knew that the instant he saw the file name—"Acquisition_Targets_CONFIDENTIAL"—and realized it wasn't meant for his department, or even his company. Yet, he had clicked. Now, the spreadsheet glowed on his screen, a map of a competitor's vulnerabilities and a clear path to an easy, brutal victory for his own firm. His mind raced, calculating the commission, the promotion, the corner office that would surely follow if he just forwarded this to his director. It would be so simple. A single click was the only thing standing between him and everything he'd been working for. He could just pretend it was an anonymous tip; no one would ever have to know the truth of its origin, or the ethics he had quietly set aside in the silent hum of the server room.
