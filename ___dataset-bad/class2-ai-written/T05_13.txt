The microphone felt slick and unnervingly heavy in his palm. He was supposed to be talking about Mark, his friend since the days of scraped knees and shared juice boxes. He’d even rehearsed the anecdote about the time Mark crashed his dad’s mower into the Klines’ prize-winning petunias; it was a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. Then his eyes found Clara, the wedding planner, signaling the DJ from the back of the room with a crisp, no-nonsense flick of her wrist. He opened his mouth, ready to launch into the petunia story, but what nearly came out was a detailed, heartfelt appreciation for how she’d managed to get a gluten-free cake delivered on thirty minutes’ notice after a catering disaster. Mark and his new wife were beaming at him, completely oblivious to the internal debate raging behind his fixed smile. He cleared his throat, took a breath, and focused on the single, most important promise he’d ever made: don’t make this about you. It was, he had to admit, a promise he was struggling to keep.
