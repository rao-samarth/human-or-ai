The cursor blinked in the empty message field, a patient, rhythmic pulse in the deep quiet of the house. We so often speak of allegiance as if it were a grand, public declaration, a flag planted for all to see. But that’s the easy part, the performance. The true substance of it is decided in these silent, solitary moments, when an opportunity arises that requires you to quietly redraw the map of your loyalties. It’s here, with only the low hum of the refrigerator for an audience, that you discover what a promise is actually made of. Is it a flimsy shelter, abandoned at the first sign of a storm, or is it a load-bearing wall in the architecture of your own character? The choice is never as loud as you’d think; it is often just the simple, unobserved act of typing a message or closing the screen.
