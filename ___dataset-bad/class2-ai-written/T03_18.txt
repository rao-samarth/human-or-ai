The low hum of the dryers filled the quiet space between them. He stared into the spinning clothes, not at the younger man beside him. "You think it was just about the money, about the building," he said, his voice barely a murmur. "You don't understand what was coming. Our name was about to be a joke, a piece of town gossip told with a sneer. I could hear it already. So I made a choice. I let the house go, let them take it all, just to make the noise stop. I know what we lost. I walk past that empty lot every day. But what we kept is the silence. It’s a heavy, expensive silence, but it’s ours. We can still hold our heads up, and for me, that had to be enough."
