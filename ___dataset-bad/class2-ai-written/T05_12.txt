The old truck groaned under the weight of the last hay bale, and from the driver's seat, he could see the lights of his mother's house flicker on against the twilight. He had promised her he wouldn't be late for dinner again, a small promise that was part of a much larger, unspoken one to stay and manage the farm. But then his phone buzzed. It was a picture from Maya, a city skyline at sunset with the simple caption, "The train leaves at 10. There's a ticket for you." His fingers hovered over the screen, the image a vivid portal to a world of noise, art, and anonymity he craved. He looked back at the familiar silhouette of the barn and the warm, waiting light from the kitchen window. Every ounce of him was anchored here by a debt of affection and responsibility, yet his heart was a frantic bird beating against his ribs, desperate for the sky Maya was offering.
